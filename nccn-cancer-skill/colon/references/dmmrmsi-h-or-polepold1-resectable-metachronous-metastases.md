# dMMR/MSI-H or POLE/POLD1: Resectable Metachronous Metastases (COL-15)

<!-- Source: NCCN Colon Cancer v2.2026, pages 23-24 -->

## Overview

Patients with dMMR/MSI-H or POLE/POLD1 mutation with ultra-hypermutated phenotype (e.g., TMB >50 mut/Mb) presenting with resectable metachronous metastases [p.23].

## Initial Treatment

### Resection and/or Local Therapies [p.23]

- **Resection** is preferred over locally ablative procedures [p.23]
- For small liver lesions (≤3 cm), thermal ablation is equivalent to resection [p.23]
- Other local techniques can be considered for liver oligometastases when thermal ablation is not indicated (COL-C and COL-E) [p.23]
- For lung metastases, surgery, thermal ablation, and SBRT can be considered (COL-C and COL-E) [p.23]
- Hepatic artery infusion ± systemic chemotherapy (VEGF inhibitor contraindicated) (Category 2B) is also an option at institutions with experience in both the surgical and medical oncologic aspects of this procedure [p.23]

### Checkpoint Inhibitor Immunotherapy [p.23]

For patients with **no previous immunotherapy**:
- **Preferred**: Checkpoint inhibitor immunotherapy (COL-D 5 of 17) [p.23]

### Chemotherapy Options [p.23]

For patients with **previous immunotherapy or contraindication**:
- FOLFOX or CAPEOX [p.23]
- **or** FOLFOX + atezolizumab [p.23]
- **or** CAPEOX + atezolizumab (role of adjuvant immunotherapy not defined) [p.23]
- **or** Observation (preferred for previous oxaliplatin-based therapy) [p.23]

## Adjuvant Treatment [p.23]

Up to 6 months perioperative treatment options:

- **Resection** and/or local therapies [p.23]
- **or** Neoadjuvant chemotherapy (2-3 months) [p.23]
  - FOLFOX or CAPEOX [p.23]
- **or** Observation [p.23]

### Post-Treatment [p.23]

After initial therapy:
- FOLFOX or CAPEOX [p.23]
- **or** Observation [p.23]
- **or** Resection and/or local therapies [p.23]

## Follow-Up: Unresectable Metastases

### Initial Treatment for Unresectable Disease [p.24]

For patients **candidate for immunotherapy and no prior immunotherapy received**:
- **Preferred**: Checkpoint inhibitor immunotherapy (COL-D 5 of 17) [p.24]

For patients with **prior immunotherapy received**:
- **Preferred**: Continue checkpoint inhibitor immunotherapy (COL-D 5 of 17) [p.24]
- **or** Ipilimumab + nivolumab (if checkpoint inhibitor monotherapy was previously received) [p.24]
- **or** Systemic therapy (COL-D) [p.24]
- **or** Surgery ± RT [p.24]

### Subsequent Treatment [p.24]

- Re-evaluate disease status every 2-3 months [p.24]
- Surveillance (COL-8) [p.24]
- Continue immunotherapy (COL-D 5 of 17) [p.24]
- **or** Ipilimumab + nivolumab (if checkpoint inhibitor monotherapy was previously received) [p.24]
- **or** Systemic therapy (COL-D) [p.24]

## Additional Notes

- **Note**: All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.23, p.24]
- Patients who are not candidates for immunotherapy should be treated as recommended for pMMR/MSS disease. See NCCN Guidelines for Management of Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor-Related Toxicities [p.23]
- If disease response, consider discontinuing checkpoint inhibitor after 2 years of treatment [p.24]
- Imaging principles: See COL-A [p.23]
