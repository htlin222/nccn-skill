## TNM Staging System

Staging in colon cancer is based on the TNM system [p.81]. The TNM categories reflect very similar survival outcomes for rectal and colon cancer; these diseases therefore share the same staging system [p.81].

### T Classification (AJCC 8th Edition)

In the 8th edition of the AJCC Staging Manual [p.81]:

- **T1 tumors**: Involve the submucosa [p.81]
- **T2 tumors**: Penetrate through the submucosa into the muscularis propria [p.81]
- **T3 tumors**: Penetrate through the muscularis propria [p.81]
- **T4a tumors**: Directly penetrate to the surface of the visceral peritoneum [p.81]
- **T4b tumors**: Directly invade or are adherent to other organs or structures [p.81]

The T component of colon cancer staging is very important in prognostication, because analyses have shown that patients with T4,N0 tumors have a lower survival than those with T1–2,N1–2 tumors [p.81].

#### T4 Survival Analysis

In an analysis of 109,953 patients with invasive colon cancer included in the SEER colon cancer database from 1992 to 2004, the relative 5-year survival rate (5-year survival corrected by age-related morbidity) was considerably higher for patients with node-negative T4a tumors compared with patients with node-negative T4b tumors [p.81]:
- T4a (node-negative): 79.6% 5-year survival [p.81]
- T4b (node-negative): 58.4% 5-year survival [p.81]

### N Classification (Regional Lymph Node)

Regional lymph node classification [p.81]:

- **N1a**: 1 positive lymph node [p.81]
- **N1b**: 2–3 positive lymph nodes [p.81]
- **N2a**: 4–6 positive nodes [p.81]
- **N2b**: 7 or more positive nodes [p.81]
- **N1c**: Tumor deposit(s) in the subserosa, mesentery, or non-peritonealized pericolic or perirectal tissues without regional nodal metastasis (satellite tumor nodules) [p.81]

Within each T stage, survival is inversely correlated with N stage (N0, N1a, N1b, N2a, and N2b) [p.81].

### M Classification (Metastatic Disease)

Metastatic disease classification [p.81]:

- **M1a**: Metastases limited to only one site/solid organ (including lymph nodes outside the primary tumor regional drainage area) [p.81]
- **M1b**: Metastases to multiple distant sites or solid organs, exclusive of peritoneal carcinomatosis [p.81]
- **M1c**: Peritoneal carcinomatosis with or without blood-borne metastasis to visceral organs (8th edition AJCC Cancer Staging Manual) [p.81]

Patients with peritoneal metastases have a shorter PFS and OS than those without peritoneal involvement [p.81].
