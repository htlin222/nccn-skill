# Recurrence and Workup (COL-9) -- Algorithm Reference

<!-- Source: NCCN Colon Cancer v2.2026, page 17 -->

## Recurrence Detection and Initial Workup

### Clinical Presentation [p.17]

**Serial CEA elevation** triggers the workup pathway [p.17].

### Initial Workup Procedures [p.17]

When serial CEA elevation is detected:

- **Physical examination** [p.17]
- **Colonoscopy** [p.17]
- **Chest/Abdomen/Pelvis (C/A/P) CT with contrast** [p.17]

### FDG-PET/CT Consideration [p.17]

**Consider FDG-PET/CT** if initial imaging findings are equivocal [p.17].

## Diagnostic Pathways

### Negative Findings [p.17]

If workup shows **negative findings**:

- **Consider FDG-PET/CT** [p.17]
- **Re-evaluate C/A/P CT with contrast in 3 months** [p.17]
- Proceed to surveillance monitoring [p.17]

### Positive Findings: Documented Metachronous Metastases [p.17]

If imaging confirms **documented metachronous metastases** by CT, MRI, and/or biopsy [p.17]:

Classification by resectability status [p.17]:

- **Resectable** (See [COL-10](pmmrmss-findings-primary-treatment-colon-cancer-appropriate-for-resection-evidence.md) for pMMR/MSS treatment or COL-15 for dMMR/MSI-H treatment) [p.17]
- **Unresectable, potentially convertible or unconvertible** (See [COL-11](pmmrmss-findings-primary-treatment-colon-cancer-appropriate-for-resection-evidence.md) for pMMR/MSS treatment or COL-16 for dMMR/MSI-H treatment) [p.17]

## Molecular Testing for Metachronous Metastases [p.17]

For patients with documented metachronous metastases, determination of tumor gene status is required:

**Tumor Gene Status:**
- KRAS mutations [p.17]
- NRAS mutations [p.17]
- BRAF mutations [p.17]
- HER2 (ERBB2) overexpression/amplification [p.17]

**Testing approach:** Individually or as part of tissue-based or blood-based multi-gene panel testing (MGPT) [p.17]. MGPT has the ability to pick up rare and actionable mutations and gene fusions [p.17].

**Mismatch Repair (MMR) Status:**
- Determination of tumor MMR or MSI status (if not previously done) [p.17]

See Principles of Pathologic Review (COL-B) for KRAS, NRAS, and BRAF Mutation Testing and MSI or MMR Testing [p.17].

## Multidisciplinary Evaluation [p.17]

Patients should be evaluated by a multidisciplinary team including surgical consultation for patients with potentially resectable disease [p.17].

## Treatment Pathways

### pMMR/MSS Disease

- **Resectable metachronous metastases:** See [COL-10](pmmrmss-findings-primary-treatment-colon-cancer-appropriate-for-resection-evidence.md) [p.17]
- **Unresectable metachronous metastases:** See [COL-11](pmmrmss-findings-primary-treatment-colon-cancer-appropriate-for-resection-evidence.md) [p.17]

### dMMR/MSI-H or POLE/POLD1 Mutation Disease

For patients with dMMR/MSI-H or POLE/POLD1 mutation with ultra-hypermutated phenotype (e.g., TMB >50 mut/Mb):

- **Resectable metachronous metastases:** See [COL-15](pmmrmss-findings-primary-treatment-colon-cancer-appropriate-for-resection-evidence.md) [p.17]
- **Unresectable metachronous metastases:** See [COL-16](pmmrmss-findings-primary-treatment-colon-cancer-appropriate-for-resection-evidence.md) [p.17]

Note: Patients who are not candidates for immunotherapy should be treated as recommended for pMMR/MSS disease. See NCCN Guidelines for Management of Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor-Related Toxicities [p.17].

---

*All recommendations are category 2A unless otherwise indicated* [p.17]
