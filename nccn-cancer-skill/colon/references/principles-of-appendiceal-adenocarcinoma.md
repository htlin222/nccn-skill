## Summary of Key Evidence

**Note: All recommendations are (Category 2A) unless otherwise indicated** [p.63]

## Risk Assessment for Stage II Disease

### Patient/Physician Discussion

Patient/physician discussion should take place regarding the potential risks of therapy compared to potential benefits, including prognosis [p.63]. This should include:

- Discussion of evidence supporting treatment [p.63]
- Assumptions of benefit from indirect evidence [p.63]
- Morbidity associated with treatment [p.63]
- High-risk characteristics [p.63]
- Patient preferences [p.63]

### Determining Adjuvant Therapy Eligibility

When determining if adjuvant therapy should be administered, the following should be taken into consideration [p.63]:

- Number of lymph nodes analyzed after surgery (less than 12) [p.63]
- Poor prognostic features (e.g., poorly differentiated histology [exclusive of those that are MSI-H]; lymphatic/vascular invasion; bowel obstruction; PNI; localized perforation; close, indeterminate, or positive margins; or high tumor budding) [p.63]
- Assessment of other comorbidities and anticipated life expectancy [p.63]

### Key Efficacy Findings

- The benefit of adjuvant chemotherapy does not improve survival by more than 5% [p.63]
- MSI or MMR testing (COL-B 4 of 9) [p.63]

## First-Line Therapy Evidence

### Stage III Disease Chemotherapy

**CAPEOX or FOLFOX superiority** [p.64]

- CAPEOX or FOLFOX is superior to fluorouracil/leucovorin for patients with stage III colon cancer [p.64]

**Capecitabine efficacy** [p.64]

- Capecitabine appears to be equivalent to bolus fluorouracil/leucovorin in patients with stage III colon cancer [p.64]

### Stage II Disease Chemotherapy

**Oxaliplatin addition findings** [p.64]

- A survival benefit has not been demonstrated for the addition of oxaliplatin to fluorouracil/leucovorin in stage II colon cancer [p.64]
- FOLFOX is reasonable for patients with stage II colon cancer with multiple high-risk factors and is not indicated for patients with good- or average-risk stage II colon cancer [p.64]

### Duration of Therapy

**3 vs 6 months CAPEOX in Stage III** [p.64]

- While noninferiority of 3 versus 6 months of CAPEOX has not been proven, 3 months of CAPEOX numerically appeared similar to 6 months of CAPEOX for 5-year overall survival (82.1% vs. 81.2%; hazard ratio [HR], 0.96), with considerably less toxicity [p.64]
- These results support the use of 3 months of adjuvant CAPEOX over 6 months of adjuvant CAPEOX in the vast majority of patients with stage III colon cancer [p.64]

**Low-risk stage III disease** [p.64]

- In patients with colon cancer staged as T1-3, N1 (low-risk stage III), 3 months of CAPEOX is noninferior to 6 months of CAPEOX for DFS [p.64]
- Noninferiority of 3 versus 6 months of FOLFOX has not been proven [p.64]

**High-risk stage III disease** [p.64]

- In patients with colon cancer staged as T4, N1-2 or T any, N2 (high-risk stage III), 3 months of FOLFOX is inferior to 6 months of FOLFOX for DFS [p.64]
- Noninferiority of 3 versus 6 months of CAPEOX has not been proven [p.64]

**Neurotoxicity reduction** [p.64]

- Grade 3+ neurotoxicity rates are lower for patients who receive 3 versus 6 months of treatment (3% vs. 16% for FOLFOX; 3% vs. 9% for CAPEOX) [p.64]

**High-risk stage II disease** [p.64]

- A pooled analysis of patients with high-risk stage II disease in the IDEA collaboration did not show noninferiority of 3 months compared to 6 months of adjuvant treatment [p.64]
- Similar to stage III, the duration of therapy was associated with a small (and not statistically significant) difference in DFS between 3 and 6 months of CAPEOX [p.64]
- There were significantly fewer grade 3-5 toxicities with 3 versus 6 months [p.64]

### Immunotherapy Addition

**Atezolizumab with mFOLFOX6 in MSI-H/dMMR** [p.64]

- The addition of atezolizumab to mFOLFOX6 is superior to mFOLFOX6 alone with a 3-year DFS rate of 86.4% versus 76.6% (HR, 0.50; 2-sided P-value: <.0001) in patients with stage III (any T, N1-2 M0) dMMR/MSI-H colon cancer [p.64]

## Chemotherapy Regimens

### Modified FOLFOX6 [p.65]

- Oxaliplatin 85 mg/m² IV, Day 1
- Leucovorin 400 mg/m² IV, Day 1
- Fluorouracil 400 mg/m² IV bolus on Day 1, followed by 1200 mg/m²/day x 2 days (total 2400 mg/m² over 46-48 hours) continuous infusion
- Repeat every 2 weeks [p.65]

### Capecitabine [p.65]

- Capecitabine 1000-1250 mg/m² PO twice daily for 14 days every 3 weeks [p.65]

### FOLFOX + Atezolizumab [p.65]

- Atezolizumab 840 mg IV every 2 weeks for 12 cycles (6 months) then maintenance Atezolizumab 840 mg IV every 2 weeks for 13 cycles (12 months total) [p.65]

### CAPEOX + Atezolizumab [p.65]

- Atezolizumab 1200 mg IV every 3 weeks for 8 cycles (6 months) then maintenance Atezolizumab 1200 mg IV every 3 weeks for 8 cycles (12 months total) [p.65]

### CAPEOX [p.65]

- Oxaliplatin 130 mg/m² IV Day 1
- Capecitabine 1000 mg/m² PO twice daily for 14 days every 3 weeks [p.65]

### Fluorouracil/Leucovorin Regimens [p.65]

**Weekly bolus** [p.65]

- Leucovorin 500 mg/m² given as a 2-hour infusion and repeated weekly x 6
- Fluorouracil 500 mg/m² given bolus 1 hour after the start of Leucovorin and repeated 6 x weekly
- Every 8 weeks for 4 cycles [p.65]

**Simplified biweekly infusional (sLV5FU2)** [p.65]

- Leucovorin 400 mg/m² IV Day 1, followed by Fluorouracil bolus 400 mg/m², followed by 1200 mg/m²/day x 2 days (total 2400 mg/m² over 46-48 hours) continuous infusion
- Repeat every 2 weeks [p.65]

## Dose and Administration Notes

**Oxaliplatin infusion** [p.65]

Oxaliplatin may be given either over 2 hours, or may be infused over a shorter time at a rate of 1 mg/m²/min. Leucovorin infusion should match infusion time of oxaliplatin [p.65]

**Leucovorin equivalency** [p.65]

Leucovorin 400 mg/m² is the equivalent of levoleucovorin 200 mg/m² [p.65]

**Capecitabine dosing considerations** [p.65]

The majority of safety and efficacy data for this regimen have been developed in Europe, where a capecitabine starting dose of 1000 mg/m² twice daily for 14 days, repeated every 21 days, is standard. Evidence suggests that North American patients may experience greater toxicity with capecitabine (as well as with other fluoropyrimidines) than European patients, and may require a lower dose of capecitabine [p.65]

**Atezolizumab formulation** [p.65]

Atezolizumab and hyaluronidase-tqjs subcutaneous injection may be substituted for IV atezolizumab. Atezolizumab and hyaluronidase-tqjs has different dosing and administration instructions compared to IV atezolizumab [p.65]
