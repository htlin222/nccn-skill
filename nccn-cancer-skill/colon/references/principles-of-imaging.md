# Principles of Imaging (COL-A)

<!-- Source: NCCN Colon Cancer v2.2026, pages 25-26 -->

## Initial Workup/Staging

### Chest, Abdomen, and Pelvis CT (C/A/P CT) [p.25]

**Standard Protocol:**
- Evaluate local extent of tumor or infiltration into surrounding structures [p.25]
- Assess for distant metastatic disease to lungs, thoracic and abdominal lymph nodes, liver, peritoneal cavity, and other organs [p.25]
- Performed with intravenous iodinated contrast and oral contrast material unless contraindicated [p.25]
- Intravenous contrast is not required for the chest CT (but usually given if performed with abdominal CT) [p.25]

**Contrast Alternatives:**
- If IV iodinated contrast material is contraindicated due to significant contrast allergy, MRI examination of the abdomen and pelvis with IV gadolinium-based contrast agent (GBCA) can be obtained instead [p.25]
- In patients with chronic renal failure (glomerular filtration rate [GFR] <30 mL/min) who are not on dialysis, IV iodinated contrast material is contraindicated, and IV GBCA can be administered in select cases using gadofosveset trisodium, gadoxetate disodium, gadobenate dimeglumine, or gadoteridol [p.25]
- If iodinated and gadolinium contrast are both contraindicated due to significant allergy or chronic renal failure without dialysis, consider MRI without IV contrast or consider FDG-PET/CT imaging [p.25]

### MRI Studies [p.25]

**Abdomen/Pelvis MRI:**
- Consider to assist with diagnosis of rectal cancer versus colon cancer (e.g., low-lying sigmoid tumor) [p.25]
- Rectum lies below a virtual line from the sacral promontory to the upper edge of the symphysis as determined by MRI [p.25]

**Liver MRI:**
- Consider MRI of liver for liver metastases if potentially resectable [p.25]
- If liver-directed therapy or surgery is contemplated, a hepatic MRI with intravenous routine extracellular or hepatobiliary GBCA is preferred over CT to assess exact number and distribution of metastatic foci for local treatment planning [p.25]

### FDG-PET/CT Imaging [p.25]

- Not routinely indicated [p.25]
- Does not supplant a contrast-enhanced diagnostic CT or MRI and should only be used to evaluate an equivocal finding on a contrast-enhanced CT or MRI or in patients with strong contraindications to IV contrast administration [p.25]

**Consider FDG-PET/CT (skull base to mid-thigh) in:**
- Potentially surgically curable M1 disease in selected cases [p.25]
- Selected patients considered for image-guided liver-directed therapies (i.e., thermal ablation, radioembolization) [p.25]

## Monitoring

### Pre-Treatment Assessment [p.25]

- **C/A/P CT with contrast** prior to adjuvant treatment to assess response to primary therapy or resection [p.25]
- **C/A/P CT with contrast** during re-evaluation of conversion to resectable disease [p.25]

### Post-Treatment Response Assessment [p.25]

- FDG-PET/CT, FDG-PET/MRI, or contrast-enhanced MRI can be considered for assessment of response and liver recurrence after image-guided liver-directed therapies (i.e., thermal ablation, radioembolization) [p.25]

## Surveillance

### Stage I Disease [p.26]

- Imaging is not routinely indicated and should only be based on symptoms and clinical concern for recurrent/metastatic disease [p.26]

### Stage II & III Disease [p.26]

- **C/A/P CT every 6 to 12 months** (Category 2B for frequency <12 months) for a total of 5 years [p.26]
- FDG-PET/CT is not indicated [p.26]

### Stage IV Disease [p.26]

- **C/A/P CT every 3 to 6 months** (Category 2B for frequency <6 months) x 2 years, then every 6 to 12 months for a total of 5 years [p.26]
- FDG-PET/CT, FDG-PET/MRI, or contrast-enhanced MRI can be considered for assessment of response and liver recurrence after image-guided liver-directed therapies (i.e., thermal ablation, radioembolization) or serial CEA elevation during follow-up [p.26]
