# pMMR/MSS: Treatment for Resectable Synchronous Liver and/or Lung Metastases Only (COL-6) -- Algorithm Reference

<!-- Source: NCCN Colon Cancer v2.2026, page 14 -->

## Treatment Approaches for Resectable Synchronous Liver and/or Lung Metastases

### Primary Treatment Options [p.14]

For patients with pMMR/MSS colon cancer with resectable synchronous liver and/or lung metastases only, the following approaches are recommended:

#### Option 1: Upfront Resection (Preferred) [p.14]

- **Synchronous or staged colectomy with liver or lung resection (preferred) and/or local therapy**

#### Option 2: Neoadjuvant Chemotherapy [p.14]

- **Neoadjuvant therapy (2-3 months)**
  - FOLFOX (preferred) [p.14]
  - OR CAPEOX (preferred) [p.14]
  - OR FOLFIRI (Category 2B) [p.14]
  - OR FOLFIRINOX (Category 2B) [p.14]

- **Followed by:**
  - Synchronous or staged colectomy and resection (preferred) and/or local therapy of metastatic disease [p.14]

#### Option 3: Adjuvant Chemotherapy [p.14]

- **Colectomy, followed by:**
  - Chemotherapy (2-3 months)
    - FOLFOX (preferred) [p.14]
    - OR CAPEOX (preferred) [p.14]
    - OR FOLFIRI (Category 2B) [p.14]
    - OR FOLFIRINOX (Category 2B) [p.14]

- **Followed by:**
  - Staged resection (preferred) and/or local therapy of metastatic disease [p.14]

## Adjuvant Treatment (Post-Resection of Metastatic Disease) [p.14]

After complete resection of metastatic disease, adjuvant chemotherapy options (up to 6 months perioperative treatment) include:

**Preferred agents:** [p.14]
- **FOLFOX (preferred)** [p.14]
- **CAPEOX (preferred)** [p.14]

**Other options:** [p.14]
- **Capecitabine** [p.14]
- **Fluorouracil/leucovorin** [p.14]

## Surveillance [p.14]

Following treatment, patients proceed to **Surveillance (COL-8)** [p.14].

## Special Considerations

### Hepatic Artery Infusion [p.14]

Hepatic artery infusion ± systemic chemotherapy (VEGF inhibitor contraindicated) **(Category 2B)** is also an option at institutions with experience in both the surgical and medical oncologic aspects of this procedure [p.14].

### Local Therapy Preferences [p.14]

**For liver metastases:** Resection is preferred over locally ablative procedures [p.14]. For small liver lesions (≤3 cm), thermal ablation is equivalent to resection [p.14]. Other local techniques can be considered for liver oligometastases when thermal ablation is not indicated [p.14].

**For lung metastases:** Surgery, thermal ablation, and SBRT can be considered [p.14].

---

*All recommendations are category 2A unless otherwise indicated* [p.14]
