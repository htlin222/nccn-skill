# Staging (ST-1)

## dMMR/MSI-H or POLE/POLD1 Mutation Management

### Disease Presentation Categories

#### Suspected or Proven Metastatic Synchronous Adenocarcinoma
*Classification: Any T, Any N, M1 [p.22]*

##### Resectable Synchronous Liver and/or Lung Only Metastases

**Adjuvant Treatment (up to 6 months perioperative treatment for resected metastatic disease) [p.22]:**

Recommended regimens [p.22]:
- **Preferred:** FOLFOX or CAPEOX
- **Preferred Option with Immunotherapy:** FOLFOX + atezolizumab or CAPEOX + atezolizumab [p.22]

Procedure [p.22]:
- Synchronous or staged colectomy with liver or lung resection and/or local therapies

**Imaging:** --> See Principles of Imaging (COL-A) [p.22]

**Surgery:** --> See Principles of Surgery and Locoregional Therapies (COL-C) [p.22]

##### Synchronous Unresectable Metastases

Initial treatment approach [p.22]:
- **Preferred (Category 2B):** Checkpoint inhibitor immunotherapy followed by synchronous or staged colectomy and resection (preferred) and/or local therapy of metastatic disease
- **Alternative:** Surveillance --> See (COL-8) [p.22]

Note regarding immunotherapy: The role of adjuvant immunotherapy for those who received neoadjuvant immunotherapy is not defined [p.22].

Caution: Data are limited and the risk of early progression may be higher than with chemotherapy [p.22].

##### Synchronous Abdominal/Peritoneal Metastases

Management approach [p.22]:
- Proceed with colectomy and/or local therapies as indicated

##### Nonobstructing and Obstructed Presentations

**Nonobstructing:** Colon resection --> See (COL-16) [p.22]

**Obstructed or Imminent Obstruction:** Management options [p.22]:
- Colon resection
- Diverting ostomy
- Bypass of impending obstruction
- Stenting

All procedures reference: COL-16 [p.22]

### Special Considerations

**Considerations for Colon Resection:** Consider colon resection only if imminent risk of obstruction, significant bleeding, perforation, or other significant tumor-related symptoms [p.22].

**Hepatic Artery Infusion:** Hepatic artery infusion ± systemic chemotherapy (VEGF inhibitor contraindicated) (Category 2B) is also an option at institutions with experience in both the surgical and medical oncologic aspects of this procedure [p.22].

**Liver Metastases:** Resection is preferred over locally ablative procedures for liver metastases [p.22]. For small liver lesions (≤3 cm), thermal ablation is equivalent to resection [p.22]. Other local techniques can be considered for liver oligometastases when thermal ablation is not indicated [p.22].

**Lung Metastases:** For lung metastases, surgery, thermal ablation, and SBRT can be considered [p.22].

**Immunotherapy Eligibility:** Patients who are not candidates for immunotherapy should be treated as recommended for pMMR/MSS disease [p.22]. See NCCN Guidelines for Management of Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor-Related Toxicities [p.22].

*Note: All recommendations are category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.22].*
