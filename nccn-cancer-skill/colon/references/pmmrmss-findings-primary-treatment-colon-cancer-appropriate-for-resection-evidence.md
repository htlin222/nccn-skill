## Clinical Presentation and Findings

### Resectable, Nonobstructing Disease [p.11]

Primary treatment includes **colectomy with en bloc removal of regional lymph nodes** [p.11]. Following surgery, patients proceed to pathologic staging and adjuvant treatment decisions (COL-4) with concurrent surveillance (COL-8) [p.11].

### Resectable, Obstructing Disease [p.11]

Treatment options include:

- **One-stage colectomy with en bloc removal of regional lymph nodes**, OR [p.11]
- **Resection with diversion**, OR [p.11]
- **Diversion**, OR [p.11]
- **Stent (in selected cases)** [p.11]

### Locally Unresectable or Medically Inoperable Disease [p.11]

Initial treatment approach:

- **Colectomy with en bloc removal of regional lymph nodes**, OR [p.11]
- **Consider systemic therapy (COL-D) and consider radiation therapy (RT) ± infusional fluorouracil or capecitabine prior to surgery** [p.11]

For Clinical T4b or bulky nodal disease, options include:

- **Surgery ± intraoperative RT (IORT)**, OR [p.11]
- **Systemic therapy (COL-D)** [p.11]
- **Re-evaluate for conversion to resectable disease** [p.11]

Neoadjuvant therapy options include FOLFOX or CAPEOX [p.11].

## Adjuvant Treatment (pMMR/MSS)

Treatment depends on pathologic stage [p.12]:

### Stage 0-II (Tis; T1, N0, M0; T2, N0, M0)

**Observation (preferred)** [p.12]

OR

**Consider capecitabine (6 months) or fluorouracil/leucovorin (6 months)** [p.12]

### Stage II with High-Risk Features (T3, N0, M0 at high risk for systemic recurrence or T4, N0, M0)

Treatment options [p.12]:

- **Capecitabine (6 months), OR** [p.12]
- **Fluorouracil/leucovorin (6 months), OR** [p.12]
- **FOLFOX (6 months), OR** [p.12]
- **CAPEOX (3 months), OR** [p.12]
- **Observation** [p.12]

Note: A survival benefit has not been demonstrated for the addition of oxaliplatin to fluorouracil/leucovorin in stage II colon cancer [p.12].

### Stage III - Low Risk (T1-3, N1)

Preferred options [p.12]:

- **CAPEOX (3 months), OR** [p.12]
- **FOLFOX (3-6 months)** [p.12]

Other options include: capecitabine (6 months) or fluorouracil/leucovorin (6 months) [p.12]

### Stage III - High Risk (T4, N1-2; T Any, N2)

Preferred options [p.12]:

- **CAPEOX (3-6 months), OR** [p.12]
- **FOLFOX (6 months)** [p.12]

Other options include: capecitabine (6 months) or fluorouracil/leucovorin (6 months) [p.12]

## Special Considerations

### PI3K Pathway Alterations [p.12]

For stage II and III disease with somatic PI3K pathway alterations, start **aspirin 100-162 mg orally daily for 3 years after recovery from surgery** (concurrent with chemotherapy if given) [p.12]. Molecular profiling should be done on all stage II and III CRC tumors [p.12].

Somatic PI3K pathway alterations include mutations in PI3KCA exon 9 and 20; other PIK3CA, PI3K3R1, and PTEN mutations; and deep deletions of PTEN [p.12].

### Risk Assessment [p.12]

Historical high-risk factors for recurrence (exclusive of MSI-H cancers) include:

- Poorly differentiated/undifferentiated histology [p.12]
- Lymphatic/vascular invasion [p.12]
- Bowel obstruction [p.12]
- <12 lymph nodes examined [p.12]
- Perineural invasion (PNI) [p.12]
- Localized perforation [p.12]
- Close, indeterminate, or positive margins [p.12]
- High-tier tumor budding [p.12]

Circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) is prognostic but not predictive [p.12]. There are insufficient data to recommend the use of multigene assay panels to determine adjuvant therapy [p.12].

### ctDNA Monitoring [p.12]

ctDNA is a prognostic marker; however, there is currently insufficient evidence to recommend routine use of ctDNA assays outside of a clinical trial [p.12]. De-escalation of care and treatment decision-making are not recommended based on ctDNA results [p.12].

## Survivorship and Surveillance

Patients should discuss and facilitate options to achieve a goal of at least 150 minutes of moderate to vigorous physical activity per week (COL-H) [p.12].

Concurrent surveillance follows COL-8 protocol [p.12].
