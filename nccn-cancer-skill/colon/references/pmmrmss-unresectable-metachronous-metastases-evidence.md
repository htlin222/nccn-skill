## Clinical Presentation [p.19]

pMMR/MSS colon cancer with unresectable metachronous metastases [p.19].

## Initial Treatment Strategy [p.19]

Treatment selection depends on prior chemotherapy exposure:

### Prior Treatment Categories [p.19]

**Category 1: Previous FOLFOX/CAPEOX within past 12 months** [p.19]
**Category 2: Previous FOLFOX/CAPEOX >12 months** [p.19]
**Category 3: Previous fluorouracil/leucovorin or capecitabine** [p.19]
**Category 4: No previous chemotherapy** [p.19]

All categories proceed to **Systemic therapy (COL-D)** [p.19].

## Assessment for Conversion to Resectable Disease [p.19]

- **Re-evaluate for conversion to resectable disease every 2 months if conversion to resectability is a reasonable goal** [p.19]

## Treatment Pathways Based on Response

### Converted to Resectable Disease [p.19]

For patients achieving conversion:

- **Resection (preferred) and/or local therapy** [p.19]

**Followed by adjuvant treatment (up to 6 months perioperative treatment):** [p.19]
- **Systemic therapy ± biologic therapy (Category 2B for biologic therapy) (COL-D)**, OR [p.19]
- **Observation** [p.19]

### Remains Unresectable [p.19]

For patients with persistent unresectable disease:

- **Systemic therapy (COL-D) and consider local therapy for select patients** [p.19]

## Systemic Therapy Options [p.19]

### Second-Line and Subsequent Therapy Options [p.19]

**(FOLFIRI or irinotecan) ± (bevacizumab [preferred] or ziv-aflibercept or ramucirumab)**, OR [p.19]

**(FOLFIRI or irinotecan) ± (cetuximab or panitumumab) (KRAS/NRAS/BRAF WT and left-sided tumors only)**, OR [p.19]

**Encorafenib + (cetuximab or panitumumab) ± FOLFIRI or FOLFOX or CAPEOX (BRAF V600E mutation)**, OR [p.19]

**(Pertuzumab or tucatinib) + trastuzumab (HER2 (ERBB2) overexpression/amplification and KRAS/NRAS/BRAF WT)**, OR [p.19]

**Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki (HER2-overexpression, IHC 3+)**, OR [p.19]

**(Adagrasib or sotorasib) + (cetuximab or panitumumab) (KRAS G12C mutation)** [p.19]

## Surveillance [p.19]

Following treatment completion, patients proceed to **Surveillance (COL-8)** [p.19].

## Special Considerations and Drug-Specific Notes

### Bevacizumab [p.19]

Bevacizumab is the preferred anti-angiogenic agent based on toxicity and/or cost [p.19].

### Anti-EGFR Therapy [p.19]

Patients with BRAF mutations other than V600E may be considered for anti-EGFR therapy [p.19].

### HER2-Targeted Therapy [p.19]

Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki may show some activity after previous treatment with a HER2-targeted regimen [p.19]. However, it is not indicated in patients with underlying lung issues due to lung toxicity (drug-related deaths from interstitial lung disease were reported in 3.5% of patients on the DESTINY-CRC01 trial) [p.19].

### KRAS G12C Inhibitors [p.19]

If patient is unable to tolerate EGFR inhibitor due to toxicity, single-agent adagrasib or sotorasib can be considered [p.19].

### Chemotherapy Addition [p.19]

The addition of FOLFOX or CAPEOX can be considered for patients without residual neuropathy and good prior tolerance of chemotherapy [p.19].

### Biologic Therapy [p.19]

Biologic therapy is only appropriate for continuation of favorable response from conversion therapy [p.19].

### Infection Management [p.19]

For infection risk, monitoring, and prophylaxis recommendations for targeted therapies, see INF-A in the NCCN Guidelines for Prevention and Treatment of Cancer-Related Infections [p.19].

## Local Therapy Options [p.19]

### Hepatic Artery Infusion [p.19]

Hepatic artery infusion ± systemic chemotherapy (VEGF inhibitor contraindicated) **(Category 2B)** is also an option at institutions with experience in both the surgical and medical oncologic aspects of this procedure [p.19].

### Liver Metastases [p.19]

Resection is preferred over locally ablative procedures for liver metastases [p.19]. For small liver lesions (≤3 cm), thermal ablation is equivalent to resection [p.19]. Other local techniques can be considered for liver oligometastases when thermal ablation is not indicated [p.19].

### Lung Metastases [p.19]

For lung metastases, surgery, thermal ablation, and SBRT can be considered [p.19].

---

*All recommendations are category 2A unless otherwise indicated* [p.19]
