# dMMR/MSI-H or POLE/POLD1: Suspected or Proven Metastatic Synchronous Adenocarcinoma (COL-14) -- Evidence Summary

<!-- Source: NCCN Colon Cancer v2.2026, page 22 -->

## Clinical Presentation [p.22]

dMMR/MSI-H or POLE/POLD1 mutation with ultra-hypermutated phenotype (e.g., TMB >50 mut/Mb) [p.22]

Suspected or proven metastatic synchronous adenocarcinoma (any T, any N, M1) [p.22]

## Treatment Approach by Metastasis Type

### Resectable Synchronous Liver and/or Lung Only Metastases [p.22]

**Surgical approach:**
- **Synchronous or staged colectomy with liver or lung resection and/or local therapy** [p.22]

**Adjuvant chemotherapy options (up to 6 months perioperative treatment) for resected metastatic disease:** [p.22]
- **FOLFOX or CAPEOX**, OR [p.22]
- **FOLFOX + atezolizumab**, OR [p.22]
- **CAPEOX + atezolizumab** [p.22]

### Synchronous Abdominal/Peritoneal Metastases [p.22]

**Primary treatment:** See [COL-16](pmmrmss-findings-primary-treatment-colon-cancer-appropriate-for-resection-evidence.md) (Immunotherapy approaches) [p.22]

### Synchronous Unresectable Metastases [p.22]

**Primary treatment:** [p.22]

- **Checkpoint inhibitor immunotherapy (preferred) (COL-D 5 of 17)** [p.22]

**Followed by:** [p.22]
- **Synchronous or staged colectomy and resection (preferred) and/or local therapy of metastatic disease**, OR [p.22]
- **Observation** [p.22]

## Clinical Presentation Considerations

### Obstructed or Imminently Obstructed Primary Tumor [p.22]

For patients with nonobstructing primary tumors, proceed with standard treatment [p.22].

For obstructed or imminently obstructed primary tumors, consider: [p.22]
- **Colon resection**, OR [p.22]
- **Diverting ostomy**, OR [p.22]
- **Bypass of impending obstruction**, OR [p.22]
- **Stenting** [p.22]

See [COL-16](pmmrmss-findings-primary-treatment-colon-cancer-appropriate-for-resection-evidence.md) for subsequent metastatic disease treatment [p.22].

## Surveillance [p.22]

Following treatment, patients proceed to **Surveillance (COL-8)** [p.22].

## Special Considerations

### Immunotherapy Status [p.22]

Patients who are not candidates for immunotherapy should be treated as recommended for pMMR/MSS disease [p.22]. See NCCN Guidelines for Management of Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor-Related Toxicities [p.22].

### Adjuvant Immunotherapy [p.22]

The role of adjuvant immunotherapy for those who received neoadjuvant immunotherapy is not defined [p.22].

### Neoadjuvant Immunotherapy Data [p.22]

Data are limited and the risk of early progression may be higher than with chemotherapy [p.22].
*Reference: Andre T, et al. N Engl J Med 2020;383:2207-2218* [p.22]

### Local Therapy Preferences [p.22]

**For liver metastases:** Resection is preferred over locally ablative procedures [p.22]. For small liver lesions (≤3 cm), thermal ablation is equivalent to resection [p.22]. Other local techniques can be considered for liver oligometastases when thermal ablation is not indicated [p.22].

**For lung metastases:** Surgery, thermal ablation, and SBRT can be considered [p.22].

### Hepatic Artery Infusion [p.22]

Hepatic artery infusion ± systemic chemotherapy (VEGF inhibitor contraindicated) **(Category 2B)** is also an option at institutions with experience in both the surgical and medical oncologic aspects of this procedure [p.22].

### Colon Resection in Metastatic Disease [p.22]

Consider colon resection only if imminent risk of obstruction, significant bleeding, perforation, or other significant tumor-related symptoms [p.22].

---

*All recommendations are category 2A unless otherwise indicated* [p.22]
