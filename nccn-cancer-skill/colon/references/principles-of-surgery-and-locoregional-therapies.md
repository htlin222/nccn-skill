# Principles of Risk Assessment for Stage II Disease (COL-F)

<!-- Source: NCCN Colon Cancer v2.2026, page 63 -->

## Patient/Physician Discussion [p.63]

Patient/physician discussion should take place regarding the potential risks of therapy compared to potential benefits, including prognosis. This should include discussion of [p.63]:
- Evidence supporting treatment
- Assumptions of benefit from indirect evidence
- Morbidity associated with treatment
- High-risk characteristics
- Patient preferences

## Determining Adjuvant Therapy Eligibility [p.63]

When determining if adjuvant therapy should be administered, the following should be taken into consideration [p.63]:

### Lymph Node Assessment [p.63]
- Number of lymph nodes analyzed after surgery (<12) [p.63]

### Poor Prognostic Features [p.63]
- Poorly differentiated histology (exclusive of those that are MSI-H) [p.63]
- Lymphatic/vascular invasion [p.63]
- Bowel obstruction [p.63]
- Perineural invasion (PNI) [p.63]
- Localized perforation [p.63]
- Close, indeterminate, or positive margins [p.63]
- High tumor budding [p.63]

### Patient Factors [p.63]
- Assessment of other comorbidities and anticipated life expectancy [p.63]

## Treatment Efficacy [p.63]

- The benefit of adjuvant chemotherapy does not improve survival by >5% [p.63]

## Molecular Testing Requirements [p.63]

- MSI or MMR testing (COL-B 4 of 9) [p.63]
