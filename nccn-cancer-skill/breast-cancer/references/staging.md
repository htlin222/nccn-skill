## Primary Tumor (T) Classification

The T category of the primary tumor is defined by the same criteria regardless of whether it is based on clinical or pathological criteria, or both [p.115]. The T category is based primarily on the size of the invasive component of the cancer [p.115]. The maximum size of a tumor focus is used as an estimate of disease volume. The largest contiguous dimension of a tumor focus is used, and small satellite foci of noncontiguous tumor are not added to the size [p.115].

### T Definitions

| T Classification | Definition |
|---|---|
| TX | Primary tumor cannot be assessed [p.115] |
| T0 | No evidence of primary tumor [p.115] |
| Tis (DCIS) | Ductal carcinoma in situ [p.115] |
| Tis (Paget) | Paget disease of the nipple NOT associated with invasive carcinoma and/or carcinoma in situ (DCIS) in the underlying breast parenchyma [p.115] |
| T1 | Tumor ≤20 mm in greatest dimension [p.115] |
| T1mi | Tumor ≤1 mm in greatest dimension [p.115] |
| T1a | Tumor >1 mm but ≤5 mm in greatest dimension [p.115] |
| T1b | Tumor >5 mm but ≤10 mm in greatest dimension [p.115] |
| T1c | Tumor >10 mm but ≤20 mm in greatest dimension [p.115] |
| T2 | Tumor >20 mm but ≤50 mm in greatest dimension [p.115] |
| T3 | Tumor >50 mm in greatest dimension [p.115] |
| T4 | Tumor of any size with direct extension to the chest wall and/or to the skin (ulceration or macroscopic nodules) [p.115] |
| T4a | Extension to the chest wall; invasion or adherence to pectoralis muscle in the absence of invasion of chest wall structures does not qualify as T4 [p.115] |
| T4b | Ulceration and/or ipsilateral macroscopic satellite nodules and/or edema (including peau d'orange) of the skin that does not meet the criteria for inflammatory carcinoma [p.115] |
| T4c | Both T4a and T4b are present [p.115] |
| T4d | Inflammatory carcinoma [p.115] |

**Note:** Lobular carcinoma in situ (LCIS) is a benign entity and is removed from TNM staging in the AJCC Cancer Staging Manual, 8th Edition [p.115].

### T Size Measurement Guidance

Clinical tumor size (cT) should be based on the clinical findings that are judged to be most accurate for a particular case [p.115]. Size should be measured to the nearest millimeter [p.115]. If the tumor size is slightly less than or greater than a cutoff for a given T classification the size should be rounded to the millimeter reading that is closest to the cutoff [p.115]. For example, a reported size of 4.9 mm is reported as 5 mm, or a size of 2.04 cm is reported as 2.0 cm (20 mm) [p.115]. The exception to this rounding rule is for a breast tumor sized between 1.0 and 1.4 mm. These sizes are rounded up to 2 mm, because rounding down would result in the cancer's being categorized as microinvasive carcinoma (T1mi) defined as a size of 1.0 mm or less [p.115].

## Regional Lymph Nodes (N) Classification

### Clinical (cN)

| cN Classification | Definition |
|---|---|
| cNX | Regional lymph nodes cannot be assessed (e.g., previously removed) [p.116] |
| cN0 | No regional lymph node metastases (by imaging or clinical examination) [p.116] |
| cN1 | Metastases to movable ipsilateral level I, II axillary lymph node(s) [p.116] |
| cN1mi | Micrometastases (approximately 200 cells, larger than 0.2 mm, but none larger than 2.0 mm) [p.116] |
| cN2 | Metastases in ipsilateral level I, II axillary lymph nodes that are clinically fixed or matted; or in ipsilateral internal mammary nodes in the absence of axillary lymph node metastases [p.116] |
| cN2a | Metastases in ipsilateral level I, II axillary lymph nodes fixed to one another (matted) or to other structures [p.116] |
| cN2b | Metastases only in ipsilateral internal mammary nodes in the absence of axillary lymph node metastases [p.116] |
| cN3 | Metastases in ipsilateral infraclavicular (level III axillary) lymph node(s) with or without level I, II axillary lymph node involvement; or in ipsilateral internal mammary lymph node(s) with level I, II axillary lymph node metastases; or metastases in ipsilateral supraclavicular lymph node(s) [p.116] |
| cN3a | Metastases in ipsilateral infraclavicular lymph node(s) [p.116] |
| cN3b | Metastases in ipsilateral internal mammary lymph node(s) and axillary lymph node(s) [p.116] |
| cN3c | Metastases in ipsilateral supraclavicular lymph node(s) [p.116] |

**Note:** (sn) and (f) suffixes should be added to the N category to denote confirmation of metastasis by sentinel node biopsy or fine needle aspiration/core needle biopsy respectively [p.116]. The cNX category is used sparingly in cases where regional lymph nodes have previously been surgically removed or where there is no documentation of physical examination of the axilla [p.116]. cN1mi is rarely used but may be appropriate in cases where sentinel node biopsy is performed before tumor resection [p.116].

### Pathologic (pN)

| pN Classification | Definition |
|---|---|
| pNX | Regional lymph nodes cannot be assessed (e.g., not removed for pathological study or previously removed) [p.116] |
| pN0 | No regional lymph node metastasis identified or ITCs only [p.116] |
| pN0(i+) | ITCs only (malignant cells clusters no larger than 0.2 mm) in regional lymph node(s) [p.116] |
| pN0(mol+) | Positive molecular findings by reverse transcriptase polymerase chain reaction (RT-PCR); no ITCs detected [p.116] |
| pN1 | Micrometastases; or metastases in 1–3 axillary lymph nodes; and/or in clinically negative internal mammary nodes with micrometastases or macrometastases by sentinel lymph node biopsy [p.116] |
| pN1mi | Micrometastases (approximately 200 cells, larger than 0.2 mm, but none larger than 2.0 mm) [p.116] |
| pN1a | Metastases in 1–3 axillary lymph nodes, at least one metastasis larger than 2.0 mm [p.116] |
| pN1b | Metastases in ipsilateral internal mammary sentinel nodes, excluding ITCs [p.116] |
| pN1c | pN1a and pN1b combined [p.116] |
| pN2 | Metastases in 4–9 axillary lymph nodes; or positive ipsilateral internal mammary lymph nodes by imaging in the absence of axillary lymph node metastases [p.116] |
| pN2a | Metastases in 4–9 axillary lymph nodes (at least one tumor deposit larger than 2.0 mm) [p.116] |
| pN2b | Metastases in clinically detected internal mammary lymph nodes with or without microscopic confirmation; with pathologically negative axillary nodes [p.116] |
| pN3 | Metastases in 10 or more axillary lymph nodes; or in infraclavicular (level III axillary) lymph nodes; or positive ipsilateral internal mammary lymph nodes by imaging in the presence of one or more positive level I, II axillary lymph nodes [p.117] |
| pN3a | Metastases in 10 or more axillary lymph nodes (at least one tumor deposit larger than 2.0 mm); or metastases to the infraclavicular (level III axillary) lymph nodes [p.117] |
| pN3b | pN1a or pN2a in the presence of cN2b (positive internal mammary nodes by imaging); or pN2a in the presence of pN1b [p.117] |
| pN3c | Metastases in ipsilateral supraclavicular lymph nodes [p.117] |

**Note:** (sn) and (f) suffixes should be added to the N category to denote confirmation of metastasis by sentinel node biopsy or FNA/core needle biopsy respectively, with NO further resection of nodes [p.117].

## Distant Metastasis (M) Classification

| M Classification | Definition |
|---|---|
| M0 | No clinical or radiographic evidence of distant metastases [p.117] |
| cM0(i+) | No clinical or radiographic evidence of distant metastases in the presence of tumor cells or deposits no larger than 0.2 mm detected microscopically or by molecular techniques in circulating blood, bone marrow, or other nonregional nodal tissue in a patient without symptoms or signs of metastases [p.117] |
| cM1 | Distant metastases detected by clinical and radiographic means [p.117] |
| pM1 | Any histologically proven metastases in distant organs; or if in non-regional nodes, metastases greater than 0.2 mm [p.117] |

## Anatomic Stage Groups

The Anatomic Stage Group table should only be used in global regions where biomarker tests are not routinely available [p.117]. Cancer registries in the U.S. must use the Clinical and Pathological Prognostic Stage Group tables for case reporting [p.117].

### Anatomic Stages

| Stage | T | N | M |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Tis | N0 | M0 [p.117] |
| IA | T1 | N0 | M0 [p.117] |
| IB | T0 | N1mi | M0 [p.117] |
| | T1 | N1mi | M0 [p.117] |
| IIA | T0 | N1 | M0 [p.117] |
| | T1 | N1 | M0 [p.117] |
| | T2 | N0 | M0 [p.117] |
| IIB | T2 | N1 | M0 [p.117] |
| | T3 | N0 | M0 [p.117] |
| IIIA | T0 | N2 | M0 [p.117] |
| | T1 | N2 | M0 [p.117] |
| | T2 | N2 | M0 [p.117] |
| | T3 | N1 | M0 [p.117] |
| IIIB | T4 | N0 | M0 [p.117] |
| | T4 | N1 | M0 [p.117] |
| | T2 | N0 | M0 [p.117] |
| IIIC | Any T | N3 | M0 [p.117] |
| IV | Any T | Any N | M1 [p.117] |

### Anatomic Stage Notes

1. T1 includes T1mi [p.117]
2. T0 and T1 tumors with nodal micrometastases (N1mi) are staged as Stage IB [p.117]
3. T2, T3, and T4 tumors with nodal micrometastases (N1mi) are staged using the N1 category [p.117]
4. M0 includes M0(i+) [p.117]
5. The designation pM0 is not valid; any M0 is clinical [p.117]
6. If a patient presents with M1 disease prior to neoadjuvant systemic therapy, the stage is considered Stage IV and remains Stage IV regardless of response to neoadjuvant therapy [p.117]
7. Stage designation may be changed if postsurgical imaging studies reveal the presence of distant metastases, provided the studies are performed within 4 months of diagnosis in the absence of disease progression, and provided the patient has not received neoadjuvant therapy [p.117]
8. Staging following neoadjuvant therapy is designated with "yc" or "yp" prefix to the T and N classification. There is no anatomic stage group assigned if there is a complete pathological response (pCR) to neoadjuvant therapy [p.117]

## Histologic Grade (G)

All invasive breast carcinomas should be assigned a histologic grade [p.118]. The Nottingham combined histologic grade (Nottingham modification of the SBR grading system) is recommended and is stipulated for use by the College of American Pathologists [p.118].

### Invasive Cancer Grading (SBR System, Nottingham Modification)

| Grade | Definition |
|---|---|
| GX | Grade cannot be assessed [p.118] |
| G1 | Low combined histologic grade (favorable); SBR score of 3–5 points [p.118] |
| G2 | Intermediate combined histologic grade (moderately favorable); SBR score of 6–7 points [p.118] |
| G3 | High combined histologic grade (unfavorable); SBR score of 8–9 points [p.118] |

**Grading Method:** The grade for a tumor is determined by assessing morphologic features (tubule formation, nuclear pleomorphism, and calibrated mitotic count), assigning a value from 1 (favorable) to 3 (unfavorable) for each feature, and totaling the scores for all three categories [p.118]. The use of subjective grading alone is discouraged [p.118].

### Ductal Carcinoma in Situ: Nuclear Grade

| Grade | Definition |
|---|---|
| GX | Grade cannot be assessed [p.118] |
| G1 | Low nuclear grade [p.118] |
| G2 | Intermediate nuclear grade [p.118] |
| G3 | High nuclear grade [p.118] |

## Histopathologic Types

### Favorable Histologic Types

The following are considered favorable histologic types [p.119]:
- Tubular carcinoma [p.119]
- Cribriform carcinoma [p.119]
- Mucinous carcinoma [p.119]
- Adenoid cystic [p.119]
- Low-grade adenosquamous carcinoma metaplastic carcinoma [p.119]
- Low-grade fibromatosis-like metaplastic carcinoma [p.119]

### In Situ Carcinomas (WHO Classification, 5th Edition)

- Ductal carcinoma in situ (DCIS) (low nuclear grade, intermediate nuclear grade, and high nuclear grade) [p.119]
- In situ papillary neoplasms (papillary DCIS, encapsulated papillary carcinoma, solid papillary carcinoma in situ) [p.119]

### Invasive Carcinomas (WHO Classification, 5th Edition)

- Invasive breast carcinoma of no special type (ductal and other special patterns) [p.119]
- Microinvasive carcinoma [p.119]
- Invasive lobular carcinoma [p.119]
- Tubular carcinoma [p.119]
- Cribriform carcinoma [p.119]
- Mucinous carcinoma [p.119]
- Mucinous cystadenocarcinoma [p.119]
- Invasive micropapillary carcinoma [p.119]
- Invasive papillary carcinoma [p.119]
- Invasive solid papillary carcinoma [p.119]
- Carcinoma with apocrine differentiation [p.119]
- Metaplastic carcinoma (spindle cell, squamous, with heterologous differentiation, low-grade adenosquamous carcinoma, low-grade fibromatosis-like and mixed metaplastic) [p.119]
- Neuroendocrine tumor (NET) [p.119]
- Neuroendocrine carcinoma (NEC) [p.119]
- Salivary gland-type (acinic cell, adenoid cystic, secretory, mucoepidermoid, polymorphous adenocarcinoma) [p.119]
- Tall cell carcinoma with reversed polarity [p.119]

## Clinical Prognostic Stage

Clinical Prognostic Stage applies to ALL patients with breast cancer for clinical classification and staging [p.120]. It uses clinical tumor (T), node (N) and metastases (M) information based on history, physical examination, any imaging performed (not necessary for clinical staging) and relevant biopsies [p.120].

**Note:** Genomic profile information is not included in Clinical Prognostic Stage as pathologic information from surgery is necessary to ascertain the prognosis using these tools [p.120].

### Clinical Prognostic Stage Table

The Clinical Prognostic Stage is determined by TNM classification combined with Grade, HER2, ER, and PR status [p.120-122]. Complete staging tables are provided in pages 120-122 with specific stage assignments for all TNM/biomarker combinations.

**Key Notes for Clinical Prognostic Staging:**

1. T1 includes T1mi [p.121]
2. N1 does not include N1mi. T1 N1mi M0 and T0 N1mi M0 cancers are included for prognostic staging with T1 N0 M0 cancers of the same prognostic factor status [p.120]
3. N1 includes N1mi. T2, T3, and T4 cancers and N1mi are included for prognostic staging with T2 N1, T3 N1 and T4 N1, respectively [p.121]
4. For cases with lymph node involvement with no evidence of primary tumor (e.g. T0 N1, etc.) or with breast ductal carcinoma in situ (e.g. Tis N1, etc.), the grade, HER2, ER, and PR information from the tumor in the lymph node should be used for assigning stage group [p.122]
5. For cases where HER2 is determined to be "equivocal" by ISH (FISH or CISH) testing under the 2013 ASCO/CAP HER2 testing guidelines, the HER2 "negative" category should be used for staging in the Clinical Prognostic Stage Group [p.122]
6. The prognostic value of these Prognostic Stage Groups is based on populations of persons with breast cancer that have been offered and mostly treated with appropriate endocrine and/or systemic chemotherapy (including anti-HER2 therapy) [p.122]

## Pathological Prognostic Stage

Pathological Prognostic Stage applies to patients with breast cancer treated with surgery as the initial treatment [p.123]. It includes all information used for clinical staging plus findings at surgery and pathological findings from surgical resection [p.123]. Pathological Prognostic Stage does not apply to patients treated with systemic or radiation prior to surgical resection (neoadjuvant therapy) [p.123].

### Pathological Prognostic Stage Table

The Pathological Prognostic Stage is determined by TNM classification combined with Grade, HER2, ER, and PR status [p.123-125]. Complete staging tables are provided in pages 123-125 with specific stage assignments for all TNM/biomarker combinations.

**Key Notes for Pathological Prognostic Staging:**

1. T1 includes T1mi [p.123-124]
2. N1 does not include N1mi. T1 N1mi M0 and T0 N1mi M0 cancers are included for prognostic staging with T1 N0 M0 cancers of the same prognostic factor status [p.123]
3. N1 includes N1mi. T2, T3, and T4 cancers and N1mi are included for prognostic staging with T2 N1, T3 N1 and T4 N1, respectively [p.124-125]
4. For cases with lymph node involvement with no evidence of primary tumor (e.g. T0 N1, etc.) or with breast ductal carcinoma in situ (e.g. Tis N1, etc.), the grade, HER2, ER and PR information from the tumor in the lymph node should be used for assigning stage group [p.125]
5. For cases where HER2 is determined to be "equivocal" by ISH (FISH or CISH) testing under the 2013 ASCO/CAP HER2 testing guidelines, HER2 "negative" category should be used for staging in the Pathological Prognostic Stage Group [p.125]
6. The prognostic value of these Prognostic Stage Groups is based on populations of persons with breast cancer that have been offered and mostly treated with appropriate endocrine and/or systemic chemotherapy (including anti-HER2 therapy) [p.125]

## Genomic Profile for Pathologic Prognostic Staging

Obtaining genomic profiles is NOT required for assigning Pathological Prognostic Stage [p.125]. However genomic profiles may be performed for use in determining appropriate treatment [p.125].

### Oncotype DX Score Less Than 11

For cases with T1 N0 M0 or T2 N0 M0 cancer that is HER2-negative and ER-positive, if the Oncotype DX test is performed and the recurrence score is less than 11, the case should be assigned Pathological Prognostic Stage Group IA [p.125].

**Key Notes on Genomic Profiling:**

1. If OncotypeDx is not performed, or if it is performed and the OncotypeDx score is not available, or is 11 or greater for patients with T1–2 N0 M0 HER2–negative, ER-positive cancer, then the Prognostic Stage Group is assigned based on the anatomic and biomarker categories [p.125]
2. OncotypeDx is the only multigene panel included to classify Pathologic Prognostic Stage because prospective Level I data supports this use for patients with a score less than 11 [p.125]
3. Future updates to the staging system may include results from other multigene panels to assign cohorts of patients to Prognostic Stage Groups based on the then available evidence [p.125]
4. Inclusion or exclusion in this staging table of a genomic profile assay is not an endorsement of any specific assay and should not limit appropriate clinical use of any genomic profile assay based on evidence available at the time of treatment [p.125]

## Category Definitions

### NCCN Categories of Evidence and Consensus

- **(Category 1)** Based upon high-level evidence (≥1 randomized phase 3 trials or high-quality, robust meta-analyses), there is uniform NCCN consensus (≥85% support of the Panel) that the intervention is appropriate [p.127]
- **(Category 2A)** Based upon lower-level evidence, there is uniform NCCN consensus (≥85% support of the Panel) that the intervention is appropriate [p.127]
- **(Category 2B)** Based upon lower-level evidence, there is NCCN consensus (≥50%, but <85% support of the Panel) that the intervention is appropriate [p.127]
- **(Category 3)** Based upon any level of evidence, there is major NCCN disagreement that the intervention is appropriate [p.127]

All recommendations are category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.127].

### NCCN Categories of Preference

- **Preferred:** Interventions that are based on superior efficacy, safety, and evidence; and, when appropriate, affordability [p.127]
- **Other recommended:** Other interventions that may be somewhat less efficacious, more toxic, or based on less mature data; or significantly less affordable for similar outcomes [p.127]
- **Useful in certain circumstances:** Other interventions that may be used for selected patient populations (defined with recommendation) [p.127]

All recommendations are considered appropriate [p.127].
