# Phyllodes Tumor (PHYLL-1) -- Algorithm Reference

## Diagnosis

**Clinical Presentation** [p.108]

Clinical suspicion of phyllodes tumor is based on:
- Palpable mass [p.108]
- Rapid growth [p.108]
- Large size (>3 cm) [p.108]
- Imaging with ultrasound suggestive of fibroadenoma except for size and/or history of growth [p.108]

**Workup** [p.108]

Diagnostic evaluation includes:
- History and physical examination [p.108]
- Ultrasound [p.108]
- Mammogram for patients ≥30 years of age [p.108]
- Assess for distress (refer to NCCN Guidelines for Distress Management [DIS-A] for the NCCN Distress Thermometer and Problem List, which includes social determinants of health) [p.108]

## Core Biopsy and Results

**Core Needle Biopsy** [p.108]

Core needle biopsy is performed for suspected lesions. [p.108]

**Important Note on Biopsy Limitations:** FNA or core biopsy may not distinguish a fibroadenoma from a phyllodes tumor in some cases. The sensitivity of core biopsy for the diagnosis of phyllodes tumor is greater than that of FNA biopsy, but neither core biopsy nor FNA biopsy can always differentiate phyllodes tumors from fibroadenomas. In cases with clinical suspicion for phyllodes tumor, excision of the lesion may be needed for definitive pathologic classification. [p.108]

**Possible Core Biopsy Results:** [p.108]

1. **Phyllodes, borderline or malignant** [p.108]
   - Requires treatment per Soft Tissue Sarcoma guidelines (Page PHYLLSARC-1) [p.108]
   - Genetic counseling and testing if patient is at risk for hereditary cancer syndromes, particularly breast, ovarian, and pancreatic cancer [p.108]
   - Panel endorses the College of American Pathologists Protocol for standardized pathology reporting for all phyllodes tumors [p.108]

2. **Fibroadenoma or fibroepithelial lesion** [p.108]
   - Observe [p.108]

3. **Indeterminate or suspicious for a benign phyllodes** [p.108]
   - Excisional biopsy indicated [p.108]
   - Excisional biopsy includes complete mass removal, but without the intent of obtaining surgical margins [p.108]

4. **Invasive or in situ cancer** [p.108]
   - Refer to NCCN Guidelines for Noninvasive Breast Cancer for DCIS (DCIS-1) or Invasive Breast Cancer (BINV-1) [p.108]

## Treatment

**Benign Phyllodes** [p.108]

Clinical follow-up for 3 years (no radiation therapy) [p.108]
- Adjuvant radiation therapy is not indicated for positive margins [p.108]

**Suspicious for Borderline/Malignant Phyllodes** [p.108]

Refer to NCCN Guidelines for Soft Tissue Sarcoma (Page PHYLLSARC-1) [p.108]

---

**All recommendations are category 2A unless otherwise indicated.** [p.108]
