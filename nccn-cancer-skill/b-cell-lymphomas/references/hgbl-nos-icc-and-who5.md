## Overview

Note: All recommendations are (Category 2A) unless otherwise indicated [p.84].

## Diagnosis and Additional Testing

### Cytogenetic and Molecular Workup

- Perform FISH for BCL2 rearrangement [t(14;18)], and MYC rearrangements [t(8;14) or variants, t(8;22), t(2;8)] [p.84]
- Perform FISH for BCL6 and MYC rearrangements [p.84]
- NGS may be useful for treatment selection [p.84]
- Rebiopsy to confirm CD20 positivity is recommended prior to initiating CD3 x CD20 bispecific antibody therapy [p.88]

## Histologic Transformation of Indolent Lymphomas to DLBCL

### After Minimal or No Prior Therapy

#### Initial Assessment and Testing

- Perform PET/CT [p.84]
- Repeat biopsy should be strongly considered if PET-positive prior to additional therapy because PET positivity may represent post-treatment inflammation [p.85]
- If biopsy negative, follow CR pathway [p.85]

#### Treatment Approach

**Chemoimmunotherapy** (anthracycline-based regimens preferred unless contraindicated) ± ISRT [p.84]

See --> BCEL-C (First-Line Therapy) [UNRESOLVED: bcel-c.md] [p.84]

Consider ISRT for localized presentations, bulky disease, and/or limited osseous disease [p.84]

#### Response Assessment

Assess response using Lugano Response Criteria for Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma (NHODG-C). PET/CT scan should be interpreted via the PET 5-PS [p.85]

- **Complete Response (CR)** (PET negative [5-PS 1–3]) [p.85]
  - Active surveillance, or
  - Clinical trial
  - Follow-up includes diagnostic tests and imaging using the same modalities performed during workup as clinically indicated. Imaging should be performed whenever there are clinical indications [p.85]
  - Patients with a durable response for transformed disease may recur with the original indolent lymphoma. In that case, the management should be as per FOLL-5 or NMZL-4 [p.85]

- **Partial Response (PR)** (PET positive [5-PS 4]) or **No Response (NR)** or **Progressive Disease** (PET positive [5-PS 5]) [p.85]
  - Repeat biopsy [p.85]
  - CAR T-cell therapy (HTBCEL-A) with or without bridging therapy (if eligible) [p.85]
  - If not eligible, see HTBCEL-A for suggested systemic therapy regimens [p.85]

- **Relapsed Disease** [p.85]
  - Proceed to Histologic Transformation After Multiple Prior Therapies (HTBCEL-3) [p.85]

### After Multiple Lines of Prior Therapies

#### Treatment Indication

Histologic transformation after ≥2 of chemoimmunotherapy regimens for indolent lymphomas prior to histologic transformation. For example, prior treatment with BR and RCHOP [p.84], [p.86]

#### Response Assessment

Assess response using Lugano Response Criteria for Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma (NHODG-C). PET/CT scan should be interpreted via the PET 5-PS [p.86]

#### Treatment Pathways by Response

- **Complete Response (CR)** (PET negative [5-PS 1–3]) [p.86]
  - Active surveillance, or
  - If transplant eligible, HDT/ASCR ± ISRT (if not previously given), or
  - Allogeneic HCT in selected cases ± ISRT (if not previously given)
  - Note: Data on transplant after treatment with CAR T-cell (CD19-directed) therapy are not available. HDT/ASCR is not recommended after CAR T-cell (CD19-directed) therapy. Allogeneic HCT could be considered but remains investigational [p.86]
  - Selected cases include mobilization failures and persistent bone marrow involvement [p.86]

- **Partial Response (PR)** (PET positive [5-PS 4]) [p.86]
  - CAR T-cell therapy (preferred, if not previously given) (HTBCEL-A), or
  - If transplant eligible, allogeneic HCT in selected cases ± ISRT (if not previously given), or
  - ISRT for localized residual and/or residual FDG-avid disease not previously irradiated, or
  - Active surveillance
  - Note: If proceeding to transplant, consider additional systemic therapy not previously given ± ISRT to induce CR prior to transplant [p.86]

- **No Response (NR)** or **Progressive Disease** (PET positive [5-PS 5]) [p.86]
  - Candidate for additional therapy:
    - Clinical trial or
    - See HTBCEL-A for suggested systemic therapy regimens (Selection of regimen must be highly individualized taking into account prior treatment history) ± ISRT
  - Not a candidate for additional therapy:
    - Best supportive care (See NCCN Guidelines for Palliative Care) [p.86]

- **Relapsed or Progressive Disease** [p.86]
  - Best supportive care (See NCCN Guidelines for Palliative Care) [p.86]

## Second-Line and Subsequent Therapy

### Cell Therapy and Bispecific Antibody Options

#### CAR T-Cell Therapy [p.87]

- Axicabtagene ciloleucel (CD-19 directed) [p.87]
- Lisocabtagene maraleucel (CD-19 directed) [p.87]
- Tisagenlecleucel (CD-19 directed) [p.87]

Note: Patients should have received at least one anthracycline-based regimen, unless contraindicated [p.86]

#### Bispecific Antibody Therapy [p.87]

Only after at least two lines of systemic therapy; including patients with disease progression after transplant or CAR T-cell therapy [p.87]

- Epcoritamab-bysp [p.87]
- Glofitamab-gxbm [p.87]

Note: In the setting of CD20-negative lymphomas, the activity of CD3 x CD20 bispecific antibody therapy is unclear. Rebiopsy to confirm CD20 positivity is recommended prior to initiating CD3 x CD20 bispecific antibody therapy [p.88]

### Systemic Therapy Regimens

#### For Patients With Intention to Proceed to Transplant [p.87]

**Preferred Regimens**

- RCHOP (if not previously given) [p.87]
- If previously treated with anthracycline-based regimen (in alphabetical order) [p.87]
  - DHA (dexamethasone, cytarabine) + platinum (carboplatin, cisplatin, or oxaliplatin) ± rituximab [p.87]
  - GDP (gemcitabine, dexamethasone, carboplatin or cisplatin) [p.87]
  - ICE (ifosfamide, carboplatin, etoposide) ± rituximab [p.87]

#### For Patients With No Intention to Proceed to Transplant [p.87]

**Preferred Regimens**

- RCHOP (if not previously given) [p.87]
- If previously treated with anthracycline-based regimen (in alphabetical order) [p.87]
  - Polatuzumab vedotin-piiq ± bendamustine ± rituximab [p.87]
  - Tafasitamab-cxix + lenalidomide [p.87]

**Other Recommended Regimens** (in alphabetical order) [p.87]

- CEOP (cyclophosphamide, etoposide, vincristine, prednisone) ± rituximab [p.87]
- GDP (gemcitabine, dexamethasone, carboplatin or cisplatin) ± rituximab [p.87]
- GemOx ± rituximab [p.87]
- Loncastuximab tesirine-lpyld [p.87]
- Selinexor (transformed FL; only after at least two lines of systemic therapy; including patients with disease progression after transplant or CAR T-cell therapy) [p.87]

### Treatment Considerations

- An FDA-approved biosimilar is an appropriate substitute for any recommended systemic biologic therapy in the NCCN Guidelines [p.88]
- Rituximab and hyaluronidase human injection for subcutaneous use may be substituted for rituximab after patients have received the first full dose of rituximab by intravenous infusion [p.88]
- Inclusion of any anthracycline in patients with impaired cardiac functioning should have more frequent cardiac monitoring [p.88]
- In patients intended to receive CAR T-cell therapy or CD3 x CD20 bispecific antibody therapy, bendamustine should be used with caution. Delay bendamustine until after CAR-T leukapheresis [p.88]
- It is unclear if tafasitamab, loncastuximab tesirine, or any other CD19–directed therapy would have a negative impact on the efficacy of subsequent CAR T-cell (CD19-directed) therapy [p.88]

## Follow-Up and Surveillance

Follow-up includes diagnostic tests and imaging using the same modalities performed during workup as clinically indicated. Imaging should be performed whenever there are clinical indications [p.85], [p.86]

For surveillance imaging, see Discussion for consensus imaging recommendations [p.85], [p.86]
