## Diagnosis and Immunophenotyping

**Essential diagnostic requirements** [p.97]:

- Adequate immunophenotyping to establish diagnosis and subclassification (DLBCL, BL, plasmablastic lymphoma, PEL) [p.97]

**Immunohistochemistry (IHC) panel** [p.97]:
- CD45, CD20, CD3, CD10, BCL2, BCL6, Ki-67, CD138, kappa/lambda [p.97]
- HHV8 latency-associated nuclear antigen (LANA) [p.97]
- CD30 for PEL [p.97]

**Flow cytometry** [p.97]:
- Peripheral blood and/or biopsy specimen [p.97]
- Markers: kappa/lambda, CD45, CD3, CD5, CD19, CD10, CD20 [p.97]

**Molecular and cytogenetic testing** [p.97]:
- Karyotype or FISH for MYC [p.97]
- FISH for BCL2, BCL6 rearrangements if MYC positive [p.97]
- EBER-ISH [p.97]

Note: HHV8 can also be detected by polymerase chain reaction (PCR) [p.97]

## Workup and Additional Diagnostic Testing

**Essential testing** [p.98]:

- Physical exam with attention to node-bearing areas, including Waldeyer's ring, and to size of liver and spleen [p.98]
- Performance status [p.98]
- B symptoms [p.98]
- CBC with differential [p.98]
- LDH [p.98]
- Comprehensive metabolic panel [p.98]
- Uric acid, phosphate [p.98]
- PET/CT scan (preferred) or C/A/P CT with contrast of diagnostic quality [p.98]
- CD4 count [p.98]
- HIV viral load [p.98]
- Hepatitis B testing (HBsAg and core antibody for patients with no risk factors; add e-antigen for patients with risk factors or previous history) [p.98]
  - If positive, check viral load and consider consult with gastroenterologist [p.98]
- Hepatitis C testing (antibody and if positive, viral load and consult with hepatologist) [p.98]
- Echocardiogram or MUGA scan if anthracycline or anthracenedione-based regimen is indicated [p.98]
- Pregnancy testing in patients of childbearing age (if chemotherapy or RT planned) [p.98]

**Useful in selected cases** [p.98]:

- Upper GI/barium enema/endoscopy [p.98]
- Adequate bone marrow biopsy (>1.6 cm) ± aspirate; bone marrow biopsy is not necessary if PET/CT scan demonstrates bone disease [p.98]
- Lumbar puncture, except for PEL [p.98]
- Neck CT with contrast [p.98]
- Plain bone radiographs and bone scan [p.98]
- Brain MRI with and without contrast, or head CT with contrast [p.98]
- Beta-2-microglobulin [p.98]
- EBV polymerase chain reaction (PCR) [p.98]
- Quantitative Ig [p.98]
- Discuss fertility preservation (options include: sperm banking, semen cryopreservation, IVF, or ovarian tissue or oocyte cryopreservation) [p.98]

## Burkitt Lymphoma — Treatment

**Default evidence category** (Category 2A) [p.97]

- First-line therapy → See Suggested Treatment Regimens (HIVLYM-A) [p.99]
- If CD4 <50, maximize supportive care and monitor closely for cytopenias and infections while administering lymphoma therapy [p.99]
- Granulocyte colony-stimulating factor (G-CSF) for all patients [p.99]

**Antiretroviral therapy (ART) principles** [p.99]:
- ART can be administered safely with chemotherapy, but consultation with an HIV specialist or pharmacist is important to optimize compatibility [p.99]
- With continued development of new ARTs, effective alternatives are often available when existing ARTs are expected to affect metabolism of or share toxicities with chemotherapy [p.99]
- In general, avoidance of zidovudine, cobicistat, and ritonavir is strongly recommended [p.99]
- Concurrent ART is associated with higher CR rates [p.99]
- Patients with HIV-related DLBCL receiving ART are suitable candidates for CAR T-cell therapies with appropriate supportive care measures for HIV control [p.99]

**For relapse** [p.99]:
→ See Second-Line Therapy (BURK-3)

## Primary CNS Lymphoma — Treatment

**Default evidence category** (Category 2A) [p.97]

- Initiate ART, if not already receiving [p.99]
- Even with poorly controlled HIV and/or marginal performance status, consider high-dose methotrexate [p.99]
- For select patients with good performance status on ART → See NCCN Guidelines for CNS - Primary CNS Lymphoma [p.99]
- Consider RT alone as initial treatment for patients who are not candidates for systemic therapy at presentation, then reassess eligibility for systemic therapy after RT [p.99]
- Best supportive care (See NCCN Guidelines for Palliative Care) [p.99]

**For relapse** [p.99]:
→ See NCCN Guidelines for CNS - Primary CNS Lymphoma

## Plasmablastic Lymphoma — Treatment

**Default evidence category** (Category 2A) [p.97]

Note: Management can also apply to HIV-negative plasmablastic lymphoma [p.100]

- Standard CHOP is not adequate therapy [p.100]
- First-line therapy → See Suggested Treatment Regimens (HIVLYM-A) [p.100]
- Consider HDT/ASCR in first complete remission in select patients with high-risk disease [p.100]
  - High-risk features: age-adjusted IPI >2, presence of MYC gene rearrangement, or TP53 gene deletion [p.100]
  - Note that patients negative for HIV with plasmablastic lymphoma are generally considered to have higher risk disease [p.100]
  - Optimization of HIV control with ART is important [p.100]
- IT therapy as per BCEL-A 3 of 3 [p.100]

## DLBCL, HHV8-Positive DLBCL NOS, and PEL — Treatment

**Default evidence category** (Category 2A) [p.97]

- First-line therapy → See Suggested Treatment Regimens (HIVLYM-A) [p.100]
- G-CSF for all patients [p.100]
- IT therapy as per BCEL-A 3 of 3 [p.100]
- If CD20-, rituximab is not indicated [p.100]
- If CD4 <50, maximize supportive care and monitor closely for cytopenias and infections while administering lymphoma therapy [p.100]

**For relapse** [p.100]:
- <12 months → See BCEL-7 [p.100]
- >12 months → See BCEL-8 [p.100]

**Alternative relapse option** [p.100]:
- Bortezomib-ICE ± rituximab (Category 2B) [p.100]

**Additional option** [p.100]:
- Brentuximab vedotin OR See BCEL-C second-line therapy (without rituximab) [p.100]

**Antiretroviral therapy (ART) principles** [p.100]:
- ART can be administered safely with chemotherapy, but consultation with an HIV specialist or pharmacist is important to optimize compatibility [p.100]
- With continued development of new ARTs, effective alternatives are often available when existing ARTs are expected to affect metabolism of or share toxicities with chemotherapy [p.100]
- In general, avoidance of zidovudine, cobicistat, and ritonavir is strongly recommended [p.100]
- Concurrent ART is associated with higher CR rates [p.100]
- Patients with HIV-related DLBCL receiving ART are suitable candidates for CAR T-cell therapies with appropriate supportive care measures for HIV control [p.100]

## First-Line Treatment Regimens (HIVLYM-A)

**Default evidence category** (Category 2A) unless otherwise noted [p.97]

### Burkitt Lymphoma — First-Line Therapy

**Preferred regimens** [p.101]:

- **CODOX-M/IVAC (modified)**: cyclophosphamide, vincristine, doxorubicin, high-dose methotrexate alternating with ifosfamide, etoposide, high-dose cytarabine + rituximab [p.101]
  - Patients presenting with high-risk and symptomatic CNS disease should be started with the portion of the systemic therapy that contains CNS-penetrating drugs [p.101]

- **DA-EPOCH-R**: etoposide, prednisone, vincristine, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin + rituximab [p.101]
  - For dosing, see BURK-A 1 of 3 [p.101]

**Other recommended regimens** [p.101]:

- **R-HyperCVAD**: rituximab, cyclophosphamide, vincristine, doxorubicin, and dexamethasone alternating with high-dose methotrexate and cytarabine [p.101]

**Prophylaxis requirements** [p.101]:
- Prophylaxis for tumor lysis syndrome is mandatory for BL [p.101]
- See monoclonal antibody and viral reactivation (NHODG-B) [p.101]

Note: An FDA-approved biosimilar is an appropriate substitute for any recommended systemic biologic therapy. Rituximab and hyaluronidase human injection for subcutaneous use may be substituted for rituximab after patients have received the first full dose of rituximab by intravenous infusion [p.101]

### DLBCL, HHV8-Positive DLBCL NOS, PEL — First-Line Therapy

**Caveat** [p.101]:
- If CD20-, rituximab is not indicated [p.101]

**Preferred regimens** [p.101]:

- **R-EPOCH** [p.101]

**Other recommended regimens** [p.101]:

- **RCHOP** [p.101]

### Plasmablastic Lymphoma — First-Line Therapy

**Preferred regimens** [p.101]:

- **EPOCH** [p.101]

**Other recommended regimens** [p.101]:

- **CODOX-M/IVAC (modified)** [p.101]
- **HyperCVAD** (cyclophosphamide, vincristine, doxorubicin, and dexamethasone alternating with high-dose methotrexate and cytarabine) [p.101]

## EPOCH ± Rituximab Dosing and Dose Modifications

**EPOCH ± rituximab dosing recommendations for cycle 1** [p.103]:

- Rituximab (if CD20-positive): 375 mg/m² IV on Day 1 [p.103]
- Etoposide: 50 mg/m²/day continuous IV infusion for 4 days (96 hours) [p.103]
- Doxorubicin: 10 mg/m²/day continuous IV infusion for 4 days (96 hours) [p.103]
- Vincristine: 0.4 mg/m²/day continuous IV infusion for 4 days (96 hours) [p.103]
- Day 5 cyclophosphamide dosing [p.103]:
  - If baseline CD4 count is >200 cells/mm³, start cyclophosphamide at 750 mg/m² [p.103]
  - If baseline CD4 count is 50–200 cells/mm³, start cyclophosphamide at 375 mg/m² [p.103]
  - For baseline CD4 counts <50 cells/mm³, cycle 1 doses of cyclophosphamide above 187.5 mg/m² have not been published [p.103]
- Prednisone: 60 mg/m²/day for 5 days [p.103]

**EPOCH dose modifications for subsequent cycles based on cytopenias (non-Burkitt lymphomas)** [p.103]:

| Event | Action |
|-------|--------|
| ANC nadir on any cycle <500/mm³ on 2 nonconsecutive days at least 3 days apart AND/OR platelet nadir <25,000/mm³ in the previous cycle | Reduce cyclophosphamide dose by 187 mg/m² [p.103] |
| ANC nadir <500/mm³ × ≥3 days OR platelets <25,000/mm³ × ≥3 days, AND patient was receiving no cyclophosphamide in the previous cycle | Reduce doxorubicin and etoposide by 25% of the full dose [p.103] |
| ANC nadir ≥500/mm³ AND platelet nadir ≥50,000/mm³ in the previous cycle | Increase cyclophosphamide dose by 187 mg/m² each cycle to a maximum dose of 750 mg/m² [p.103] |
