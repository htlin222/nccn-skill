# ALK-Positive Large B-Cell Lymphomas -- Evidence Summary

## Summary of Key Evidence

- ALK-positive LBCL are mature aggressive B-cell lymphomas characterized by large cells with plasmablastic differentiation, cytoplasmic ALK expression, and lack of CD20 expression [p.271][8,9]
- Cytoplasmic ALK expression and t(2;17)(p23;q23) translocation (CLTC/ALK fusion) differentiate this entity from other CD20-negative aggressive lymphomas such as plasmablastic lymphoma, primary effusion lymphoma (PEL), and HHV8-positive DLBCL [p.271][188,189]
- Median age at diagnosis is approximately 40 years with strong male predominance; most patients present with rapidly progressive disease at advanced stage [p.271][190,191]
- Age <40 years and early-stage disease are associated with more favorable long-term survival [p.271][192,193]
- EBV and HHV8 are negative in ALK-positive LBCL, with no association with immune deficiency [p.271]

## Special Considerations

### Disease Characteristics and Prognosis

ALK-positive LBCL are mature aggressive B-cell lymphomas defined by large cells with plasmablastic differentiation and cytoplasmic (not nuclear) ALK expression, distinguished by the absence of CD20 expression [p.271][8,9]. Cytogenetic/FISH analysis most commonly demonstrates t(2;17)(p23;q23), denoting the CLTC (clathrin heavy chain)/ALK fusion [p.271][188,189].

Epidemiologically, the median age at diagnosis is approximately 40 years, with a strong male predominance [p.271][190,191]. Most patients present with rapidly progressive disease at an advanced stage [p.271][190,191]. Prognostic factors include: age <40 years and early-stage disease, both of which are associated with more favorable long-term survival [p.271][192,193].

### Immunophenotypic and Molecular Features

Cytoplasmic ALK expression helps differentiate ALK-positive LBCL from other aggressive CD20-negative large B-cell lymphomas with plasmacytic differentiation, including plasmablastic lymphoma, primary effusion lymphoma (PEL), and HHV8-positive DLBCL [p.271]. EBV and HHV8 are negative in ALK-positive LBCL, and there is no association with immune deficiency [p.271].
