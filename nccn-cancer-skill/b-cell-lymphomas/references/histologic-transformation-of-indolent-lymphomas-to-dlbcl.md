## Diagnosis and Additional Testing

**FISH Testing (Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization)** [p.84]

- Perform FISH for BCL2 rearrangement [t(14;18)] [p.84]
- Perform FISH for MYC rearrangements [t(8;14) or variants, t(8;22), t(2;8)] [p.84]
- For histologic transformation after multiple lines of prior therapies: Perform FISH for BCL6 and MYC rearrangements [p.84]

**Molecular/Genetic Testing** [p.84]

- NGS may be useful for treatment selection [p.84]

**Imaging** [p.84]

- PET/CT imaging [p.84]

## Treatment Algorithm Overview

### Histologic Transformation After Minimal or No Prior Therapy [p.84]

**Initial Diagnostic Categorization** [p.84]

→ PET/CT imaging [p.84]

**Treatment Decision Points** [p.84]

- **DLBCL or HGBL with MYC and BCL6 rearrangements** [p.84]
  - Chemoimmunotherapy (anthracycline-based regimens preferred unless contraindicated) [p.84]
  - ± ISRT for localized presentations, bulky disease, and/or limited osseous disease [p.84]
  - --> See BCEL-C for first-line therapy options [p.84]

- **HGBL with MYC and BCL2 rearrangements** [p.84]
  - Treatment regimens as listed on HGBL-1 [p.84]

**Therapeutic Considerations** [p.84]

- ISRT alone or one course of single-agent therapy including rituximab (for minimal/no prior therapy presentations) [p.84]

**Response Assessment** [p.84]

- → Response to Therapy (HTBCEL-2) [p.84]

### Histologic Transformation After Multiple Lines of Prior Therapies [p.84]

**Treatment Pathway** [p.84]

- Refer to HTBCEL-3 for management [p.84]

## General Notes

**Evidence Category** [p.84]

- All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.84]

**Principles of Radiation Therapy** [p.84]

- Consider ISRT for localized presentations, bulky disease, and/or limited osseous disease [p.84]
- Refer to NHODG-D for detailed principles of radiation therapy [p.84]

**Prior Treatment Definition** [p.84]

- "Multiple lines of prior therapies" includes ≥2 of chemoimmunotherapy regimens for indolent lymphomas prior to histologic transformation. For example, prior treatment with BR and RCHOP [p.84]
