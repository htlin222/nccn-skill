## Diagnosis

- Establish histologic subtype with adequate tissue for biomarker testing (consider rebiopsy or plasma testing if appropriate) [p.45]
- Smoking cessation counseling [p.45]
- Integrate palliative care (NCCN Guidelines for Palliative Care) [p.45]

### Histologic Subtypes [p.45]
- Adenocarcinoma
- Large cell
- NSCLC not otherwise specified (NOS)
- Squamous cell carcinoma

## Workup

### Biomarker Testing Requirements [p.45]

**Category 1 (Mandatory):**
- EGFR mutation testing [p.45]
- ALK testing [p.45]
- RET testing [p.45]
- PD-L1 testing [p.45]

**Standard Testing Panel (via MGPT - Multigene Panel Testing):** [p.45]
- EGFR mutation (exon 19 deletion, L858R, S768I, L861Q, G719X, exon 20 insertion)
- KRAS
- ALK
- ROS1
- BRAF
- NTRK1/2/3
- MET exon 14 skipping
- RET
- ERBB2 (HER2)
- NRG1
- HER2 immunohistochemistry (IHC)
- HGF receptor (c-Met) IHC

**Testing Method:** [p.45]
Complete biomarker testing including molecular assessment via biopsy and/or plasma testing. Combinations of tissue and plasma testing, either concurrently or in sequence are acceptable.

**Important Notes:** [p.45]
- Concurrent testing can improve time to test results and should be considered in the appropriate clinical situation
- Negative results by one method suggests use of complementary method
- Treatment is guided by available results; if unknown, patients are treated as though they do not have driver oncogenes
- For patients requiring urgent therapy start but with pending biomarker testing, consider holding immunotherapy for one cycle unless confirmed absence of driver mutations

## Staging and Treatment Pathways

### EGFR Exon 19 Deletion or L858R Mutation

#### First-Line Therapy [p.47]

**Mutation Discovered Prior to First-Line Systemic Therapy:**

**Preferred (Category 1):** [p.47]
- Osimertinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** (Carboplatin or Cisplatin)/Osimertinib/Pemetrexed (nonsquamous) (Category 1)
- **OR** Lazertinib + Amivantamab-vmjw (Category 1) - Prophylactic anticoagulation recommended; dermatologic and infusion-related reaction prophylaxis recommended [p.47]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances:** [p.47]
- Afatinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** Dacomitinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** Erlotinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** Erlotinib + Bevacizumab
- **OR** Erlotinib + Ramucirumab
- **OR** Gefitinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** Lazertinib (for PS 0-4)

**Mutation Discovered During First-Line Systemic Therapy:** [p.47]
- Interrupt current therapy and start therapy as noted above
- **OR** Add Osimertinib to (Carboplatin or Cisplatin)/Pemetrexed (nonsquamous)

**Note:** If systemic therapy regimen contains immune checkpoint inhibitor (ICI), be aware of long half-life and increased pneumonitis risk within 3 months of osimertinib use [p.47]

#### Subsequent Therapy (Progression on Osimertinib or Lazertinib) [p.48]

**Brain Disease - Symptomatic:** [p.48]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SRS)
- Continue Osimertinib or Lazertinib ± Amivantamab-vmjw
- NCCN Guidelines for CNS Cancers

**Brain Disease - Asymptomatic:** [p.48]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SRS)
- Continue Osimertinib or Lazertinib ± Amivantamab-vmjw
- NCCN Guidelines for CNS Cancers

**Systemic Disease - Limited Progression (up to 3-5 sites):** [p.48]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SABR or surgery)
- Continue Osimertinib or Lazertinib ± Amivantamab-vmjw
- **Preferred:** Carboplatin/Pemetrexed + Amivantamab-vmjw (if not previously given, nonsquamous) (Category 1)
- **OR Useful in Certain Circumstances:** Lazertinib + Amivantamab-vmjw (if not previously given)
- **OR** Systemic therapy

**Systemic Disease - Multiple Lesions:** [p.48]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SABR or surgery)
- Continue Osimertinib or Lazertinib ± Amivantamab-vmjw
- **OR** See therapy for multiple lesions

**Note on Disease Flare:** Beware of flare phenomenon in subset of patients who discontinue TKI. If disease flare occurs, restart TKI [p.48]

#### Subsequent Therapy (Progression on Afatinib, Dacomitinib, Erlotinib, or Gefitinib) [p.49]

**T790M Testing:** [p.49]
Required (Category 1) at progression

**T790M+ Results:** [p.49]
- Osimertinib (for PS 0-4) (Category 1)

**T790M- Results - Brain Disease:** [p.49]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SRS)
- Continue Afatinib, Dacomitinib, Gefitinib, or Erlotinib (± Bevacizumab or Ramucirumab)
- NCCN Guidelines for CNS Cancers

**T790M- Results - Systemic Disease - Limited Progression:** [p.49]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SABR or surgery)
- Continue Afatinib, Dacomitinib, Gefitinib, or Erlotinib (± Bevacizumab or Ramucirumab)

**T790M- Results - Systemic Disease - Multiple Lesions:** [p.49]
- Continue Afatinib, Dacomitinib, Gefitinib, or Erlotinib (± Bevacizumab or Ramucirumab)
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SABR or surgery)
- **OR** See therapy for multiple lesions

### EGFR S768I, L861Q, and/or G719X Mutations

#### First-Line Therapy [p.50]

**Mutation Discovered Prior to First-Line Systemic Therapy:**

**Preferred:** [p.50]
- Afatinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** Osimertinib (for PS 0-4)

**Other Recommended:** [p.50]
- Dacomitinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** Erlotinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** Gefitinib (for PS 0-4)

**Mutation Discovered During First-Line Systemic Therapy:** [p.50]
- Interrupt current therapy and start:
  - Afatinib (preferred)
  - **OR** Osimertinib (preferred)
  - **OR** Dacomitinib
  - **OR** Erlotinib
  - **OR** Gefitinib

### EGFR Exon 20 Insertion Mutation

#### First-Line Therapy [p.51]

**Preferred (Category 1):** [p.51]
- Carboplatin/Pemetrexed + Amivantamab-vmjw (nonsquamous) - Prophylactic anticoagulation and dermatologic/infusion-related reaction prophylaxis recommended [p.51]

**Alternate Options:** [p.51]
- Systemic Therapy (see NSCL-K)

**Maintenance Options:** [p.51]
- Amivantamab-vmjw
- **OR** Sunvozertinib

#### Subsequent Therapy (Progression) [p.51]

- **Preferred:** Amivantamab-vmjw or Sunvozertinib
- **OR** Systemic Therapy (Subsequent, NSCL-K)

**If not previously received:** [p.51]
- Amivantamab-vmjw
- **OR** Sunvozertinib
- **OR** Systemic Therapy (Subsequent)

### KRAS G12C Mutation

#### First-Line Therapy [p.52]

- Systemic Therapy (initial) [p.52]

**Based on PD-L1 Status:**
- PD-L1 ≥1% --> See (NSCL-38) [p.52]
- PD-L1 <1% --> See (NSCL-39) [p.52]

#### Subsequent Therapy [p.52]

**For maintenance (after 4-6 cycles initial systemic therapy):** [p.52]
- Adagrasib (may be used after at least one line of therapy if no previous KRAS G12C-targeted therapy)
- **OR** Sotorasib (may be used after at least one line of therapy if no previous KRAS G12C-targeted therapy)

### ALK Gene Fusion

#### First-Line Therapy [p.53]

**Mutation Discovered Prior to First-Line Systemic Therapy:**

**Preferred (Category 1):** [p.53]
- Alectinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** Brigatinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** Ensartinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** Lorlatinib (for PS 0-4)

**Useful in Certain Circumstances (Category 1):** [p.53]
- Ceritinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** Crizotinib (for PS 0-4)

**Mutation Discovered During First-Line Systemic Therapy:** [p.53]
- Interrupt current therapy and start:
  - Alectinib (preferred)
  - **OR** Brigatinib (preferred)
  - **OR** Ensartinib (preferred)
  - **OR** Lorlatinib (preferred)
  - **OR** Ceritinib
  - **OR** Crizotinib

#### Subsequent Therapy (Progression on Alectinib, Brigatinib, Ceritinib, Ensartinib, or Lorlatinib) [p.54]

**Brain Disease - Symptomatic:** [p.54]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SRS)
- Continue Alectinib or Brigatinib or Ceritinib or Ensartinib or Lorlatinib
- Lorlatinib (if not previously given)
- NCCN Guidelines for CNS Cancers

**Brain Disease - Asymptomatic:** [p.54]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SRS)
- Continue Alectinib or Brigatinib or Ceritinib or Ensartinib or Lorlatinib
- Lorlatinib (if not previously given)

**Systemic Disease - Limited Progression (up to 3-5 sites):** [p.54]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SABR or surgery)
- Continue Alectinib or Brigatinib or Ceritinib or Ensartinib or Lorlatinib
- Lorlatinib (if not previously given)

**Systemic Disease - Multiple Lesions:** [p.54]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SABR or surgery)
- Continue Alectinib or Brigatinib or Ceritinib or Ensartinib or Lorlatinib
- Lorlatinib (if not previously given)
- **OR** Systemic therapy
- **OR** See therapy for multiple lesions

**Note on Resistant Mutations:** Lorlatinib is an option for resistant mutations such as ALK G1202R and L1196M (except compound L1196M/G1202R) [p.54]

#### Subsequent Therapy (Progression on Crizotinib) [p.55]

**Brain Disease - Symptomatic:** [p.55]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SRS)
- Alectinib or Brigatinib or Ceritinib or Ensartinib or Lorlatinib (for PS 0-4)
- NCCN Guidelines for CNS Cancers

**Brain Disease - Asymptomatic:** [p.55]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SRS)
- Alectinib or Brigatinib or Ceritinib or Ensartinib or Lorlatinib (for PS 0-4)

**Systemic Disease - Limited Progression (up to 3-5 sites):** [p.55]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SABR or surgery)
- Continue Crizotinib
- **OR** Alectinib or Brigatinib or Ceritinib or Ensartinib or Lorlatinib (for PS 0-4)

**Systemic Disease - Multiple Lesions:** [p.55]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SABR or surgery)
- Continue Crizotinib
- **OR** Alectinib or Brigatinib or Ceritinib or Ensartinib or Lorlatinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** Systemic therapy
- **OR** See therapy for multiple lesions

**Note on Intolerance:** Patients who are intolerant to crizotinib may be switched to ceritinib, alectinib, brigatinib, ensartinib, or lorlatinib [p.55]

### ROS1 Gene Fusion

#### First-Line Therapy [p.56]

**Mutation Discovered Prior to First-Line Systemic Therapy:**

**Preferred:** [p.56]
- Crizotinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** Entrectinib (for PS 0-4) - Better for patients with brain metastases [p.56]
- **OR** Repotrectinib (for PS 0-4) - Better for patients with brain metastases [p.56]
- **OR** Taletrectinib (for PS 0-4) - Better for patients with brain metastases [p.56]

**Mutation Discovered During First-Line Systemic Therapy:** [p.56]
- Interrupt current therapy and start:
  - Crizotinib
  - **OR** Entrectinib - Better for patients with brain metastases [p.56]
  - **OR** Repotrectinib - Better for patients with brain metastases [p.56]
  - **OR** Taletrectinib - Better for patients with brain metastases [p.56]

#### Subsequent Therapy (Progression) [p.57]

**Brain Disease - Symptomatic:** [p.57]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SRS)
- Repotrectinib (preferred, if not previously given) - Better for resistant mutations such as ROS1 G2032R [p.57]
- **OR** Taletrectinib (preferred, if not previously given) - Better for resistant mutations such as ROS1 G2032R [p.57]
- **OR** Lorlatinib (preferred)
- **OR** Entrectinib (useful in certain circumstances, if previously treated with crizotinib)
- **OR** Clinical trial
- NCCN Guidelines for CNS Cancers

**Brain Disease - Asymptomatic:** [p.57]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SRS)
- Continue Crizotinib, Entrectinib, Repotrectinib, or Taletrectinib
- **OR** Repotrectinib (if not previously given)
- **OR** Taletrectinib (if not previously given)
- **OR** Lorlatinib

**Systemic Disease - Limited Progression (up to 3-5 sites):** [p.57]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SABR or surgery)
- Continue Entrectinib, Crizotinib, Repotrectinib, or Taletrectinib
- **OR** Repotrectinib (if not previously given)
- **OR** Taletrectinib (if not previously given)
- **OR** Lorlatinib

**Systemic Disease - Multiple Lesions:** [p.57]
- Consider definitive local therapy (e.g., SABR or surgery)
- Continue Entrectinib, Crizotinib, Repotrectinib, or Taletrectinib
- **OR** Systemic therapy options
- **OR** See therapy for multiple lesions

### BRAF V600E Mutation

#### First-Line Therapy [p.58]

**Mutation Discovered Prior to First-Line Systemic Therapy:**

**Preferred:** [p.58]
- Dabrafenib/Trametinib (for PS 0-4)
- **OR** Binimetinib/Encorafenib (for PS 0-4)

**Useful in Certain Circumstances:** [p.58]
- Dabrafenib (for PS 0-4) - Single-agent option if combination not tolerated [p.58]
- **OR** Vemurafenib (for PS 0-4) - Single-agent option if combination not tolerated [p.58]

**Other Recommended:** [p.58]
- Systemic Therapy

**Mutation Discovered During First-Line Systemic Therapy:** [p.58]
- Complete planned systemic therapy, including maintenance therapy, and start Dabrafenib/Trametinib or Binimetinib/Encorafenib
- **OR** Interrupt current therapy and start Dabrafenib/Trametinib or Binimetinib/Encorafenib

#### Subsequent Therapy (Progression) [p.58]

- Dabrafenib/Trametinib (single-agent option if combination not tolerated)
- **OR** Binimetinib/Encorafenib
- **OR** Systemic Therapy (Subsequent)

### PD-L1 Positive (≥1%) -- Negative for Actionable Biomarkers Above

## Key Clinical Principles

### General Recommendations [p.45]

All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated.

### Performance Status Notation [p.47]

"PS 0-4" in treatment recommendations indicates dosing and selection considerations for performance status.

### Biomarker-Directed Therapy Principles [p.47]

Refer to Principles of Biomarker-Directed Therapy for Advanced or Metastatic Disease (NSCL-J).

### Biomarker Analysis [p.45, p.47]

Refer to Principles of Biomarker Analysis (NSCL-H).

### CNS Disease Management [p.48, p.54, p.57]

Refer to NCCN Guidelines for CNS Cancers for definitive local therapy options including SRS, SABR, and surgery.

### Image-Guided Thermal Ablation [p.48, p.54, p.57]

IGTA therapy (e.g., cryotherapy, microwave, radiofrequency) may be an option for select patients. Refer to Principles of Image-Guided Thermal Ablation Therapy (NSCL-D).

### Monitoring at Progression [p.48, p.49, p.54]

Plasma or tissue-based testing via MGPT should be considered at progression for genomic resistance mechanisms. If plasma-based testing is negative, tissue-based testing with rebiopsy material is strongly recommended [p.49, p.54].

Consider biopsy at time of progression to rule out SCLC transformation (approximately 6%) [p.48, p.49, p.54].

### TKI Flare Phenomenon [p.48, p.54, p.55, p.60, p.61]

Beware of flare phenomenon in subset of patients who discontinue TKI. If disease flare occurs, restart TKI [p.48, p.54, p.55, p.60, p.61].
