## Summary of Key Evidence

### Classification

The WHO divides lung cancer into two major classes based on its biology, therapy, and prognosis: NSCLC (discussed in these guidelines) and SCLC [p.133]. NSCLC accounts for more than 80% of all lung cancer cases and includes two major types: 1) nonsquamous (including adenocarcinoma, large-cell carcinoma, and other subtypes), and 2) squamous cell (epidermoid) carcinoma [p.133].

Adenocarcinoma is the most common subtype of lung cancer seen in the United States and is also the most frequently occurring histology in individuals who have never smoked cigarettes [p.133]. The lung adenocarcinoma classification was developed by an international panel and adopted by WHO [p.133]. All NSCLC should be classified according to subtype using the WHO Guidelines [p.133].

### Prognostic Factors

Certain prognostic factors are predictive of survival in patients with NSCLC. Good prognostic factors include:

- Early-stage disease at diagnosis [p.133]
- Good performance status (PS) (ECOG 0, 1) [p.133]
- No significant weight loss (<5%) [p.133]
- Female gender [p.133]
