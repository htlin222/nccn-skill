## Summary of Key Evidence

**Epidemiology** [p.129]
- Estimated 229,410 individuals diagnosed with lung cancer in the United States in 2026 [p.129]
- Estimated 124,990 lung cancer-related deaths in the United States in 2026 [p.129]
- NSCLC accounts for approximately 87% of lung cancers [p.129]

**Survival Trends** [p.129]
- Survival rates for patients with NSCLC have improved over the past few decades, possibly due to increased screening, biomarker testing, and treatment advances [p.129]
- Lung cancer is projected to remain the leading cause of cancer-related death in the United States despite improvements [p.129]
- 5-year overall survival (OS) rate for patients with NSCLC in the United States is approximately 32% [p.129]
- Survival is influenced by multiple factors, including histology, disease stage, age, and presence of actionable biomarkers [p.129]

## Guideline Development

**NCCN Guidelines Panel** [p.129]
- NCCN Guidelines are updated by a multidisciplinary panel of NSCLC-focused experts including:
  - Medical oncology [p.129]
  - Surgical oncology [p.129]
  - Pulmonary medicine [p.129]
  - Radiation oncology [p.129]
  - Pathology [p.129]
  - Radiology [p.129]
  - Patient advocacy [p.129]

**Guideline Scope** [p.129]
- These Guidelines include recommendations for treatment of patients with NSCLC [p.129]
- This document describes clinical evidence supporting recommendations related to systemic therapy for patients with advanced or metastatic NSCLC with an actionable biomarker [p.129]
