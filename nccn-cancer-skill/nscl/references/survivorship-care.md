## Monitoring and Managing Survivorship Needs During Systemic Therapy [p.97]

### Symptom and Patient-Reported Outcome Monitoring [p.97]
- Monitor symptoms using NCCN Guidelines for Palliative Care (PAL-2) [p.97]
- Consider ESMO Clinical Practice Guideline – The Role of Patient-Reported Outcome Measures in the Continuum of Cancer Clinical Care [p.97]

### Identification and Management of Cancer-Related Complications [p.97]
- Manage cancer-related complications and side effects of therapy [p.97]
- Refer to NCCN Guidelines for Cancer-Associated Venous Thromboembolic Disease [p.97]
- Refer to NCCN Guidelines for Management of Immune Checkpoint-Related Toxicities [p.97]
- Refer to NCCN Guidelines for Cancer-Related Fatigue [p.97]
- Refer to NCCN Guidelines for Antiemesis [p.97]

### Psychosocial Evaluation and Support [p.97]
- Conduct psychosocial evaluation and referrals per NCCN Guidelines for Distress Management [p.97]
- Address the Stigma of Lung Cancer (American Lung Association resource) [p.97]

### Financial Toxicity and Social Determinants of Health [p.97]
- Monitor financial toxicity and social determinants of health (SDOH) [p.97]
- Refer to Financial Advocacy Services Guidelines (Association of Cancer Care Centers) [p.97]
- Refer to Financial Toxicity and Cancer Treatment (National Cancer Institute) [p.97]

### Palliative Care [p.97]
- Make palliative care referrals per NCCN Guidelines for Palliative Care [p.97]

### Care Coordination [p.97]
- Coordinate care for optimization of comorbid conditions [p.97]
- Consider pulmonary rehabilitation referral for patients with COPD or pulmonary hypertension [p.97]

## NSCLC Long-Term Follow-Up Care [p.97]

### Cancer Surveillance [p.97]
- Implement cancer surveillance per NSCL-17 [p.97]

### Immunizations [p.97]
- Annual influenza vaccination [p.97]
- Herpes zoster vaccine [p.97]
- Pneumococcal vaccination with revaccination as appropriate [p.97]
- COVID vaccination as per the guidance of the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) [p.97]
- Hepatitis vaccination [p.97]

### Exercise and Pulmonary Rehabilitation [p.97]
- Refer to NCCN Guidelines for Survivorship [p.97]
- Implement lung cancer-specific exercise recommendations [p.97]
- Consider perioperative pulmonary rehabilitation for patients with comorbid COPD and those who have undergone lung surgery [p.97]

### Smoking Cessation [p.97]
- Refer to NCCN Guidelines for Smoking Cessation [p.97]

### Chronic Cancer Pain Management [p.97]
- Refer to NCCN Guidelines for Adult Cancer Pain [p.97]

### Psychosocial Support and Referrals [p.97]
- Conduct psychosocial evaluation and referrals [p.97]

### Financial Toxicity and Social Determinants [p.97]
- Monitor financial toxicity and SDOH with appropriate referrals [p.97]

### Care Coordination with Primary Care [p.97]
- Coordinate care with primary care clinicians [p.97]

### Fatigue Management [p.97]
- Refer to NCCN Guidelines for Cancer-Related Fatigue [p.97]
- Refer to Cancer-Related Fatigue in Lung Cancer: A Research Agenda: An Official American Thoracic Society Research Statement [p.97]

## Counseling Regarding Health Promotion and Wellness [p.98]

- Maintain a healthy weight [p.98]
- Adopt a physically active lifestyle (regular physical activity: 30 minutes of moderate-intensity physical activity on most days of the week) [p.98]
- Consume a healthy diet with emphasis on plant sources [p.98]
- Limit consumption of alcohol if one consumes alcoholic beverages [p.98]

## Additional Health Monitoring [p.98]

### Routine Monitoring [p.98]
- Routine blood pressure, cholesterol, and glucose monitoring [p.98]

### Bone Health [p.98]
- Bone density testing as appropriate [p.98]

### Dental Health [p.98]
- Routine dental examinations [p.98]

### Skin Health [p.98]
- Routine sun protection [p.98]

## Resources [p.98]

- National Cancer Institute Facing Forward: Life After Cancer Treatment: https://www.cancer.gov/publications/patient-education/facing-forward [p.98]

## Cancer Screening Recommendations [p.98]

Note: These recommendations are for individuals at average risk for cancer; patients at high risk should be individualized. [p.98]

### Colorectal Cancer Screening [p.98]
- Refer to NCCN Guidelines for Colorectal Cancer Screening [p.98]

### Prostate Cancer Screening [p.98]
- Refer to NCCN Guidelines for Prostate Cancer Early Detection [p.98]

### Breast Cancer Screening [p.98]
- Refer to NCCN Guidelines for Breast Cancer Screening and Diagnosis [p.98]
