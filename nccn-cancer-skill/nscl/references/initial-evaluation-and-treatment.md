## Diagnosis

### Pathologic Diagnosis of NSCLC

Initial workup requires pathological confirmation of non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) prior to staging and treatment planning [p.26].

### Clinical Assessment

All patients require:
- Pathology review [p.26]
- History and Physical examination (include performance status and weight loss) [p.26]
- CT chest and upper abdomen with contrast, including adrenals [p.26]
- CBC, platelets [p.26]
- Chemistry profile [p.26]

## Workup

### Initial Evaluation Components

- **Smoking Cessation:** Advice, counseling, and pharmacotherapy using the 5 A's Framework (Ask, Advise, Assess, Assist, Arrange) [p.26]
- **Palliative Care Integration:** Integrate palliative care as appropriate (see NCCN Guidelines for Palliative Care) [p.26]
- **Geriatric Assessment:** For tools to aid in the optimal assessment and management of NSCLC in older adults, see the NCCN Guidelines for Older Adult Oncology [p.26]

**Note:** Enhanced frailty or geriatric assessments may predict complications better following treatment modalities, particularly surgery. A preferred frailty assessment system has not been established [p.26].

### HIV Considerations

Modification to cancer treatment should not be made solely based on HIV status. See NCCN Guidelines for Cancer in People with HIV [p.26].

## Staging

### TNM Classification and Stage-Specific Pathways

#### Stage IA (Peripheral T1, N0)

**Pretreatment Evaluation:**
- Pulmonary function tests (PFTs), if not previously done [p.27]
- Bronchoscopy (intraoperative preferred) [p.27]
- Consider pathologic lymph node evaluation [p.27]
- FDG-PET/CT scan, if not previously done [p.27]

**Mediastinal Node Assessment:**
- Methods for evaluation include mediastinoscopy, mediastinotomy, EBUS, EUS, and CT-guided biopsy [p.27]
- An EBUS-TBNA negative for malignancy in a clinically (FDG-PET/CT and/or CT) positive mediastinum should undergo subsequent mediastinoscopy prior to surgical resection [p.27]
- There is low likelihood of positive mediastinal lymph nodes when these nodes are CT and FDG-PET/CT negative in peripheral tumors (outer third of lung) ≤3 cm. Thus, pretreatment pathologic evaluation is optional in these settings [p.27]
- Invasive mediastinal staging is recommended for central tumors [p.27]

**FDG-PET/CT Interpretation:**
- FDG-PET/CT performed skull base to mid-thigh [p.27]
- Positive FDG-PET/CT scan findings for mediastinal nodal or distant disease need pathologic or other radiologic confirmation [p.27]
- If FDG-PET/CT scan is positive in the mediastinum, lymph node status needs pathologic confirmation [p.27]

#### Stage IB (Peripheral T2a, N0), Stage I (Central T1-T2a, N0), Stage II, and Stage IIB/IIIA

**Pretreatment Evaluation:**
- PFTs, if not previously done [p.28]
- Bronchoscopy (intraoperative preferred) [p.28]
- Consider pathologic lymph node evaluation [p.28]
- FDG-PET/CT scan, if not previously done [p.28]
- Evaluate for perioperative systemic therapy [p.28]
- MRI brain with contrast (or CT of head with contrast if MRI not possible) [p.28]

## First-Line Therapy

### Treatment Pathways by Stage and Operability Status

#### Operable Patients with Negative Mediastinal Nodes

**Treatment:** Surgical resection with lymph node dissection or systematic lymph node sampling [p.27]

**Note:** Surgical resection is considered the first option for operable patients with cancer with negative nodes, but patients may also be informed about SABR (stereotactic ablative radiation therapy) as an alternative option through shared decision-making [p.27].

#### Medically Inoperable or High Surgical Risk

**Treatment Options:**
- Definitive radiation therapy (RT), preferably SABR [p.27]
- If SABR is not feasible, definitive RT or concurrent chemoradiation could be considered [p.28]

**Definitions:**
- Peripheral tumors = outer third of lung; Central tumors = inner two thirds of lung [p.26]
- T3, N0 related to size or satellite nodules [p.26]

#### Patients with Positive Mediastinal Nodes

Refer to:
- Stage IIB/IIIA/IIIB → Pretreatment Evaluation (NSCL-8) or Treatment algorithms
- Stage IIIB/IIIC → Pretreatment Evaluation (NSCL-13) or Treatment algorithms

### Surgical and Radiation Therapy Principles

- **Surgical Therapy Principles:** See NSCL-B [p.27]
- **Radiation Therapy Principles:** See NSCL-C [p.27]
- **Image-Guided Thermal Ablation Therapy (IGTA):** May be an option for select patients (eg, cryotherapy, microwave, radiofrequency); see Principles of Image-Guided Thermal Ablation Therapy (NSCL-D) [p.27]
- **Perioperative Systemic Therapy:** See NSCL-E [p.28]
- **Concurrent Chemoradiation Regimens:** See NSCL-F [p.28]

### Multidisciplinary Evaluation Requirement

Prior to treatment, multidisciplinary evaluation that includes treating physicians and specialists in obtaining tissue diagnosis (thoracic surgery, interventional pulmonology, and interventional radiology) is required to determine the safest and most efficient approach for biopsy, or to provide consensus that a biopsy is too risky or difficult, that a clinical diagnosis of lung cancer is appropriate, and that treatment is warranted [p.27].
