## First-Line Systemic Therapy for Adenocarcinoma, Large Cell, NSCLC NOS (PS 0–2) [p.115]

### NO Contraindications to PD-1/PD-L1 Inhibitors AND NO EGFR/ALK/RET/ROS1 Alterations

**Preferred** [p.115]
- Carboplatin/Pemetrexed + Pembrolizumab (Category 1) [p.115]
- Cisplatin/Pemetrexed + Pembrolizumab (Category 1) [p.115]
- (Carboplatin or Cisplatin)/Pemetrexed + Cemiplimab-rwlc (Category 1) [p.115]

**Other Recommended** [p.115]
- Carboplatin/Paclitaxel + Atezolizumab + Bevacizumab (Category 1) [p.115]
- Albumin-bound Paclitaxel/Carboplatin + Atezolizumab [p.115]
- Ipilimumab + Nivolumab [p.115]
- (Carboplatin or Cisplatin)/Pemetrexed + Ipilimumab + Nivolumab (Category 1) [p.115]
- (Carboplatin or Cisplatin)/Paclitaxel + Cemiplimab-rwlc (Category 1) [p.115]
- Albumin-bound Paclitaxel/Carboplatin + Durvalumab + Tremelimumab-actl (Category 1) [p.115]
- (Carboplatin or Cisplatin)/Pemetrexed + Durvalumab + Tremelimumab-actl (Category 1) [p.115]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances** [p.115]
- Carboplatin/Paclitaxel + Bevacizumab (Category 1) [p.115]
- Carboplatin/Pemetrexed + Bevacizumab [p.115]
- Cisplatin/Pemetrexed + Bevacizumab [p.115]
- Carboplatin-combination therapy (Category 1): Albumin-bound Paclitaxel, Docetaxel, Etoposide, Gemcitabine, Paclitaxel, or Pemetrexed [p.115]
- Cisplatin-combination therapy (Category 1): Docetaxel, Etoposide, Gemcitabine, Paclitaxel, or Pemetrexed [p.115]
- Docetaxel/Gemcitabine [p.115]
- Gemcitabine/Vinorelbine [p.115]
- Albumin-bound Paclitaxel [p.115]
- Docetaxel [p.115]
- Gemcitabine [p.115]
- Paclitaxel [p.115]
- Pemetrexed [p.115]

Same regimen options listed above apply [p.115]

## Maintenance Therapy Principles [p.117]

**Continuation Maintenance:** Use of at least one agent given in first line, beyond 4–6 cycles, in the absence of disease progression [p.117]

**Switch Maintenance:** Initiation of a different agent, not included in first-line regimen, in the absence of disease progression, after 4–6 cycles of initial therapy [p.117]

**Duration:** [p.117]
- Patients should receive maintenance therapy for 2 years if they received front-line immunotherapy [p.117]
- Patients should receive maintenance therapy until progression if they received second-line immunotherapy [p.117]

### Adenocarcinoma, Large Cell, NSCLC NOS and Squamous Cell Carcinoma (PS 0–2) [p.117]

**Continuation Maintenance** [p.117]
- Bevacizumab (Category 1) [p.117]
- Pemetrexed (Category 1) [p.117]
- Pemetrexed + Bevacizumab (if bevacizumab used with first-line pemetrexed/platinum) [p.117]
- Pemetrexed + Pembrolizumab (Category 1, if pembrolizumab/carboplatin/pemetrexed or pembrolizumab/cisplatin/pemetrexed given) [p.117]
- Atezolizumab + Bevacizumab (Category 1, if atezolizumab/carboplatin/paclitaxel/bevacizumab given) [p.117]
- Ipilimumab + Nivolumab (if nivolumab + ipilimumab ± chemotherapy given) [p.117]
- Atezolizumab (if atezolizumab/carboplatin/albumin-bound paclitaxel given) [p.117]
- Gemcitabine (Category 2B) [p.117]
- Cemiplimab-rwlc (Category 1, if cemiplimab-rwlc combination therapy given) [p.117]
- Pemetrexed + Cemiplimab-rwlc (Category 1, if cemiplimab-rwlc + pemetrexed + (carboplatin or cisplatin) given) [p.117]
- Durvalumab (if tremelimumab-actl combination therapy given) [p.117]
- Pemetrexed + Durvalumab (if tremelimumab-actl + durvalumab + (carboplatin or cisplatin) + pemetrexed given) [p.117]

**Switch Maintenance** [p.117]
- Pemetrexed [p.117]

**Continuation Maintenance for Squamous Cell Carcinoma** [p.117]
- Pembrolizumab (if pembrolizumab/carboplatin/(paclitaxel or albumin-bound paclitaxel) given) [p.117]
- Ipilimumab + Nivolumab [p.117]
- Gemcitabine (Category 2B) [p.117]
- Cemiplimab-rwlc (Category 1) [p.117]
- Durvalumab [p.117]

### Adenocarcinoma, Large Cell, NSCLC NOS and Squamous Cell Carcinoma (PS 3–4) [p.117]

## Additional Options for Further Progression [p.118]

**Note:** EGFR mutations include exon 19 deletion, L858R, S768I, L861Q, G719X mutations and exon 20 insertion [p.118]

**Pembrolizumab** is approved for patients with NSCLC tumors with PD-L1 expression levels ≥1%, as determined by an FDA-approved test [p.118]

**Options for further progression** are best supportive care or clinical trial [p.118]

## NCCN Categories of Evidence and Consensus [p.127]

**Category 1:** Based upon high-level evidence (≥1 randomized phase 3 trials or high-quality, robust meta-analyses), there is uniform NCCN consensus (≥85% support of the Panel) that the intervention is appropriate [p.127]

**Category 2A:** Based upon lower-level evidence, there is uniform NCCN consensus (≥85% support of the Panel) that the intervention is appropriate [p.127]

**Category 2B:** Based upon lower-level evidence, there is NCCN consensus (≥50%, but <85% support of the Panel) that the intervention is appropriate [p.127]

**Category 3:** Based upon any level of evidence, there is major NCCN disagreement that the intervention is appropriate [p.127]

All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.115, p.127]

---

## NCCN Categories of Preference [p.127]

**Preferred:** Interventions that are based on superior efficacy, safety, and evidence; and, when appropriate, affordability [p.127]

**Other Recommended:** Other interventions that may be somewhat less efficacious, more toxic, or based on less mature data; or significantly less affordable for similar outcomes [p.127]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances:** Other interventions that may be used for selected patient populations (defined with recommendation) [p.127]

All recommendations are considered appropriate [p.127]
