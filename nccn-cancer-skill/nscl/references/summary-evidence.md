## Summary of Key Evidence

The NCCN Guidelines for NSCLC outline treatment strategies for patients diagnosed with non-small cell lung cancer [p.226]. Multiple treatment modalities are available as options for the management of early-stage disease [p.226]. For advanced or metastatic disease, systemic therapy is the primary treatment modality; however, definitive local therapy may also be used for certain patients [p.226].

Biomarker testing should be incorporated into the treatment plan for most patients with NSCLC to identify the most appropriate systemic therapy options [p.226]. The Panel will continue to update the NCCN Guidelines for NSCLC based on clinical evidence and consensus [p.226].
