# Surveillance (NSCL-16) -- Algorithm Reference

<!-- Source: NCCN NSCLC v5.2026, page 43 -->

## Follow-Up

### No Evidence of Clinical/Radiographic Disease

#### Stage I–II (Primary Treatment Included Surgery ± Systemic Therapy) [p.43]

- **H&P and chest CT** [p.43] ± contrast every 6 months for 2–3 years
- Then H&P and a low-dose non–contrast-enhanced chest CT annually [p.43]
- Note: Residual or new radiographic abnormalities may require more frequent imaging [p.43]

#### Stage I–II (Primary Treatment Included RT) or Stage III or Stage IV (Oligometastatic with All Sites Treated with Definitive Intent) [p.43]

- **H&P and chest CT** [p.43] ± contrast every 3–6 months for 3 years
- Then H&P and chest CT ± contrast every 6 months for 2 years [p.43]
- Then H&P and a low-dose non–contrast-enhanced chest CT annually [p.43]
- Note: Residual or new radiographic abnormalities may require more frequent imaging [p.43]

#### Additional Surveillance Recommendations [p.43]

- Smoking cessation advice, counseling, and pharmacotherapy [p.43]
- **FDG-PET/CT** is not routinely indicated [p.43]; however, it can be used as a problem-solving tool to differentiate benign conditions (such as atelectasis, consolidation, and radiation fibrosis) from neoplasm on standard CT imaging [p.43]
  - If FDG-PET/CT is used after RT, histopathologic confirmation of recurrent disease is needed when possible because persistent inflammatory hypermetabolism is common after prior RT [p.43]
  - Increasing intensity and/or size of focal hypermetabolism over time is the most specific indicator of recurrence that warrants confirmation [p.43]
- Brain MRI with and without contrast as clinically indicated based on risk assessment [p.43]
  - Note: Risk factors include EGFR mutations, ALK gene fusions, and node-positive disease [p.43]
- Cancer Survivorship Care (NSCL-G) [p.43]

### Recurrence Detection

#### Suspected Locoregional Recurrence or Distant Metastases [p.43]

- FDG-PET/CT [p.43]
- Brain MRI with and without contrast [p.43]

→ See Therapy for Recurrence and Metastasis (NSCL-18) [UNRESOLVED: nscl-18.md]

---

**Note:** All recommendations are (Category 2A) unless otherwise indicated [p.43]

<!-- Appendix Notes -->

**mm** Timing of CT scans within Guidelines parameters is a clinical decision [p.43]

**nn** FDG-PET/CT is currently not warranted in routine surveillance and follow-up of patients with NSCLC. However, many benign conditions are difficult to differentiate from neoplasm on standard CT imaging, and FDG-PET/CT can differentiate true malignancy in these settings [p.43]

**oo** Examples of high-risk factors include, but are not limited to, EGFR mutations, ALK gene fusions, and node-positive disease [p.43]
