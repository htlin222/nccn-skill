## Emerging Biomarkers to Identify Novel Therapies for Patients with Metastatic NSCLC

All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.107].

### Genetic Alterations and Available Targeted Agents

**High-level MET amplification** [p.107]
- Definition: Copy number ≥10 consistent with high-level MET amplification for NGS-based results; definition may evolve and differ by assay [p.107]
- **Preferred/Other Recommended:**
  - Capmatinib [p.107]
  - Tepotinib [p.107]
  - Crizotinib [p.107]
- Note: In patients with EGFR-mutant NSCLC who develop high-level MET amplifications, these agents with continuation of osimertinib is acceptable [p.107]

**FGFR mutations or gene fusions** [p.107]
- For oncogenic or likely oncogenic FGFR mutations, refer to definitions at oncokb.org [p.107]
- **Preferred/Other Recommended:**
  - Erdafitinib [p.107]

## Principles of Biomarker-Directed Therapy for Advanced or Metastatic Disease

### General Principles [p.108]

- Contraindications for treatment with PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitors may include active or previously documented autoimmune disease and/or current use of immunosuppressive agents [p.108]
- Some oncogenic drivers (EGFR exon 19 deletion or L858R mutation; ALK, RET, or ROS1 gene fusions) have been shown to be associated with less benefit from PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitors [p.108]
- An FDA-approved biosimilar is an appropriate substitute for any recommended systemic biologic therapy [p.108]
- Atezolizumab and hyaluronidase-tqjs subcutaneous injection may be substituted for IV atezolizumab with different dosing and administration instructions [p.108]
- Pembrolizumab and berahyaluronidase alfa-pmph subcutaneous injection may be substituted for IV pembrolizumab with different dosing and administration instructions [p.108]
- Amivantamab and hyaluronidase-lpuj subcutaneous injection may be substituted for IV amivantamab-vmjw with different dosing and administration instructions [p.108]
- If progression on PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitor, using a PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitor is not recommended [p.108]
- Criteria for bevacizumab treatment: nonsquamous NSCLC and no recent history of hemoptysis [p.108]
- Bevacizumab should not be given as a single agent unless as maintenance if initially used with chemotherapy [p.108]
- Bevacizumab should be given until progression [p.108]

### Monitoring [p.108]

**During Initial Therapy:**
- Response assessment after 2 cycles, then every 2–4 cycles with CT of known or high-risk sites of disease (chest, abdomen, pelvis) with or without contrast or when clinically indicated [p.108]
- Timing of CT scans within Guidelines parameters is a clinical decision [p.108]

**During Subsequent Therapy or Maintenance Therapy:**
- Response assessment with CT of known or high-risk sites of disease (chest, abdomen, pelvis) with or without contrast every 6–12 weeks [p.108]
- Timing of CT scans within Guidelines parameters is a clinical decision [p.108]

## EGFR Mutations — Biomarker-Directed Therapy [p.109]

### EGFR Exon 19 Deletion or L858R Mutation

**First-line therapy** [p.109]
- **Preferred:**
  - Afatinib (Category 1) [p.109]
  - Erlotinib (Category 1) [p.109]
  - Dacomitinib (Category 1) [p.109]
  - Gefitinib (Category 1) [p.109]
  - Osimertinib (Category 1) [p.109]
  - (Carboplatin or Cisplatin)/Osimertinib/Pemetrexed (nonsquamous) (Category 1) [p.109]
  - Erlotinib + Ramucirumab (Category 1) [p.109]
  - Erlotinib + Bevacizumab (nonsquamous) (Category 1) [p.109]
  - Lazertinib + Amivantamab-vmjw (Category 1) [p.109]
  - Lazertinib (Category 1) [p.109]

**Subsequent therapy** [p.109]
- **Preferred:**
  - Osimertinib [p.109]
  - Carboplatin/Pemetrexed + Amivantamab-vmjw (nonsquamous) [p.109]
  - Datopotamab deruxtecan-dlnk (nonsquamous) [p.109]
  - Lazertinib + Amivantamab-vmjw [p.109]

### EGFR S768I, L861Q, and/or G719X Mutations

**First-line therapy** [p.109]

## PD-L1 ≥50% First-Line Therapy (PS 0–2) [p.110]

### Adenocarcinoma, Large Cell, NSCLC NOS

**Preferred** [p.110]
- Pembrolizumab followed by maintenance Pembrolizumab (Category 1) [p.110]
- (Carboplatin or Cisplatin)/Pemetrexed + Pembrolizumab followed by maintenance Pemetrexed + Pembrolizumab, then Pemetrexed (Category 1) [p.110]
- Atezolizumab followed by maintenance Atezolizumab (Category 1) [p.110]
- Cemiplimab-rwlc followed by maintenance Cemiplimab (Category 1) [p.110]
- (Carboplatin or Cisplatin)/Pemetrexed + Cemiplimab-rwlc followed by maintenance Cemiplimab ± Pemetrexed (Category 1) [p.110]

**Other Recommended** [p.110]
- Carboplatin/Paclitaxel + Atezolizumab + Bevacizumab followed by maintenance Bevacizumab ± Atezolizumab (Category 1) [p.110]
- Albumin-bound Paclitaxel/Carboplatin + Atezolizumab followed by maintenance Atezolizumab [p.110]
- (Carboplatin or Cisplatin)/Pemetrexed + Ipilimumab + Nivolumab followed by maintenance Ipilimumab + Nivolumab (Category 1) [p.110]
- (Carboplatin or Cisplatin)/Paclitaxel + Cemiplimab-rwlc followed by maintenance Cemiplimab (Category 1) [p.110]
- Albumin-bound Paclitaxel/Carboplatin + Durvalumab + Tremelimumab-actl followed by maintenance Durvalumab (Category 2B) [p.110]
- (Carboplatin or Cisplatin)/Pemetrexed + Durvalumab + Tremelimumab-actl followed by maintenance Durvalumab ± Pemetrexed (Category 2B) [p.110]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances** [p.110]
- Ipilimumab + Nivolumab followed by maintenance Ipilimumab + Nivolumab (Category 1) [p.110]

### Squamous Cell Carcinoma
