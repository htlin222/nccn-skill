## Literature Search Methodology

**Search Strategy** [p.129]
- Electronic search of the PubMed database was performed prior to the update of the NCCN Guidelines for Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer [p.129]
- Objective: Obtain key literature in NSCLC published since the previous Guidelines update [p.129]
- Search term used: non-small cell lung cancer [p.129]

**Database Selection Rationale** [p.129]
- PubMed database was chosen because it is the most widely used resource for medical literature [p.129]
- PubMed indexes peer-reviewed biomedical literature [p.129]

**Study Selection Criteria** [p.129]
- Search results were narrowed by selecting studies in humans published in English [p.129]
- Results were confined to the following article types:
  - Clinical Trial, Phase 2 [p.129]
  - Clinical Trial, Phase 3 [p.129]
  - Clinical Trial, Phase 4 [p.129]
  - Guideline [p.129]
  - Practice Guideline [p.129]
  - Meta-Analysis [p.129]
  - Randomized Controlled Trial [p.129]
  - Systematic Reviews [p.129]
  - Validation Studies [p.129]

## Evidence Inclusion

**Data Sources** [p.129]
- Data from key PubMed articles included in this version of the Discussion section [p.129]
- Articles from additional sources deemed as relevant to these guidelines as discussed by the panel during the Guidelines update have been included [p.129]

**Evidence for Recommendations Without High-Level Evidence** [p.129]
- Recommendations for which high-level evidence is lacking are based on:
  - The panel's review of lower-level evidence [p.129]
  - Expert opinion [p.129]
