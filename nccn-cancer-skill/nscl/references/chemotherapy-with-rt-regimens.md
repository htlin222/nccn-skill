# Chemotherapy with RT regimens (NSCL-E) -- Algorithm Reference

## Concurrent Chemoradiation Regimens

**Preferred (nonsquamous)** [p.95]

- Carboplatin AUC 5 on Day 1, Pemetrexed 500 mg/m² on Day 1 every 21 days for 4 cycles; concurrent thoracic RT [p.95]
- Cisplatin 75 mg/m² on Day 1, Pemetrexed 500 mg/m² on Day 1 every 21 days for 3 cycles; concurrent thoracic RT [p.95]
- Carboplatin AUC 2; Paclitaxel 45–50 mg/m² weekly, concurrent thoracic RT [p.95]
- Cisplatin 50 mg/m² on Days 1, 8, 29, and 36; Etoposide 50 mg/m² Days 1–5 and 29–33; concurrent thoracic RT [p.95]

**Preferred (squamous)** [p.95]

- Carboplatin AUC 2; Paclitaxel 45–50 mg/m² weekly, concurrent thoracic RT [p.95]
- Cisplatin 50 mg/m² on Days 1, 8, 29, and 36; Etoposide 50 mg/m² Days 1–5 and 29–33; concurrent thoracic RT [p.95]

## Consolidation Therapy

**For patients with unresectable Stage II/III NSCLC, PS 0–1, and no disease progression after definitive concurrent chemoradiation** [p.95]

- **Durvalumab** 10 mg/kg IV every 2 weeks or 1500 mg every 4 weeks for up to 12 months (patients with body weight ≥30 kg) [p.95]
  - (Category 1 for stage III; Category 2A for stage II) [p.95]
  - Except tumors positive for EGFR exon 19 deletion or L858R mutation [p.95]

- **Osimertinib** 80 mg once daily until disease progression [p.95]
  - (Category 1 for stage III; Category 2A for stage II) [p.95]
  - For EGFR exon 19 deletion or L858R mutation [p.95]

## Clinical Notes

### Special Populations [p.95]

For patients with **superior sulcus tumors**: recommend 2 cycles concurrent with RT and 2 more cycles after surgery [p.95]

### Regimen Uses [p.95]

- Regimens can be used as preoperative/adjuvant chemotherapy/RT
- Regimens can be used as definitive concurrent chemotherapy/RT

### Additional Chemotherapy Guidance [p.95]

**For cisplatin/pemetrexed regimen:**
- If using durvalumab or osimertinib after concurrent chemoradiation: additional chemotherapy after radiation is not recommended [p.95]
- If NOT using durvalumab or osimertinib: additional 4 cycles of pemetrexed 500 mg/m² may be used [p.95]

**For carboplatin/paclitaxel regimen:**
- If using durvalumab or osimertinib after concurrent chemoradiation: additional chemotherapy after radiation is not recommended [p.95]
- If NOT using durvalumab or osimertinib: additional 2 cycles every 21 days of paclitaxel 200 mg/m² and carboplatin AUC 6 may be used [p.95]

### Immunotherapy-TKI Toxicity Consideration [p.95]

In patients with tumors positive for EGFR exon 19 deletion or L858R mutation: risk of toxicity when TKI is administered in temporal proximity to immunotherapy [p.95]

### Sequential Chemoradiation [p.95]

For patients who have received sequential chemoradiation:
- Durvalumab can be considered as consolidation immunotherapy, OR
- If EGFR exon 19 deletion or L858R mutation: osimertinib is recommended [p.95]

---

**Note:** All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.95]
