## High-Risk APL Induction (Without Cardiac Issues)

For patients with high-risk APL (WBC count >10 x 10^9/L) without cardiac contraindications

### Preferred Regimens

**1. ATRA + Idarubicin** [p.16]
- ATRA dosing per protocol
- Idarubicin dosing per protocol
- Footnotes: f, 3
- Include prophylaxis with prednisone; change to dexamethasone if differentiation syndrome develops [p.16]

**2. ATRA + Idarubicin + Arsenic Trioxide** [p.16]
- ATRA dosing per protocol
- Idarubicin dosing per protocol
- Arsenic trioxide dosing per protocol
- Footnotes: f, g, 5
- Include prophylaxis with prednisone; change to dexamethasone if differentiation syndrome develops [p.16]

**3. ATRA + Daily Arsenic Trioxide + Gemtuzumab Ozogamicin** [p.16]
- ATRA dosing per protocol
- Arsenic trioxide daily dosing per protocol
- Gemtuzumab ozogamicin dosing per protocol
- Footnotes: f, 6
- Include prophylaxis with prednisone; change to dexamethasone if differentiation syndrome develops [p.16]

**4. ATRA + Intermittent Arsenic Trioxide + Gemtuzumab Ozogamicin** [p.16]
- ATRA dosing per protocol
- Arsenic trioxide intermittent dosing per protocol
- Gemtuzumab ozogamicin dosing per protocol
- Footnotes: f, g, 2
- Include prophylaxis with prednisone; change to dexamethasone if differentiation syndrome develops [p.16]

### Useful in Certain Circumstances

(if arsenic is not available or contraindicated during induction) [p.16]

**1. ATRA + Daunorubicin (3 days) + Cytarabine** [p.16]
- ATRA dosing per protocol
- Daunorubicin dosing for 3 days
- Cytarabine dosing per protocol
- Footnotes: f, 8
- BM aspirate and biopsy at day 28 to document remission before proceeding with consolidation [p.16]

**2. ATRA + Daunorubicin (4 days) + Cytarabine** [p.16]
- ATRA dosing per protocol
- Daunorubicin dosing for 4 days
- Cytarabine dosing per protocol
- Footnotes: f, 7
- BM aspirate and biopsy at day 28 to document remission before proceeding with consolidation [p.16]

## Induction Response Assessment

### Bone Marrow Assessment (Day 28)

BM aspirate and biopsy at day 28 to document remission before proceeding with consolidation [p.16]

For all patients with high-risk APL, consider lumbar puncture (LP) before proceeding with consolidation [p.16]

## APL-3: High-Risk Induction in Patients WITH Cardiac Issues

For patients with high-risk APL AND cardiac contraindications (low ejection fraction [EF] or QTcF prolongation)

### Preferred Regimens

**1. ATRA + Gemtuzumab Ozogamicin** [p.17]
- ATRA dosing per protocol
- Gemtuzumab ozogamicin dosing per protocol
- Footnotes: f, 4
- BM aspirate and biopsy at day 28 to document remission before proceeding with consolidation [p.17]

**2. ATRA + Intermittent Arsenic Trioxide + Gemtuzumab Ozogamicin** [p.17]
- ATRA dosing per protocol
- Arsenic trioxide intermittent dosing per protocol
- Gemtuzumab ozogamicin dosing per protocol
- Footnotes: f, g, 2
- BM aspirate and biopsy at day 28 to document remission before proceeding with consolidation [p.17]

### For Low Ejection Fraction

**Recommended Regimens:** [p.17]

1. ATRA + Daunorubicin (3 days) + Cytarabine [p.17]
   - Footnotes: f, 8
   - BM aspirate and biopsy at day 28 to document remission before proceeding with consolidation [p.17]

2. ATRA + Idarubicin [p.17]
   - Footnotes: f, 3
   - BM aspirate and biopsy at day 28 to document remission before proceeding with consolidation [p.17]

### For Prolonged QTcF

Same regimens as documented for low ejection fraction, with careful cardiac monitoring [p.17]

## Important Clinical Notes

### Community Center Treatment

For patients with APL being treated at a community center, collaboration with a center with expertise has been shown to reduce induction mortality (Jillella AP, et al. JAMA Oncol 2025;11:400-407) [p.16, p.17]

### Supportive Care

Refer to Principles of Supportive Care for APL --> See APL-A [p.16, p.17]

### Regimen Consistency

Several groups have published large trials with excellent outcomes. However, to achieve the expected results, one needs to use the regimen consistently through all components and not mix induction from one trial with consolidation from another [p.16]

### ATRA/Arsenic Requirement

It is important for the management of APL that regimens containing ATRA and arsenic trioxide be administered unless there is a contraindication based on extenuating patient circumstances [p.16]

### Mortality and Morbidity

Early mortality is related to bleeding, differentiation syndrome, or infection. Persistent disease is rare [p.16]

## Footnote Details

**Footnote f:** Data suggest that lower doses of ATRA (25 mg/m2) in divided doses until clinical remission may be used in children and adolescents (Kutny MA, et al. J Clin Oncol 2017;35:3021-3029) [p.16, p.17]

**Footnote g:** Begin prophylaxis with prednisone; the optimal duration of steroid prophylaxis is unknown. If differentiation syndrome develops, change to dexamethasone (Lo-Coco F, et al. N Engl J Med 2013;369:111-121). See Principles of Supportive Care for APL (APL-A) [p.16]

**Footnote h:** If no evidence of morphologic disease (<5% blasts and no abnormal promyelocytes), discontinue ATRA and arsenic trioxide to allow for peripheral blood recovery since arsenic trioxide can be associated with significant myelosuppression. If evidence of morphologic disease, continue ATRA and arsenic trioxide and repeat BM 1 week later [p.16]

**Footnote i:** The presence of measurable cytogenetic and molecular markers post-induction does not carry prognostic or therapeutic implications [p.16, p.17]

**Footnote k:** For regimens using high cumulative doses of cardiotoxic agents, consider reassessing cardiac function prior to each anthracycline/mitoxantrone-containing course [p.16, p.17]

**Footnote l:** It is important for the management of APL that regimens containing ATRA and arsenic trioxide be administered unless there is a contraindication based on extenuating patient circumstances [p.16]

**Footnote m:** For all patients with high-risk APL, consider LP before proceeding with consolidation [p.16]
