## Post-Consolidation Management Algorithm

### Step 1: Document Molecular Remission After Consolidation

**Test:** Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) on blood sample [p.18]

**Timing:** At completion of consolidation therapy [p.18]

**Purpose:** To document molecular remission status [p.18]

---

## If PCR Negative (Molecular Remission Achieved)

### Maintenance Therapy

**Option:** Maintenance therapy if included in the initial treatment protocol [p.18]

### Long-Term Monitoring

**Duration:** Monitor by PCR for up to 2 years after completion of treatment [p.18]

**Frequency:** Every 3 months for 2 years [p.18]

**Risk-Based Approach:** [p.18]
- **High-risk disease:** Continue monitoring every 3 months for 2 years
- **Low-risk disease with molecular remission:** Risk of relapse is low; monitoring may not be necessary outside the setting of a clinical trial
- **Patients with long interruptions during consolidation:** Continue monitoring every 3 months for 2 years

---

## If PCR Positive (Molecular Persistence Detected)

### Initial Response: Repeat PCR for Confirmation

**Timing:** Repeat PCR within 4 weeks [p.18]

**Lab Requirements:**
- Use a reliable laboratory
- Maintain same lab for consistency in sensitivity level (most clinical labs have a sensitivity level of 10^-4)
- Consider consultation with a physician experienced in molecular diagnostics if results are equivocal [p.18]

### If Confirmatory PCR is Negative

**Action:** Frequent monitoring is strongly recommended to confirm that the test remains negative [p.18]

**Monitoring Schedule:** Every 3 months for 2 years [p.18]

### If Confirmatory PCR is Positive (Molecular Relapse Confirmed)

**Diagnosis:** First relapse [p.18]

**Treatment:** Proceed to Therapy for Relapse --> See [APL-6](apl-classification-and-treatment-recommendations.md) [p.18]

---

## Second PCR Monitoring Cycle (for Initial PCR Negative Patients)

### If Ongoing Monitoring Shows PCR Positive Conversion

**Action:** Repeat PCR within 4 weeks for confirmation [p.18]

### If Confirmatory PCR is Negative

**Action:** Continue monitoring as above [p.18]

### If Confirmatory PCR is Positive

**Diagnosis:** Molecular relapse (conversion from MRD negativity to MRD positivity) [p.18]

**Treatment:** Proceed to Therapy for Relapse --> See [APL-6](apl-classification-and-treatment-recommendations.md) [p.18]

---

## Important Clinical Notes

### PCR Testing Protocol

PCR should be performed on a blood sample at completion of consolidation to document molecular remission [p.18]

The PCR testing laboratory should indicate the level of sensitivity of assay for positivity. Most clinical labs have a sensitivity level of 10^-4. Testing should be done in the same lab to maintain the same level of sensitivity [p.18]

### Monitoring Strategies

**For high-risk disease or those with long interruptions during consolidation:** Continue monitoring blood by PCR every 3 months for 2 years to detect molecular relapse [p.18]

**For low-risk disease in molecular remission:** Clinical experience indicates that risk of relapse is low and monitoring may not be necessary outside the setting of a clinical trial. While long-term monitoring has been standard, with newer, more effective regimens, the value is less certain [p.18]

### Equivocal Results

Consider consultation with a physician experienced in molecular diagnostics if PCR results are equivocal [p.18]

### Treatment Path for Relapse

If molecular relapse is confirmed by a second positive test, treat as first relapse --> See [APL-6](apl-classification-and-treatment-recommendations.md) for Therapy for Relapse [p.18]
