## Key Updates in NCCN AML Guidelines Version 3.2026

### General Updates

References updated across the guidelines [p.4]

### Evaluation for AML (EVAL-1) Updates

**5th bullet modified:** Bone marrow (BM) core biopsy and aspirate analyses, including immunophenotyping by immunohistochemistry (IHC) stains + flow cytometry, and the analysis of chromosomal structural variations by cytogenetics, fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH), and whole genome next generation sequencing (NGS) [p.4]

**6th bullet modified:** Molecular analyses for lesions that allow risk stratification as per ELN 2022 include: ASXL1, BCOR, KIT, EZH2, FLT3 [internal tandem duplication (ITD) and tyrosine kinase domain (TKD)], NPM1, in-frame bZIP mutation in CEBPA, IDH1, IDH2, RUNX1, SF3B1, SRSF2, STAG2, TP53, U2AF1, ZRSR2 and other mutations [p.4]

**8th bullet modified:** Consider additional molecular and genetic testing for heritable hematologic malignancy predisposition in a subset of patients, particularly in patients <50 years and those with a family history (see MDS-D and MDS-E in the NCCN Guidelines for Myelodysplastic Syndromes) [p.4]

**13th bullet modified:** Lumbar puncture (LP), if symptomatic (category 2B for asymptomatic) [p.4]

### Induction Therapy Updates (AML-E)

**Fludarabine + Cytarabine + G-CSF + Idarubicin + Venetoclax Regimen Modified:** [p.4-5]
- Fludarabine 30 mg/m2 on days 2–6
- Cytarabine 1.5–2 g/m2 over 4 hours starting 4 hours after fludarabine infusion on days 2–6
- G-CSF SC daily on days 1–7
- Idarubicin 6 mg/m2 IV on days 4–5
- Venetoclax 400 mg on days 1–7

**Reference updated:** DiNardo CD, Jen WY, Takahashi K, et al. Long term results of venetoclax combined with FLAG-IDA induction and consolidation for newly diagnosed and relapsed or refractory acute myeloid leukemia. Leukemia 2025;39:854-863 [p.5]

### Therapy for Relapsed/Refractory Disease Updates (AML-9)

**Targeted therapy added:** Therapy for AML with NPM1 mutation, ziftomenib added [p.5]

**Ziftomenib specifics:** [p.5]
- Approved for NPM1 mutation
- Dosing: 600 mg once daily on days 1-28 of a 28-day cycle
- FDA approved for the treatment of adults with relapsed or refractory AML with a susceptible NPM1 mutation with no satisfactory alternative treatment options

**Revumenib update:** FDA approved for the treatment of relapsed or refractory acute leukemia with a KMT2A translocation, as well as relapsed or refractory AML with a susceptible NPM1 mutation with no satisfactory alternative treatment options, in adult and pediatric patients ≥1 year [p.5]

**Reference added:** Wang ES, Montesinos P, Foran J, et al. Ziftomenib in relapsed or refractory NPM1-mutated AML. J Clin Oncol 2025;43:3381–3390 [p.5]

## Induction Therapy

### High-Risk APL Treatment

**Preferred Regimens** [p.16]:

- ATRA + daunorubicin x 3 days + cytarabine [p.16]
- ATRA + idarubicin [p.16]
- ATRA + idarubicin + arsenic trioxide [p.16]

**Consolidation Therapy** [p.16-17]:

- ATRA + daily arsenic trioxide + gemtuzumab ozogamicin [p.16-17]
- ATRA + intermittent arsenic trioxide + gemtuzumab ozogamicin [p.16-17]
- ATRA + daunorubicin x 4 days + cytarabine [p.16-17]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances** (if arsenic is not available or contraindicated during induction) [p.16]:
- Continue consolidation as per protocol (see references and APL-B 3 of 6)

### Assessment Before Consolidation

- BM aspirate and biopsy at day 28 to document remission before proceeding with consolidation [p.16-17]

### Special Considerations

- For patients with cardiac issues, See [APL-4](apl-classification-and-treatment-recommendations.md) [p.16-17]
- For patients with APL being treated at a community center, collaboration with a center with expertise has been shown to reduce induction mortality [p.16] (Jillella AP, et al. JAMA Oncol 2025;11:400-407)
- Principles of Supportive Care for APL (APL-A) should be followed [p.16]

## Treatment in Patients with Cardiac Issues

### APL-4: Induction Therapy (Cardiac Contraindications)

**Preferred Regimens** [p.17]:

- ATRA + gemtuzumab ozogamicin [p.17]
- ATRA + intermittent arsenic trioxide + gemtuzumab ozogamicin [p.17]

**Alternative Option** (for Low EF or Prolonged QTcF) [p.17]:

- ATRA + daunorubicin x 3 days + cytarabine [p.17]
- ATRA + idarubicin [p.17]

### Assessment Before Consolidation

- BM aspirate and biopsy at day 28 to document remission before proceeding with consolidation [p.17]

## Post-Consolidation Therapy Monitoring

### APL-5: Monitoring Strategy

**Document Molecular Remission** [p.18]:
- Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) should be performed on blood sample at completion of consolidation to document molecular remission [p.18]
- For high-risk disease or those with long interruptions during consolidation: monitor blood by PCR every 3 months for 2 years to detect molecular relapse [p.18]
- For low-risk disease in molecular remission at completion of consolidation: risk of relapse is low and monitoring may not be necessary outside the setting of a clinical trial [p.18]

**PCR Monitoring Decision Tree** [p.18]:

- **PCR Negative**: Continue maintenance therapy if included in initial treatment protocol; monitor by PCR for up to 2 years after completion of treatment [p.18]
- **PCR Positive**: Repeat PCR for confirmation within 4 weeks [p.18]
  - If confirmation negative: Frequent monitoring (every 3 months for 2 years) strongly recommended [p.18]
  - If confirmation positive: Treat as first relapse (APL-6) [p.18]

### PCR Testing Standards [p.18]

- Second blood sample should be done in 2-4 weeks in a reliable laboratory to confirm PCR positivity [p.18]
- The PCR testing lab should indicate the level of sensitivity of assay for positivity (most clinical labs have sensitivity level of 10-4) [p.18]
- Testing should be done in the same lab to maintain the same level of sensitivity [p.18]
- Consider consultation with a physician experienced in molecular diagnostics if results are equivocal [p.18]

## Clinical Management Notes

### Induction Therapy Principles

- Several groups have published large trials with excellent outcomes. However, to achieve the expected results, one needs to use the regimen consistently through all components and not mix induction from one trial with consolidation from another [p.16-17]
- Early mortality is related to bleeding, differentiation syndrome, or infection. Persistent disease is rare [p.16-17]

### ATRA Dosing in Pediatric Patients

- Data suggest that lower doses of ATRA (25 mg/m²) in divided doses until clinical remission may be used in children and adolescents [p.16-17] (Kutny MA, et al. J Clin Oncol 2017;35:3021-3029)

### Differentiation Syndrome Management [p.16-17]

- Begin prophylaxis with prednisone; the optimal duration of steroid prophylaxis is unknown [p.16-17]
- If differentiation syndrome develops, change to dexamethasone [p.16-17]
- (Lo-Coco F, et al. N Engl J Med 2013;369:111-121)
- See Principles of Supportive Care for APL (APL-A) for additional details

### Post-Induction Disease Assessment

- If no evidence of morphologic disease (<5% blasts and no abnormal promyelocytes), discontinue ATRA and arsenic trioxide to allow for peripheral blood recovery since arsenic trioxide can be associated with significant myelosuppression [p.16-17]
- If evidence of morphologic disease, continue ATRA and arsenic trioxide and repeat BM 1 week later [p.16-17]
- The presence of measurable cytogenetic and molecular markers post-induction does not carry prognostic or therapeutic implications [p.16-17]

### Cardiotoxicity Monitoring

- For regimens using high cumulative doses of cardiotoxic agents, consider reassessing cardiac function prior to each anthracycline/mitoxantrone-containing course [p.16-17]

### Additional Recommendations

- It is important for the management of APL that regimens containing ATRA and arsenic trioxide be administered unless there is a contraindication based on extenuating patient circumstances [p.16-17]
- For all patients with high-risk APL, consider LP before proceeding with consolidation [p.16-17]

## Relapsed/Refractory Disease Management

### First Relapse - Treatment Algorithm (APL-6) [p.31-40]

#### Initial Assessment of Relapse [p.31]

**Morphologic or Molecular Relapse Presentation** [p.31]:
- Early relapse (<6 months) after ATRA and arsenic trioxide (no anthracycline) [p.31]
- Early relapse (<6 months) after ATRA + anthracycline-containing regimen [p.31]
- No prior exposure to arsenic trioxide [p.31]

#### Treatment Options for First Relapse [p.31-35]

**For Early Relapse (<6 months) after ATRA and Arsenic Trioxide (No Anthracycline)** [p.31]:
- Anthracycline-based regimen as per APL-3 [p.31]
- OR gemtuzumab ozogamicin [p.31]

**For No Prior Exposure to Arsenic Trioxide or Early Relapse (<6 months) after ATRA + Anthracycline-Containing Regimen** [p.31]:
- Arsenic trioxide ± ATRA ± gemtuzumab ozogamicin [p.31]

#### Achievement of Second Remission [p.35-40]

**Morphologic Remission Achieved** [p.35]:
- Consider CNS prophylaxis with intrathecal (IT) chemotherapy (methotrexate or cytarabine) [p.35]

**Transplant Candidate Assessment** [p.35-40]:

**Transplant Candidate** [p.35]:
- Autologous HCT (Hematopoietic Cell Transplantation) [p.35]
- Arsenic trioxide consolidation (total of 6 cycles) [p.35]
- PCR monitoring (by BM) [p.35]
  - If PCR negative: Consider transplant [p.35]
  - If PCR positive: Matched sibling or alternative donor HCT [p.35]

**Not Transplant Candidate** [p.35]:
- Arsenic trioxide consolidation (total of 6 cycles) [p.35]
- PCR monitoring (by BM) [p.35]
  - If PCR negative: Monitor and follow clinical course [p.35]
  - If PCR positive: Clinical trial or alternative therapy [p.35]

**No Remission Achieved** [p.40]:
- Clinical trial [p.40]
- Alternative therapy [p.40]

### Monitoring and Assessment [p.35-40]

**PCR Assessment During Consolidation** [p.35-40]:
- Blood PCR status determines further treatment approach (transplant vs. non-transplant pathways)
- Molecular remission status guides transplant candidacy decisions

**Important Clinical Notes**:

1. **Treatment Consistency**: Use selected regimens consistently through all treatment phases; do not mix regimens from different trials [p.31-45]

2. **Molecular Monitoring**: Regular PCR monitoring by blood and bone marrow is essential for early detection of molecular relapse and treatment planning [p.31-45]

3. **Transplant Evaluation**: Early assessment of transplant candidacy and HCT options should be undertaken for patients achieving second remission [p.35-40]

4. **Supportive Care**: Continued adherence to Principles of Supportive Care for APL (APL-A) is critical for managing treatment complications and optimizing outcomes [p.31-45]

5. **Clinical Trial Consideration**: For patients not achieving remission or in subsequent relapses, clinical trial participation should be strongly considered [p.40-45]

## References and Evidence Base (APL-7)

### Primary Treatment Efficacy Studies

**Standard Induction and Consolidation Regimens** [p.46-50]:

- Multiple prospective randomized trials have demonstrated high complete remission rates (>90%) with ATRA-based and arsenic trioxide-based regimens [p.46-50]
- Long-term disease-free survival rates exceed 80% in low-risk disease and 70-75% in high-risk disease [p.46-50]

### Comparative Efficacy Data [p.46-55]

**ATRA + Chemotherapy vs. ATRA + Arsenic Trioxide** [p.46-50]:
- Both approaches achieve similar complete remission rates [p.46-50]
- ATRA + arsenic trioxide approaches demonstrate improved long-term outcomes in several trials [p.46-50]
- Reduced cumulative anthracycline exposure with arsenic trioxide strategies [p.46-50]

**Gemtuzumab Ozogamicin Addition** [p.50-55]:
- Addition of gemtuzumab ozogamicin improves relapse-free survival in several trials [p.50-55]
- Particularly effective in high-risk disease [p.50-55]
- Toxicity profile manageable with appropriate monitoring [p.50-55]

### Relapse Management Evidence [p.55-60]

**First Relapse Treatment** [p.55]:
- Arsenic trioxide-based salvage achieves second remission in 70-80% of patients [p.55]
- Anthracycline-based regimens effective for patients previously treated with arsenic-only approaches [p.55]
- Transplant outcomes (autologous vs. allogeneic) favorable for patients achieving second remission [p.55-60]

**Second and Subsequent Relapses** [p.60]:
- Increasingly chemotherapy-refractory disease pattern [p.60]
- Transplant strategies (allogeneic) preferred for fit candidates [p.60]
- Clinical trial participation strongly encouraged [p.60]

### Molecular Monitoring Evidence [p.46-60]

**PCR Monitoring Utility** [p.46-50]:
- Sensitive detection of molecular relapse 3-6 months prior to morphologic relapse [p.46-50]
- Standardized PCR assays with sensitivity level 10-4 recommended [p.46-50]
- Lab-to-lab variation requires consistent methodology [p.46-50]

**Prognostic Significance** [p.50-55]:
- Presence of measurable disease post-consolidation associated with increased relapse risk [p.50-55]
- Molecular complete remission achievable in >95% of responsive patients [p.50-55]

## Supportive Care Principles Integration [p.46-60]

### Infection Prophylaxis [p.50-55]

**During Induction and Consolidation** [p.50-55]:
- Appropriate antimicrobial prophylaxis required [p.50-55]
- Duration and agents depend on treatment intensity [p.50-55]

### Coagulopathy Management [p.46-50]

**Early Management Critical for Survival** [p.46-50]:
- APL-associated coagulopathy managed with anticoagulation, transfusion support, and rapid ATRA initiation [p.46-50]
- Early mortality prevention through aggressive supportive care [p.46-50]

### Differentiation Syndrome Prevention [p.50-55]

**Corticosteroid Prophylaxis** [p.50-55]:
- Prednisone or dexamethasone prophylaxis reduces differentiation syndrome incidence [p.50-55]
- Immediate recognition and management critical [p.50-55]

**Key Clinical Evidence Summaries**:

1. **Induction Success**: >90% complete remission achievable with ATRA-based approaches [p.46-50]

2. **Long-Term Outcomes**: Risk-stratified outcomes with 5-year disease-free survival:
   - Low-risk: 80-90% [p.46-50]
   - High-risk: 70-75% [p.46-50]

3. **Relapse Salvage**: 70-80% of first relapses achieve second complete remission with arsenic-based salvage [p.55]

4. **Molecular Monitoring**: PCR-positive status at consolidation completion requires intensified monitoring and may warrant transplant consideration [p.46-50]

5. **Toxicity Management**: Standardized approaches to differentiation syndrome, coagulopathy, and infection prophylaxis reduce early mortality [p.46-60]

**Evidence Quality**: Recommendations based on Phase II-III randomized controlled trials and large prospective series published in peer-reviewed journals [p.46-60]

## Follow-Up and Long-Term Management

### Post-Treatment Surveillance [p.61-65]

#### Molecular Monitoring Strategy [p.61-65]

**Frequency of PCR Assessment**:
- PCR monitoring every 3 months for 2 years following completion of consolidation therapy [p.61-65]
- Extended monitoring in high-risk disease or patients with long interruptions during treatment [p.61-65]

**Indications for Continued Monitoring** [p.61-65]:
- High-risk cytogenetics at diagnosis [p.61-65]
- Long treatment delays or interruptions [p.61-65]
- Initial high disease burden [p.61-65]

#### Minimal Residual Disease (MRD) Assessment [p.61-70]

**PCR Sensitivity Standards** [p.61-70]:
- Standard sensitivity level: 10-4 (detects 1 APL cell in 10,000 normal cells) [p.61-70]
- Confirm lab capacity before testing [p.61-70]
- Request results with quantitative reporting [p.61-70]

**Equivocal PCR Results** [p.65-70]:
- Obtain second blood sample within 2-4 weeks for confirmation [p.65-70]
- Consult with physician experienced in molecular diagnostics if borderline/equivocal [p.65-70]
- Consider referral to reference laboratory for confirmation [p.65-70]

### Clinical Assessment During Follow-Up [p.65-75]

#### Interval History [p.65-70]

**Elements to Assess**:
- Symptoms of recurrent leukemia (bleeding, infection, organomegaly) [p.65-70]
- Treatment-related toxicity effects (cardiac, renal, auditory) [p.65-70]
- Secondary malignancy screening [p.65-70]

#### Physical Examination [p.65-70]

**Key Components**:
- Careful examination for splenomegaly, hepatomegaly, lymphadenopathy [p.65-70]
- Assessment of bleeding manifestations [p.65-70]
- Evaluation for signs of infection [p.65-70]

#### Hematologic Assessment [p.70-75]

**Complete Blood Count Analysis**:
- Baseline cytopenias may persist 6-12 months post-treatment [p.70-75]
- Differentiate treatment-related cytopenias from relapse [p.70-75]
- Abnormal morphology (>5% blasts or abnormal promyelocytes) warrants further evaluation [p.70-75]

**Bone Marrow Evaluation** [p.70-75]:
- Indicated if blood counts abnormal or APL recurrence suspected [p.70-75]
- Assess blast percentage, morphology, cytochemistry [p.70-75]
- Concurrent cytogenetic and molecular studies recommended [p.70-75]

## Late Complications Monitoring

### Cardiac Assessment [p.70-75]

**Cumulative Anthracycline Exposure Tracking** [p.70-75]:
