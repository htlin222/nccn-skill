## Classification

Confirmed APL can be stratified into two risk categories:

- **High risk** (WBC count >10 x 10^9/L) [p.14]
- **Low risk** (WBC count ≤10 x 10^9/L) [p.14]

Additional consideration for patients with cardiac issues (low ejection fraction [EF] or QTcF prolongation) [p.14]

> **Note**: Therapy-related APL is treated the same as de novo APL. FLT3 inhibitors are not recommended for FLT3-positive APL [p.14]

> **Clinical Recommendation**: For patients with APL being treated at a community center, collaboration with a center with expertise has been shown to reduce induction mortality [p.14] - Jillella AP, et al. JAMA Oncol 2025;11:400-407

## Induction Therapy

### Low-Risk Disease (WBC ≤10 x 10^9/L)

**Preferred Regimens** (Category 1) [p.15]:

1. **ATRA + daily arsenic trioxide** [p.15]
   - ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily
   - Arsenic trioxide 0.15 mg/kg IV daily

2. **ATRA + intermittent arsenic trioxide** [p.15]
   - ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily for 60 days or until CR achievement
   - Arsenic trioxide 0.3 mg/kg IV on days 1–5 of week 1
   - Arsenic trioxide 0.25 mg/kg twice weekly during weeks 2–8

3. **ATRA + idarubicin** [p.15]
   - ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily
   - Idarubicin 12 mg/m² on days 2, 4, 6, 8

**Useful in Certain Circumstances** (if arsenic is not available or contraindicated) [p.15]:

- **ATRA + gemtuzumab ozogamicin**
  - ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily
  - Gemtuzumab ozogamicin 6 or 9 mg/m² single dose on day 5

**Assessment After Induction** [p.15]:
- Blood count recovery by day 28 (platelet >100 x 10^9/L, absolute neutrophil count [ANC] >1 x 10^9/L)
- BM aspirate and biopsy may be considered to document <5% blasts and no abnormal promyelocytes [p.15]

### High-Risk Disease (WBC >10 x 10^9/L)

**Preferred Regimens** [p.16]:

1. **ATRA + idarubicin + arsenic trioxide** [p.16]
   - ATRA 45 mg/m² (days 1–36, 2 divided doses daily)
   - Age-adjusted idarubicin 6–12 mg/m² on days 2, 4, 6, 8
   - Arsenic trioxide 0.15 mg/kg (days 9–36 as 2 h IV infusion)

2. **ATRA + daily arsenic trioxide + gemtuzumab ozogamicin** [p.16]
   - ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily
   - Arsenic trioxide 0.15 mg/kg/day IV
   - Gemtuzumab ozogamicin 6 or 9 mg/m² may be given on day 1, 2, 3, or 4

3. **ATRA + intermittent arsenic trioxide + gemtuzumab ozogamicin** [p.16]
   - ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily for 60 days or until CR achievement
   - Arsenic trioxide 0.3 mg/kg IV on days 1–5 of week 1
   - Arsenic trioxide 0.25 mg/kg twice weekly during weeks 2–8
   - Gemtuzumab ozogamicin 6 or 9 mg/m² may be given on day 1, 2, 3, or 4

**Alternatives** (if arsenic is not available or contraindicated during induction) [p.16]:

- **ATRA + daunorubicin + cytarabine** (4 days or 3 days regimens) [p.16]
- **ATRA + idarubicin** [p.16]

**Assessment After Induction** [p.16]:
- BM aspirate and biopsy at day 28 to document remission [p.16]
- For patients with high-risk APL, consider lumbar puncture (LP) before proceeding with consolidation [p.16]

### High-Risk Disease with Cardiac Issues

(Low ejection fraction or prolonged QTcF) [p.17]

**Preferred Regimens** [p.17]:

1. **ATRA + idarubicin** [p.17]
   - ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily
   - Idarubicin 12 mg/m² on days 2, 4, 6, 8

2. **ATRA + daunorubicin + cytarabine** (3 days or 4 days) [p.17]

3. **ATRA + gemtuzumab ozogamicin** [p.17]
   - ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily
   - Gemtuzumab ozogamicin 6 or 9 mg/m² single dose on day 5

**Alternative** [p.17]:

- **ATRA + intermittent arsenic trioxide + gemtuzumab ozogamicin** [p.17]

**Assessment After Induction** [p.17]:
- BM aspirate and biopsy at day 28 to document remission before proceeding with consolidation [p.17]

## Consolidation Therapy

### Low-Risk Disease

**Consolidation Regimens** [p.15]:

1. **ATRA + daily arsenic trioxide** (Category 1) [p.15]
   - Arsenic trioxide 0.15 mg/kg/day IV 5 days/week for 4 weeks every 8 weeks for a total of 4 cycles
   - ATRA 45 mg/m²/day for 2 weeks every 4 weeks for a total of 7 cycles

2. **ATRA + intermittent arsenic trioxide** (Category 1) [p.15]
   - First 3 consolidation cycles = 56-day cycles
   - ATRA 45 mg/m²/day in 2 divided doses daily on days 1–14 and 29–42 (2 weeks on followed by 2 weeks off)
   - Arsenic trioxide 0.3 mg/kg on days 1–5 of week 1 followed by 0.25 mg/kg twice weekly during weeks 2–4
   - 4th consolidation cycle = 28-day cycle
   - ATRA 45 mg/m²/day in 2 divided doses daily on days 1–14
   - Arsenic trioxide 0.3 mg/kg on days 1–5 of week 1 followed by 0.25 mg/kg twice weekly during weeks 2–4

3. **ATRA + idarubicin** (Category 1) [p.22]
   - ATRA 45 mg/m² x 15 days + idarubicin 5 mg/m² x 4 days x 1 cycle
   - Then ATRA x 15 days + mitoxantrone 10 mg/m²/day x 3 days x 1 cycle
   - Then ATRA x 15 days + idarubicin 12 mg/m² x 1 day x 1 cycle

**Useful in Certain Circumstances** [p.15]:

- **ATRA + gemtuzumab ozogamicin**
  - ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily during weeks 1–2, 5–6, 9–10, 13–14, 17–18, 21–22, and 25–26
  - Gemtuzumab ozogamicin 6 or 9 mg/m² single dose given monthly until achievement of complete molecular remission

### High-Risk Disease

**Consolidation Regimens** [p.16]:

1. **ATRA + daily arsenic trioxide + gemtuzumab ozogamicin** [p.23]
   - Arsenic trioxide 0.15 mg/kg/day IV 5 days/week for 4 weeks every 8 weeks for a total of 4 cycles
   - ATRA 45 mg/m²/day for 2 weeks every 4 weeks for a total of 7 cycles
   - If ATRA or arsenic trioxide discontinued due to toxicity: gemtuzumab ozogamicin 6 or 9 mg/m² every 4–5 weeks (platelets and ANC ≥100 x 10^9/L and ≥1 x 10^9/L) until molecular CR

2. **ATRA + intermittent arsenic trioxide + gemtuzumab ozogamicin** [p.23]
   - First 3 consolidation cycles = 56-day cycles
   - ATRA 45 mg/m²/day in 2 divided doses daily on days 1–14 and 29–42 (2 weeks on followed by 2 weeks off)
   - Arsenic trioxide 0.3 mg/kg on days 1–5 of week 1 followed by 0.25 mg/kg twice weekly during weeks 2–4
   - 4th consolidation cycle = 28-day cycle
   - ATRA 45 mg/m²/day in 2 divided doses daily on days 1–14
   - Arsenic trioxide 0.3 mg/kg on days 1–5 of week 1 followed by 0.25 mg/kg twice weekly during weeks 2–4

3. **ATRA + idarubicin + arsenic trioxide** [p.23]
   - ATRA 45 mg/m² x 28 days + arsenic trioxide 0.15 mg/kg/day x 28 days x 1 cycle
   - Then ATRA 45 mg/m² x 7 days every 2 weeks x 3 + arsenic trioxide 0.15 mg/kg/day x 5 days for 5 weeks x 1 cycle

**Alternatives** (if arsenic is not available or contraindicated) [p.16]:

- **ATRA + daunorubicin + cytarabine** (various schedules) [p.16]
- **ATRA + idarubicin** [p.16]

## Post-Consolidation Therapy & Monitoring

**Molecular Remission Assessment** [p.18]:

- PCR should be performed on a blood sample at completion of consolidation to document molecular remission [p.18]
- Prior guidelines recommended monitoring blood by PCR every 3 months for 2 years [p.18]
- **Current practice**: For high-risk disease or those with long interruptions during consolidation, continue PCR monitoring every 3 months for 2 years [p.18]
- **For low-risk disease** in molecular remission at completion of consolidation: Risk of relapse is low and monitoring may not be necessary outside clinical trial setting [p.18]

**Maintenance Therapy** [p.18]:
- If included in the initial treatment protocol [p.18]

**Monitoring Decision Tree** [p.18]:

PCR Negative → Continue PCR monitoring for up to 2 years after completion of treatment [p.18]

PCR Positive:
- Repeat PCR for confirmation within 4 weeks [p.18]
- If repeat PCR negative → Continue frequent monitoring (every 3 months for 2 years) to confirm results remain negative [p.18]
- If repeat PCR positive → Treat as first relapse (see Relapsed/Refractory APL section) [p.18]

## Relapsed/Refractory APL

### First Relapse (Morphologic or Molecular)

**Early Relapse** (<6 months after ATRA and arsenic trioxide without anthracycline) [p.19]:

**Option 1**: Anthracycline-based regimen [p.19]
- Per APL-3 recommendations (see Induction section)

**Option 2**: Gemtuzumab ozogamicin [p.19]

**No Prior Arsenic Trioxide Exposure OR Early Relapse** (<6 months after ATRA + anthracycline-containing regimen) [p.19]:

- **Arsenic trioxide ± ATRA ± gemtuzumab ozogamicin** [p.19]

**Late Relapse** (≥6 months after arsenic trioxide-containing regimen) [p.19]:

- **Arsenic trioxide ± ATRA ± (anthracycline or gemtuzumab ozogamicin)** [p.19]

### Response Assessment

**Second Remission (Morphologic Achievement)** [p.19]:

**Consolidation Options**:
- Arsenic trioxide consolidation (total of 6 cycles) [p.19]

**CNS Prophylaxis** [p.19]:
- Consider intrathecal (IT) chemotherapy with methotrexate or cytarabine [p.19]

**PCR Assessment** [p.19]:
- If PCR negative (by BM):
  - Transplant candidates: Matched sibling or alternative donor HCT [p.19]
  - Not transplant candidates: Consider clinical trial [p.19]

- If PCR positive (by BM):
  - Transplant candidates: Matched sibling or alternative donor HCT [p.19]
  - Not transplant candidates: Consider clinical trial [p.19]

> **Note**: PCR testing should be done at least 2–3 weeks after completion of arsenic trioxide to avoid false positives [p.19]

**No Remission** [p.19]:
- Clinical trial [p.19]
- Or matched sibling/alternative donor HCT [p.19]

## Supportive Care for APL

### Clinical Coagulopathy Management

**Aggressive Transfusion Support** [p.21]:
- Maintain platelets ≥50 x 10^9/L [p.21]
- Fibrinogen replacement with cryoprecipitate and fresh frozen plasma to maintain level >150 mg/dL [p.21]
- PT and PTT should be kept close to normal values [p.21]
- Monitor daily until coagulopathy resolves [p.21]

**Catheter Precautions** [p.21]:
- Avoid use of tunneled catheter or port-a-cath [p.21]

### Leukapheresis Considerations

- Not routinely recommended due to differences in leukemia biology [p.21]
- In life-threatening cases with leukostasis unresponsive to other modalities, leukapheresis can be considered with caution [p.21]

### Leukocytosis Management

- **Hydroxyurea** can be used to treat leukocytosis in individuals with low-risk disease who experience WBC rise after ATRA/arsenic trioxide-based regimen [p.21]

### APL Differentiation Syndrome (ADS)

**Clinical Recognition** [p.21]:
- Fever (often associated with increasing WBC count >10 x 10^9/L)
- Usually occurs at initial diagnosis or relapse [p.21]
- Shortness of breath [p.21]
- Hypoxemia [p.21]
- Pleural or pericardial effusions [p.21]

**Management** [p.21]:
- Close monitoring of volume overload and pulmonary status [p.21]
- At first signs/symptoms of respiratory compromise (hypoxemia, pulmonary infiltrates, pericardial/pleural effusions):
  - Initiate dexamethasone 10 mg BID for 3–5 days with taper over 2 weeks [p.21]
  - Consider interrupting ATRA therapy until hypoxia resolves [p.21]

**Prophylaxis** [p.21]:
- For patients at high risk (WBC count >10 x 10^9/L) for developing differentiation syndrome
- For those receiving ATRA/arsenic trioxide-based regimen
- Initiate corticosteroid prophylaxis with either:
  - Prednisone 0.5 mg/kg day 1, OR
  - Dexamethasone 10 mg every 12 hours [p.21]
  - Taper steroid dose over several days [p.21]
  - If differentiation syndrome develops: change prednisone to dexamethasone 10 mg every 12 hours until count recovery or risk abates [p.21]

**Leukocytosis Treatment** [p.21]:
- Hydroxyurea can be used for leukocytosis associated with differentiation syndrome [p.21]
- For difficult-to-treat cases: anthracycline (daunorubicin or idarubicin) or gemtuzumab ozogamicin [p.21]

### Arsenic Trioxide Monitoring

**Prior to Initiating Therapy** [p.21]:
- Electrocardiogram (ECG) for prolonged QTc interval assessment [p.21]
- Serum electrolytes and creatinine [p.21]

**During Therapy** [p.21]:
- Weekly during induction therapy
- Before each course of post-remission therapy
- Minimize use of drugs that may prolong QT interval [p.21]
- Maintain K and Mg concentrations within middle or upper range of normal [p.21]

**QTc Prolongation Management** [p.21]:
- In patients with QTc interval >500 milliseconds:
  - Correct electrolytes
  - Proceed with caution [p.21]
  - QTcF is recommended [p.21]
  - If QTcF corrections unavailable: cardiology consult may be appropriate [p.21]

### Growth Factor Use

- **Myeloid growth factors should NOT be used during induction** [p.21]
- May be considered during consolidation in selected cases (life-threatening infections, signs/symptoms of sepsis) [p.21]
- No outcomes data regarding prophylactic use of growth factors in consolidation [p.21]

### Additional Supportive Care

- **Antiviral prophylaxis** against varicella-zoster virus for duration of treatment may be appropriate [p.21]

## Important Clinical Notes

### Monitoring and Assessment

**Bone Marrow Assessment** [p.15]:
- If no evidence of morphologic disease (<5% blasts and no abnormal promyelocytes), discontinue ATRA and arsenic trioxide to allow for peripheral blood recovery [p.15]
- Arsenic trioxide can be associated with significant myelosuppression [p.15]
- If evidence of morphologic disease, continue ATRA and arsenic trioxide and repeat BM 1 week later [p.15]

**Cytogenetic and Molecular Markers** [p.15]:
- The presence of measurable cytogenetic and molecular markers post-induction does not carry prognostic or therapeutic implications [p.15]

**Cumulative Cardiotoxicity** [p.15]:
- For regimens using high cumulative doses of cardiotoxic agents, consider reassessing cardiac function prior to each anthracycline/mitoxantrone-containing course [p.15]

### Treatment Consistency

- Several groups have published large trials with excellent outcomes [p.15]
- To achieve expected results, the regimen must be used consistently through all components [p.15]
- **Do NOT mix induction from one trial with consolidation from another** [p.15]

### Early Mortality Risk

- Early mortality is related to: [p.15]
  - Bleeding
  - Differentiation syndrome
  - Infection
- Persistent disease is rare [p.15]

### Community Center Management

- For patients with APL being treated at a community center, collaboration with a center with expertise has been shown to reduce induction mortality [p.14] - Jillella AP, et al. JAMA Oncol 2025;11:400-407

### Dosing in Specific Populations

**Pediatric/Adolescent Patients** [p.15]:
- Data suggest lower doses of ATRA (25 mg/m²) in divided doses until clinical remission may be used in children and adolescents [p.15]
- Kutny MA, et al. J Clin Oncol 2017;35:3021-3029

**Obesity** [p.22]:
- Optimal dosing of arsenic trioxide for patients with obesity is unknown [p.23]
- Retrospective studies suggest there may be a role for dose capping [p.23]
- Zacholski K, et al. J Oncol Pharm Pract 2022;28:1340-1349
- Jen WY, et al. Leuk Lymphoma 2024;65:378-382

### FLT3 Status

- FLT3 inhibitors are NOT recommended for FLT3-positive APL [p.14]
- Gale RE, et al. Blood 2005;106:3768-3776
