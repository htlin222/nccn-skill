## Induction Therapy

**Category:** All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.76]

### Eligible for Tagraxofusp-erzs [p.76]

**Preferred:** [p.76]

- **Tagraxofusp-erzs** (formerly SL-401) 12 mcg/kg IV over 15 minutes once daily on days 1–5 of a 21-day cycle [p.76]
  - For management of adverse events, see --> See [bpdcn-principles-of-supportive-care-for-bpdcn.md](bpdcn-principles-of-supportive-care-for-bpdcn.md) [p.76]
  - **Eligibility criteria:** Albumin ≥3.2 g/dL, LVEF ≥ institutional lower limit of normal, creatinine ≤1.5 mg/dL, bilirubin ≤1.5 mg/dL, AST/ALT ≤2.5 x ULN and no clinically significant cardiovascular disease [p.77]

**Other Recommended:** [p.76]

- **HMA (azacitidine or decitabine) + venetoclax** [p.76]

#### CNS-Directed Therapy [p.76]

- IT chemotherapy in patients with documented CNS disease at diagnosis and as prophylaxis for patients without documented CNS disease (methotrexate, cytarabine) --> See [bpdcn-evaluation-and-treatment-of-cns-disease.md](bpdcn-evaluation-and-treatment-of-cns-disease.md) [p.76]

### Intensive Therapy Approaches (if eligible for tagraxofusp-erzs) [p.76]

**Note:** These are alternative options for those eligible for tagraxofusp-erzs [p.76]

- **ALL-type induction:** HyperCVAD (hyperfractionated cyclophosphamide, vincristine, doxorubicin, dexamethasone, alternating with high-dose methotrexate and cytarabine) [p.76]
- **AML-type induction:** Standard-dose cytarabine 100 or 200 mg/m2 continuous infusion x 7 days with idarubicin 12 mg/m2 or daunorubicin 60–90 mg/m2 x 3 days [p.76]
- **Lymphoma induction:** CHOP (cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine, and prednisone) [p.76]

#### CNS-Directed Therapy [p.76]

- IT chemotherapy in patients with documented CNS disease at diagnosis and as prophylaxis for patients without documented CNS disease (methotrexate, cytarabine) --> See [bpdcn-evaluation-and-treatment-of-cns-disease.md](bpdcn-evaluation-and-treatment-of-cns-disease.md) [p.76]

### Lower Intensity Therapy [p.76]

**Preferred:** [p.76]

## Consolidation / Post-Induction Response

### Complete Response (CR) [p.77]

**Definition:** CR in BPDCN has the same hematologic criteria as AML (AML-I), but it is also important to document resolution of any extramedullary sites including CNS and skin lesions. If the skin still shows microscopic disease, consider continuing additional cycles (at least 4) of therapy before managing as relapsed/refractory disease. [p.77]

**Preferred Management:** [p.76]

- **Allogeneic HCT** [p.76]

**Other Recommended:** [p.76]

- Autologous HCT [p.76]
