## Risk Stratification

The ELN risk stratification categorizes non-APL AML patients treated with intensive induction chemotherapy by genetic abnormalities. [p.42]

Note: All recommendations are category 2A unless otherwise indicated. [p.42]

### Favorable Risk

- **t(8;21)(q22;q22.1)/RUNX1::RUNX1T1** [p.42]
- **inv(16)(p13.1q22) or t(16;16)(p13.1;q22)/CBFB::MYH11** [p.42]
- **Mutated NPM1** without FLT3-ITD [p.42] (Note: Concurrent KIT and/or FLT3 gene mutation does not alter risk categorization [p.42])
- **bZIP in-frame mutated CEBPA** [p.42] (Only in-frame mutations affecting the bZIP region of CEBPA, irrespective of whether they occur as monoallelic or biallelic mutations, have been associated with favorable outcome [p.42])

### Intermediate Risk

- **Mutated NPM1** with FLT3-ITD [p.42]
- **Wild-type NPM1 with FLT3-ITD** (without adverse-risk genetic lesions) [p.42]
- **t(9;11)(p21.3;q23.3)/MLLT3::KMT2A** [p.42] (The presence of t(9;11)(p21.3;q23.3) takes precedence over rare, concurrent adverse-risk gene mutations [p.42])
- **Cytogenetic and/or molecular abnormalities** not classified as favorable or adverse [p.42]

### Poor/Adverse Risk

- **t(6;9)(p23.3;q34.1)/DEK::NUP214** [p.42]
- **t(v;11q23.3)/KMT2A-rearranged** [p.42] (Excluding KMT2A partial tandem duplication (PTD) [p.42])
- **t(9;22)(q34.1;q11.2)/BCR::ABL1** [p.42]
- **t(8;16)(p11.2;p13.3)/KAT6A::CREBBP** [p.42]
- **inv(3)(q21.3q26.2) or t(3;3)(q21.3;q26.2)/GATA2, MECOM(EVI1)** [p.42]
- **t(3q26.2;v)/MECOM(EVI1)-rearranged** [p.42]
- **-5 or del(5q); -7; -17/abn(17p)** [p.42]
- **Complex karyotype** [p.42] (≥3 unrelated chromosome abnormalities in the absence of other class-defining recurring genetic abnormalities; excludes hyperdiploid karyotypes with three or more trisomies (or polysomies) without structural abnormalities [p.42])
- **Monosomal karyotype** [p.42] (Presence of two or more distinct monosomies (excluding loss of X or Y), or one single autosomal monosomy in combination with at least one structural chromosome abnormality (excluding CBF-AML) [p.42])
- **Mutated ASXL1, BCOR, EZH2, RUNX1, SF3B1, SRSF2, STAG2, U2AF1, and/or ZRSR2** [p.42] (Note: These markers should not be used as an adverse prognostic marker if they co-occur with favorable-risk AML subtypes [p.42])
- **Mutated TP53** [p.42] (TP53 mutation at a variant allele fraction of at least 10%, irrespective of the TP53 allelic status (mono- or biallelic mutation); TP53 mutations are significantly associated with AML with complex and monosomal karyotype [p.42])

## Notes

- Frequencies, response rates, and outcome measures should be reported by risk category, and, if sufficient numbers are available, by specific genetic lesions indicated. [p.42]
- Risk assignment is mainly based on results observed in intensively treated patients. Initial risk assignment may change during the treatment course based on the results from analyses of MRD. [p.42]
- AML with NPM1 mutation and adverse-risk cytogenetic abnormalities are categorized as adverse-risk. [p.42]
