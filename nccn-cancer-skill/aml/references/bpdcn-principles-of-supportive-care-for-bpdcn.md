## Tagraxofusp-erzs Administration and Toxicity Management

**Category:** All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.83]

### Pre-Treatment Requirements [p.83]

- Patients must have a baseline serum albumin of 3.2 g/dL or higher to be able to start tagraxofusp-erzs [p.83]
- Replace serum albumin if <3.5 g/dL or if there is a reduction of ≥0.5 from baseline [p.83]

### Serious Adverse Events [p.83]

**Capillary Leak Syndrome:** [p.83]

- Life-threatening/fatal capillary leak syndrome can occur in patients receiving this drug [p.83]

### Administration Protocol [p.83]

**Initial Dosing:** [p.83]

- The first cycle of this drug should be administered in the inpatient setting [p.83]
- Closely monitor toxicity during and after drug administration [p.83]
- It is recommended that patients remain in the hospital for at least 24 hours after completion of the first cycle [p.83]

**Premedication:** [p.83]

Premedicate with an H1-histamine antagonist, acetaminophen, corticosteroid, and H2-histamine antagonist prior to each infusion [p.83]

**Dosing Schedule:** [p.83]

- Administer tagraxofusp-erzs at 12 mcg/kg IV over 15 minutes once daily on days 1–5 of a 21-day cycle [p.83]
- Alternately, 5 doses can be administered over a 10-day period, if needed for dose delays [p.83]

### Monitoring Before Each Dose [p.83]

Prior to each dose of drug, check: [p.83]

- Vital signs [p.83]
- Albumin [p.83]
- Transaminases [p.83]
- Creatinine [p.83]

### Supportive Care Coordination [p.83]

- Collaboration with a dermatologist for supportive care is essential [p.83]

## Dose Holding Criteria

**Hold tagraxofusp-erzs dosing for the following reasons:** [p.83]

### Albumin and Fluid Status [p.83]

- Serum albumin <3.5 g/dL or a reduction from baseline of ≥0.5 [p.83]
- Body weight ≥1.5 kg over prior day [p.83]
- Edema, fluid overload, and/or hypotension [p.83]

### Hepatic Function [p.83]

- Alanine aminotransferase (ALT)/aspartate aminotransferase (AST) increase >5 times the upper limit of normal [p.83]

### Renal Function [p.83]

- Serum creatinine >1.8 or CrCl ≤60 mL/min [p.83]

### Cardiovascular Parameters [p.83]

- Systolic blood pressure (SBP) ≥160 or ≤80 mmHg [p.83]
- Heart rate (HR) ≥130 bpm or ≤40 bpm [p.83]

### Infectious and Hypersensitivity [p.83]

- Temperature ≥38°C [p.83]
- Mild to severe hypersensitivity reaction [p.83]
