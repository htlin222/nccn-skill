## Evaluation/Workup for BPDCN

### History and Physical Examination

- H&P [p.75]

### Laboratory Studies

**Basic Laboratory Assessment** [p.75]:
- CBC, platelets, differential
- CMP (Comprehensive Metabolic Panel)

### Diagnostic Assessment

**Skin Lesion Analysis** [p.75]:
- Analysis of skin lesions (collaboration with dermatology is recommended)
- Peripheral blasts assessment

**Bone Marrow and Lymph Node Evaluation** [p.75]:
- BM aspirate/biopsy
- Lymph node biopsy (if indicated)

### Diagnostic Testing Components

Bone marrow aspirate/biopsy and/or lymph node biopsy must include [p.75]:

**Morphologic Assessment** [p.75]:
- Dendritic cell morphology assessment

**Immunologic Testing** [p.75]:
- IHC (Immunohistochemistry)
- Flow cytometry

**Cytogenetic Analysis** [p.75]:
- Cytogenetic analysis

**Molecular Analysis** [p.75]:
- Molecular analysis
- Most common aberrations include: TET2, ASXL1, ZRSR2, SRSF2, TP53, NRAS, IDH2, and ETV6
- RUNX1 mutations are rare in this entity

### Imaging Studies

**FDG-PET/CT Scan** [p.75]:
- FDG-PET/CT scan of other sites, if clinical suspicion for extramedullary disease and/or lymphadenopathy

### CNS Evaluation

**Lumbar Puncture with Intrathecal Chemotherapy** [p.75]:
- All patients require a diagnostic LP with IT chemotherapy at the time of initial diagnosis
- Also at disease relapse
- Or any other time when there is a clinical suspicion for CNS involvement (see BPDCN-B)

## Diagnosis - Flow Cytometric Immunophenotyping

### Stepwise Immunophenotypic Evaluation

**pDC (Plasmacytoid Dendritic Cell) Markers** [p.75]:
- CD123
- TCF4
- TCL1
- CD303
- CD304

**Other Markers** [p.75]:
- CD4
- CD56

**Expected Negative Markers** [p.75]:
- CD3
- CD14
- CD19
- CD34
- Lysozyme
- Myeloperoxidase

### Diagnostic Criteria for BPDCN

**Expression Pattern for Diagnosis** [p.75]:

Expression of CD4 and/or CD56 plus expression of CD123 and one other pDC marker

**OR**

Expression of any three pDC markers and absent expression of expected negative markers

**Plus** [p.75]:
- Immature cells with blastoid morphology

## Treatment Decision Pathway

### Initial Disease Assessment

**BPDCN Confirmed** [p.75]:

After confirmation, patients are evaluated for eligibility for tagraxofusp-erzs-based induction:

- **Eligible for tagraxofusp-erzs** → See [BPDCN-2](bpdcn-introduction.md) [p.75]
- **Not eligible for tagraxofusp-erzs** → See [BPDCN-3](bpdcn-introduction.md) [p.75]

## Differential Diagnosis Considerations

Differential diagnoses may include certain similar entities, such as AML with plasmacytoid dendritic cell differentiation, but can generally be ruled out by careful adherence to established diagnostic criteria [p.75, ref.63].
