## AML Surveillance (After Completion of Consolidation)

**Monitoring Schedule** [p.40]

- CBC and platelets every 1–3 months for 2 years, then every 3–6 months up to 5 years [p.40]

- BM aspirate and biopsy only if peripheral smear is abnormal or cytopenias develop [p.40]

**Molecular Monitoring** [p.40]

- Confirm molecular remission and monitor for molecular relapse, if applicable [p.40]
  - See Measurable (Minimal) Residual Disease Assessment (AML-H) [p.40]

- Studies are ongoing to evaluate the role of molecular monitoring in surveillance for early relapse in patients with AML [p.40]

**HCT Preparation** [p.40]

- Donor search should be initiated at first relapse in appropriate patients concomitant with institution of other therapy [p.40]

## Therapy for Relapsed/Refractory Disease

**Assessment at Relapse** [p.40]

- Confirm relapse (per Response criteria, see AML-I) [p.40]

- Molecular profiling to determine mutation status of actionable genes [p.40]
  - Molecular and cytogenetic analyses (including testing for IDH1/IDH2, FLT3 mutations, and KMT2A rearrangements) are suggested as they may assist with selection of therapy and appropriate clinical trials [p.40]
  - Molecular testing should be repeated at each relapse or progression [p.40]

**Treatment Options** [p.40]

- **Strongly Preferred**: Clinical trial [p.40]

- **Other Options**:
  - Targeted therapy alone or in combination (AML-9) followed by allogeneic HCT [p.40]
  - Chemotherapy (AML-9) followed by allogeneic HCT [p.40]
  - Best supportive care [p.40]
    - See NCCN Guidelines for Palliative Care [p.40]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances** [p.40]

- Allogeneic HCT [p.40]
  - May be considered for patients who did not achieve CR following first induction therapy or for those with first relapse who had previously been scheduled for allogeneic HCT [p.40]
