## Maintenance Therapy

For patients with non-CBF-AML who received prior intensive chemotherapy and whose disease is in remission, with no allogeneic HCT planned: [p.39]

**Preferred:**
- **Oral azacitidine** until progression or unacceptable toxicity (Category 1 for age ≥55 years) [p.39]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances:**
- **Azacitidine or decitabine** for a maximum of 12 cycles (unable to receive oral azacitidine) [p.39]

Note: Oral azacitidine is not intended to replace consolidation chemotherapy. Patients who are fit may benefit from HCT in first CR, and there are no data to suggest that maintenance therapy with oral azacitidine can replace HCT. The trial was restricted to patients ≥55 years of age with AML with intermediate or adverse cytogenetics who were not felt to be candidates for HCT. Most patients received at least 1 cycle of consolidation prior to starting oral azacitidine. [p.39]

### Post-Allogeneic HCT (in remission, with history of FLT3 mutation)

**Preferred:**
- **Gilteritinib (FLT3-ITD or TKD)** (preferred for FLT3-ITD in CR1 without MRD negativity by ultrasensitive assay pre-transplant) [p.39]

**Other Recommended:**
- **Sorafenib (FLT3-ITD only)** [p.39]
- **Midostaurin (FLT3-ITD or TKD)** [p.39]
- **Quizartinib (FLT3-ITD only)** [p.39]

### Post-Chemotherapy (patient with history of FLT3 mutation, previously received FLT3 inhibitor, no allogeneic HCT planned)

**Preferred:**
- **Quizartinib (FLT3-ITD only)** [p.39]

**Other Recommended:**
- **Midostaurin (FLT3-ITD or TKD)** [p.39]

### Post-Allogeneic HCT (in remission, with history of AML with high-risk features)

**Other Recommended:**
- **Low-dose decitabine + G-CSF (Category 2B)** [p.40]

## Surveillance and Therapy for Relapsed/Refractory Disease

After completion of consolidation: [p.40]

### Surveillance Monitoring

- CBC, platelets every 1-3 months for 2 years, then every 3-6 months up to 5 years [p.40]
- BM aspirate and biopsy only if peripheral smear is abnormal or cytopenias develop [p.40]
- Confirm molecular remission and monitor for molecular relapse, if applicable. See Measurable (Minimal) Residual Disease Assessment (AML-H) [p.40]
- Donor search should be initiated at first relapse in appropriate patients concomitant with institution of other therapy [p.40]

### At First Relapse

Perform molecular profiling to determine mutation status of actionable genes. [p.40]

Molecular and cytogenetic analyses (including testing for IDH1/IDH2, FLT3 mutations, and KMT2A rearrangements) are suggested as they may assist with selection of therapy and appropriate clinical trials. Molecular testing should be repeated at each relapse or progression. [p.40]

**Treatment Options:**

1. **Clinical trial (strongly preferred)** [p.40]

2. **Targeted therapy** alone or in combination (AML-9) followed by allogeneic HCT [p.40]

3. **Chemotherapy** (AML-9) followed by allogeneic HCT [p.40]

4. **Best supportive care** (See NCCN Guidelines for Palliative Care) [p.40]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances:**
- **Allogeneic HCT** [p.40] (Allogeneic HCT may be considered for patients who did not achieve CR following first induction therapy or for those with first relapse who had previously been scheduled for allogeneic HCT.) [p.40]

## Therapy for Relapsed/Refractory Disease

### Targeted Therapy

#### Therapy for AML with FLT3-ITD Mutation

- **Gilteritinib (Category 1)** [p.41]
- **HMAs (azacitidine or decitabine) + sorafenib** [p.41]
- **Quizartinib (Category 2B)** [p.41]

#### Therapy for AML with FLT3-TKD Mutation

- **Gilteritinib (Category 1)** [p.41]

#### Therapy for AML with IDH2 Mutation

- **Enasidenib** [p.41]

#### Therapy for AML with IDH1 Mutation

- **Ivosidenib** [p.41]
- **Olutasidenib** [p.41]

#### Therapy for CD33-Positive AML

- **Gemtuzumab ozogamicin** [p.41]

#### Therapy for AML with Lysine Methyltransferase 2A Gene (KMT2A) Rearrangement

- **Revumenib** [p.41]

#### Therapy for AML with NPM1 Mutation

- **Revumenib** [p.41]
- **Ziftomenib** [p.41]

Note: There are promising ongoing clinical trials investigating targeted therapies based on molecular mutations for relapsed/refractory disease. Molecular profiling should be considered if not done at diagnosis, or repeated to determine clonal evolution. There have been trials conducted combining targeted therapies with other agents. Best sequencing of targeted therapies is unknown at this time and depends on clinical context. [p.41]

### Intensive Therapy (for appropriate patients)

- **Cladribine + cytarabine + G-CSF ± (mitoxantrone or idarubicin)** [p.41]
- **Cytarabine ± (daunorubicin or idarubicin or mitoxantrone)** [p.41]
- **Fludarabine + cytarabine + G-CSF ± idarubicin ± venetoclax** [p.41]
- **Etoposide + cytarabine ± mitoxantrone** [p.41]
- **Clofarabine ± cytarabine ± idarubicin** [p.41]
- **CLIA (cladribine + idarubicin + cytarabine) + venetoclax (Category 2B)** [p.41]

Note: Reinduction therapy may be appropriate in certain circumstances, such as in patients with long first remission. This strategy primarily applies to cytotoxic chemotherapy and excludes the re-use of targeted agents due to the potential development of resistance. Targeted therapies may be retried if agents were not administered continuously and not stopped due to development of clinical resistance. If a second CR is achieved, then consolidation with allogeneic HCT should be considered. [p.41]

### Less Intensive Therapy

- **HMAs (azacitidine or decitabine)** [p.41] (Patients whose disease has progressed to AML from MDS after significant exposure to HMAs may be less likely to derive benefit from continued treatment with HMAs compared to patients who are HMA-naïve. Alternative treatment strategies should be considered.) [p.41]
- **LDAC (Category 2B)** [p.41]
- **(HMA or LDAC) + venetoclax** [p.41]

## Evaluation and Treatment of CNS Leukemia

Further CNS prophylaxis per institutional practice. [p.43]

### At Diagnosis (with neurologic symptoms)

1. **CT/MRI** to rule out bleed or mass effect [p.43]

2. If no mass effect:
   - Perform **LP** [p.43]
   - If negative: Observe and repeat LP if symptoms persist [p.43]

3. If positive for mass effect or increased intracranial pressure:
   - Consider **fine-needle aspiration (FNA) or biopsy** [p.43]
   - If negative: Observe and repeat LP if symptoms persist [p.43]
   - If positive: Proceed to treatment [p.43]

4. If **CSF positive by morphology or immunotype by flow cytometry**: [p.43]
   - **IT chemotherapy** 2x/week until clear, then weekly x 4-6 weeks [p.43]
   - **OR**
   - **Radiation therapy** followed by IT chemotherapy 2x/week until clear, then weekly x 4-6 weeks [p.43]
   - **OR**
   - **Cytarabine-based therapy** with doses ≥2 g/m² + dexamethasone to reduce intracranial pressure [p.43]

Note: For patients with major neurologic signs or symptoms at diagnosis, appropriate imaging studies should be performed to detect meningeal disease, chloromas, or CNS bleeding. LP should be performed if no mass, lesion, or hemorrhage was detected on the imaging study with central shift making an LP relatively contraindicated. [p.43]

### First CR (screening, no neurologic symptoms)

**Screening LP should be considered** for asymptomatic patients with: [p.43]
- WBC count >40 x 10⁹/L at diagnosis
- Extramedullary disease
- High-risk APL
- Intraparenchymal hemorrhage at diagnosis
- FLT3 mutations
- MLLT3::KMT2A fusion
- Monocytic differentiation
- MPAL

If **negative**: Observe and repeat LP if symptoms present [p.43]

If **positive by morphology or immuntype by flow cytometry**: [p.43]
- **IT chemotherapy** 2x/week until clear [p.43]
- If patient is to receive doses of cytarabine ≥2 g/m², follow up with LP post-completion of therapy to document clearance [p.43]

Note: In the presence of circulating blasts, administer IT chemotherapy with diagnostic LP. If equivocal, consider repeating LP with morphology or immuntype by flow cytometry to delineate involvement. Induction chemotherapy should be started concurrently. However, for patients receiving doses of cytarabine ≥2 g/m², since this agent crosses the blood-brain barrier, IT therapy can be deferred until induction is completed. IT chemotherapy may consist of methotrexate, cytarabine, or a combination of these agents. Concurrent use of CNS RT with doses of cytarabine ≥2 g/m² or IT methotrexate may increase risk of neurotoxicity. [p.43]
