## Patient Selection

**Patient Status** [p.34]

Patients who are **not a candidate for intensive induction therapy or decline** [p.34]:
- Elevated blast counts at risk for tumor lysis and organ dysfunction secondary to leukostasis; measures to rapidly reduce WBC include leukapheresis, hydroxyurea, and/or single dose cytarabine [p.35]
- Poor performance/functional status and comorbid medical conditions, in addition to age, influence ability to tolerate standard induction therapy [p.35]
- Web-based tools available to evaluate probability of CR and early death after standard induction therapy in patients aged ≥60 years [p.35]
- Consider geriatric assessment for patients with AML ≥60 years of age [p.35]
- See NCCN Guidelines for Older Adult Oncology [p.35]

For patients who decline induction chemotherapy and/or targeted therapy, best supportive care may include hydroxyurea and/or transfusion support [p.35]

## Induction Therapy by Risk Group

### AML Without IDH1 Mutation

**Preferred** [p.34]

- Azacitidine + venetoclax (Category 1) [p.34]
- Azacitidine + ivosidenib (Category 1) [p.34]
- Decitabine + venetoclax [p.34]

**Other Recommended** [p.34]

- Ivosidenib [p.34]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances** [p.34]

- Low-dose cytarabine (LDAC) + venetoclax (prior exposure to HMA) [p.34]
- Azacitidine or decitabine (contraindication to venetoclax) [p.34]
- Olutasidenib (Category 2B) (not eligible for preferred regimen and not eligible for ivosidenib due to prolonged QTcF) [p.34]

### AML With IDH1 Mutation

**Preferred** [p.34]

- Azacitidine + venetoclax (Category 1) [p.34]
- Decitabine + venetoclax [p.34]

**Other Recommended** [p.34]

- Cladribine + LDAC + venetoclax (Category 2B) [p.34]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances** [p.34]

- LDAC + venetoclax (prior exposure to hypomethylating agent [HMA]) [p.34]
- Azacitidine or decitabine (contraindication to venetoclax) [p.34]
- LDAC + glasdegib [p.34]
- LDAC (prior exposure to HMA or contraindication to venetoclax) [p.34]
- Gilteritinib ± azacitidine (FLT3-ITD or TKD, not eligible for preferred regimen) [p.34]
- Enasidenib ± azacitidine (IDH2 mutation, not eligible for preferred regimen) [p.34]
- Gemtuzumab ozogamicin (CD33 positive, not eligible for preferred regimen) [p.34]
  - Threshold for CD33 is not well-defined and may be ≥1% by flow cytometry [p.35]
  - Regimens that include gemtuzumab ozogamicin have limited benefit in poor-risk disease [p.35]

## Special Considerations

### Venetoclax Regimens

- Venetoclax combination regimens may be continued for patients whose disease demonstrates clinical improvement (CR/CRi), with consideration of subsequent transplant where appropriate [p.35]
- Patients whose disease progressed to AML from MDS after significant exposure to HMAs (azacitidine, decitabine) may be less likely to derive benefit from continued HMA treatment compared to HMA-naïve patients; alternative treatment strategies should be considered [p.35]
- Patients with cytopenias with disease in remission should take breaks between cycles (see Principles of Venetoclax, AML-J) [p.35]

### IDH Inhibitors

- Response to treatment with IDH inhibitors may take 3–5 months [p.35]
- IDH inhibitors increase risk for differentiation syndrome and hyperleukocytosis that may require treatment with hydroxyurea and steroids; monitor closely [p.35]
- Differentiation syndrome can occur later (up to several months after induction) [p.35]
- Azacitidine + ivosidenib approved for newly diagnosed AML with IDH1 mutation meeting at least one criterion: aged >75 years, baseline ECOG performance status ≥2, severe cardiac or pulmonary disease, hepatic impairment with bilirubin >1.5× upper limit of normal, CrCl <45 mL/min, or other comorbidity [p.35]

### Hypomethylating Agents (HMA)

- Response may not be evident before 3–4 cycles of HMA treatment (azacitidine, decitabine); continue HMA treatment until progression if patient tolerating therapy [p.35]
- Similar delays in response likely with novel agents in clinical trials [p.35]

### LDAC + Glasdegib

- For newly diagnosed AML in patients ≥75 years of age or with significant comorbid conditions (severe cardiac disease, ECOG performance status ≥2, baseline creatinine >1.3 mg/dL) [p.35]
- Associated with improved overall survival in a randomized trial [p.35]
