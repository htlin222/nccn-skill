## Follow-Up After Cytarabine-Based Induction

**Initial Assessment** [p.32]

- Consider follow-up BM aspirate and biopsy 14–21 days after start of therapy [p.32]
  - When using cytarabine-based induction regimens with doses >100 to 200 mg/m², consider delaying BM aspirate and biopsy to Day 21 [p.33]
  - Limited prospective data to support this recommendation [p.33]

**Response Assessment** [p.32]

Following initial assessment, disease status may show:

- **Hypoplasia**: Await recovery [p.32]
  - Defined as cellularity <20% with residual blasts <5% of residual cellularity [p.33]

- **Response** (per Response criteria, see AML-I): [p.32]
  - Proceed to Consolidation (if received intensive re-induction) → See [AML-6](aml-risk-group-and-induction-intensive-induction-eligible.md) [p.32]
  - Screening LP per AML-B [p.32]

- **Lack of Response/Primary Refractory** (per Response criteria, see AML-I): [p.32]
  - Consider regimens for relapsed/refractory disease, including targeted therapies [p.32]
  - See Therapy for Relapsed/Refractory Disease (AML-9) [p.32]
  - Allogeneic HCT (See NCCN Guidelines for Hematopoietic Cell Transplantation) [p.32]
  - Best supportive care [p.32]

**BM Assessment at Count Recovery** [p.32-33]

- BM aspirate and biopsy to document remission status upon count recovery, or by day 42 at the latest in setting of delayed count recovery [p.32-33]
  - When performed, BM aspirate and biopsy should include cytogenetic and molecular studies as appropriate [p.33]
  - For measurable (minimal) residual disease (MRD) assessment, see AML-H [p.33]
  - If residual disease present and ambiguous, repeat BM biopsy within 7 days before proceeding with therapy [p.32]

## Reinduction Therapy

**Preferred Regimens** [p.32]

- **Intensive chemotherapy regimens** [p.32]
  - Standard 7 + 3: Cytarabine + (daunorubicin or idarubicin) [p.32]
  - 5 + 2: Cytarabine + (daunorubicin or idarubicin) or (mitoxantrone for age ≥60 years) [p.32]
  - Standard 7 + 3: Cytarabine + (daunorubicin or idarubicin) + midostaurin (FLT3 mutated [ITD or TKD]) [p.32]
  - Standard 7 + 3 or 5 + 2: Cytarabine + (daunorubicin or idarubicin) + quizartinib (FLT3-ITD only) [p.32]

- **Lower-intensity regimens** [p.32]
  - HMA (azacitidine or decitabine) + venetoclax [p.32]
    - Venetoclax combination regimens may be continued for patients whose disease demonstrates clinical improvement (CR/CR with incomplete hematologic recovery [CRi]), with consideration of subsequent transplant where appropriate [p.33]
    - Patients whose disease progressed to AML from MDS after significant HMA exposure may be less likely to derive benefit from continued HMA treatment compared to HMA-naïve patients; alternative treatment strategies should be considered [p.33]
    - Patients with cytopenias with disease in remission should take breaks between cycles (see Principles of Venetoclax, AML-J) [p.33]

- **CPX-351 (Dual-drug liposomal encapsulation)** [p.32]
  - Cytarabine and daunorubicin liposomal encapsulation [p.32]
  - For therapy-related AML (other than CBF-AML), antecedent MDS/CMML, or cytogenetic/molecular changes consistent with MDS [p.32]
  - Preferred only if given in induction [p.32]
  - BM aspirate and biopsy 14–21 days after start of therapy [p.32]

- **Cytarabine monotherapy** [p.32]
  - May be used if not previously used as treatment for persistent disease at day 15 [p.32]
  - May be given ± anthracycline (daunorubicin or idarubicin) if clinical trial not available while awaiting donor identification [p.32]

**Cardiac Function Monitoring** [p.33]

- For regimens using high cumulative doses of cardiotoxic agents, consider reassessing cardiac function prior to each anthracycline/mitoxantrone-containing course [p.33]

## Treatment After Residual Disease

**With Residual Disease (if ambiguous, repeat BM biopsy within 7 days)** [p.32]

- If residual disease present: Continue lower-intensity therapy (if received HMA + venetoclax re-induction) [p.32]
  - See [AML-5](aml-risk-group-and-induction-intensive-induction-eligible.md) [p.32]

## Additional Considerations

- Consider clinical trials for patients with disease with targeted molecular abnormalities [p.33]
- Principles of Systemic Therapy for AML (AML-E) apply [p.33]
- Monitoring During Therapy (AML-G) [p.33]
