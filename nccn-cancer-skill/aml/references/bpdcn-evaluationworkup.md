## Diagnosis

**Category:** All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.75]

### Initial Assessment [p.75]

- H&P [p.75]
- CBC, platelets, differential, CMP [p.75]

### Diagnostic Workup [p.75]

**Analysis of skin lesions** (collaboration with dermatology is recommended) [p.75]
- Peripheral blasts [p.75]
- BM aspirate/biopsy [p.75]
- Lymph node biopsy (if indicated) [p.75]

#### Includes:

- Dendritic cell morphology assessment [p.75]
- IHC [p.75]
- Flow cytometry [p.75]
- Cytogenetic analysis [p.75]

#### Molecular Analysis [p.75]

Most common aberrations include: TET2, ASXL1, ZRSR2, SRSF2, TP53, NRAS, IDH2, and ETV6 [p.75]

Note: RUNX1 mutations are rare in this entity [p.75]

### Immunophenotypic Evaluation [p.75]

**Stepwise evaluation required:**

**pDC markers:** [p.75]
- CD123
- TCF4
- TCL1
- CD303
- CD304

**Other markers:** [p.75]
- CD4
- CD56

**Expected negative markers:** [p.75]
- CD3
- CD14
- CD19
- CD34
- Lysozyme
- Myeloperoxidase

**Diagnostic criteria:** [p.75]

Expression of CD4 and/or CD56 plus expression of CD123 and one other pDC marker [p.75]

OR

Expression of any three pDC markers and absent expression of expected negative markers [p.75]

Plus immature cells with blastoid morphology [p.75]

### Additional Imaging [p.75]

- FDG-PET/CT scan of other sites, if clinical suspicion for extramedullary disease and/or lymphadenopathy [p.75]

## CNS Assessment

**All patients require:** [p.75]
- Diagnostic LP with IT chemotherapy at the time of initial diagnosis, at disease relapse, or any other time when there is a clinical suspicion for CNS involvement --> See [bpdcn-evaluation-and-treatment-of-cns-disease.md](bpdcn-evaluation-and-treatment-of-cns-disease.md) [p.75]
