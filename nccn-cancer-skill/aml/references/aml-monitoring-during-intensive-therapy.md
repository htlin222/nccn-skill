# AML: Monitoring During Intensive Therapy (AML-G)

<!-- Source: NCCN AML v3.2026, page 64 -->
<!-- Chunk: aml-monitoring-during-intensive-therapy-algorithm -->

All recommendations are category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.64].

## Induction Therapy Monitoring

### Complete Blood Count (CBC)

CBC daily [p.64]:
- Differential daily or as clinically indicated during chemotherapy
- Every other day after recovery of ANC >0.5 × 10⁹/L until either normal differential or persistent leukemia is documented [p.64]
- Platelets daily while in the hospital until platelet-transfusion independent [p.64]

### Chemistry Profile

Chemistry profile, including electrolytes, liver function test (LFT), blood urea nitrogen (BUN), creatinine, uric acid, and phosphorous [p.64]:
- At least daily during active treatment until risk of tumor lysis is past [p.64]
- If the patient is receiving nephrotoxic agents, closer monitoring is required through the period of hospitalization [p.64]

### Lab Frequency Adjustment

Consider reduced frequency of labs to 1–2 times per week following completion of chemotherapy based on individual circumstances, such as [p.64]:
- Degree of count recovery
- Intensity of induction
- Patient preference [p.64]

### Coagulation Panel

Coagulation panel 1–2 times per week [p.64].

**For Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (DIC)**: For patients who have evidence of disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC), coagulation parameters including fibrinogen should be monitored daily until resolution of DIC [p.64].

### Bone Marrow Assessment

Bone marrow (BM) aspirate/biopsy timeline [p.64]:
- **Day 14-21 after start of therapy**: to document hypoplasia [p.64]
- **If hypoplasia not documented or indeterminate**: repeat biopsy within 7 days to clarify persistence of leukemia [p.64]
- **If hypoplasia confirmed**: repeat biopsy at time of hematologic recovery to document remission [p.64]
- **Cytogenetics**: If cytogenetics were initially abnormal, include cytogenetics as part of the remission documentation [p.64]

## Post-Remission Therapy Monitoring

### During Chemotherapy

- CBC, platelets: 2 times per week during chemotherapy [p.64]
- Chemistry profile, electrolytes: daily during chemotherapy [p.64]

### Outpatient Monitoring

Outpatient monitoring post chemotherapy [p.64]:
- CBC, platelets, differential, and electrolytes: 2–3 times per week until recovery [p.64]

### Bone Marrow Assessment

BM aspirate/biopsy only if [p.64]:
- Peripheral blood counts are abnormal, OR
- Counts have not recovered within 5 weeks [p.64]

## Risk-Based Considerations

Patients with AML with high-risk features are at increased risk for relapse and should be considered for early alternate donor search [p.64]:
- Poor-prognosis cytogenetics
- Therapy-related AML
- Prior MDS
- Possibly 2 or more inductions to achieve a CR [p.64]

See [AML-6](aml-risk-group-and-induction-intensive-induction-eligible.md) for additional risk stratification guidance [p.64].
