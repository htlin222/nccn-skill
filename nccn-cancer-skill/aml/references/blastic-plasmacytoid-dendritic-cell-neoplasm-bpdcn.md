## Introduction

Decisions about diagnosis and management for BPDCN should involve multidisciplinary consultation at a high-volume center with use of appropriate interventions [p.74]. Consider referral to an academic institution [p.74].

## Diagnosis

### Clinical Presentation
- BPDCN is a disorder of immature dendritic cells that regulate effector T-cell function [p.81]
- Most commonly presents as asymptomatic skin lesions, cytopenias, circulating peripheral blasts (leukemic phase), lymphadenopathy, and CNS manifestations [p.81]
- Constitutes only 0.44% of hematologic malignancies and <1% of acute leukemia presentations [p.81]
- Occurs in all races and geographic areas [p.81]
- More common in adults (median age, 65–67 years) with an approximate male-to-female ratio of 3:1 [p.81]

### Diagnostic Workup

**Hematologic Assessment:**
- H&P [p.75]
- CBC, platelets, differential, CMP [p.75]

**Morphologic and Tissue Evaluation:**
- Analysis of skin lesions (collaboration with dermatology is recommended) [p.75]
- Peripheral blasts [p.75]
- BM aspirate/biopsy [p.75]
- Lymph node biopsy (if indicated) [p.75]

**Dendritic Cell Assessment:**
- Dendritic cell morphology assessment [p.75]
- IHC [p.75]
- Flow cytometry [p.75]

### Immunophenotypic Evaluation

**Diagnostic Criteria - Expression Pattern:**
- Expression of CD4 and/or CD56 plus expression of CD123 and one other pDC marker [p.75]

OR

- Expression of any three pDC markers and absent expression of expected negative markers [p.75]

**Plus:**
- Immature cells with blastoid morphology [p.75]

**pDC Markers:**
- CD123, TCF4, TCL1, CD303, CD304 [p.75]

**Other Markers:**
- CD4, CD56 [p.75]

**Expected Negative Markers:**
- CD3, CD14, CD19, CD34, lysozyme, myeloperoxidase [p.75]

### Cytogenetic and Molecular Analysis

- Cytogenetic analysis [p.75]
- Molecular analysis: most common aberrations include TET2, ASXL1, ZRSR2, SRSF2, TP53, NRAS, IDH2, and ETV6 [p.75]
  - RUNX1 mutations are rare in this entity [p.75]

### Imaging and Additional Studies

- FDG-PET/CT scan of other sites, if clinical suspicion for extramedullary disease and/or lymphadenopathy [p.75]

## CNS Disease Evaluation

**All patients require a diagnostic lumbar puncture (LP) with intrathecal (IT) chemotherapy at the time of initial diagnosis, at disease relapse, or any other time when there is a clinical suspicion for CNS involvement** [p.75].

## Treatment Overview

BPDCN patients are initially stratified by **eligibility for tagraxofusp-erzs** to determine induction therapy pathway [p.76, p.78].

### Induction Therapy Eligibility Criteria for Tagraxofusp-erzs

Patient must meet ALL of the following:
- Albumin ≥3.2 g/dL [p.77]
- LVEF ≥ institutional lower limit of normal [p.77]
- Creatinine ≤1.5 mg/dL [p.77]
- Bilirubin ≤1.5 mg/dL [p.77]
- AST/ALT ≤2.5 x ULN [p.77]
- No clinically significant cardiovascular disease [p.77]

## Induction Therapy for Eligible Patients

### **Preferred: Tagraxofusp-erzs** (Category 2A)

- **Tagraxofusp-erzs (formerly SL-401)**: 12 mcg/kg IV over 15 minutes once daily on days 1–5 of a 21-day cycle [p.76]
- IT chemotherapy in patients with documented CNS disease at diagnosis and as prophylaxis for patients without documented CNS disease (methotrexate, cytarabine) [p.76]
- For management of adverse events, see Supportive Care (BPDCN-C) [p.76]

### **Other Recommended Options:**

**Intensive Chemotherapy Regimens:**

- **ALL-type induction**: HyperCVAD (hyperfractionated cyclophosphamide, vincristine, doxorubicin, dexamethasone, alternating with high-dose methotrexate and cytarabine) [p.76]
- **AML-type induction**: Standard-dose cytarabine 100 or 200 mg/m² continuous infusion x 7 days with idarubicin 12 mg/m² or daunorubicin 60–90 mg/m² x 3 days [p.76]
- **Lymphoma induction**: CHOP (cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine, and prednisone) [p.76]
- IT chemotherapy in patients with documented CNS disease at diagnosis and as prophylaxis for patients without documented CNS disease (methotrexate, cytarabine) [p.76]

**Lower Intensity Option:**

- **HMA (azacitidine or decitabine) + venetoclax** [p.76]
- IT chemotherapy in patients with documented CNS disease at diagnosis and as prophylaxis for patients without documented CNS disease (methotrexate, cytarabine) [p.76]

**Palliative Options:**
- Systemic steroids [p.76]
- See Supportive Care (BPDCN-C) [p.76]

## Induction Therapy for Ineligible Patients

### Patients Not Eligible for Tagraxofusp-erzs (Category 2A)

**Eligible for Intensive Therapy:**

- **ALL-type induction**: HyperCVAD (hyperfractionated cyclophosphamide, vincristine, doxorubicin, dexamethasone, alternating with high-dose methotrexate and cytarabine) [p.78]
- **AML-type induction**: Standard-dose cytarabine 100 or 200 mg/m² continuous infusion x 7 days with idarubicin 12 mg/m² or daunorubicin 60–90 mg/m² x 3 days [p.78]
- **Lymphoma induction**: CHOP (cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine, and prednisone) [p.78]
- IT chemotherapy in patients with documented CNS disease at diagnosis and as prophylaxis for patients without documented CNS disease (methotrexate, cytarabine) [p.78]

**Not Eligible for Intensive Therapy or Declines:**

- **HMA (azacitidine or decitabine) + venetoclax** [p.78]
- IT chemotherapy in patients with documented CNS disease at diagnosis and as prophylaxis for patients without documented CNS disease (methotrexate, cytarabine) [p.78]

**Palliative Options:**
- Systemic steroids [p.78]
- See Supportive Care (BPDCN-C) [p.78]

## Response Assessment

**Complete Remission (CR):** CR in BPDCN has the same hematologic criteria as AML, but it is also important to document resolution of any extramedullary sites including CNS and skin lesions [p.77]. If the skin still shows microscopic disease, consider continuing additional cycles (at least 4) of therapy before managing as relapsed/refractory disease [p.77].

## Relapsed/Refractory Disease

### Surveillance

- **CBC, platelets**: every 1–3 months for 2 years, then every 3–6 months up to 5 years [p.79]
- **BM aspirate and biopsy**: only if peripheral smear is abnormal or cytopenias develop [p.79]
- **Repeat FDG-PET/CT scan**: for patients with prior evidence of extramedullary disease [p.79]
- **Consider re-biopsy**: for any suspicious skin or extramedullary lesions [p.79]

### Treatment Options for Relapsed/Refractory BPDCN

- **Evaluate CNS for disease/prophylaxis** [p.79]
- **Preferred: Clinical trial** [p.79]
- **Tagraxofusp-erzs** (if not already used): For management of adverse events, see Supportive Care (BPDCN-C) [p.79]
- **Chemotherapy** (if not already used): See Treatment Induction (BPDCN-2) [p.79]
- **Local RT**: to isolated lesions/areas [p.79]
- **Systemic steroids** [p.79]
- **HMA (azacitidine or decitabine) + venetoclax** [p.79]

### Hematopoietic Cell Transplantation (HCT)

For patients achieving CR or lacking response to induction:

- **Preferred: Allogeneic HCT** [p.76, p.78]
  - Donor search should be initiated at first relapse in appropriate patients concomitant with institution of other therapy if no sibling donor has been identified [p.79]
  - Studies suggest that being in first remission during receipt of allogeneic HCT significantly enhances the median OS [p.81]
  - Reduced-intensity conditioning may be considered in patients whose disease achieves CR but cannot tolerate myeloablative HCT [p.81]

- **Other Recommended: Autologous HCT** [p.76, p.78]

## CNS-Directed Therapy

### With CNS Disease

- **CNS-directed IT chemotherapy** [p.82]
  - Twice weekly dosing until CSF is clear [p.82]
  - Once the CSF is clear (negative on cytology), continue weekly IT treatments for at least 4 doses [p.82]
  - Then twice per month for a total of at least 8 doses [p.82]
  - Consider continuing IT treatments once or twice per month for ongoing prophylaxis [p.82]

### Without CNS Disease (Prophylactic)

- **Administer prophylactic CNS-directed IT chemotherapy** [p.82]
  - Twice per month for a total of at least 8 doses [p.82]
  - Consider continuing IT treatment once or twice per month for ongoing prophylaxis [p.82]

**Note:** Chemotherapy regimens may follow institutional standards, but would preferably be aggressive including alternating cytarabine with methotrexate, or triple IT agents (i.e., cytarabine, methotrexate, steroid) [p.82].

**Rationale:** Consider IT chemotherapy prophylaxis even in the absence of known CNS disease, given the high percentage (30%) of primary CNS involvement at relapse [p.82].

## Prognosis and Clinical Considerations

- Prognosis for BPDCN is poor and the median OS is approximately 8–12 months when patients are treated with chemotherapy without consolidative allogeneic HCT [p.81]
- Diagnosis can be extremely challenging and whenever possible should involve an experienced hematopathologist [p.81]

## Treatment Selection Considerations

- For patients who are fit, current treatment options for BPDCN include tagraxofusp-erzs and chemotherapy [p.81]
- Those with low albumin and/or comorbidities should receive non-intensive regimen options as shown in the algorithm [p.81]

## Supportive Care

### Tagraxofusp-erzs Management

**Baseline Requirements:**
- Baseline serum albumin must be ≥3.2 g/dL to start tagraxofusp-erzs [p.83]
- Replace serum albumin if <3.5 g/dL or if there is a reduction of ≥0.5 from baseline [p.83]

**Serious Adverse Events:**
- Hypoalbuminemia and capillary leak syndrome are known, potentially serious adverse events associated with tagraxofusp-erzs treatment [p.81]
- Capillary leak syndrome (life-threatening/fatal) can occur in patients receiving this drug [p.83]

**Administration Setting and Monitoring:**
- The first cycle of this drug should be administered in the inpatient setting [p.83]
- Closely monitor toxicity during and after drug administration [p.83]
- It is recommended that patients remain in the hospital for at least 24 hours after completion of the first cycle [p.83]

**Premedication:**
- Premedicate with an H1-histamine antagonist, acetaminophen, corticosteroid, and H2-histamine antagonist prior to each infusion [p.83]

**Dosing:**
- Administer tagraxofusp-erzs at 12 mcg/kg IV over 15 minutes once daily on days 1–5 of a 21-day cycle [p.83]
- Alternately, 5 doses can be administered over a 10-day period, if needed for dose delays [p.83]

**Prior to Each Dose:**
- Check vital signs, albumin, transaminases, and creatinine [p.83]

**Hold Tagraxofusp-erzs Dosing for:**
- Serum albumin <3.5 g/dL or a reduction from baseline of ≥0.5 [p.83]
- Body weight ≥1.5 kg over prior day [p.83]
- Edema, fluid overload, and/or hypotension [p.83]
- ALT/AST increase >5 times the upper limit of normal [p.83]
- Serum creatinine >1.8 or CrCl ≤60 mL/min [p.83]
- Systolic blood pressure (SBP) ≥160 or ≤80 mmHg [p.83]
- Heart rate (HR) ≥130 bpm or ≤40 bpm [p.83]
- Temperature ≥38°C [p.83]
- Mild to severe hypersensitivity reaction [p.83]

**Dermatologic Collaboration:**
- Collaboration with a dermatologist for supportive care is essential [p.83]
