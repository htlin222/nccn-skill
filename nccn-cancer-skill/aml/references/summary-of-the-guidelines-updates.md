## Diagnosis

### AML

**Key Updates [p.5]:**
- For patients with hyperleukocytosis uncontrolled with hydroxyurea or leukapheresis, one dose of cytarabine (0.5–2 g) may be considered prior to receiving diagnostic results [p.5]
- Leukapheresis may be considered for certain patients with symptomatic hyperleukocytosis, though data for benefit are limited [p.5]

### Evaluation for AML [p.4-5]

**Diagnostic Procedures Modified [p.4]:**
- Bone marrow (BM) core biopsy and aspirate analyses, including immunophenotyping by immunohistochemistry (IHC) stains + flow cytometry, and the analysis of chromosomal structural variations by cytogenetics, fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH), and/or whole genome next generation sequencing (NGS) [p.4]

**Molecular Analyses [p.4]:**
- Molecular analyses for lesions that allow risk stratification as per ELN 2022 (ASXL1, BCOR, KIT, EZH2, FLT3 [internal tandem duplication (ITD) and tyrosine kinase domain (TKD)], NPM1, in-frame bZIP mutation in CEBPA, IDH1, IDH2, RUNX1, SF3B1, SRSF2, STAG2, TP53, U2AF1, ZRSR2 and other mutations) [p.4]

**Heritable Predisposition Testing [p.4]:**
- Consider additional molecular and genetic testing for heritable hematologic malignancy predisposition in a subset of patients, particularly in patients <50 years and those with a family history [p.4]

**CNS Screening [p.4]:**
- Lumbar puncture (LP), if symptomatic (category 2B for asymptomatic) [p.4]

**Distress Assessment [p.5]:**
- Refer to the NCCN Distress Thermometer and Problem List, which includes social determinants of health. See NCCN Guidelines for Distress Management (DIS-A) [p.5]

## Workup

### APL Treatment Induction (High Risk) [p.5]

**Preferred Regimens [p.5]:**
- BM aspirate and biopsy at day 28 to document remission; consider LP before proceeding with consolidation [p.5]
- For prophylaxis: Begin with prednisone; optimal duration of steroid prophylaxis is unknown. If differentiation syndrome develops, change to dexamethasone [p.5]
- For high-risk APL, consider LP before proceeding with consolidation [p.5]

### Supportive Care for APL [p.5]

**Corticosteroid Prophylaxis [p.5]:**
- For patients at high risk (WBC count >10 × 10⁹/L) for developing differentiation syndrome and for those receiving an ATRA/arsenic trioxide-based regimen, initiate prophylaxis with corticosteroids, either prednisone 0.5 mg/kg day 1 or dexamethasone 10 mg every 12 hours [p.5]
- Taper the steroid dose over a period of several days. If patient develops differentiation syndrome, change prednisone to dexamethasone 10 mg every 12 hours until count recovery or risk of differentiation has abated [p.5]

**Electrolyte Monitoring [p.5]:**
- Serum electrolytes (Ca, K, Mg, phosphorus) and creatinine [p.5]

**Arsenic Trioxide Dosing [p.5]:**
- The optimal dosing of arsenic trioxide for patients with obesity is unknown, though retrospective studies suggest there may be a role for dose capping [p.5]

## Risk Stratification

### Molecular Profiling and Testing [p.7]

**Targeted NGS Recommendation [p.7]:**
- Multi-gene molecular profiling/targeted NGS (including testing for IDH1/IDH2, FLT3 mutations, and KMT2A rearrangements) is suggested as it may assist with selection of therapy and appropriate clinical trials [p.7]
- Molecular testing should be repeated at each relapse or progression [p.7]

### Prognostic Risk Classification for HMA/Venetoclax Patients [p.8]

**Intermediate Benefit Genetic Abnormalities [p.8]:**
- Mutations in KRAS and/or NRAS and/or FLT3-ITD; negative for mutation in TP53 [p.8]

## Induction Therapy

### Intensive Induction Eligible [p.5-6]

**Favorable-Risk AML by Cytogenetics (Core Binding Factor [CBF]-AML) [p.5]:**
- Regimen removed: 7 + 3 (mitoxantrone) (for age ≥60 y) [p.5]

**Favorable-Risk AML by Molecular Mutation or Intermediate-Risk AML per ELN [p.5-6]:**
- Regimen removed: 7 + 3 (mitoxantrone) (for age ≥60 y) [p.5]
- CLAG-M (cladribine + cytarabine + G-CSF + mitoxantrone) **Other Recommended** (category 2B; use with caution in patients >60 y) [p.5]

**Poor/Adverse-Risk Groups [p.6]:**
- Cytogenetic or molecular changes consistent with MDS (previously classified as AML with myelodysplasia-related changes [AML-MRC]) [p.6]

### Intensive Induction Treatment [p.6]

**Therapy-Related AML, Antecedent MDS/CMML, or MDS-Consistent Changes – Other Recommended [p.6]:**
- CLIA (cladribine + idarubicin + cytarabine) + venetoclax (category 2B) [p.6]
- FLAG-IDA + venetoclax (category 2B; use with caution in patients >60 y) [p.6]

**Poor-Risk AML without TP53 Mutation or del(17p) Abnormality – Other Recommended [p.6]:**
- CLIA + venetoclax (category 2B) [p.6]
- FLAG-IDA + venetoclax (category 2B; use with caution in patients >60 y) [p.6]

**Poor-Risk AML without TP53 Mutation or del(17p) Abnormality – Useful in Certain Circumstances [p.6]:**
- Regimen removed: 7 + 3 (mitoxantrone) (for age ≥60 y) (category 2B) [p.6]
- Regimen removed: Cytarabine + (daunorubicin or idarubicin) + etoposide (category 2B for patients >45 y) (use with caution in patients >60 y) [p.6]

**CBF-AML FLT3 Mutation Guidance [p.6]:**
- For CBF-AML with FLT3-TKD mutation, the Panel prefers gemtuzumab ozogamicin over an FLT3 inhibitor [p.6]
- For CBF-AML with FLT3-ITD, there is insufficient data to recommend one as preferred over the other [p.6]

**CPX-351 Use Guidance [p.6]:**
- Limited data supporting use in patients aged <60 years [p.6]
- For patients with AML with cytogenetic changes consistent with MDS and previous hypomethylating agent (HMA) exposure, the benefit from standard induction did not differ from the benefit with CPX-351/dual-drug liposomal encapsulation of cytarabine and daunorubicin [p.6]
- Emerging data suggests CPX-351 provides most benefit for patients with mutations in SRSF2, SF3B1, EZH2, U2AF1, ZRSR2, BCOR, STAG2, or ASXL1 [p.6]

### FLAG-IDA + Venetoclax Regimen Details [p.4]

**Dosing Schedule [p.4]:**
- Fludarabine 30 mg/m² on days 2–6
- Cytarabine 1.5–2 g/m² over 4 hours starting 4 hours after fludarabine infusion on days 2–6
- G-CSF SC daily on days 1–7
- Idarubicin 6 mg/m² IV on days 4–5
- Venetoclax 400 mg on days 1–7 [p.4]

**Clinical Reference [p.4]:**
- DiNardo CD, Jen WY, Takahashi K, et al. Long term results of venetoclax combined with FLAG-IDA induction and consolidation for newly diagnosed and relapsed or refractory acute myeloid leukemia. Leukemia 2025;39:854-863 [p.4]

### Standard 7 + 3 Dosing [p.8]

**Daunorubicin Preference [p.8]:**
- Daunorubicin 60 mg/m² is preferred over 90 mg/m² [p.8]

**Fludarabine Shortage Guidance [p.8]:**
- In times of fludarabine shortage, cladribine can be substituted for fludarabine [p.8]

### Lower Intensity Therapy (Intensive Induction Ineligible or Declines) [p.6-7]

**AML with IDH1 Mutation – Preferred [p.7]:**
- Decitabine + venetoclax [p.7]

**AML with IDH1 Mutation – Useful in Certain Circumstances [p.7]:**
- Olutasidenib (category 2B) (not eligible for preferred regimen and not eligible for ivosidenib due to prolonged QTcF) [p.7]

**IDH Inhibitor Response Timeline [p.7]:**
- Response to treatment with IDH inhibitors enasidenib or ivosidenib may take 3–5 months [p.7]

**IDH Inhibitor Adverse Effects [p.7]:**
- IDH inhibitors enasidenib or ivosidenib increase the risk for differentiation syndrome and hyperleukocytosis that may require treatment with hydroxyurea and steroids [p.7]
- Differentiation syndrome can occur later (up to several months after induction) [p.7]

**Olutasidenib [p.8]:**
- Added as 8th regimen for lower intensity therapy [p.8]

## Consolidation / Maintenance

### Follow-Up and Reinduction After Cytarabine-Based Induction [p.6]

**Regimens [p.6]:**
- Regimen removed: 7 + 3 (mitoxantrone) (for age ≥60 y) [p.6]
- HMA (azacitidine or decitabine) + venetoclax [p.6]

**CPX-351 Guidance [p.6]:**
- CPX-351/dual-drug liposomal encapsulation of cytarabine and daunorubicin (therapy-related AML other than CBF-AML, antecedent MDS/CMML, or cytogenetic or molecular changes consistent with MDS AML-MRC) (preferred only if given in induction; BM aspirate and biopsy 14–21 days after start of therapy) [p.6]

**Lack of Response to Induction [p.6]:**
- Consider regimens for relapsed/refractory disease, including targeted therapies, if lack of response to induction [p.6]

### Reinduction Therapy [p.8]

**HMA + Venetoclax Regimen [p.8]:**
- Added as regimen for reinduction in the setting of residual disease after cytarabine-based induction [p.8]

### Principles of Venetoclax Use [p.8]

**Drug-Drug Interactions [p.8]:**
- Drug-drug interactions occur with strong or moderate CYP3A4 inhibitors and may require dose adjustment [p.8]
- Strong or moderate CYP3A4 inducers (eg, carbamazepine, phenytoin, rifampin) should be avoided [p.8]

**Cytopenias and Cycle Management [p.8]:**
- Patients with cytopenias with disease in remission should take breaks between cycles [p.6, p.8]
- Delay next cycle for up to 14 days to allow recovery of ANC >0.5 × 10⁹/L and platelets >50 × 10⁹/L [p.8]
- G-CSF may be added [p.8]
- Consider further reduction in venetoclax duration (eg, 14 days, 7 days, or 5 days) if cytopenias recur in subsequent cycles [p.8]

**Venetoclax Continuation [p.7]:**
- Venetoclax combination regimens may be continued for patients whose disease demonstrates clinical improvement (CR/CR with incomplete hematologic recovery [CRi]), with consideration of subsequent transplant, where appropriate [p.7]

**HMA Resistance [p.7]:**
- Patients whose disease has progressed to AML from MDS after significant exposure to HMAs (ie, azacitidine, decitabine) may be less likely to derive benefit from continued treatment with HMAs compared to patients who are HMA-naïve [p.7]
- Alternative treatment strategies should be considered [p.7]

**Therapy for Substantial Comorbidities [p.8]:**
- New section added to Principles of Venetoclax Use With HMA or LDAC [p.8]

**NPM1 MRD Monitoring in HMA/Venetoclax [p.8]:**
- NPM1 molecular MRD is strongly prognostic in patients receiving venetoclax-based low-intensity therapy [p.8]
- The Panel supports monitoring NPM1 by RQ-PCR every 6 to 12 weeks in the BM or peripheral blood in patients receiving venetoclax-based low-intensity therapy [p.8]

## Relapsed/Refractory

### Therapy for Relapsed/Refractory Disease [p.6, p.8]

**Targeted Therapy – NPM1 Mutation [p.7, p.8]:**
- Revumenib added [p.7, p.8]
- Ziftomenib added [p.4]

**Revumenib – NPM1 and KMT2A Fusion [p.4]:**
- Revumenib is FDA approved for the treatment of relapsed or refractory acute leukemia with a KMT2A translocation, as well as relapsed or refractory AML with a susceptible NPM1 mutation with no satisfactory alternative treatment options, in adult and pediatric patients ≥1 year [p.4]

**Ziftomenib – NPM1 Mutation [p.4]:**
- Ziftomenib is FDA approved for the treatment of adults with relapsed or refractory AML with a susceptible NPM1 mutation with no satisfactory alternative treatment options [p.4]
- Dosing: 600 mg once daily on days 1-28 of a 28-day cycle [p.4]
- Reference: Wang ES, Montesinos P, Foran J, et al. Ziftomenib in relapsed or refractory NPM1-mutated AML. J Clin Oncol 2025;43:3381–3390 [p.4]

**Best Sequencing of Targeted Therapies [p.7]:**
- Best sequencing of targeted therapies is unknown at this time and depends on clinical context [p.7]

**Combined Targeted Therapies [p.7]:**
- There have been trials conducted combining targeted therapies with other agents [p.7]

**Revumenib Regimen Modified [p.8]:**
- Revumenib (KMT2A rearrangement or NPM1 mutation) [p.8]

### Follow-Up Pathways After Induction [p.6]

**Response CR with Screening LP [p.6]:**
- Continue to therapy for relapsed/refractory disease (AML-9) if indicated [p.6]

**Consolidation After Intensive Re-induction [p.6]:**
- Consolidation (if received intensive re-induction eligible) (AML-6) [p.6]

**Continue Lower Intensity After HMA + Venetoclax Re-induction [p.6]:**
- Continue lower intensity therapy (if received HMA + venetoclax reinduction) (AML-5) [p.6]

### AML Surveillance and Therapy After Consolidation Completion [p.7]

**Molecular Assessment [p.7]:**
- Confirm molecular remission and monitor for molecular relapse, if applicable [p.7]

**Treatment Options [p.7]:**
- Targeted therapy alone or in combination (AML-9) followed by allogeneic HCT [p.7]

## Supportive Care

### Evaluation and Treatment of CNS Leukemia [p.7]

**Screening LP Indications [p.7]:**
- Screening LP should be considered at first remission before first intensive consolidation for asymptomatic patients with:
  - Monocytic differentiation [p.7]
  - MPAL [p.7]
  - WBC count >40 × 10⁹/L at diagnosis [p.7]
  - Extramedullary disease [p.7]
  - High-risk APL [p.7]
  - Intraparenchymal hemorrhage at diagnosis [p.7]
  - FLT3 mutations [p.7]
  - MLLT3::KMT2A fusion [p.7]

### PCR Monitoring for APL [p.5]

**Molecular Remission Confirmation [p.5]:**
- PCR should be performed on a blood sample at completion of consolidation to document molecular remission [p.5]
- In patients receiving the ATRA/arsenic regimen, consider earlier sampling at 3–4 months during consolidation [p.5]

**Long-Term Monitoring [p.5]:**
- Prior practice guidelines have recommended monitoring blood by PCR every 3 mo for 2 y to detect molecular relapse [p.5]
- Risk of relapse in patients with low-risk disease who are in molecular remission at completion of consolidation is low and monitoring may not be necessary outside the setting of a clinical trial [p.5]
- With newer, more effective regimens, the value of long-term monitoring is less certain [p.5]

## Follow-Up

### Measurable (Minimal) Residual Disease Assessment [p.9]

**General Principles [p.9]:**
- There are commercially available tests that can be used for MRD assessments, as well as further testing methods at certain academic centers [p.9]
- The most frequently used methods for MRD assessment include quantitative molecular assays such as real-time quantitative PCR (RQ-PCR) and multicolor flow cytometry (MFC) assays specifically designed to detect abnormal MRD immunophenotypes [p.9]
- The optimal sample for initial MRD assessment is a first, dedicated pull of the BM aspirate. Quality of sample is paramount for reliable evaluation [p.9]
- Once MRD-negative remission by BM is achieved, peripheral blood can be utilized for surveillance of MRD for PML:RAR alpha, NPM1, CBFB::MYH11, and RUNX1::RUNX1T1 [p.9]
- Mutations associated with clonal hematopoiesis of indeterminate potential (CHIP) and aging (ie, DNMT3A, TET2, potentially ASXL1) are not considered reliable markers for MRD [p.9]

**Flow Cytometry Methods [p.9]:**
- If using flow cytometry to assess MRD, it is recommended that a specific MRD assay that incorporates both different from normal and leukemia-associated immunophenotypes (LAIPs) assessment is utilized [p.9]
- Interpretation by an experienced hematopathologist, preferably at the same laboratory as diagnostic flow cytometry for consistency [p.9]
- Utilization of an assay with minimum limit of detection of ≤10⁻³ is recommended [p.9]

**Molecular Methods [p.9]:**
- The Panel recommends RQ-PCR for detection of PML::RAR alpha, NPM1, CBFB::MYH11, and RUNX1::RUNX1T1 [p.9]
- Utilization of an assay with minimum limit of detection of ≤10⁻⁴ is recommended [p.9]

**Timing of MRD Assessment [p.9]:**
- For NPM1-mutated, CBFB::MYH11, and RUNX1::RUNX1T1 AML: after two cycles of intensive chemotherapy (eg, one cycle of induction and one cycle of consolidation) and for serial monitoring every 6 to 12 weeks for 24 months [p.9]

**Management of MRD Positivity [p.9]:**
- The Panel recommends post-transplant maintenance gilteritinib for patients with BM FLT3-ITD MRD ≥10⁻⁶ by a highly sensitive, NGS-based, targeted, deep-sequencing assay with a sensitivity level of ≤10⁻⁵ pre- or post-allogeneic HCT [p.9]
- As there is no clear optimal management of MRD positivity, the Panel favors a clinical trial for MRD positivity if available [p.9]
- If a clinical trial, allogeneic HCT, targeted therapy, or other consolidation strategies are not pursued, venetoclax-based low-intensity therapy has demonstrated high rates of MRD reduction in MRD and oligoblastic AML [p.9]

### Response Criteria Definitions for Acute Myeloid Leukemia [p.9]

**Complete Remission (CR) [p.9]:**
- Morphologic CR – transfusion independence [p.9]

**Relapse [p.9]:**
- Reappearance of leukemic blasts in the peripheral blood or the finding of ≥5% blasts in the BM, not attributable to another cause (eg, BM regeneration after consolidation therapy) or extramedullary relapse [p.9]

**Primary Refractory Disease [p.9]:**
- Inability to attain CR, CRh, or CRi following exposure to at least 2 courses of intensive induction therapy [p.9]

**MRD Relapse [p.9]:**
- Conversion from MRD negativity to MRD positivity, independent of method of testing [p.9]

## Blastic Plasmacytoid Dendritic Cell Neoplasm (BPDCN)

### BPDCN Diagnosis and Treatment [p.10]

**General Principles [p.10]:**
- Diagnosis can be extremely challenging and whenever possible should involve an experienced hematopathologist [p.10]
- Prognosis for BPDCN is poor and the median OS is approximately 8–12 months when patients are treated with chemotherapy without consolidative allogeneic HCT [p.10]

**Treatment Options [p.10]:**
- For patients who are fit, current treatment options for BPDCN include tagraxofusp-erzs and chemotherapy [p.10]
- Those with low albumin and/or comorbidities should receive non-intensive regimen options, localized therapy, or supportive care [p.10]

### BPDCN Relapsed/Refractory Disease [p.10]

**Treatment Modifications [p.10]:**
- Tagraxofusp-erzs (preferred, if not already used) [p.10]
- Venetoclax-based therapy, see BPDCN-2 AML-4 [p.10]

### BPDCN CNS Disease [p.10]

**CNS Prophylaxis [p.10]:**
- Consider administering prophylactic CNS-directed IT chemotherapy for patients without overt CNS disease [p.10]
