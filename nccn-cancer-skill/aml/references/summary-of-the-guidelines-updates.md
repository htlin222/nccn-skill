## Overview

NCCN Guidelines Version 3.2026 for Acute Myeloid Leukemia (Age ≥18 years) [p.4]

### General Updates

References updated across the guidelines [p.4]

## Evaluation Section (EVAL-1)

Modified: Bone marrow (BM) core biopsy and aspirate analyses, including immunophenotyping by immunohistochemistry (IHC) stains + flow cytometry, and the analysis of chromosomal structural variations by cytogenetics, fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH), and whole genome next generation sequencing (NGS) [p.4]

Modified: Molecular analyses for lesions that allow risk stratification as per ELN 2022 (ASXL1, BCOR, KIT, EZH2, FLT3 [internal tandem duplication (ITD) and tyrosine kinase domain (TKD)], NPM1, in-frame bZIP mutation in CEBPA, IDH1, IDH2, RUNX1, SF3B1, SRSF2, STAG2, TP53, U2AF1, ZRSR2 and other mutations) [p.4]

Modified: Consider additional molecular and genetic testing for heritable hematologic malignancy predisposition in a subset of patients, particularly in patients <50 years and those with a family history [p.4]

Modified: Lumbar puncture (LP), if symptomatic (category 2B for asymptomatic) [p.4]

## Induction Therapy Updates

### Fludarabine + Cytarabine + G-CSF + Idarubicin + Venetoclax Regimen

Modified regimen: [p.4-5]
- Fludarabine 30 mg/m2 on days 2–6
- Cytarabine 1.5–2 g/m2 over 4 hours starting 4 hours after fludarabine infusion on days 2–6
- G-CSF SC daily on days 1–7
- Idarubicin 6 mg/m2 IV on days 4–5
- Venetoclax 400 mg on days 1–7

Reference updated: DiNardo CD, Jen WY, Takahashi K, et al. Long term results of venetoclax combined with FLAG-IDA induction and consolidation for newly diagnosed and relapsed or refractory acute myeloid leukemia. Leukemia 2025;39:854-863 [p.5]

### APL Treatment Induction (High Risk)

Modified: BM aspirate and biopsy at day 28 to document remission consider LP before proceeding with consolidation [p.5]

Footnote g added: Begin prophylaxis with prednisone; the optimal duration of steroid prophylaxis is unknown. If differentiation syndrome develops, change to dexamethasone. Lo-Coco F, et al. N Engl J Med 2013;369:111-121 [p.5]

Footnote m added: For all patients with high-risk APL, consider LP before proceeding with consolidation [p.5]

### Intensive Induction Eligible

Modified for Favorable-risk AML by cytogenetics (core binding factor [CBF]-AML):
- Removed: 7 + 3 (mitoxantrone) (for age ≥60 y) [p.5]

Modified for Favorable-risk AML by molecular mutation profile or Intermediate-risk AML per ELN:
- Removed: 7 + 3 (mitoxantrone) (for age ≥60 y)
- Modified: CLAG-M (cladribine + cytarabine + G-CSF - mitoxantrone) (category 2B; use with caution in patients >60 y) [p.5]

## Poor/Adverse-Risk Groups

Modified: Cytogenetic or molecular changes consistent with MDS (previously classified as AML with myelodysplasia-related changes [AML-MRC]) [p.6]

### Therapy-related AML, Antecedent MDS/CMML, or AML-MRC Pathway

Other Recommended regimens added:
- CLIA (cladribine + idarubicin + cytarabine) + venetoclax (category 2B) [p.6]
- FLAG-IDA + venetoclax (category 2B; use with caution in patients >60 y) [p.6]

Poor-risk AML without TP53 mutation or del(17p) abnormality pathway added:
- CLIA + venetoclax (category 2B) [p.6]
- FLAG-IDA + venetoclax (category 2B; use with caution in patients >60 y) [p.6]

Useful in Certain Circumstances:
- Removed: 7 + 3 (mitoxantrone) (for age ≥60 y) (category 2B) [p.6]
- Removed: Cytarabine + (daunorubicin or idarubicin) + etoposide (category 2B for patients >45 y) (use with caution in patients >60 y) [p.6]

### Footnote k (CBF-AML)

Modified: For CBF-AML with FLT3-TKD mutation, the Panel prefers gemtuzumab ozogamicin over an FLT3 inhibitor. For CBF-AML with FLT3-ITD, there is insufficient data to recommend one as preferred over the other [p.6]

### Footnote r (CPX-351 Use)

Modified: There are limited data supporting the use of this regimen in patients aged <60 years. For patients with AML with cytogenetic changes consistent with MDS and previous hypomethylating agent (HMA) exposure, the benefit from standard induction did not differ from the benefit with CPX-351/dual-drug liposomal encapsulation of cytarabine and daunorubicin. Lancet JE, et al. J Clin Oncol 2018;36:2684-2692. Emerging data suggests CPX-351 provides most benefit for patients with mutations in SRSF2, SF3B1, EZH2, U2AF1, ZRSR2, BCOR, STAG2, or ASXL1 (Shimony SO, et al. Blood 2024;144:60) [p.6]

### Footnote u (Cycle Breaks)

Modified: Patients with cytopenias with disease in remission should take breaks between cycles. For more details about cycle length, see Principles of Venetoclax [p.6]

## Follow-Up and Reinduction (AML-3)

Removed: 7 + 3 (mitoxantrone) (for age ≥60 y) [p.6]

Added: HMA (azacitidine or decitabine) + venetoclax [p.6]

Modified: CPX-351/dual-drug liposomal encapsulation of cytarabine and daunorubicin (therapy-related AML other than CBFAML, antecedent MDS/CMML, or cytogenetic or molecular changes consistent with MDS AML-MRC) (preferred only if given in induction; BM aspirate and biopsy 14–21 days after start of therapy) [p.6]

Modified: Consider regimens for relapsed/refractory disease, including targeted therapies, if lack of response to induction [p.6]

### Therapy for Relapsed/Refractory Disease (AML-9)

Modified: Response CR (Screening LP) [p.6]

Added: Continue lower intensity therapy (if received HMA + venetoclax reinduction) [p.6]

## Venetoclax Combination Regimens

Added footnote s: Venetoclax combination regimens may be continued for patients whose disease demonstrates clinical improvement (CR/CR with incomplete hematologic recovery [CRi]), with consideration of subsequent transplant, where appropriate [p.7]

Added footnote t: Patients whose disease has progressed to AML from MDS after significant exposure to HMAs may be less likely to derive benefit from continued treatment with HMAs compared to patients who are HMA-naïve. Alternative treatment strategies should be considered [p.7]

## Lower Intensity Therapy (AML-4)

### AML with IDH1 Mutation

Preferred regimen moved here:
- Decitabine + venetoclax [p.7]

Added to Useful in Certain Circumstances:
- Olutasidenib (category 2B) (not eligible for preferred regimen and not eligible for ivosidenib due to prolonged QTcF) [p.7]

### IDH Inhibitor Footnotes

Modified footnote cc: Response to treatment with IDH inhibitors enasidenib or ivosidenib may take 3–5 months [p.7]

Modified footnote dd: IDH inhibitors enasidenib or ivosidenib increases the risk for differentiation syndrome and hyperleukocytosis that may require treatment with hydroxyurea and steroids. Monitor closely for differentiation syndrome and initiate therapy to resolve symptoms according to indications. Note that differentiation syndrome can occur later (up to several months after induction) [p.7]

## AML Surveillance and Relapsed/Refractory Disease (AML-8)

Modified: Confirm molecular remission and monitor for molecular relapse, if applicable [p.7]

Modified: Targeted therapy alone or in combination followed by allogeneic HCT [p.7]

Modified footnote ww: Molecular and cytogenetic analyses/Multi-gene molecular profiling/targeted NGS (including testing for IDH1/IDH2, FLT3 mutations, and KMT2A rearrangements) are suggested as it may assist with selection of therapy and appropriate clinical trials [p.7]

## Targeted Therapy for Relapsed/Refractory Disease (AML-9)

Added: Therapy for AML with NPM1 mutation, revumenib added [p.7]

Added footnote zz: There have been trials conducted combining targeted therapies with other agents [p.7]

Added footnote aaa: Best sequencing of targeted therapies is unknown at this time and depends on clinical context [p.7]

## Principles of Systemic Therapy (AML-E)

Removed: Standard 7 + 3 (mitoxantrone) [p.8]

Added footnote f: Daunorubicin 60 mg/m2 is preferred over 90 mg/m2. Rollig C, et al. J Clin Oncol 2025;43:65-74 [p.8]

### Intensive Induction Regimens

Added: CLIA + venetoclax [p.8]

Added: FLAG-IDA + venetoclax [p.8]

Removed: Cytarabine (HiDAC) + (daunorubicin or idarubicin) + etoposide [p.8]

Added footnote g: In times of fludarabine shortage, cladribine can be substituted for fludarabine [p.8]

Added footnote j: Principles of Venetoclax Use With HMA or LDAC [p.8]

Added footnote k: Use with caution in patients >60 years [p.8]

## Measurable (Minimal) Residual Disease (MRD) Assessment (AML-H)

### General Principles

Moved from Methods section: There are commercially available tests that can be used for MRD assessments, as well as further testing methods at certain academic centers [p.9]

Moved: The most frequently used methods for MRD assessment include quantitative molecular assays such as real-time quantitative PCR (RQ-PCR) and multicolor flow cytometry (MFC) assays [p.9]

Moved: The optimal sample for initial MRD assessment is a first, dedicated pull of the BM aspirate. Once MRD-negative remission by BM is achieved, peripheral blood can be utilized for surveillance of MRD for PML:RAR alpha, NPM1, CBFB::MYH11, and RUNX1::RUNX1T1 [p.9]

Moved: Mutations associated with clonal hematopoiesis of indeterminate potential (CHIP) and aging (ie, DNMT3A, TET2, potentially ASXL1) are not considered reliable markers for MRD [p.9]

### Flow Cytometry

Modified: If using flow cytometry to assess MRD, it is recommended that a specific MRD assay that incorporates both different from normal and leukemia-associated immunophenotypes (LAIPs) assessment is utilized. Utilization of an assay with minimum limit of detection of ≤10-3 is recommended [p.9]

### Molecular Testing

Modified: The Panel recommends RQ-PCR for detection of PML::RAR alpha, NPM1, CBFB::MYH11, and RUNX1::RUNX1T1. Utilization of an assay with minimum limit of detection of ≤10-4 is recommended [p.9]

### Timing of MRD Assessment

Modified: For NPM1-mutated, CBFB::MYH11, and RUNX1::RUNX1T1 AML: after two cycles of intensive chemotherapy and for serial monitoring every 6 to 12 weeks for 24 months [p.9]

Added: NPM1 molecular MRD is strongly prognostic in patients receiving venetoclax-based low-intensity therapy. The Panel supports monitoring NPM1 by RQ-PCR every 6 to 12 weeks in the BM or peripheral blood in patients receiving venetoclax-based low-intensity therapy [p.9]

### Management of MRD Positivity

Added: The Panel recommends post-transplant maintenance gilteritinib for patients with BM FLT3-ITD MRD ≥10-6 by a highly sensitive, NGS-based, targeted, deep-sequencing assay with a sensitivity level of ≤10-5 pre- or post-allogeneic HCT [p.9]

Added: As there is no clear optimal management of MRD positivity, the Panel favors a clinical trial for MRD positivity if available. If a clinical trial, allogeneic HCT, targeted therapy, or other consolidation strategies are not pursued, the Panel notes that venetoclax-based low-intensity therapy has demonstrated high rates of MRD reduction [p.9]

## Response Criteria Definitions (AML-I)

Modified: Morphologic CR – transfusion independence [p.9]

Modified: Reappearance of leukemic blasts in the peripheral blood or the finding of ≥ > 5% blasts in the BM, not attributable to another cause [p.9]

Modified: Inability to attain CR, CRh, or CRi following exposure to at least 2 courses of intensive induction therapy [p.9]

Added: MRD relapse - Conversion from MRD negativity to MRD positivity, independent of method of testing [p.9]

## Principles of Venetoclax Use With HMA or LDAC (AML-J)

Modified: Drug-drug interactions occur with strong or moderate CYP3A4 inhibitors and may require dose adjustment. Refer to venetoclax prescribing information and consult with a pharmacist for potential drug interactions. Strong or moderate CYP3A4 inducers (eg, carbamazepine, phenytoin, rifampin) should be avoided [p.10]

### Prognostic Risk Classification

Modified: Mutations in KRAS and/or NRAS and/or FLT3-ITD; negative for mutation in TP53 for Intermediate benefit genetic abnormality [p.10]

### New Section: Therapy for Patients with Substantial Comorbidities

Section added to AML-J [p.10]

### Cycle Management

Modified: G-CSF may be added [p.10]

Added: Delay next cycle for up to 14 days to allow recovery of ANC >0.5 x 109/L and platelets >50 x 109/L [p.10]

Added: G-CSF is encouraged [p.10]

Modified: Consider further reduction in venetoclax duration (eg, 14 days, 7 days, or 5 days) if cytopenias recur in subsequent cycles [p.10]

## BPDCN Updates

Pages BPDCN-1, BPDCN-2, BPDCN-3, and BPDCN-4 were extensively revised [p.10]

### Treatment for Relapsed/Refractory Disease (BPDCN-4)

Modified: Tagraxofusp-erzs (preferred, if not already used) [p.10]

Modified: Venetoclax-based therapy, See [BPDCN-2](bpdcn-introduction.md) [p.10]

Removed footnote: Consider CNS prophylaxis for patients with overt systemic disease [p.10]

### Principles of BPDCN General

Added: Diagnosis can be extremely challenging and whenever possible should involve an experienced hematopathologist [p.10]

Modified: Prognosis for BPDCN is poor and the median OS is approximately 8–12 months when patients are treated with chemotherapy without consolidative allogeneic HCT [p.10]

Modified: For patients who are fit, current treatment options for BPDCN include tagraxofusp-erzs and chemotherapy, whereas those with low albumin and/or comorbidities should receive non-intensive regimen options localized therapy or supportive care [p.10]

### Evaluation and Treatment of CNS Disease

Modified: Consider administering prophylactic CNS-directed IT chemotherapy [p.10]
