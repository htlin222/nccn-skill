## APL Classification and Risk Stratification

For confirmed APL, risk stratification is based on white blood cell count and cardiac status.

### High-Risk APL

**Risk Definition:** WBC count >10 x 10^9/L [p.14]

**Treatment Path:** Treatment Induction (APL-3) --> See [APL-3](apl-classification-and-treatment-recommendations.md) [p.14]

### Low-Risk APL

**Risk Definition:** WBC count ≤10 x 10^9/L [p.14]

#### Without Cardiac Issues

**Treatment Path:** Treatment Induction (APL-2) --> See [APL-2](apl-classification-and-treatment-recommendations.md) [p.14]

#### With Cardiac Issues

**Cardiac Issues Definition:** Low ejection fraction (EF) or QTcF prolongation [p.14]

Refer to Principles of Supportive Care for APL for cardiac monitoring details --> See APL-A [p.14]

**Treatment Path:** Treatment Induction (APL-4) [p.14]

## Important Notes on APL Management

**Therapy-Related APL:** Therapy-related APL is treated the same as de novo APL [p.14]

**FLT3 Mutations:** FLT3 inhibitors are not recommended for FLT3-positive APL (Gale RE, et al. Blood 2005;106:3768-3776) [p.14]

**Community Center Treatment:** For patients with APL being treated at a community center, collaboration with a center with expertise has been shown to reduce induction mortality (Jillella AP, et al. JAMA Oncol 2025;11:400-407) [p.14]
