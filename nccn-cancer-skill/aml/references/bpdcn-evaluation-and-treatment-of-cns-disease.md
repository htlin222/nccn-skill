## CNS-Directed Therapy

**Category:** All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.82]

### With Documented CNS Disease [p.82]

**CNS-directed IT chemotherapy:** [p.82]

- Chemotherapy regimens may follow institutional standards, but would preferably be aggressive including alternating cytarabine with methotrexate, or triple IT agents (i.e., cytarabine, methotrexate, steroid) [p.82]

**Dosing Schedule:** [p.82]

1. **Initial phase (until CSF clear):** Twice weekly dosing until CSF is clear [p.82]

2. **Consolidation phase:** Once the CSF is clear (negative on cytology) continue weekly IT treatments for at least 4 doses, then twice per month for a total of at least 8 doses [p.82]

3. **Maintenance phase:** Consider continuing IT treatments once or twice per month for ongoing prophylaxis [p.82]
   - Decision should be based on shared decision-making with patient after discussion of risks and benefits [p.82]

### Without Documented CNS Disease [p.82]

**Prophylactic CNS-directed IT chemotherapy:** [p.82]

- Chemotherapy regimens may follow institutional standards, but would preferably be aggressive including alternating cytarabine with methotrexate, or triple IT agents (i.e., cytarabine, methotrexate, steroid) [p.82]

**Dosing Schedule:** [p.82]

1. **Initial prophylaxis:** Twice per month for a total of at least 8 doses [p.82]

2. **Maintenance phase:** Consider continuing IT treatment once or twice per month for ongoing prophylaxis [p.82]
   - Decision should be based on shared decision-making with patient after discussion of risks and benefits [p.82]

### Clinical Justification [p.82]

Prophylactic CNS-directed IT chemotherapy is recommended even in the absence of known CNS disease, given the high percentage (30%) of primary CNS involvement at relapse [p.82]

**Note:** All chemotherapy regimens should preferably be aggressive, including alternating cytarabine with methotrexate, or triple IT agents (cytarabine, methotrexate, steroid) [p.82]
