## Response Criteria Definitions

These response criteria were defined in the context of intensive chemotherapy regimens, and may not be predictive of outcomes for patients who receive other therapies [p.69, ref.1].

All recommendations are category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.69].

### Morphologic Leukemia-Free State (MLFS)

**Definition** [p.69]:
- BM <5% blasts in an aspirate with spicules
- At least 200 cells must be enumerated
- No blasts with Auer rods or persistence of extramedullary disease

**Confirmation**: If there is a question of residual leukemia, a BM aspirate/biopsy should be repeated in one week. A BM biopsy/aspirate should be performed [p.69].

### Complete Remission (CR)

#### Morphologic CR

**Criteria** [p.69]:
- ANC ≥1 × 10⁹/L with blasts <5%
- Platelets ≥100 × 10⁹/L with blasts <5%

#### CR Without MRD (CR_MRD-)

**Definition** [p.69]:
- If studied pretreatment, CR with negativity for a genetic marker by reverse transcriptase PCR (RT-PCR)
- OR CR with negativity by MFC

**Important Notes** [p.69]:
- Sensitivity varies by marker and method used; analyses should be done in experienced laboratories
- Molecular CR: molecular studies negative

**Clinical Context** [p.69, ref.56]:
- This is clinically relevant in APL and Ph+ leukemia
- Inability to achieve a significant reduction (e.g., >3 log) in molecular evidence of t(8;21) or inv(16) has a very high predictive value of relapse
- Molecular remission for APL should be performed after consolidation, not after induction as in non-APL AML
- NPM1 is a target that can be included in the molecular response assessment

#### CR with Partial Hematologic Recovery (CRh)

**Definition** [p.69, ref.2]:
- <5% blasts in the BM
- No evidence of disease (NED)
- Partial recovery of peripheral blood counts:
  - Platelets ≥50 × 10⁹/L
  - ANC ≥0.5 × 10⁹/L

#### CRi (CR with Incomplete Recovery)

**Definition** [p.69]:
- All CR criteria
- Transfusion independence
- Persistence of neutropenia (<1 × 10⁹/L) OR thrombocytopenia (<100 × 10⁹/L)

#### Special Note on Responses Less Than CR

Responses less than CR may still be meaningful depending on the therapy [p.69].

### Partial Remission (PR)

**Definition** [p.69]:
- Decrease of at least 50% in the percentage of blasts
- Blasts reduced to 5%-25% in the BM aspirate
- Meeting hematologic criteria for CR as noted above

**Clinical Context**: Partial remissions are useful in assessing potential activity of new investigational agents, usually in phase I trials [p.69].

### Relapse Following CR

**Definition** [p.69]:
- Reappearance of leukemic blasts in the peripheral blood
- OR finding of ≥5% blasts in the BM
- Not attributable to another cause (e.g., BM regeneration after consolidation therapy)
- OR extramedullary relapse

### Primary Refractory AML

**Definition** [p.69]:
- Inability to attain CR, CRh, or CRi following exposure to at least 2 courses of intensive induction therapy

### MRD Relapse

**Definition** [p.69]:
- Conversion from MRD negativity to MRD positivity
- Independent of method of testing

## Principles of Venetoclax Use with HMA or LDAC (AML-J)

### General Principles

**Consultation**: Recommend consultation with a high-volume tertiary care/academic medical center throughout course of treatment [p.70].

**Clinical Trials**: There are ongoing studies into the addition of a third agent to the combinations described in this section, and participation in clinical trials is encouraged [p.70].

**Treatment Duration**: The maximum number of cycles for these regimens is unknown, and treatment may continue as long as tolerated and effective [p.70].

**Antimicrobial Prophylaxis**: Consider antimicrobial prophylaxis per institutional protocol for patients with newly diagnosed disease [p.70].

### Drug-Drug Interactions

**CYP3A4 Interactions**: Drug-drug interactions occur with strong or moderate CYP3A4 inhibitors and may require dose adjustment [p.70].

Refer to venetoclax prescribing information (https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/cder/daf/index.cfm) and consult with a pharmacist for potential drug interactions [p.70].

**CYP3A4 Inducers to Avoid**: Strong or moderate CYP3A4 inducers (e.g., carbamazepine, phenytoin, rifampin) should be avoided [p.70].

### Prognostic Risk Classification for Patients Ineligible for Intensive Therapy

Classification based on genetic abnormalities and benefit with HMA/Venetoclax [p.70]:

**Higher Benefit** [p.70]:
- Negative for mutations in TP53, KRAS, NRAS, and FLT3-ITD

**Intermediate Benefit** [p.70]:
- Mutations in KRAS and/or NRAS and/or FLT3-ITD
- Negative for mutation in TP53

**Lower Benefit** [p.70]:
- Mutation in TP53

### Tumor Lysis Syndrome (TLS) Risk Management

**WBC Reduction**: To decrease the risk of severe TLS, aim to achieve WBC count of <25 × 10⁹/L with hydroxyurea/leukapheresis prior to starting therapy [p.71].

**Inpatient Treatment**: Inpatient treatment is strongly recommended during first cycle of treatment, especially through dose escalation [p.71].

**Dose Escalation** [p.71]:
- **Venetoclax with HMA**: 100 mg, 200 mg, and 400 mg daily on days 1–3
- **Venetoclax with LDAC**: 100 mg, 200 mg, 400 mg, and 600 mg daily on days 1–4
- Note: Concomitant interacting medications may require changes to these dosages

**Uric Acid Management**: Recommend treatment with allopurinol or other uric acid–lowering agent until no further risk of TLS [p.71].

**Monitoring** [p.71]:
- Monitor blood chemistries every 6–8 hours after initiation for a total of 24 hours following maximum dose escalation
- If within normal limits, recheck once daily and continue monitoring until no further risk of TLS
- Aggressively monitor and manage electrolyte imbalances

### Therapy for Patients with Substantial Comorbidities

**Reduced Duration**: Consider initiating therapy with a reduced duration of venetoclax (e.g., 7 days) [p.71, ref.2].

### Therapy for Relapsed/Refractory Disease

**TLS Measures**: Consider the same TLS and intrapatient dose escalation measures as described for "TLS Risk/Monitoring During Cycle 1" [p.71].

**Bone Marrow Assessment**: Consider the same recommendations for early BM biopsy and cytopenia mitigation plan as per AML-J 3 of 4 [p.71].

**Antifungal Prophylaxis**: Recommend antifungal prophylaxis if indicated [p.71, ref.3].

### Cycle Management

#### Cycle 1: Initial Treatment and Assessment

**Treatment**: Venetoclax + HMA or LDAC [p.72].

**Bone Marrow Assessment**: BM aspirate and biopsy on days 21–28 [p.72].

**Response-Based Management** [p.72]:

**If CR (Response Criteria, see AML-I)**:
- Delay cycle 2 for up to 14 days to allow recovery of ANC >0.5 × 10⁹/L and platelets >50 × 10⁹/L [p.72]
- G-CSF is encouraged [p.72]
- Start cycle 2 [p.72]
- Consider reduction of venetoclax duration to 21 days [p.72]

**If MLFS or CRi (Response Criteria, see AML-I)**:
- Start cycle 2 without delay [p.72]
- BM aspirate and biopsy on days 21–28 [p.72]

**If Lack of Response (Response Criteria, see AML-I)**:
- Continue therapy up to 4 cycles and if no response, see Therapy for Relapsed/Refractory Disease (AML-9) [p.72]
- Start cycle 2 on day 29 or later if marrow results not available on day 29 [p.72]

#### Cycles 3 and Beyond

**Timing** [p.72]:
- Delay next cycle for up to 14 days to allow recovery of ANC >0.5 × 10⁹/L and platelets >50 × 10⁹/L
- G-CSF is encouraged
- Longer delays may be considered

**Dose Modifications** [p.72]:
- Consider further reduction in venetoclax duration (e.g., 14 days, 7 days, or 5 days) if cytopenias recur in subsequent cycles
- Dose modifications to HMA or LDAC may also be considered in accordance with their label

**Continuation of Therapy** [p.72]:
- Continue therapy up to 4 cycles
- Decrease in blasts/response is most commonly seen following 2 cycles
- While venetoclax + HMA or LDAC may be continued for up to 4 cycles to monitor for delayed response, it is also reasonable to stop therapy after 2 cycles in the setting of lack of response
