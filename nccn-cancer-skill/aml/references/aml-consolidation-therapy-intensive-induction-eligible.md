## Consolidation Therapy by Risk Group

### Favorable-Risk AML by Cytogenetics (CBF-AML) or Molecular Mutation Profile per ELN

**Treatment Options** [p.37]

- Allogeneic HCT (if not previously performed) [p.37]

- Maintenance (AML-7) [p.37]

- Surveillance (AML-8) [p.37]

**Key Considerations** [p.38]

- In-frame bZIP mutations in CEBPA are more predictive of favorable outcomes than double mutations [p.38]
- See Measurable (Minimal) Residual Disease Assessment (AML-H) for additional guidance on MRD monitoring for patients with CBF-AML and NPM1-mutated AML [p.38]
- Consider NPM1 molecular MRD status, if applicable [p.38]
- Allogeneic transplant recommended for patients with favorable-risk disease who are unable to complete consolidation or who have high-risk features such as MRD-positivity or KIT mutation [p.38]

### AML with FLT3 Mutation

**Preferred** [p.37]

- Allogeneic HCT (preferred for FLT3-ITD) [p.37]

**Other Recommended** [p.37]

- Cytarabine + midostaurin (FLT3-ITD or TKD) [p.37]
  - RATIFY trial studied patients aged 18–60 years with FLT3-mutated AML; extrapolation suggests patients aged 61–70 years with FLT3-mutated AML who are fit to receive 7 + 3 should be offered midostaurin as it provides survival benefit without undue toxicity [p.38]

- Cytarabine + quizartinib (FLT3-ITD only) [p.37]

**Follow-Up Options** [p.37]

- Allogeneic HCT (if not previously performed)
- Maintenance (AML-7)
- Surveillance (AML-8)

### Intermediate-Risk AML

**Preferred** [p.37]

- Allogeneic HCT [p.37]

**Other Recommended** [p.37]

- Cytarabine ± gemtuzumab ozogamicin (CD33 positive) [p.37]
  - Only if gemtuzumab ozogamicin was given during induction [p.37]

- Cytarabine (5 or 7 days) ± ([daunorubicin or idarubicin] or [mitoxantrone for age ≥60 years]) [p.37]

- Cytarabine + (daunorubicin or idarubicin) + gemtuzumab ozogamicin (CD33 positive) [p.37]
  - Only if gemtuzumab ozogamicin was given during induction [p.37]

**Follow-Up Options** [p.37]

- Consider allogeneic HCT [p.37]
- Maintenance (AML-7) [p.37]
- Surveillance (AML-8) [p.37]

### Poor-Risk AML (With and Without TP53 Mutation or del(17p) Abnormality), Therapy-Related AML, Antecedent MDS/CMML, or Cytogenetic Changes Consistent with MDS

**Preferred** [p.37]

- Allogeneic HCT [p.37]

**Other Recommended** [p.37]

- Cytarabine [p.37]

- CPX-351 (Dual-drug liposomal encapsulation of cytarabine and daunorubicin) [p.37]
  - Preferred only if given during induction [p.37]
  - Limited data supporting use in patients aged <60 years [p.38]
  - For patients with AML with cytogenetic changes consistent with MDS (previously classified as AML-MRC) and previous HMA exposure, benefit from standard induction did not differ from benefit with CPX-351 [p.38]
  - CPX-351 provides most benefit for patients with AML with mutations in SRSF2, SF3B1, EZH2, U2AF1, ZRSR2, BCOR, STAG2, or ASXL1 [p.38]

- FLAG-IDA (use with caution in patients >60 years) [p.37]
  - Preferred only if given during induction [p.37]
  - In times of fludarabine shortage, cladribine can be substituted for fludarabine [p.38]

- Continuation of lower intensity regimen used for induction (e.g., HMA [azacitidine or decitabine] + venetoclax) [p.37]
  - See [AML-4](aml-risk-group-and-induction-intensive-induction-eligible.md) [p.37]

**Follow-Up Options** [p.37]

- Allogeneic HCT (if not previously performed)
- Maintenance (AML-7)
- Surveillance (AML-8)

## Cytarabine Dosing and Special Considerations

**Dosing** [p.38]

- Alternate dosing of cytarabine for postremission therapy has been reported [p.38]
- Doses of cytarabine ≥2 g/m² should be used with caution in patients ≥60 years and patients with renal failure due to concern for neurotoxicity [p.38]
- No evidence that cytarabine doses ≥2 g/m² are superior to doses 1–2 g/m² in patients with AML with intermediate-risk cytogenetics [p.38]

**Cardiac Function Monitoring** [p.38]

- For regimens using high cumulative doses of cardiotoxic agents, consider reassessing cardiac function prior to each anthracycline/mitoxantrone-containing course [p.38]

## Gemtuzumab Ozogamicin Considerations

**CD33 Expression** [p.38]

- Threshold for CD33 is not well-defined and may be ≥1% by flow cytometry [p.38]

**HCT Timing** [p.38]

- Patients who receive transplant shortly following gemtuzumab ozogamicin administration may be at risk for developing sinusoidal obstruction syndrome (SOS) [p.38]
- If transplant is planned, prior studies have used a 60- to 90-day interval between last administration of gemtuzumab ozogamicin and HCT [p.38]

**KIT Mutations** [p.38]

- Gemtuzumab ozogamicin-containing regimen may also be used in patients with AML with KIT mutations because outcomes are similar in patients without KIT mutations [p.38]

## HCT Considerations

**Donor Search and Timing** [p.38]

- For appropriate patients, begin alternate donor search (haploidentical, unrelated donor, or cord blood) if no appropriate matched sibling donor available and patient is candidate for allogeneic HCT [p.38]
- For lack of response to induction, alternative therapy to achieve remission is encouraged prior to HCT [p.38]
- Patients eligible for allogeneic HCT may require at least one cycle of consolidation while donor search is in progress or while awaiting collaboration with transplant center to maintain remission [p.38]
- Patients may proceed directly to transplant following achievement of remission if donor is available [p.38]

## Systemic Therapy Principles

- Principles of Systemic Therapy for AML (AML-E) apply [p.38]
