## Treatment Approach

**Strongly Preferred** [p.41]

- Clinical trial [p.41]

**Recommended Options** [p.41]

Patients may receive targeted therapy, intensive therapy, or less intensive therapy based on clinical status.

## Targeted Therapies

**Therapy for AML with FLT3-ITD Mutation** [p.41]

- Gilteritinib (Category 1) [p.41]

- HMAs (azacitidine or decitabine) + sorafenib [p.41]

- Quizartinib (Category 2B) [p.41]

**Therapy for AML with FLT3-TKD Mutation** [p.41]

- Gilteritinib (Category 1) [p.41]

**Therapy for AML with IDH2 Mutation** [p.41]

- Enasidenib [p.41]

**Therapy for AML with IDH1 Mutation** [p.41]

- Ivosidenib [p.41]

- Olutasidenib [p.41]

**Therapy for CD33-Positive AML** [p.41]

- Gemtuzumab ozogamicin [p.41]

**Therapy for AML with Lysine Methyltransferase 2A Gene (KMT2A) Rearrangement** [p.41]

- Revumenib [p.41]

**Therapy for AML with NPM1 Mutation** [p.41]

- Revumenib [p.41]

- Ziftomenib [p.41]

**Important Considerations** [p.41]

- There have been trials combining targeted therapies with other agents [p.41]
- Best sequencing of targeted therapies is unknown at this time and depends on clinical context [p.41]
- Molecular profiling should be considered if not done at diagnosis, or repeated to determine clonal evolution [p.41]
- There are promising ongoing clinical trials investigating targeted therapies based on molecular mutations for relapsed/refractory disease [p.41]

## Intensive Therapy

**For Appropriate Patients** [p.41]

Appropriate patients include those eligible for intensive therapy with relatively short first remission [p.41]

**Chemotherapy Regimens** [p.41]

- Cladribine + cytarabine + G-CSF ± (mitoxantrone or idarubicin) [p.41]

- Cytarabine ± (daunorubicin or idarubicin or mitoxantrone) [p.41]

- Fludarabine + cytarabine + G-CSF ± idarubicin ± venetoclax [p.41]

- Etoposide + cytarabine ± mitoxantrone [p.41]

- Clofarabine ± cytarabine ± idarubicin [p.41]

- CLIA (cladribine + idarubicin + cytarabine) + venetoclax (Category 2B) [p.41]

**Reinduction Considerations** [p.41]

- Reinduction therapy may be appropriate in certain circumstances, such as in patients with long first remission [p.41]
- This strategy primarily applies to cytotoxic chemotherapy and excludes re-use of targeted agents due to potential development of resistance [p.41]
- Targeted therapies may be retried if agents were not administered continuously and not stopped due to development of clinical resistance [p.41]
- If second CR is achieved, consolidation with allogeneic HCT should be considered [p.41]

## Less Intensive Therapy

**Hypomethylating Agents (HMA)** [p.41]

- Azacitidine or decitabine [p.41]
  - Patients whose disease progressed to AML from MDS after significant exposure to HMAs may be less likely to derive benefit from continued HMA treatment compared to HMA-naïve patients; alternative treatment strategies should be considered [p.41]

**Low-Dose Cytarabine (LDAC)** [p.41]

- LDAC (Category 2B) [p.41]

**Combination Regimens** [p.41]

- (HMA or LDAC) + venetoclax [p.41]
