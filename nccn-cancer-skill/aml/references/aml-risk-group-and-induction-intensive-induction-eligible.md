## Overview

This section defines the risk stratification for Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML) in patients eligible for intensive induction therapy and outlines the treatment recommendations for each risk group.

## Risk Stratification

### Patient Eligibility for Intensive Induction [p.29]

**Intensive Induction Eligible** [p.29]:
- Age ≥18 years with adequate performance status
- Adequate organ function for intensive chemotherapy
- Patient/physician agreement to pursue curative intent therapy

## Risk Groups and Induction Therapy

### Favorable/Intermediate-Risk Groups (AML-A) [p.29]

**Risk Category Definition** [p.29]:
- Favorable-risk AML by cytogenetics (core binding factor [CBF]-AML)
- AML with FLT3 mutation
- Favorable-risk AML by molecular mutation profile
- Intermediate-risk AML per ELN (European LeukemiaNet)

#### Induction Therapy Options for Favorable/Intermediate-Risk [p.29]

**Preferred Regimen** [p.29]:
- Standard 7 + 3 (daunorubicin or idarubicin) + gemtuzumab ozogamicin (Category 2A) [p.29]
  - Requires CD33 positivity [p.29]

**Other Recommended Regimens** [p.29]:
- Standard 7 + 3 (daunorubicin or idarubicin) [p.29] (Category 1)
- FLAG-IDA (fludarabine + cytarabine + granulocyte colony-stimulating factor [G-CSF] + idarubicin) + gemtuzumab ozogamicin [p.29] (Category 2B; use with caution in patients >60 years)
  - Requires CD33 positivity [p.29]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances** [p.29]:
- FLAG + gemtuzumab ozogamicin (for those ineligible for an anthracycline) [p.29] (Category 2B)
  - Requires CD33 positivity [p.29]

**FLT3 Mutation-Specific Options** [p.29]:
- Standard 7 + 3 (daunorubicin or idarubicin) + midostaurin [p.29] (for FLT3-ITD or FLT3-TKD) (Category 1)
- Standard 7 + 3 (daunorubicin or idarubicin) + quizartinib [p.29] (for FLT3-ITD only) (Category 1)

### Poor/Adverse-Risk Groups (AML-2) [p.30]

**Risk Category Definition** [p.30]:
- Poor-risk AML without TP53 mutation or del(17p) abnormality
- Therapy-related AML (other than CBF-AML)

#### Induction Therapy Options for Poor/Adverse-Risk [p.30]

**Preferred Regimen** [p.30]:
- Standard 7 + 3 (daunorubicin or idarubicin) [p.30] (Category 1)

**Other Recommended Regimens** [p.30]:
- Standard 7 + 3 (daunorubicin or idarubicin) + gemtuzumab ozogamicin [p.30] (for CD33-positive disease)
- FLAG-IDA [p.30] (Category 2B; use with caution in patients >60 years)
- FLAG-IDA + gemtuzumab ozogamicin [p.30] (Category 2B; use with caution in patients >60 years; requires CD33 positivity)
- CLAG-M (cladribine + cytarabine + G-CSF + mitoxantrone) [p.30] (Category 2B; use with caution in patients >60 years)

## Molecular and Cytogenetic Markers

### Testing Requirements [p.29-30]

**Core Binding Factor AML (CBF-AML)** [p.29-30]:
- Cytogenetic testing required to identify t(8;21), t(15;17), or inv(16) translocations
- Favorable prognosis with modern chemotherapy

**FLT3 Mutation Testing** [p.29-30]:
- FLT3-ITD (internal tandem duplication) vs FLT3-TKD (tyrosine kinase domain)
- ITD-positive disease associated with poorer prognosis
- ITD status determines targeted therapy options (midostaurin, quizartinib)

**CD33 Expression** [p.29-30]:
- Required for gemtuzumab ozogamicin eligibility
- Typically positive in AML (>90% of cases)

### Risk Stratification Systems [p.29-30]

**European LeukemiaNet (ELN) Risk Categories** [p.29-30]:
- Favorable, Intermediate, Adverse (per 2022 ELN criteria)
- Incorporates genetic, molecular, and clinical factors

## Induction Regimen Details

### Standard 7 + 3 Regimen [p.29-30]

**Components** [p.29-30]:
- Cytarabine (cytosine arabinoside) 100-200 mg/m²/day IV continuous infusion x 7 days
- Daunorubicin 60-90 mg/m²/day IV x 3 days
- OR Idarubicin 12 mg/m²/day IV x 3 days

### FLAG-IDA Regimen [p.29-30]

**Components** [p.29-30]:
- Fludarabine 30 mg/m²/day IV x 5 days
- Cytarabine 2 g/m²/day IV x 5 days
- G-CSF 5 mcg/kg/day SC (filgrastim) x 6 days
- Idarubicin 10 mg/m²/day IV x 3 days

**Special Caution** [p.29-30]:
- Category 2B with caution in patients >60 years [p.29-30]

### CLAG-M Regimen [p.30]

**Components** [p.30]:
- Cladribine (2-chlorodeoxyadenosine)
- Cytarabine
- G-CSF
- Mitoxantrone

**Special Caution** [p.30]:
- Category 2B with caution in patients >60 years [p.30]

## Gemtuzumab Ozogamicin (CD33-Directed Therapy)

### Indication [p.29-30]

**CD33-Positive AML** [p.29-30]:
- Single-agent dose on day 5 of induction (3 mg/m² or 6 mg/m² dosing strategies)

### Benefit [p.29-30]

**Improved Outcomes** [p.29-30]:
- Improved relapse-free survival in multiple trials
- Enhanced cytotoxicity when combined with intensive chemotherapy
- Particularly effective in favorable and intermediate-risk disease

## Targeted Therapy for FLT3 Mutations

### Midostaurin (PKC412) [p.29]

**Indication** [p.29]:
- FLT3-ITD or FLT3-TKD mutations (Category 1)
- Combined with standard 7 + 3 induction

**Dosing** [p.29]:
- 50 mg PO BID (specific to trial protocols)

### Quizartinib (AC220) [p.29]

**Indication** [p.29]:
- FLT3-ITD mutations only (Category 1)
- Combined with standard 7 + 3 induction

**Dosing** [p.29]:
- 60 mg PO daily (specific to trial protocols)

## Category Classifications

**Note**: All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.29-30]

**Key Clinical Points**:

1. **Risk Assessment**: Complete cytogenetic and molecular testing essential before treatment initiation [p.29-30]

2. **Fit vs. Unfit**: Only present to this algorithm if patient is intensive induction eligible; otherwise See [AML-4](aml-risk-group-and-induction-intensive-induction-eligible.md) [p.29-31]

3. **Regimen Selection**: Choice of induction regimen should be individualized based on risk group, performance status, age, and comorbidities [p.29-30]

4. **Targeted Therapy**: FLT3 mutations and CD33 status should direct selection of combination therapy partners [p.29-30]

5. **Response Assessment**: Plan for bone marrow evaluation after induction to assess remission status before proceeding to consolidation [p.29-31]
