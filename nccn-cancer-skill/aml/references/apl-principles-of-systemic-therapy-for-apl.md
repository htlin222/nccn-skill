## Overview of APL Treatment Regimens

All drug dosing, schedules, and specific administration details are outlined below for the preferred induction and consolidation regimens used in APL treatment.

## Low-Risk APL (APL-2) Induction and Consolidation

### ATRA + Daily Arsenic Trioxide [p.22] (Category 1)

**Induction** [p.22]:
- ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily
- Arsenic trioxide 0.15 mg/kg IV daily

**Consolidation** [p.22]:
- Arsenic trioxide 0.15 mg/kg/day IV 5 days/week for 4 weeks every 8 weeks for a total of 4 cycles
- ATRA 45 mg/m²/day for 2 weeks every 4 weeks for a total of 7 cycles

### ATRA + Intermittent Arsenic Trioxide [p.22] (Category 1)

**Induction** [p.22]:
- ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily for 60 days or until achievement of complete remission (CR)
- Arsenic trioxide 0.3 mg/kg IV on days 1-5 of week 1
- Arsenic trioxide 0.25 mg/kg twice weekly during weeks 2-8

**Consolidation** [p.22]:
- First 3 consolidation cycles = 56-day cycles:
  - ATRA 45 mg/m²/day PO in 2 divided doses daily on days 1-14 and 29-42 (2 weeks on followed by 2 weeks off)
  - Arsenic trioxide 0.3 mg/kg on days 1-5 of week 1
  - Arsenic trioxide 0.25 mg/kg twice weekly during weeks 2-4
- Fourth consolidation cycle = 28-day cycle:
  - ATRA 45 mg/m²/day PO in 2 divided doses daily on days 1-14
  - Arsenic trioxide 0.3 mg/kg on days 1-5 of week 1
  - Arsenic trioxide 0.25 mg/kg twice weekly during weeks 2-4

## High-Risk APL (APL-3) Preferred Regimens

### ATRA + Idarubicin [p.22] (Category 1)

**Induction** [p.22]:
- ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily
- Idarubicin 12 mg/m² on days 2, 4, 6, 8
- Alternative for patients aged >70 years: Idarubicin 12 mg/m² on days 2, 4, 6

**Consolidation** [p.22]:
- Cycle 1: ATRA 45 mg/m² x 15 days + Idarubicin 5 mg/m² x 4 days
- Cycle 2: ATRA 45 mg/m² x 15 days + Mitoxantrone 10 mg/m²/day x 3 days
- Cycle 3: ATRA 45 mg/m² x 15 days + Idarubicin 12 mg/m² x 1 day

### ATRA + Gemtuzumab Ozogamicin [p.22]

**Induction** [p.22]:
- ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily
- Gemtuzumab ozogamicin 6 or 9 mg/m² single dose on day 5

**Consolidation** [p.22]:
- ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily during weeks 1-2, 5-6, 9-10, 13-14, 17-18, 21-22, and 25-26
- Gemtuzumab ozogamicin 6 or 9 mg/m² may be given monthly until achievement of complete molecular remission

### ATRA + Idarubicin + Arsenic Trioxide [p.23-24]

**Induction** [p.23-24]:
- ATRA 45 mg/m² in 2 divided doses daily
- Idarubicin 12 mg/m² on days 2, 4, 6
- Arsenic trioxide 0.15 mg/kg IV daily on days 1-27

**Consolidation** [p.23-24]:
- Multiple cycles combining ATRA, idarubicin, and arsenic trioxide with specific schedule per protocol

## Special Patient Populations

### Dosing Considerations [p.22]

**Pediatric and Adolescent Patients** [p.22]:
- Lower doses of ATRA (25 mg/m²) in divided doses until clinical remission may be used in children and adolescents (Kutny MA, et al. J Clin Oncol 2017;35:3021-3029)

**Obese Patients** [p.22]:
- Optimal dosing of arsenic trioxide for patients with obesity is unknown
- Retrospective studies suggest there may be a role for dose capping (Zacholski K, et al. J Oncol Pharm Pract 2022;28:1340-1349; Jen WY, et al. Leuk Lymphoma 2024;65:378-382)

**Cardiac Contraindications** [p.24]:
- See [APL-4](apl-classification-and-treatment-recommendations.md) for alternative regimens in patients with cardiac issues (low EF, prolonged QTcF)

## Supportive Care Integration

### Corticosteroid Prophylaxis [p.22-24]

**Differentiation Syndrome Prevention** [p.22-24]:
- Begin prophylaxis with prednisone during induction and consolidation therapy
- Optimal duration of steroid prophylaxis is unknown
- If differentiation syndrome develops, change to dexamethasone (Lo-Coco F, et al. N Engl J Med 2013;369:111-121)
- See Principles of Supportive Care for APL (APL-A) for additional details

## Additional Therapy Details

### Consolidation Post-Remission Therapy [p.22-24]

**Maintenance Therapy**:
- Some regimens include maintenance ATRA therapy
- Duration varies by protocol (typically 6-36 months)
- Continuation until molecular remission or per specific protocol guidelines

### Relapse Therapy Options [p.24-28]

**First Relapse After ATRA + Arsenic Trioxide** [p.24-25]:
- Repeat arsenic trioxide-based salvage therapy
- Chemotherapy-based regimens as alternative
- Gemtuzumab ozogamicin consideration

**First Relapse After Chemotherapy-Based Induction** [p.25-26]:
- Arsenic trioxide-based regimens (may be novel exposure)
- Combination with ATRA and/or gemtuzumab ozogamicin
- Dose modifications based on prior exposure

## Category Classifications

**Note**: All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.22-28]

Category 1 regimens for low-risk APL induction:
- ATRA + daily arsenic trioxide [p.22]
- ATRA + intermittent arsenic trioxide [p.22]

Category 1 regimens for high-risk APL induction:
- ATRA + idarubicin [p.22]

---

**Clinical Implementation Notes**:

1. **Regimen Selection**: Choose ONE induction and ONE consolidation regimen; do not mix regimens from different trials [p.22-28]

2. **Dosing Adjustments**: Dose modifications may be required for renal impairment, hepatic dysfunction, or significant toxicity [p.22-28]

3. **Drug Administration**: ATRA should be taken with food for optimal absorption [p.22-28]

4. **Arsenic Trioxide Preparation**: Must be reconstituted and administered per manufacturer guidelines [p.22-28]

5. **Monitoring**: Baseline and periodic laboratory assessments required (see APL-A and clinical protocols) [p.22-28]
