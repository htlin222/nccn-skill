## General Principles

**Category:** All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.81]

### Disease Characteristics [p.81]

- BPDCN is a disorder of immature dendritic cells that regulate effector T-cell function [p.81]
- It constitutes only 0.44% of hematologic malignancies and <1% of acute leukemia presentations [p.81]
- It occurs in all races and geographic areas [p.81]

### Epidemiology [p.81]

- More common in adults (median age, 65–67 years) [p.81]
- Approximate male-to-female ratio of 3:1 [p.81]

### Clinical Presentation [p.81]

Most commonly presents as: [p.81]

- Asymptomatic skin lesions [p.81]
- Cytopenias [p.81]
- Circulating peripheral blasts (leukemic phase) [p.81]
- Lymphadenopathy [p.81]
- CNS manifestations [p.81]

### Diagnosis [p.81]

- Can be extremely challenging and whenever possible should involve an experienced hematopathologist [p.81]
- Close collaboration with dermatology is recommended. For guidance on classification and measurement of skin lesions, see page MFSS-3 in the NCCN Guidelines for Primary Cutaneous Lymphomas [p.81]

## Prognosis

### Overall Survival [p.81]

- Prognosis for BPDCN is poor [p.81]
- Median OS is approximately 8–12 months when patients are treated with chemotherapy without consolidative allogeneic HCT [p.81]

### HCT-Based Outcomes [p.81]

- Studies suggest that being in first remission during receipt of allogeneic HCT significantly enhances the median OS [p.81]
- Reduced-intensity conditioning may be considered in patients whose disease achieves CR but cannot tolerate myeloablative HCT [p.81]

## Treatment Principles

### Treatment Options by Fitness Status [p.81]

**For patients who are fit:** [p.81]

Current treatment options for BPDCN include:
- Tagraxofusp-erzs [p.81]
- Chemotherapy [p.81]

**For those with low albumin and/or comorbidities:** [p.81]

Should receive non-intensive regimen options as shown in the algorithm --> See [bpdcn-treatment.md](bpdcn-treatment.md) [p.81]

### Important Safety Considerations [p.81]

**Tagraxofusp-erzs Adverse Events:** [p.81]

- Hypoalbuminemia and capillary leak syndrome are known, potentially serious adverse events associated with tagraxofusp-erzs treatment [p.81]
