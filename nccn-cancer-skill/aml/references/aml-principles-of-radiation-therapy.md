## General Principles

Patients who present with isolated extramedullary disease (myeloid sarcoma) or leukemia cutis should be treated with systemic therapy. [p.44]

Local therapy (RT or surgery [rare cases]) may be used for residual disease or for symptomatic disease. [p.44]

## General Treatment Information

### CNS Leukemia

- **Radiation therapy (RT)** followed by **IT chemotherapy** 2x/week until clear, then weekly x 4-6 weeks [p.44]

#### Important Safety Considerations

- **Concurrent use of CNS RT with doses of cytarabine ≥2 g/m² or IT methotrexate may increase risk of neurotoxicity.** [p.44]

- Induction chemotherapy should be started concurrently. However, for patients receiving doses of cytarabine ≥2 g/m², since this agent crosses the blood-brain barrier, IT therapy can be deferred until induction is completed. IT chemotherapy may consist of methotrexate, cytarabine, or a combination of these agents. [p.44]

- Further CNS prophylaxis per institutional practice. [p.44]
