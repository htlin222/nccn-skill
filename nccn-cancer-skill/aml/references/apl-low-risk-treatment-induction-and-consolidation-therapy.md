## Induction Phase

For patients with low-risk APL (WBC count ≤10 x 10^9/L)

### Preferred Regimens

All preferred regimens are (Category 1) [p.15]

**1. ATRA + Daily Arsenic Trioxide** [p.15]
- ATRA dosing per protocol
- Arsenic trioxide daily dosing per protocol
- Footnotes: f, g, 1
- Include prophylaxis with prednisone; change to dexamethasone if differentiation syndrome develops [p.15]

**2. ATRA + Intermittent Arsenic Trioxide** [p.15]
- ATRA dosing per protocol
- Arsenic trioxide intermittent dosing per protocol
- Footnotes: f, g, 2
- Include prophylaxis with prednisone; change to dexamethasone if differentiation syndrome develops [p.15]

**3. ATRA + Idarubicin** [p.15]
- ATRA dosing per protocol
- Idarubicin dosing per protocol
- Footnotes: f, g, 3
- Include prophylaxis with prednisone; change to dexamethasone if differentiation syndrome develops [p.15]

### Useful in Certain Circumstances

**ATRA + Gemtuzumab Ozogamicin** (if arsenic is not available or contraindicated) [p.15]
- ATRA dosing per protocol
- Gemtuzumab ozogamicin dosing per protocol
- Footnote: f, 4

## Induction Response Assessment

### Blood Count Recovery Assessment (Day 28)

Evaluate if blood count recovery has been achieved by day 28:
- Platelet count >100 x 10^9/L
- Absolute neutrophil count (ANC) >1 x 10^9/L [p.15]

**If yes:** Proceed with consolidation [p.15]

**Assessment option:** BM aspirate and biopsy may be considered to document <5% blasts and no abnormal promyelocytes [p.15]

### Bone Marrow Assessment (Days 28-35)

BM aspirate and biopsy performed on days 28–35 to document:
- <5% blasts
- No abnormal promyelocytes [p.15]

Proceed with consolidation only after documented morphologic response [p.15]

## Important Clinical Notes

### Community Center Treatment

For patients with APL being treated at a community center, collaboration with a center with expertise has been shown to reduce induction mortality (Jillella AP, et al. JAMA Oncol 2025;11:400-407) [p.15]

### Supportive Care

Refer to Principles of Supportive Care for APL --> See APL-A [p.15]

### Regimen Consistency

Several groups have published large trials with excellent outcomes. However, to achieve the expected results, one needs to use the regimen consistently through all components and not mix induction from one trial with consolidation from another [p.15]

### Mortality and Morbidity

Early mortality is related to bleeding, differentiation syndrome, or infection. Persistent disease is rare [p.15]

## Footnote Details

**Footnote f:** Data suggest that lower doses of ATRA (25 mg/m2) in divided doses until clinical remission may be used in children and adolescents (Kutny MA, et al. J Clin Oncol 2017;35:3021-3029) [p.15]

**Footnote g:** Begin prophylaxis with prednisone; the optimal duration of steroid prophylaxis is unknown. If differentiation syndrome develops, change to dexamethasone (Lo-Coco F, et al. N Engl J Med 2013;369:111-121). See Principles of Supportive Care for APL (APL-A) [p.15]

**Footnote h:** If no evidence of morphologic disease (<5% blasts and no abnormal promyelocytes), discontinue ATRA and arsenic trioxide to allow for peripheral blood recovery since arsenic trioxide can be associated with significant myelosuppression. If evidence of morphologic disease, continue ATRA and arsenic trioxide and repeat BM 1 week later [p.15]

**Footnote i:** The presence of measurable cytogenetic and molecular markers post-induction does not carry prognostic or therapeutic implications [p.15]

**Footnote j:** If full course of induction treatment is not given, or counts have not recovered by days 28–35, a BM aspirate and biopsy is recommended to document <5% blasts and no abnormal promyelocytes before proceeding with consolidation [p.15]

**Footnote k:** For regimens using high cumulative doses of cardiotoxic agents, consider reassessing cardiac function prior to each anthracycline/mitoxantrone-containing course [p.15]
