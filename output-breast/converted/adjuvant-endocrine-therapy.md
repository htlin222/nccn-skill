## Overview

**Default Category:** All recommendations are (Category 2A) unless otherwise indicated [p.67, p.68]

**Key Reference:** Definition of Menopause (BINV-O) [p.67, p.68]

---

## Premenopausal Patients at Diagnosis (BINV-K 3 of 4)

See BINV-K 4 of 4 if postmenopausal at diagnosis [p.67]

### First-Line Endocrine Therapy Options

**Option 1: Tamoxifen-Based**

- **Tamoxifen for 5 years** (Category 1) [p.67]
- **± Ovarian suppression or ablation** (Category 1) [p.67]
  - A balanced discussion of risks and benefits associated with ovarian suppression therapy is critical, including potential side effects of premature menopause [p.67]
  - Surgical or radiation ovarian ablation in premenopausal patients with HR-positive breast cancer shows similar magnitude of benefit to CMF alone [p.67]
  - GnRH agonists can be administered before or with chemotherapy, especially if fertility preservation is a goal, or initiated after chemotherapy in patients who remain premenopausal [p.67]
- **Consider adjuvant abemaciclib in eligible patients** [p.67]

**Option 2: Aromatase Inhibitor-Based**

- **Aromatase inhibitor for 5 years** (Category 1) [p.67]
- **+ Ovarian suppression or ablation** (Category 1) [p.67]
  - A balanced discussion of risks and benefits associated with ovarian suppression therapy is critical, including potential side effects of premature menopause [p.67]
  - Surgical or radiation ovarian ablation shows similar magnitude of benefit to CMF alone [p.67]
- **Consider adjuvant abemaciclib or ribociclib in eligible patients** [p.67]

### CDK4/6 Inhibitor Considerations

Aromatase inhibitor or tamoxifen for 5 years plus ovarian suppression should be considered, based on SOFT and TEXT clinical trial outcomes, for premenopausal patients at higher risk of recurrence (i.e., young age/longevity, high-grade tumor, lymph node involvement) [p.67]

**For eligible patients with HR-positive/HER2-negative breast cancer:** [p.67-68]

1. **High-Risk Disease** (≥4 positive lymph nodes [confirmed preoperatively and/or at surgery], OR 1–3 positive lymph nodes with either grade 3 disease or tumor size ≥5 cm [on preoperative imaging and/or at surgery]):
   - **2 years of adjuvant abemaciclib** in combination with endocrine therapy (Category 1, preferred) [p.67-68]
   - VTE risk should be considered when combining abemaciclib with tamoxifen [p.67-68]

2. **Any Lymph Node Involvement** (excluding microscopic nodal involvement), OR if no nodal involvement either tumor size >5 cm, OR if tumor size 2–5 cm with either grade 2 (and high genomic risk or Ki-67 ≥20%) or grade 3:
   - **3 years of ribociclib with aromatase inhibitor** may be considered (Category 1, preferred) [p.67-68]

**Important Limitations:** Adjuvant CDK4/6 inhibitors have been studied in patients at high risk who mostly received adjuvant/neoadjuvant chemotherapy; there are limited data in those who did not receive chemotherapy [p.67-68]

**Uncertain Sequencing:** In patients eligible for adjuvant olaparib (see BINV-L) and abemaciclib or ribociclib, the benefit and optimal sequence is not known [p.67-68]

## Postmenopausal Patients at Diagnosis (BINV-K 4 of 4)

See BINV-K 3 of 4 if premenopausal at diagnosis [p.68]

### First-Line Endocrine Therapy

**Option 1: Aromatase Inhibitor (5 years)**

- **Aromatase inhibitor for 5 years** (Category 1) [p.68]
- **Consider adjuvant abemaciclib or ribociclib in eligible patients** [p.68]

**Option 2: Aromatase Inhibitor (2–3 years)**

- **Aromatase inhibitor for 2–3 years** (Category 1) [p.68]
- **Consider adjuvant abemaciclib or ribociclib in eligible patients** [p.68]

**Option 3: Tamoxifen**

- **Tamoxifen for 2–3 years** [p.68]
- **OR Tamoxifen for 4.5–6 years** [p.68]
- For patients with a contraindication to aromatase inhibitors, who decline aromatase inhibitors, or who are intolerant to aromatase inhibitors [p.68]

### Extended Adjuvant Endocrine Therapy

**After initial aromatase inhibitor therapy:**

- **Aromatase inhibitor to complete 5 years** of endocrine therapy (Category 1) [p.68]
- **OR Up to 5 years of an aromatase inhibitor** (Category 2B) [p.68]

**After initial aromatase inhibitor therapy (alternative):**

- **Aromatase inhibitor for 5 years** (Category 1) [p.68]
- **OR Consider tamoxifen for an additional 5 years to complete 10 years** [p.68]

**After initial tamoxifen therapy:**

- **Tamoxifen for 5 years** (Category 1) [p.68]
- **OR Consider tamoxifen for up to 10 years** [p.68]

**After initial tamoxifen (alternative):**

- **Tamoxifen to complete 5 years** of endocrine therapy (Category 1) [p.68]
- **Consider aromatase inhibitor for an additional 3–5 years** [p.68]

## Aromatase Inhibitor Details

The three selective aromatase inhibitors (anastrozole, exemestane, letrozole) have shown similar anti-tumor efficacy and toxicity profiles in randomized studies in the adjuvant and preoperative settings [p.67-68]

**Optimal Duration:** The optimal duration of aromatase inhibitors in adjuvant therapy is uncertain. Patients with lymph node involvement may benefit from extended aromatase inhibitor duration (7.5–10 years total) [p.67-68]
