# Systemic Therapy Regimens for Recurrent Unresectable or Stage IV (M1) Disease (BINV-Q)

<!-- Source: NCCN Breast Cancer v2.2026, pages 91-94 -->
<!-- Chunk: systemic-therapy-regimens-for-recurrent-unresectable-or-stage-iv-m1-disease-algorithm -->

## Overview

All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.91].

> According to the 5th ESO-ESMO international consensus guidelines (Cardoso F, et al. Ann Oncol 2020;31:1623-1649) for advanced breast cancer, visceral crisis is defined as: "severe organ dysfunction, as assessed by signs and symptoms, laboratory studies and rapid progression of disease. Visceral crisis is not the mere presence of visceral metastases but implies important organ compromise leading to a clinical indication for the most rapidly efficacious therapy." [p.91]

---

## HR-Positive and HER2-Negative with Visceral Crisis or Endocrine Refractory

See BINV-Q 1 of 15 for Considerations for Systemic Therapy [p.91].

### First Line

**No germline BRCA1/2 PV and/or HER2 (ERBB2) IHC 0+, 1+, or 2+/ISH negative** [p.91]

**Preferred:**
- Systemic chemotherapy (Category 1) [p.91] --> See BINV-Q 5 of 15
- **Other Recommended:**
- Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki [p.91]

**Note:** Chemotherapy (e.g., oral chemotherapy) is generally preferred in the first-line setting. Selection of chemotherapy versus fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki for first-line therapy should be individualized based on clinical features and patient preference. [p.91]

**Germline BRCA1/2 PV** [p.91]

**Preferred:**
- PARPi (Olaparib or Talazoparib) (Category 1) [p.91]

**Important Note:** Assess for germline BRCA1/2 PVs in all patients with recurrent or metastatic breast cancer to identify candidates for PARP inhibitor (PARPi) therapy. PARPi can be considered for a later line for those with germline BRCA1/2 PV; however, available evidence suggests it is more effective if used earlier. [p.91]

### Second Line

**HER2 (ERBB2) IHC 1+ or 2+/ISH negative** [p.91]

**Preferred:**
- Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki (Category 1) [p.91]

**Important Note:** Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki may be used in those previously treated with at least one line of endocrine-based therapy in the metastatic setting. Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki is associated with interstitial lung disease (ILD)/pneumonitis. Regular monitoring for this serious side effect is recommended. For patients with a history of ILD/pneumonitis, there are no data on managing safety or toxicity of this drug in a trial. [p.91]

**HER2 (ERBB2) IHC 0+** [p.91]

**Other Recommended:**
- Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki [p.91]

### Not a Candidate for Fam-trastuzumab Deruxtecan-nxki

**Preferred:**
- Sacituzumab govitecan-hziy (Category 1) [p.91]

**Other Options:**
- Systemic chemotherapy --> See BINV-Q 5 of 15 [p.91]
- Targeted therapy --> See BINV-Q 6 of 15 and BINV-Q 7 of 15 [p.91]

**For HER2 (ERBB2) IHC 0, 1+, or 2+/ISH negative:**

**Other Recommended:**
- Datopotamab deruxtecan-dlnk [p.91]

**Important Note:** Sacituzumab govitecan-hziy may be used after prior treatment including endocrine therapy, a CDK4/6 inhibitor, and at least two lines of chemotherapy, one of which was a taxane, and at least one of which was in the metastatic setting. It may be considered for later line if not used as second-line therapy. [p.91]

Datopotamab deruxtecan-dlnk is indicated as second- or subsequent-line therapy for those who have received a prior endocrine-based therapy and chemotherapy for unresectable or metastatic disease. Datopotamab deruxtecan-dlnk did not meet the OS endpoint in the TROPION-Breast01 trial. Whereas the previously approved ADCs, fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki and sacituzumab govitecan-hziy have shown a benefit in OS in randomized phase III trials. The benefit of using datopotamab deruxtecan-dlnk in patients with prior ADC treatment are not known as the TROPION-Breast01 trial did not include patients with prior ADC treatment. [p.91]

### Third Line and Beyond

**Any**

**Options:**
- Systemic chemotherapy --> See BINV-Q 5 of 15 [p.91]
- Biomarker positive (ie, MSI-H, NTRK1/2/3 and RET gene fusions, TMB-H): Targeted agents and emerging biomarker options --> See BINV-Q 6 of 15, BINV-Q 7 of 15, and BINV-Q 8 of 15 [p.91]

**Note:** See Principles of HER2 Testing (BINV-A). The distinction between HER2 (ERBB2) test results of IHC 0/absent membrane staining, IHC 0+/with membrane staining (faint, partial membrane staining in ≤10%), IHC 1+, or 2+/ISH negative is currently clinically relevant for therapy selection. [p.91]

## HR-Negative and HER2-Negative (Triple-Negative Breast Cancer; TNBC)

See BINV-Q 1 of 15 for Considerations for systemic therapy [p.92].

### First Line

**PD-L1 CPS ≥10 regardless of germline BRCA1/2 PV status** [p.92]

**Preferred (Category 1):**
- Chemotherapy (Albumin-bound Paclitaxel, Carboplatin/Gemcitabine, or Paclitaxel) + Pembrolizumab [p.92]
- Sacituzumab govitecan-hziy + Pembrolizumab [p.92]

**PD-L1 CPS <10 and no germline BRCA1/2 PV** [p.92]

**Preferred (Category 1):**
- Sacituzumab govitecan-hziy [p.92]
- Datopotamab deruxtecan-dlnk [p.92]

**Other Options:**
- Systemic chemotherapy --> See BINV-Q 5 of 15 [p.92]

**PD-L1 CPS <10 and germline BRCA1/2 PV** [p.92]

**Preferred (Category 1):**
- PARPi (Olaparib or Talazoparib) [p.92]
- Platinum (Carboplatin or Cisplatin) [p.92]

**Important Notes:**

Programmed death ligand 1 (PD-L1) expression is assessed using 22C3 antibody. Threshold for positivity combined positive score (CPS) ≥10. [p.92]

While available data are in the first-line setting, chemotherapy + pembrolizumab regimen can be used for second and subsequent lines of therapy if programmed cell death protein 1 (PD-1)/PD-L1 inhibitor therapy has not been previously used. If there is disease progression while on a PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitor, there are no data to support an additional line of therapy with another PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitor. [p.92]

See NCCN Guidelines for Hematopoietic Growth Factors. [p.92]

Assess for germline BRCA1/2 PVs in all patients with recurrent or metastatic breast cancer to identify candidates for PARPi therapy. [p.92]

### Second Line

**Germline BRCA1/2 PV** [p.92]

**Preferred (Category 1):**
- PARPi [p.92]

**Any** [p.92]

**Preferred (Category 1):**
- Sacituzumab govitecan-hziy [p.92]

**Other Options:**
- Systemic chemotherapy --> See BINV-Q 5 of 15 or targeted agents --> See BINV-Q 7 of 15 [p.92]

**No germline BRCA1/2 PV and HER2 (ERBB2) IHC 1+ or 2+/ISH negative** [p.92]

**Other Recommended:**
- Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki [p.92]

**Important Note:** Sacituzumab govitecan-hziy may be considered for later line if not used as second-line therapy. [p.92]

Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan may be considered in a later line for HER2 (ERBB2) IHC 1+ or 2+/ISH negative, if not used in second line or may be considered first-line therapy when disease has progressed during or within 6 months after completing adjuvant chemotherapy. Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki is associated with ILD/pneumonitis. Regular monitoring for this serious side effect is recommended. For patients with a history of ILD/pneumonitis, there are no data on managing safety or toxicity of this drug in a trial. [p.92]

### Third Line and Beyond

**Biomarker positive (ie, MSI-H, NTRK1/2/3 and RET gene fusions, TMB-H)** [p.92]

**Options:**
- Targeted agents and emerging biomarker options --> See BINV-Q 7 of 15 and BINV-Q 8 of 15 [p.92]

**Any** [p.92]

**Options:**
- Systemic chemotherapy --> See BINV-Q 5 of 15 [p.92]

**Note:** For treatment of brain metastases, see NCCN Guidelines for Central Nervous System Cancers. [p.92]

## HR-Positive or -Negative and HER2-Positive

See BINV-Q 1 of 15 for Considerations for systemic HER2-targeted therapy [p.93].

### First Line

**Preferred (Category 1):**
- Docetaxel + Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab with maintenance Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab [p.93]
  - If HR-positive: Aromatase inhibitor ± Palbociclib + Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab [p.93]
- Paclitaxel + Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab with maintenance Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab [p.93]
  - If HR-positive: Aromatase inhibitor ± Palbociclib + Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab [p.93]

**Other Recommended:**
- Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki + Pertuzumab [p.93]

### Second Line / Third Line

**Preferred (Category 1):**
- Capecitabine/Tucatinib + Trastuzumab [p.93]
- Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki [p.93]

**Other Options:**
- T-DM1 [p.93]

**Important Notes:**

Capecitabine/tucatinib + trastuzumab is preferred in patients with both systemic and CNS progression in the third-line setting and beyond; and it may be given in the second-line setting. [p.93]

Single-agent fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki may be considered in the first-line setting as an option for select patients (ie, those with rapid progression within 6 months of neoadjuvant or adjuvant therapy [12 months for pertuzumab-containing regimens]). [p.93]

May be used as an option for third-line and beyond; the optimal sequence for third-line therapy and beyond is not known. If not a candidate for fam-trastuzumab, T-DM1 could be considered in the second-line. [p.93]

Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki is associated with ILD/pneumonitis. Regular monitoring for this serious side effect is recommended. For patients with a history of ILD/pneumonitis, there are no data on managing safety or toxicity of this drug in a trial. [p.93]

### Fourth Line and Beyond

**Note:** Optimal sequence is not known [p.93]

**Options:**
- Docetaxel or Vinorelbine + Trastuzumab [p.93]
- Paclitaxel + Trastuzumab ± Carboplatin [p.93]
- Capecitabine/Lapatinib or Capecitabine + Trastuzumab [p.93]
- Lapatinib + Trastuzumab (without cytotoxic therapy) [p.93]
- Other chemotherapy agents + Trastuzumab [p.93]
- Capecitabine/Neratinib [p.93]
- Chemotherapy (Capecitabine, Eribulin, Gemcitabine, or Vinorelbine) + Margetuximab-cmkb [p.93]
- Abemaciclib/Fulvestrant + Trastuzumab (for HR+ only) (Category 2B) [p.93]
- Targeted Therapy and emerging biomarker Options --> See BINV-Q 7 of 15 and BINV-Q 8 of 15 [p.93]

**Important Note:** Multiple lines of concurrent chemotherapy with anti-HER2 therapy (trastuzumab or a TKI) offer clinical benefit for recurrent unresectable HER2+ metastatic breast cancer and have been studied in phase 2 or 3 trials. Clinical experience suggests frequent clinical benefit for such treatment. However, there are no meaningful data for use of any of these regimens among patients previously treated with pertuzumab-based chemotherapy, ado-trastuzumab emtansine, fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki, or capecitabine/tucatinib + trastuzumab regimens. Thus, the optimal sequence or true benefit of therapy is not known. [p.93]

Trastuzumab given in combination with an anthracycline is associated with significant cardiac toxicity. Concurrent use of trastuzumab and pertuzumab with an anthracycline should be avoided. Trastuzumab may be safely combined with all non-anthracycline–containing preferred and other single agents listed for recurrent or metastatic breast cancer. [p.93]

**BRCA1/2 Pathogenic Variant Note:** Assess for germline BRCA1/2 PVs in all patients with recurrent or metastatic breast cancer to identify candidates for PARPi therapy. While olaparib and talazoparib are FDA-indicated in HER2-negative disease, the Panel supports use in any breast cancer subtype associated with a germline PV. There is lower-level evidence for HER2-positive tumors, therefore Category 2A for this setting. [p.93]

## Cytotoxic Regimens for HR-Positive or -Negative and HER2-Negative

See BINV-Q 1 of 15 for Considerations for systemic chemotherapy [p.94].

**Sequential single agents are preferred, but chemotherapy combinations may be used in select patients with high tumor burden, rapidly progressing disease, and visceral crisis.** [p.94]

### Preferred

- **Anthracyclines:**
  - Doxorubicin [p.94]
  - Liposomal Doxorubicin [p.94]
- **Taxanes:**
  - Paclitaxel [p.94]
- **Anti-metabolites:**
  - Capecitabine [p.94]
  - Gemcitabine [p.94]
- **Microtubule inhibitors:**
  - Eribulin [p.94]
  - Vinorelbine [p.94]

### Other Recommended

- Albumin-bound Paclitaxel [p.94]
- Cyclophosphamide [p.94]
- Docetaxel [p.94]
- Epirubicin [p.94]
- Ixabepilone [p.94]

### Useful in Certain Circumstances

- AC [p.94]
- EC [p.94]
- CMF [p.94]
- Capecitabine/Docetaxel [p.94]
- GT (Gemcitabine/Paclitaxel) [p.94]
- Carboplatin/Gemcitabine [p.94]
- Carboplatin + Albumin-bound Paclitaxel or Paclitaxel [p.94]
