# Invasive Breast Cancer -- Evidence Summary

<!-- Source: NCCN Breast Cancer v2.2026, pages 148-152 -->
<!-- Chunk: invasive-breast-cancer-manuscript-part2 -->

## Summary of Key Evidence

- Regional nodal irradiation (RNI) at 45–50.4 Gy in 25–28 fractions reduces locoregional and distant recurrence with improved disease-free survival (DFS), supported by MA.20 and EORTC 22922/10925 trials [p.148-150]
- Accelerated partial breast irradiation (APBI) shows equivalent 10-year local control rates to whole breast radiation therapy (WBRT) in selected early-stage patients (NSABP B-39: IBTR 4.6% vs 3.9%, HR 1.22, 90% CI 0.94–1.58), with comparable or improved cosmetic outcomes [p.148-149]
- Post-mastectomy radiation therapy (PMRT) to chest wall is recommended for node-positive disease (category 1) with evidence from EBCTCG meta-analyses showing reduced recurrence and breast cancer mortality [p.150]
- Inclusion of internal mammary nodes in RNI should consider patient-specific risks vs. benefits; Danish trial demonstrated 15-year OS benefit (60.1% vs 55.4%) with internal mammary RT in right-sided tumors [p.149]
- For older adults (≥70 years) with ER-positive stage I disease, BCS with tamoxifen alone is acceptable (category 1) with locoregional recurrence rates of 4% without RT versus 1% with RT [p.150]

## First-Line Therapy Evidence

### Radiation Therapy After Breast-Conserving Surgery (BCS)

**Whole Breast Radiation Therapy (WBRT) - Node-Positive Disease**

The EBCTCG meta-analysis provides strong support for WBRT in node-positive disease, showing reduction in 10-year recurrence risk from 35% to 19% (RR 0.52, 95% CI 0.48–0.56) and significant reduction in 15-year breast cancer death (25% vs 21%, RR 0.82, 95% CI 0.75–0.90). [p.148-149] NCCN recommendation: WBRT strongly recommended with or without boost to tumor bed for node-positive disease (category 1). For patients with negative axillary nodes, WBRT is category 2A. [p.148]

**Accelerated Partial Breast Irradiation (APBI)**

NSABP B-39 trial (10-year follow-up): 4.6% cumulative incidence of ipsilateral breast tumor recurrence (IBTR) with APBI versus 3.9% with WBRT (HR 1.22, 90% CI 0.94–1.58). The 0.7% absolute difference did not meet prespecified equivalence criteria but is unlikely to be clinically significant in appropriately selected patients. [p.148] Other trials showed comparable or favorable toxicity/cosmetic outcomes: IMPORT-LOW study found less breast firmness and lower adverse event rates with partial breast irradiation (40 Gy in 15 fractions). [p.148] University of Florence trial (intensity-modulated APBI, 30 Gy in 5 fractions every other day) demonstrated less acute/late toxicity and better cosmetic outcomes at 10 years. [p.148] However, RAPID trial reported significantly higher fair/poor cosmetic outcome rates with 3D conformal APBI (38.5 Gy in 10 twice-daily fractions). [p.148-149]

NCCN recommendation for APBI: Panel accepts ASTRO 2016 consensus statement. APBI recommended for BRCA-negative patients meeting ASTRO "suitable" criteria—age >50 years, ER-positive invasive ductal carcinoma ≤2 cm (pT1), negative margins ≥2 mm, no lymphovascular invasion (LVI). APBI also permitted in patients >50 years with screen-detected low/intermediate-grade DCIS ≤2.5 cm resected with ≥3 mm margins. [p.149] Preferred regimen: University of Florence protocol (30 Gy/5 fractions every other day, IMRT). [p.149]

### Regional Nodal Irradiation After BCS

**For Node-Negative Disease:**

NCCN Panel recommends consideration of comprehensive RNI in patients with: central/medial tumors (EORTC 22922 trial criteria), T2 tumors with limited axillary dissection (<10 lymph nodes) plus at least one additional risk factor (high-grade histology, ER-negative disease, or LVI), or by MA.20 criteria. [p.149]

**For Node-Positive Disease (1–3 Positive Nodes):**

Use of comprehensive RNI with or without intentional axillary inclusion is discretionary if patient meets all criteria: cT1–T2 cN0, no preoperative chemotherapy, 1–2 positive sentinel lymph nodes (SLNs). [p.149] If criteria not met, NCCN recommends WBRT with inclusion of any undissected axilla (category 1) with strong consideration of comprehensive RNI. [p.149]

**For Node-Positive Disease (≥4 Positive Nodes):**

Comprehensive RNI with inclusion of any undissected axilla (category 1). [p.149]

**Internal Mammary Nodes Controversy:**

KROG 08-06 trial randomized node-positive patients to RNI with vs. without internal mammary RT; no significant DFS benefit overall, but statistically significant benefit in medially/centrally located tumors. [p.149] Danish Breast Cancer Cooperative Group 15-year follow-up (n=3,089 node-positive patients): 15-year OS 60.1% with internal mammary RT (right-sided) vs. 55.4% without (left-sided); improved distant recurrence rates and breast cancer-specific mortality with internal mammary RT. [p.149]

NCCN recommendation: Panel no longer specifies RNI field composition, now using term "comprehensive RNI." Inclusion of internal mammary nodes requires clinical judgment weighing risks (cardiac/lung toxicity) vs. benefits, considering comorbidities, age, life expectancy. Meticulous treatment planning with normal tissue dose constraints mandatory if internal mammary nodes included. [p.149]

### Radiation Therapy After BCS in Older Adults (≥70 years) with ER-Positive Tumors

Study in stage I ER-positive patients ≥70 years randomized to BCS+WBRT+tamoxifen vs. BCS+tamoxifen: locoregional recurrence rates 1% vs. 4%, respectively, with no difference in OS, DFS, or mastectomy rates. [p.150] Updated analysis at 12.6-year median follow-up: statistically significant reduction in IBTR at 10 years with RT (90% vs. 98% free of IBTR). [p.150] Results confirmed in other similar studies. [p.150]

NCCN recommendation: BCS (negative margin required) with 5 years tamoxifen or aromatase inhibitor (AI), without breast irradiation, acceptable for patients >70 years with clinically negative lymph nodes and ER-positive T1 tumors (category 1). [p.150] Individual discussion of RT risks/benefits and patient commitment to 5-year endocrine therapy recommended if RT omission considered. [p.150]

## Second-Line and Subsequent Therapy Evidence

### Radiation Therapy After Mastectomy

**Post-Mastectomy RT for Node-Positive Disease**

Randomized clinical trials demonstrate DFS and OS advantage from irradiation of chest wall and regional lymph nodes in node-positive disease. [p.150] EBCTCG meta-analyses show post-mastectomy RT after axillary lymph node dissection (ALND) reduced recurrence and breast cancer mortality in patients with 1–3 positive nodes even with systemic therapy. [p.150]

NCCN recommendation: Post-mastectomy radiation to chest wall recommended in all node-positive patients (category 1). [p.150] EORTC 22922/10925 trial supports inclusion of comprehensive RNI (including any undissected axilla) in post-mastectomy radiation, with RNI recommended for 1 or more positive nodes (category 1). [p.150]

**Post-Mastectomy RT for Node-Negative Disease**

In node-negative patients with tumor ≤5 cm and clear margins (≥1 mm), PMRT typically not recommended. [p.150] Panel notes PMRT may be considered in high-risk subsets based on RNI trial inclusion criteria (MA.20, EORTC 22922): central/medial tumors, T3 tumors, or tumors ≥2 cm with <10 axillary nodes removed and at least one additional risk factor (grade 3, ER-negative, or LVI). [p.150-151]

Features predicting high local recurrence rates: primary tumor >5 cm or positive pathologic margins. [p.151] For positive pathologic margin without re-resection possibility: Panel recommends strongly considering chest wall irradiation with comprehensive RNI including any axilla at risk. [p.151] For tumors >5 cm: chest wall irradiation considered with comprehensive RNI. [p.151] For tumors ≤5 cm with margins ≤1 mm: chest wall irradiation considered with comprehensive RNI only if high-risk features present. [p.151]

### Radiation Therapy After Preoperative Systemic Therapy

**General Principle:**

Adjuvant RT decisions should be based on maximal stage at diagnosis (before preoperative therapy) and pathologic stage at definitive surgery. [p.151] Data from stage III patients show postoperative RT improves local control even for pathologic complete response (pCR) to neoadjuvant chemotherapy. [p.151]

**RT After Preoperative Therapy and BCS:**

Patients with clinically negative nodes at diagnosis, remaining pathologically node-negative post-therapy: should receive adjuvant RT to whole breast with boost to tumor bed after SLNB. [p.151]

Patients with clinically/radiographically positive nodes at diagnosis converting to clinically/radiographically node-negative after preoperative chemotherapy: candidates for NSABP B-51 trial assessing RNI benefit. [p.151] Existing data suggest node-positive disease at presentation requires comprehensive RNI with any undissected axilla included. [p.151]

Patients with clinically/radiographically positive nodes converting to clinically/radiographically negative but with persistent nodal disease on SLNB: candidates for ALLIANCE 11202 trial assessing whether ALND can be replaced with axillary RT. [p.151] Standard trial arm is ALND; if neoadjuvant patient with node-positive disease (ypN1+) does not undergo complete axillary dissection, all undissected axilla levels included with RT. [p.151]

**RT After Preoperative Therapy and Mastectomy:**

Patients with clinically positive nodes at diagnosis responding to preoperative therapy and becoming node-negative: should be strongly considered for RT to chest wall and comprehensive RNI with any undissected axilla. [p.151]

Patients with positive nodes (ypN1+) after preoperative therapy: axillary dissection is standard arm of ongoing ALLIANCE 11202 trial. [p.151] If RT indicated, should include chest wall with comprehensive RNI and any undissected axilla. [p.151]

Patients with node-negative disease at diagnosis and after preoperative therapy with axilla assessed by SLNB or ALND: may forego RT. [p.151]

Ongoing prospective trials evaluating RT benefit in neoadjuvant patients: NSABP B-51/RTOG 1304 (NCT01872975) and ALLIANCE A011202/MAC19 (NCT01901094). [p.151]

## Special Considerations

### Sequencing of Radiation and Systemic Therapy

**Chemotherapy and Radiation Sequencing:**

When both chemotherapy and radiation indicated post-surgery, adjuvant radiation typically delivered after completion of chemotherapy. [p.151] Upfront-Outback trial: patients who underwent BCS and axillary dissection randomly assigned to chemotherapy post-RT vs. RT post-chemotherapy showed increased local recurrence rate in delayed-RT group at 58-month median follow-up. [p.151-152] However, at 135-month follow-up, differences in distant/local recurrence rates were not statistically significant between arms. [p.152] CMF (cyclophosphamide/methotrexate/fluorouracil) and RT may be given concurrently based on prospective/retrospective studies. [p.152]

**Endocrine Therapy and Radiation:**

Data from multiple studies of endocrine therapy before, during, or after RT show no difference in outcomes or toxicity. [p.152] NCCN recommendation: sequential or concurrent endocrine therapy with RT acceptable. [p.152] However, due to compounding side effects, initiating endocrine therapy at completion of RT may be preferred. [p.152]

**Capecitabine and Radiation:**

When adjuvant capecitabine indicated (known radiosensitizing agent with toxicity risk to normal tissue), should be given after completion of adjuvant RT. [p.152]

**Olaparib and Radiation:**

When adjuvant olaparib used, Panel recommends olaparib given after completion of RT. [p.152] OlympiA trial did not administer olaparib concurrently with RT; limited safety data on concurrent administration. [p.152]

**HER2-Targeted Therapy and Radiation:**

Adjuvant HER2-targeted therapy may be delivered concurrently with RT. [p.152] Clinical trial data in adjuvant setting do not suggest increased complication rate with concurrent HER2-targeted therapies and adjuvant RT. [p.152]

### Regional Nodal Irradiation Dosing and Field Design

Recommended RNI dose: 45–50.4 Gy in 25–28 fractions to regional nodal fields. [p.148] Supplemental RT boost may be delivered to grossly involved/enlarged lymph nodes (internal mammary or clavicular) not surgically addressed. [p.148] NCCN Panel recommends contouring individual nodal basins at risk using various breast atlases to ensure adequate coverage, given anatomic variations across patients. [p.148]
