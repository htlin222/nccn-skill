# Principles of Preoperative Systemic Therapy (BINV-M) -- Algorithm Reference

## Diagnosis

### Candidates for Preoperative Systemic Therapy [p.69]

**Patients with inoperable breast cancer:**
- Inflammatory breast cancer (IBC) [p.69]
- Bulky or matted cN2 axillary nodes [p.69]
- cN3 nodal disease [p.69]
- cT4 tumors [p.69]

**In select patients with operable breast cancer** [p.69]

Preoperative systemic therapy is **preferred** for:
- HER2-positive disease and TNBC, if ≥cT2 or ≥cN1 [p.69]
- Large primary tumor relative to breast size in a patient who desires breast conservation [p.69]
- cN+ disease likely to become cN0 with preoperative systemic therapy [p.69]

Preoperative systemic therapy can be considered for:
- cT1c, cN0 HER2-positive disease and TNBC [p.69]
- Patients in whom definitive surgery may be delayed [p.69]

### Non-Candidates for Preoperative Systemic Therapy [p.69]

- Patients with extensive in situ disease when extent of invasive carcinoma is not well-defined [p.69]
- Patients with a poorly delineated extent of tumor [p.69]
- Patients whose tumors are not palpable or clinically assessable [p.69]

## Workup and Principles

### Known Benefits of Preoperative Systemic Therapy [p.69]

- Facilitates breast conservation [p.69]
- Can render inoperable tumors operable [p.69]
- Treatment response provides important prognostic information at an individual patient level, particularly in patients with TNBC or HER2-positive breast cancer [p.69]
- Identifies patients with residual disease at higher risk for relapse to allow for the addition of supplemental adjuvant regimens, particularly in patients with TNBC or HER2-positive breast cancer [p.69]

### Opportunities [p.69-70]

- May allow SLNB alone if initial cN+ becomes cN0 after preoperative therapy [p.69-70]
- May provide an opportunity to modify systemic treatment if no preoperative therapy response or progression of disease [p.70]
- May allow for more limited radiation fields in patients with cN+ who become cN0/pN0 after preoperative therapy [p.70]
- Provides excellent research platform to test novel therapies and predictive biomarkers [p.70]
- May allow time for genetic testing [p.70]
- May allow time to plan breast reconstruction in patients electing mastectomy [p.70]
- May allow time for delayed decision-making for definitive surgery [p.70]

### Cautions [p.70]

- Possible overtreatment with systemic therapy if clinical stage is overestimated [p.70]
- Possible undertreatment locoregionally with radiotherapy if clinical stage is underestimated [p.70]
- Possibility of disease progression during preoperative systemic therapy [p.70]

## First-Line Therapy

### Pathology Evaluation and Response Assessment [p.70]

- Pathology evaluation of surgical resection specimens following preoperative systemic therapy should include the standardized tissue sampling and reporting elements of the residual cancer burden (RCB) (Category 2B) [p.70]
- Tumor response should be routinely assessed by clinical exam and imaging studies during delivery of preoperative therapy [p.70]
- It is preferred that the standard regimen is completed prior to surgery [p.70]

### Prognostic Value of Response [p.70]

- Pathologic complete response (pCR) to preoperative systemic therapy is associated with an extremely favorable disease-free and OS, particularly in situations in which all treatment is given preoperatively [p.70]
- The correlation between pathologic response and long-term outcome is strongest for TNBC, somewhat less so for HER2-positive disease, and least for ER-positive disease [p.70]

### General Treatment Principles [p.70]

- A number of chemotherapy regimens have activity in the preoperative setting [p.70]
- In general, those chemotherapy regimens recommended in the adjuvant setting may be considered in the preoperative setting [p.70]
- Randomized trials of chemotherapy demonstrate similar long-term outcomes when patients are given the same treatment preoperatively compared with postoperatively [p.70]

### ER-Positive Disease [p.70]

- Preoperative endocrine therapy alone may be considered for patients with ER-positive disease based on comorbidities or low-risk luminal biology based on clinical characteristics and/or genomic signatures (until desired effect is achieved) [p.70]
- Data show that optimal duration for response if achieved between 4–6 months [p.70]

### HER2-Positive Disease [p.70]

- Patients with HER2-positive, ≥cT2 and/or cN+ should be considered for HER2-directed therapy preoperatively [p.70]

### Locoregional Management Considerations [p.70]

- Some studies suggest an increased risk of locoregional recurrence following use of preoperative chemotherapy [p.70]
- However, these trials delivered chemotherapy regimens that are no longer standard, did not include targeted therapies, did not use modern imaging techniques, and/or used non-standard locoregional management [p.70]
- Care should be taken to follow appropriate locoregional management procedures [p.70]
- Not all patients are appropriate candidates for preoperative systemic therapy [p.70]
- Accurate clinical staging at baseline prior to initiation of preoperative systemic therapy is critical [p.70]

### Management of Disease Progression [p.70]

- Patients with operable breast cancer experiencing progression of disease during preoperative systemic therapy may be given an alternate systemic regimen or proceed to surgery if deemed resectable [p.70]
- If all intended treatment is not completed prior to surgery, the remainder may be given in the adjuvant setting [p.70]
- Locoregional therapy principles should be applied in the same manner as in patients treated with adjuvant systemic therapy [p.70]

## Second-Line and Subsequent Therapy

### General Considerations for All Patients [p.71]

- An FDA-approved biosimilar is an appropriate substitute for any recommended systemic biologic therapy in the NCCN Guidelines [p.71]
- Screen for and manage immunotherapy-related toxicities and endocrine dysfunctions (e.g., hypothyroidism, adrenal insufficiency) in patients treated with immune checkpoint inhibitors [p.71]
- Regular cardiac monitoring is recommended for patients on anthracyclines or HER2-targeted therapies [p.71]
- The selection, dosing, and administration of anti-cancer agents and the management of associated toxicities are complex [p.71]
- Modifications of drug dose and schedule and initiation of supportive care interventions are often necessary because of expected toxicities and individual patient variability, prior treatment, and comorbidities [p.71]
- The optimal delivery of anti-cancer agents therefore requires a health care delivery team experienced in the use of anti-cancer agents and the management of associated toxicities in patients with cancer [p.71]

### For Those Receiving Preoperative/Adjuvant Chemotherapy [p.71]

**Taxane-Induced Peripheral Neuropathy (TIPN) Management:**
- Patients at a higher risk of TIPN (e.g., African descent, diabetes) may consider docetaxel as the preferred taxane based on data supporting less TIPN and less dose reductions [p.71]
- Consider cryotherapy of hands and feet to decrease the risk of peripheral neuropathy when receiving taxane therapies [p.71]
- Use of glove constriction may be considered as it helps with reducing peripheral neuropathy [p.71]

**Alopecia Prevention:**
- Consider scalp cooling to reduce incidence of chemotherapy-induced alopecia for patients receiving neoadjuvant/adjuvant chemotherapy [p.71]
- Results may be less effective with anthracycline-containing regimens [p.71]

**Drug Substitutions:**
- Alternative taxanes (i.e., albumin-bound paclitaxel, docetaxel, paclitaxel) may be substituted for select patients due to medical necessity (i.e., hypersensitivity reaction) [p.71]
- If substituted for weekly docetaxel or paclitaxel, then the weekly dose of albumin-bound paclitaxel should not exceed 125 mg/m² [p.71]

**Pembrolizumab Formulations:**
- Pembrolizumab and berahyaluronidase alfa-pmph subcutaneous injection may be substituted for IV pembrolizumab [p.71]
- Pembrolizumab and berahyaluronidase alfa-pmph has different dosing and administration instructions compared to IV pembrolizumab [p.71]

**Therapy Sequencing:**
- Chemotherapy and endocrine therapy should be given sequentially, with endocrine therapy given after chemotherapy [p.71]
- Adjuvant olaparib can be given concurrently with endocrine therapy [p.71]

### For Those Receiving HER2-Targeted Therapy [p.71]

**Trastuzumab Formulations:**
- Trastuzumab and hyaluronidase-oysk subcutaneous injection may be substituted for trastuzumab IV; however, dosing and administration instruction differ [p.71]
- Do not substitute trastuzumab and hyaluronidase-oysk for or with ado-trastuzumab emtansine [p.71]

**Pertuzumab and Trastuzumab Combination:**
- Pertuzumab, trastuzumab, and hyaluronidase-zzxf injection for subcutaneous use may be substituted anywhere that the combination of intravenous pertuzumab and intravenous trastuzumab are given as part of systemic therapy [p.71]
- Pertuzumab, trastuzumab, and hyaluronidase-zzxf injection for subcutaneous use has different dosing and administration instructions compared to the intravenous products [p.71]

**Cardiac Monitoring:**
- Evaluate left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) prior to and during treatment [p.71]
- The optimal frequency of LVEF assessment during adjuvant trastuzumab therapy is not known [p.71]
- The FDA label recommends LVEF measurements prior to initiation of trastuzumab and every 3 months during therapy [p.71]

## Therapy Regimens

### HR-Positive, HER2-Negative [p.72]

**Preferred (Preoperative or adjuvant setting):**
- Dose-Dense AC (Doxorubicin 60 mg/m²/Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m²) followed by Paclitaxel every 2 weeks (Category 1) [p.72]
- Dose-Dense AC followed by weekly Paclitaxel (Category 1) [p.72]
- TC (Docetaxel 75 mg/m²/Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m²) (Category 1) [p.72]

**Adjuvant setting only:**
- Olaparib (300 mg PO twice daily) for germline BRCA1/2 PVs [p.72, p.75]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances:**
- Dose-Dense AC [p.72]
- AC every 3 weeks (Category 2B) [p.72]
- CMF (Cyclophosphamide/Methotrexate/Fluorouracil) [p.72]
- AC followed by weekly Paclitaxel [p.72]

**Other Recommended:**
- AC followed by Docetaxel every 3 weeks [p.72]
- EC (Epirubicin 100 mg/m²/Cyclophosphamide 830 mg/m²) [p.72, p.76]
- TAC (Docetaxel 75 mg/m²/Doxorubicin 50 mg/m²/Cyclophosphamide 500 mg/m²) [p.72, p.76]

### HR-Negative, HER2-Negative (TNBC) [p.73]

**Preferred - Stage 1:**
- Dose-dense AC followed by Paclitaxel every 2 weeks (Category 1) [p.73]
- Dose-dense AC followed by weekly Paclitaxel (Category 1) [p.73]
- TC (Docetaxel/Cyclophosphamide) (Category 1) [p.73]

**Preferred - Stage II-III (Preoperative followed by adjuvant):**
- Carboplatin/Paclitaxel + Pembrolizumab (preoperative) followed by Cyclophosphamide/Doxorubicin or Epirubicin + Pembrolizumab (preoperative) followed by Pembrolizumab (adjuvant) (Category 1) [p.73]

**Adjuvant setting only:**
- Capecitabine (1000–1250 mg/m² PO twice daily) for residual disease after preoperative therapy with taxane-, alkylator-, and anthracycline-based chemotherapy (Category 1) [p.73]
- Olaparib (300 mg PO twice daily) for germline BRCA1/2 PVs (Category 1) [p.73]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances - Preoperative only:**
- Carboplatin/Docetaxel + Pembrolizumab [p.73]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances - Preoperative or adjuvant:**
- Dose-Dense AC [p.73]
- AC every 3 weeks (Category 2B) [p.73]
- CMF [p.73]
- AC followed by weekly Paclitaxel [p.73]
- AC followed by Carboplatin + taxane (Docetaxel or Paclitaxel) [p.73]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances - Maintenance therapy:**
- Capecitabine [p.73]

**Other Recommended:**
- AC followed by Docetaxel every 3 weeks [p.73]
- EC (Epirubicin/Cyclophosphamide) [p.73]
- TAC (Docetaxel/Doxorubicin/Cyclophosphamide) [p.73]
- Carboplatin/Paclitaxel (various schedules) (Category 2A) [p.73]
- Carboplatin/Docetaxel (Category 2A) [p.73]

### HR-Positive or -Negative and HER2-Positive [p.74]

**Preferred - Preoperative or adjuvant setting:**
- TCHP (Docetaxel 75 mg/m²/Carboplatin AUC 6 + Trastuzumab + Pertuzumab) [p.74]

**Preferred - Adjuvant setting:**
- TCH (Docetaxel 75 mg/m²/Carboplatin AUC 6 + Trastuzumab) [p.74]
- Complete up to 1 year of HER2-targeted therapy with Trastuzumab (Category 1) ± Pertuzumab if no residual disease [p.74]
- Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab (Category 1) if node positive at initial staging and no residual disease [p.74]
- Ado-trastuzumab emtansine (Category 1) if residual disease after preoperative therapy [p.74]
- Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki (Category 1) for high risk of recurrence [p.74]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances - Preoperative or adjuvant setting:**
- Cyclophosphamide/Docetaxel + Trastuzumab [p.74]
- AC followed by Tc (Paclitaxel) + Trastuzumab (various schedules) [p.74]
- AC followed by Tc + Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab (various schedules) [p.74]
- Taxane (Paclitaxel or Docetaxel) + Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab [p.74]

**Useful in Certain Circumstances - Adjuvant setting only:**
- Neratinib [p.74]
- Ado-trastuzumab emtansine (T-DM1) [p.74]
- Paclitaxel + Trastuzumab [p.74]

**Other Recommended:**
- AC followed by Docetaxel + Trastuzumab [p.74]
- AC followed by Docetaxel + Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab [p.74]
- Carboplatin/Paclitaxel + Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab [p.74]

## Dosing Regimens

### HER2-Negative Regimens [p.75-76]

**Dose-Dense AC followed by Paclitaxel:**
- Doxorubicin 60 mg/m² IV Day 1
- Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 14 days for 4 cycles with myeloid growth factor support
  - Followed by: Paclitaxel 175 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 14 days for 4 cycles [p.75]

**Dose-Dense AC followed by weekly Paclitaxel:**
- Doxorubicin 60 mg/m² IV Day 1
- Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 14 days for 4 cycles with myeloid growth factor support
  - Followed by: Paclitaxel 80 mg/m² IV weekly for 12 weeks [p.75]

**TC (Docetaxel/Cyclophosphamide):**
- Docetaxel 75 mg/m² IV Day 1
- Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 4–6 cycles with myeloid growth factor support [p.75]

**Carboplatin/Paclitaxel + Pembrolizumab (preoperative) followed by Cyclophosphamide/Doxorubicin or Epirubicin + Pembrolizumab (preoperative) followed by Pembrolizumab (adjuvant):**
- Preoperative (Cycles 1-4):
  - Pembrolizumab 200 mg IV or 2 mg/kg Day 1
  - Paclitaxel 80 mg/m² IV Days 1, 8, and 15
  - Carboplatin AUC 5 IV Day 1 (or Carboplatin AUC 1.5 IV Days 1, 8, and 15)
  - Cycled every 21 days x 4 cycles with myeloid growth factor support
- Preoperative (Cycles 5-8):
  - Pembrolizumab 200 mg IV or 2 mg/kg Day 1
  - Doxorubicin 60 mg/m² IV Day 1 or Epirubicin 90 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days x 4 cycles with myeloid growth factor support
- Adjuvant:
  - Pembrolizumab 200 mg IV or 2 mg/kg Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days x 9 cycles [p.75]

**Capecitabine:**
- 1000–1250 mg/m² PO twice daily on Days 1–14
- Cycled every 21 days for 6–8 cycles [p.75]

**Olaparib:**
- 300 mg PO twice daily on Days 1–28
- Cycled every 28 days for 1 year [p.75]

**Abemaciclib:**
- 150 mg PO twice daily on Days 1–28
- Cycled every 28 days for 2 years [p.75]

**Dose-Dense AC:**
- Doxorubicin 60 mg/m² IV Day 1
- Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 14 days for 4 cycles with myeloid growth factor support [p.76]

**AC (every 21 days):**
- Doxorubicin 60 mg/m² IV on Day 1
- Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 4 cycles [p.76]

**AC followed by Carboplatin + taxane:**
- Doxorubicin 60 mg/m² IV Day 1
- Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 4 cycles
  - Followed by either:
    - Carboplatin AUC 5 IV on Day 1 + Docetaxel 75 mg/m² IV Day 1, cycled every 21 days x 4 cycles
    - OR Carboplatin AUC 5 IV on Day 1 + Paclitaxel 80 mg/m² IV on Days 1, 8, and 15, cycled every 21 days x 4 cycles [p.76]

**CMF:**
- Cyclophosphamide 100 mg/m² PO daily on Days 1–14
- Methotrexate 40 mg/m² IV Days 1 & 8
- Fluorouracil 600 mg/m² IV Days 1 & 8
  - Cycled every 28 days for 6 cycles
  - OR Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m² IV Day 1 + Methotrexate 40 mg/m² IV Day 1 + Fluorouracil 600 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 8 cycles [p.76]

**AC followed by weekly Paclitaxel:**
- Doxorubicin 60 mg/m² IV Day 1
- Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 4 cycles
  - Followed by: Paclitaxel 80 mg/m² IV weekly for 12 weeks [p.76]

**Capecitabine (maintenance therapy):**
- 650 mg/m² PO twice daily on Days 1–28
- Cycled every 28 days for 1 year [p.76]

**Carboplatin/Docetaxel + Pembrolizumab:**
- Carboplatin AUC 6 IV Day 1
- Docetaxel 75 mg/m² IV Day 1
- Pembrolizumab 200 mg IV or 2 mg/kg Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days x 6 cycles [p.76]

**AC followed by Docetaxel every 3 weeks:**
- Doxorubicin 60 mg/m² IV on Day 1
- Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 4 cycles
  - Followed by: Docetaxel 100 mg/m² IV on Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 4 cycles [p.76]

**EC:**
- Epirubicin 100 mg/m² IV Day 1
- Cyclophosphamide 830 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 8 cycles [p.76]

**TAC:**
- Docetaxel 75 mg/m² IV Day 1
- Doxorubicin 50 mg/m² IV Day 1
- Cyclophosphamide 500 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 6 cycles with myeloid growth factor support [p.76]

**Carboplatin/Paclitaxel (weekly):**
- Carboplatin AUC 5 or 6 IV Day 1
- Paclitaxel 80 mg/m² IV Days 1, 8, and 15
  - Preoperative setting only [p.76]

**Carboplatin/Docetaxel:**
- Carboplatin AUC 6 IV Day 1
- Docetaxel 75 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days x 4–6 cycles [p.76-77]

### HER2-Positive Regimens [p.77-79]

**Paclitaxel + Trastuzumab:**
- Paclitaxel 80 mg/m² IV weekly for 12 weeks
- With: Trastuzumab 4 mg/kg IV with first dose of Paclitaxel
- Followed by: Trastuzumab 2 mg/kg IV weekly to complete 1 year of treatment [p.77]

**TCH (Docetaxel/Carboplatin + Trastuzumab):**
- Docetaxel 75 mg/m² IV Day 1
- Carboplatin AUC 6 IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 6 cycles
- With: Trastuzumab 4 mg/kg IV week 1
- Followed by: Trastuzumab 2 mg/kg IV for 17 weeks
- Followed by: Trastuzumab 6 mg/kg IV
  - Cycled every 21 days to complete 1 year of therapy [p.77]

**TCHP (Docetaxel/Carboplatin + Trastuzumab + Pertuzumab):**
- Docetaxel 75 mg/m² IV Day 1
- Carboplatin AUC 6 IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 6 cycles
- With: Trastuzumab 8 mg/kg IV Day 1 + Pertuzumab 840 mg IV Day 1
- Followed by: Trastuzumab 6 mg/kg IV on Day 1 + Pertuzumab 420 mg IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days to complete 1 year of therapy [p.77]

**T-DM1 (Ado-trastuzumab emtansine):**
- 3.6 mg/kg IV Day 1
- Cycled every 21 days for 14 cycles [p.77]

**Docetaxel + Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab:**
- Docetaxel 75 mg/m² IV on Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 4 cycles
- With: Trastuzumab 8 mg/kg IV Day 1 + Pertuzumab 840 mg IV Day 1
- Followed by: Trastuzumab 6 mg/kg IV on Day 1 + Pertuzumab 420 mg IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days to complete 1 year of therapy [p.77]

**Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki:**
- 5.4 mg/kg IV Day 1
- Cycled every 21 days for 14 cycles [p.77]

**AC followed by T (Paclitaxel) + Trastuzumab:**
- Doxorubicin 60 mg/m² IV Day 1 + Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 4 cycles
- Followed by: Paclitaxel 80 mg/m² IV weekly for 12 weeks
- With: Trastuzumab 4 mg/kg IV with first dose of Paclitaxel
- Followed by: Trastuzumab 2 mg/kg IV weekly to complete 1 year of treatment [p.78]

**Dose-Dense AC followed by Paclitaxel + Trastuzumab:**
- Doxorubicin 60 mg/m² IV Day 1 + Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 14 days for 4 cycles with myeloid growth factor support
- Followed by: Paclitaxel 175 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 14 days for 4 cycles with myeloid growth factor support
- With: Trastuzumab 4 mg/kg IV with first dose of Paclitaxel
- Followed by: Trastuzumab 2 mg/kg IV weekly to complete 1 year of treatment [p.78]

**AC or Dose-Dense AC followed by T (Paclitaxel) + Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab:**
- Doxorubicin 60 mg/m² IV Day 1 + Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 4 cycles (or every 14 days for 4 cycles if dose-dense)
- Followed by: Pertuzumab 840 mg IV Day 1 followed by 420 mg IV + Trastuzumab 8 mg/kg IV Day 1 followed by 6 mg/kg IV + Paclitaxel 80 mg/m² IV Days 1, 8, and 15
  - Cycled every 21 days for 4 cycles
- Followed by: Pertuzumab 420 mg IV Day 1 + Trastuzumab 6 mg/kg IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days to complete 1 year of therapy [p.78]

**Cyclophosphamide/Docetaxel + Trastuzumab:**
- Cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m² IV Day 1 + Docetaxel 75 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 4 cycles
- With: Trastuzumab 4 mg/kg IV week 1
- Followed by: Trastuzumab 2 mg/kg IV weekly for 11 weeks
- Followed by: Trastuzumab 6 mg/kg IV
  - Cycled every 21 days to complete 1 year of therapy [p.78]

**Neratinib:**
- 120 mg PO daily on Days 1–7
- Followed by: 160 mg PO daily on Days 8–14
- Followed by: 240 mg PO daily on Days 15–28
  - Cycled every 28 days x 1 cycle
- Followed by: 240 mg PO daily on Days 1–28
  - Cycled every 28 days x 12 cycles beginning with cycle 2 [p.79]

**Paclitaxel + Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab:**
- Paclitaxel 80 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 7 days x 12–18 cycles
- Pertuzumab 840 mg IV Day 1 followed by 420 mg IV + Trastuzumab 8 mg/kg IV Day 1 followed by 6 mg/kg IV
  - Cycled every 21 days x 4 cycles
- Followed by: Pertuzumab 420 mg IV Day 1 + Trastuzumab 6 mg/kg IV
  - Cycled every 21 days to complete 1 year of therapy [p.79]

**T-DM1:**
- 3.6 mg/kg IV Day 1
- Cycled every 21 days for 17 cycles [p.79]

**AC followed by Docetaxel + Trastuzumab:**

  - Cycled every 21 days for 4 cycles
- Followed by: Docetaxel 100 mg/m² IV Day 1
  - Cycled every 21 days for 4 cycles
- With: Trastuzumab 4 mg/kg IV week 1
- Followed by: Trastuzumab 2 mg/kg IV weekly for 11 weeks
- Followed by: Trastuzumab 6 mg/kg IV
  - Cycled every 21 days to complete 1 year of therapy [p.79]

**AC followed by Docetaxel + Pertuzumab + Trastuzumab:**

  - Cycled every 21 days for 4 cycles

  - Cycled every 21 days for 4 cycles

  - Cycled every 21 days to complete 1 year of therapy [p.79]

**Source Information:**
All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.69]. Alternative taxanes (docetaxel, paclitaxel, albumin-bound paclitaxel) may be substituted for select patients due to medical necessity (i.e., hypersensitivity reaction). If substituted for weekly paclitaxel or docetaxel, then the weekly dose of albumin-bound paclitaxel should not exceed 125 mg/m² [p.72, p.73, p.74]. All cycles are with myeloid growth factor support as per NCCN Guidelines for Hematopoietic Growth Factors [p.75, p.76, p.78].
