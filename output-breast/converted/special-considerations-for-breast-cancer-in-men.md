# Special Considerations for Breast Cancer in Men (BINV-J) - Algorithm Reference

<!-- Source: NCCN Breast Cancer v2.2026, pages 63-66 -->

**Note:** All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated. [p.63]

**Important Terminology:** NCCN recommendations are inclusive of individuals of all sexual and gender identities. On this page, the terms "males" and "females" refer to sex assigned at birth. [p.63]

## Overview

### Clinical Trial Representation and Management Approach

- Few males have been included in breast cancer trials [p.63]. Therefore, recommendations regarding management of breast cancer in males are generally extrapolated from findings of clinical trials focusing on breast cancer in females. [p.63]
- Although there are some biologic and clinical differences between breast cancer in males and females, management of breast cancer in males is similar overall to management of breast cancer in females, with the following special considerations pertinent to male patients [p.63]:

## Key Clinical Considerations

### Genetics

The NCCN Panel recommends consideration of **genetic testing for all males with breast cancer** [p.63]. (See NCCN Guidelines for Genetic/Familial High-Risk Assessment: Breast, Ovarian, Pancreatic, and Prostate)

### Breast Surgery

- **Historically:** Males with breast cancer have undergone mastectomy more often than BCS [p.63]
- **Current Evidence:** Breast-conservation therapy is increasingly being performed in males, and evolving data indicate that breast conservation in males is associated with equivalent outcomes to mastectomy and that it is safe and feasible [p.63]
- **Clinical Decision-Making:** Decisions about breast conservation versus mastectomy in males should be made according to similar criteria as for females [p.63]

**References:** [p.63-64] citations 2-9

### Axillary Lymph Node Surgery

As in females, **sentinel lymph node biopsy (SLNB) should be performed** in the setting of male breast cancer with a clinically node-negative axilla [p.63]

**References:** [p.63-64] citations 2, 4

### Radiation Therapy (RT)

**Indications for radiation after breast surgery in males with breast cancer are the same as for females with breast cancer** [p.63]

**References:** [p.63-64] citations 2, 5, 10, 11

### Molecular Assays for Prognosis and Chemotherapy Prediction

- **Data Availability:** Data are limited regarding the use of molecular assays to assess prognosis and to predict benefit from chemotherapy in males with breast cancer [p.63]
- **21-Gene Assay:** Available data suggest the 21-gene assay recurrence score provides prognostic information in males with breast cancer [p.63]

**References:** [p.63-64] citations 2, 12, 13

### Preoperative and Adjuvant Systemic Therapy

**Chemotherapy and HER2-Targeted Therapy:**
- **Chemotherapy with/without HER2-targeted therapy should be recommended for males with breast cancer according to guidelines for females with breast cancer** [p.63]

**Adjuvant Endocrine Therapy Options:**
- **Tamoxifen:** 5–10 years [p.63]
- **If tamoxifen is contraindicated:** GnRH analog plus an aromatase inhibitor [p.63]

**Aromatase Inhibitor Monotherapy (NOT Recommended):**
- In males, single-agent adjuvant treatment with an aromatase inhibitor has been associated with **inferior outcomes compared to tamoxifen alone**, likely due to inadequate estradiol suppression [p.63]
- **Single-agent aromatase inhibitor is not recommended** [p.63]

**References:** [p.63-64] citations 2, 14-17

### Follow-Up After Treatment for Early-Stage Disease

**Breast Cancer Screening:**
- There are only limited data to support screening for breast cancer in males [p.63]

**Bone Density Assessment:**
- **Males with breast cancer who receive adjuvant GnRH analog therapy:** Bone density should be assessed at baseline and every 2 years [p.63]
- **Management of low bone density:** Should be managed according to standard guidelines [p.63]

**References:** [p.63-64] citation 18

### Systemic Therapy for Advanced Disease

**General Approach:**
- Management of advanced breast cancer in males is similar to that in females [p.63]
- **When using aromatase inhibitors:** It is **preferred that a GnRH analog should be given concurrently** [p.63]

**Specific Agents:**

**Fulvestrant:**
- Available data suggest single-agent fulvestrant has similar efficacy in males as in females [p.63]

**CDK4/6 Inhibitors, mTOR Inhibitors, and PIK3CA Inhibitors:**
- **Systematic evaluation:** These have not been systematically evaluated in clinical trials in males with breast cancer [p.63]
- **Real-world data:** Available real-world data suggest comparable efficacy and safety profiles [p.63]
- **Clinical recommendation:** It is reasonable to recommend these agents to males based on extrapolation of data from studies comprised largely of female participants with advanced breast cancer [p.63]

**Chemotherapy, HER2-Targeted Therapy, Immunotherapy, and PARP Inhibitors:**
- **Indications and recommendations for advanced breast cancer in males are similar to those for advanced breast cancer in females** [p.63]

**References:** [p.63-64] citations 1, 2, 19
