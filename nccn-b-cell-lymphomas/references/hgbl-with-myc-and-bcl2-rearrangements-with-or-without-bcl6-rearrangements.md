## Overview

HGBL with MYC and BCL2 rearrangements with or without BCL6 rearrangements (ICC classification) is also known as DLBCL/HGBL with MYC and BCL2 rearrangements (WHO5 classification) [p.89]. LBCL with MYC and BCL2 or BCL6 rearrangements detected by FISH or standard cytogenetics are known as "double-hit" lymphomas, and if all three rearrangements are present, they are referred to as "triple-hit" lymphomas [p.89]. The vast majority are GCB–like lymphomas [p.89].

Patients often present with poor prognostic variables, such as elevated LDH, bone marrow and CNS involvement, and a high IPI score [p.89].

## First-Line Therapy

All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.89].

### Clinical Trial Consideration

Clinical trial is recommended [p.89].

### Consolidation Therapy

Consolidative ISRT is preferred for localized disease [p.89].

### Preferred Regimens

**DA-EPOCH-R** (dose-adjusted etoposide, prednisone, vincristine, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, and rituximab) [p.89]

### Other Recommended

**RCHOP** (rituximab, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine, prednisone) may be associated with a suboptimal outcome. Could be considered for patients with low risk disease (IPI <2) [p.89].

### Useful in Certain Circumstances

**R-mini-CHOP** (rituximab, mini-dose cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine, prednisone) may be considered for patients who are frail or older [p.89].

### Potentially Toxic Regimens

The following regimens are potentially toxic; performance status and comorbidities should be considered:

- R-HyperCVAD (rituximab, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine, dexamethasone, methotrexate, cytarabine) [p.89]
- R-CODOX-M/R-IVAC (rituximab, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine, methotrexate, cytarabine, ifosfamide, etoposide, mesna) [p.89]

## Second-Line and Subsequent Therapy

For relapsed/refractory disease:

- See <12 months (BCEL-7) [p.89]
- See >12 months (BCEL-8) [p.89]

## Related Diseases

**HGBL with MYC and BCL6 rearrangements** [p.89]

HGBL with BCL6 and MYC rearrangements appear to have outcomes equivalent to DLBCL NOS; however many of these patients were managed with DA-EPOCH-R. Therefore, the optimal chemotherapy regimen remains uncertain [p.89].

### Clinical Trial Consideration

Clinical trial is recommended [p.89].

### Consolidation Therapy

Consider consolidative ISRT for early-stage disease [p.89].

### Induction Regimens

While the optimal treatment approach is not established, the following induction regimens have been used:

- DA-EPOCH-R [p.89]
- **Pola-R-CHP** (polatuzumab vedotin-piiq, rituximab, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, prednisone) (Category 2B) [p.89]
- RCHOP [p.89]
- R-mini-CHOP may be considered for patients who are frail or older [p.89]
- Potentially toxic regimens; performance status and comorbidities should be considered:
  - R-HyperCVAD [p.89]
  - R-CODOX-M/R-IVAC [p.89]

### Second-Line and Subsequent Therapy

For relapsed/refractory disease:

- See <12 months (BCEL-7) [p.89]
- See >12 months (BCEL-8) [p.89]

## HGBL-NOS

Manage as DLBCL (BCEL-1) [p.89].

HGBL-NOS includes cases that appear blastoid or cases intermediate between DLBCL and BL, but which lack MYC and BCL2 with or without BCL6 rearrangement. This category excludes HGBL with MYC and BCL2 with or without BCL6 rearrangement or clear DLBCL [p.89]. Patients often present with poor prognostic parameters, such as elevated LDH, bone marrow and CNS involvement, and a high IPI score [p.89].

## Special Considerations

FDA-approved biosimilars are appropriate substitutes for any recommended systemic biologic therapy in the NCCN Guidelines [p.89]. Rituximab and hyaluronidase human injection for subcutaneous use may be substituted for rituximab after patients have received the first full dose of rituximab by intravenous infusion [p.89].
