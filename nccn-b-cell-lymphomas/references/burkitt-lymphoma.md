## Diagnosis and Additional Testing

### Essential Immunophenotyping

- Adequate immunophenotyping to establish diagnosis [p.91]
  - IHC panel: CD45, CD20, CD3, CD5, CD10, Ki-67, BCL2, BCL6, TdT [p.91]
  - Flow cytometry with peripheral blood and/or biopsy specimen: kappa/lambda, CD45, CD20, CD3, CD5, CD19, CD10, TdT [p.91]
- Typical immunophenotype: sIg+, CD10+, CD20+, TdT-, Ki-67+ (≥95%), BCL2-, BCL6+ [p.91]
- If flow cytometry initially performed, IHC for selected markers (BCL2 and Ki-67) can supplement the flow results [p.91]

### Cytogenetics and Molecular Testing

- Karyotype ± FISH: t(8;14) or variants; MYC, BCL2; BCL6 rearrangement [p.91]
- EBER-ISH (Useful under certain circumstances) [p.91]
- Consider chromosomal microarray to evaluate for 11q aberrations if otherwise resembles BL but FISH for MYC, MYC::IGH, MYC::IGL, and MYC::IGK are negative for LBCL with 11q aberration (ICC)/HGBL with 11q aberrations (WHO5) [p.91]

## Workup

### Essential Assessments

- Physical exam: attention to node-bearing areas, including Waldeyer's ring, and to size of liver and spleen [p.91]
- Performance status [p.91]
- B symptoms [p.91]
- CBC with differential [p.91]
- LDH [p.91]
- Comprehensive metabolic panel [p.91]
- Uric acid [p.91]
- Echocardiogram or MUGA scan if anthracycline-based regimen is indicated [p.91]
- Pregnancy testing in patients of childbearing age (if chemotherapy or RT planned) [p.91]

### Essential Imaging

- PET/CT scan (preferred; initiation of therapy should not be delayed for PET/CT scan) or C/A/P CT with contrast of diagnostic quality; pretreatment imaging is essential [p.91]
- If obtaining PET scan is delayed due to logistics, a C/A/P CT scan should be obtained [p.91]

### Essential Hematologic Testing

- Lumbar puncture [p.91]
- Flow cytometry of cerebrospinal fluid [p.91]

### Essential Infection Screening

- HIV testing (if positive, See [HIVLYM-1](hiv-related-b-cell-lymphomas-evidence.md)) [p.91]
- Hepatitis B testing [p.91]
  - Hepatitis B testing is indicated because of the risk of reactivation with immunotherapy + chemotherapy. Tests include HBsAg and core antibody for a patient with no risk factors. For patients with risk factors or previous history of hepatitis B, add e-antigen (NHODG-B). If positive, check viral load and consider consult with gastroenterologist. [p.91]

### Useful in Selected Cases

- Unilateral or bilateral bone marrow biopsy ± aspirate [p.91]
- Hepatitis C testing [p.91]
- Neck CT with contrast [p.91]
- Brain MRI with and without contrast [p.91]
- Discuss fertility preservation [p.91]
  - Fertility preservation options include: sperm banking, semen cryopreservation, IVF, or ovarian tissue or oocyte cryopreservation. [p.91]

## Staging / Risk Stratification

### Risk Categories [p.92]

**Low Risk:** [p.92]
- Normal LDH AND Stage I (Single extra-abdominal mass <10 cm) OR Completely resected abdominal lesion [p.92]

**High Risk:** [p.92]
- Stage I AND (Abdominal mass OR Extra-abdominal mass >10 cm) OR Stage II–IV [p.92]

### Special Considerations

- This disease is complex and curable; it is preferred that treatment occur at centers with expertise in the management of the disease. [p.91]
- For treatment of double- or triple-hit tumors, see HGBL-1. In other cases where it is not possible to distinguish between BL and high-grade lymphoma, therapy per this guideline may be appropriate. [p.91]
- Special Considerations for Adolescent and Young Adult (AYA) Patients with B-Cell Lymphomas (NHODG-B 4 of 5). [p.91]

## First-Line Therapy (Induction Therapy)

All recommendations are (Category 2A) unless otherwise indicated. [p.91]

All regimens for BL include CNS prophylaxis/therapy. [p.94]

Prophylaxis for tumor lysis syndrome is mandatory (NHODG-B) [p.94]

### Patients <60 years

#### **Preferred -- Low Risk**

- CODOX-M (original or modified) (cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine with intrathecal methotrexate and cytarabine followed by high-dose systemic methotrexate) + rituximab (3 cycles) [p.94]
- DA-EPOCH-RR (etoposide, prednisone, vincristine, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin + rituximab [day 1 and 5]) x 2 cycles followed by PET scan [p.94]
  - CR (PET-negative) after 2 cycles: DA-EPOCH-RR (rituximab day 1 and 5) x 1 cycle (total of 3 cycles) [p.94]
  - PR (PET-positive) after 2 cycles: DA-EPOCH-R (rituximab day 1 only) x 4 cycles (total of 6 cycles) + IT methotrexate [p.94]
- HyperCVAD (cyclophosphamide, vincristine, doxorubicin, dexamethasone) alternating with high-dose methotrexate and cytarabine + rituximab (regimen includes IT therapy) [p.94]

#### **Preferred -- High Risk**

Patients presenting with symptomatic CNS disease: [p.94]
- Initiate treatment with the portion of the systemic therapy that contains CNS-penetrating drugs [p.94]

- CODOX-M (original or modified) (cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine with IT methotrexate and cytarabine followed by high-dose systemic methotrexate) alternating with IVAC (ifosfamide, cytarabine, etoposide, IT methotrexate) + rituximab [p.94]
- HyperCVAD (cyclophosphamide, vincristine, doxorubicin, dexamethasone) alternating with high-dose methotrexate and cytarabine + rituximab (regimen includes IT therapy) [p.94]
- Dose-adjusted EPOCH (etoposide, prednisone, vincristine, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin) + rituximab for 6 cycles + IT methotrexate (for patients not able to tolerate aggressive therapy) [p.94]

### Patients ≥60 years

#### **Preferred -- Low Risk**

- DA-EPOCH-RR (rituximab day 1 and 5) x 2 cycles followed by PET scan [p.94]
  - CR (PET-negative) after 2 cycles: DA-EPOCH-RR (rituximab day 1 and 5) x 1 cycle (total of 3 cycles) [p.94]
  - PR (PET-positive) after 2 cycles: DA-EPOCH-R (rituximab day 1 only) x 4 cycles (total of 6 cycles) + IT methotrexate [p.94]

#### **Preferred -- High Risk**

Patients presenting with symptomatic CNS disease: [p.94]
- Initiate treatment with the portion of the systemic therapy that contains CNS-penetrating drugs [p.94]

- Dose-adjusted EPOCH (etoposide, prednisone, vincristine, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin) + rituximab for 6 cycles + IT methotrexate (for patients presenting with symptomatic CNS disease) [p.94]

### Notes on Specific Regimens

- CHOP is not an adequate therapy. [p.94]
- Data included patients with leptomeningeal CNS disease; patients with parenchymal CNS disease were excluded in the clinical trials of this regimen. [p.94]
- An FDA-approved biosimilar is an appropriate substitute for any recommended systemic biologic therapy in the NCCN Guidelines. Rituximab and hyaluronidase human injection for subcutaneous use may be substituted for rituximab after patients have received the first full dose of rituximab by intravenous infusion. [p.94]

## Initial Response Assessment

### Response Criteria

- Lugano Response Criteria for Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma (NHODG-C) [p.92]

### Complete Response [p.92]

- Follow-up: C/A/P CT scan with contrast no more often than every 6 mo for 1 y after completion of treatment, then only as clinically indicated [p.92]
- Relapse after 1 y is uncommon; therefore, follow-up should be individualized according to patient characteristics. [p.92]

### Incomplete Response (<Complete Response) [p.92]

- Clinical trial [p.92]
- OR Palliative ISRT [p.92]

## Second-Line and Subsequent Therapy

### Disease Relapse >6 to 18 months After Appropriate First-Line Therapy [p.93, p.95]

#### **Other Recommended** [p.95]

- Dose-adjusted EPOCH (etoposide, prednisone, vincristine, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin) + rituximab for 6 cycles (if not previously given) + IT methotrexate [p.95]
- RICE (rituximab, ifosfamide, carboplatin, etoposide); IT methotrexate if have not received previously [p.95]
- RIVAC (rituximab, ifosfamide, cytarabine, etoposide); IT methotrexate if have not received previously [p.95]
- For patients presenting with symptomatic CNS disease; initiate treatment with the portion of the systemic therapy that contains CNS-penetrating drugs [p.95]

#### **Useful in Certain Circumstances** [p.95]

- High-dose cytarabine + rituximab [p.95]
- RGDP (rituximab, gemcitabine, dexamethasone, cisplatin) [p.95]

### Disease Relapse <6 months After Appropriate First-Line Therapy [p.93]

- Clinical trial [p.93]
- OR Best supportive care (See NCCN Guidelines for Palliative Care) [p.93]

### Response Assessment at Relapse [p.93]

**Complete Response:** [p.93]
- Consider HDT/ASCR ± ISRT [p.93]
- OR Consider allogeneic HCT in selected cases ± ISRT [p.93]
- Selected cases include mobilization failures and persistent bone marrow involvement. [p.93]
- OR Clinical trial [p.93]

**Partial Response:** [p.93]
- Additional second-line therapy (BURK-A) [p.93]
- OR Consider HDT/ASCR ± ISRT [p.93]
- OR Consider allogeneic HCT in selected cases ± ISRT [p.93]

**No Response or Progressive Disease:** [p.93]
- Clinical trial [p.93]
- OR Best supportive care (See NCCN Guidelines for Palliative Care) including palliative ISRT [p.93]

## Follow-Up / Surveillance

### After Complete Response to Initial Therapy [p.92]

- C/A/P CT scan with contrast no more often than every 6 mo for 1 y after completion of treatment, then only as clinically indicated [p.92]
- Relapse after 1 y is uncommon; therefore, follow-up should be individualized according to patient characteristics. [p.92]

### Principles of Radiation Therapy [p.92, p.93]

- Principles of Radiation Therapy (NHODG-D) [p.92, p.93]
- Palliative ISRT may be used in selected circumstances [p.92, p.93]

## Consolidated Treatment Algorithm Decision Trees

### Low-Risk Patients -- Initial Therapy [p.92]

- Normal LDH AND (Stage I with single extra-abdominal mass <10 cm OR Completely resected abdominal lesion)
  - --> Clinical trial [p.92]
  - OR --> See Suggested Regimens (BURK-A) [p.92]
    - If Complete Response [p.92]
      - --> Follow-up: C/A/P CT scan with contrast no more often than every 6 mo for 1 y after completion of treatment, then only as clinically indicated [p.92]
    - If <Complete Response [p.92]
      - --> Clinical trial [p.92]
      - OR --> Palliative ISRT [p.92]

### High-Risk Patients -- Initial Therapy [p.92]

- Stage I AND (Abdominal mass OR Extra-abdominal mass >10 cm) OR Stage II–IV
  - --> Clinical trial [p.92]
  - OR --> See Suggested Regimens (BURK-A) [p.92]
    - If Complete Response [p.92]
      - --> Follow-up: C/A/P CT scan with contrast no more often than every 6 mo for 1 y after completion of treatment, then only as clinically indicated [p.92]
    - If <Complete Response [p.92]
      - --> Clinical trial [p.92]
      - OR --> Palliative ISRT [p.92]
