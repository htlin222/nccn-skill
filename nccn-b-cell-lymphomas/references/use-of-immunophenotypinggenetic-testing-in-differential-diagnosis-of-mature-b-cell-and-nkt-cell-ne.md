## Diagnosis and Additional Testing

### General Principles [p.109]

- Morphology ± clinical features drive both the choice and the interpretation of special studies [p.109]
- Differential diagnosis is based on morphology ± clinical setting [p.109]
- Begin with a broad panel appropriate to morphologic diagnosis, limiting panel of antibodies based on the differential diagnosis [p.109]
- Avoid "shotgun" panels of unnecessary antibodies unless a clinically urgent situation warrants [p.109]
- Add antigens in additional panels, based on initial results [p.109]
- Follow with genetic studies as needed [p.109]
- Return to clinical picture if immunophenotype + morphology are not specific [p.109]

### Initial Morphologic, Clinical, and Immunophenotypic Analysis [p.110]

**B-cell antigens positive** [p.110] (CD19, CD20, CD79a, PAX5)

- **Morphology** [p.110]
  - Cytology: Small cells, Medium-sized cells, Large cells
  - Pattern: Diffuse, Nodular/follicular/mantle/marginal, Sinuses

- **Clinical** [p.110]
  - Age (child, adult)
  - Location: Nodal, Extranodal (specific site)

- **Immunophenotype** [p.110]
  - Naïve B cells: CD5, CD23, IgD
  - GCB cells: CD10, BCL6
  - Follicular dendritic cells (FDC): CD21, CD23
  - Post-GCB cells: IRF4/MUM1, CD138
  - Ig heavy and light chains (surface, cytoplasmic, class switch, light chain type)
  - Oncogene products: BCL2, cyclin D1, MYC, BCL6, ALK
  - Viruses: EBV, HHV8
  - Other: CD43, Ki-67, LEF1, SOX11

- **Genetic testing** [p.110]
  - BCL2, BCL6, CCND1, MYC, ALK, MYD88, BRAF, IRF4, 11q aberrations, Ig rearrangement

## Workup

### Small Cell B-Cell Neoplasms [p.112]

**Differential Diagnosis** [p.112]:
- Chronic lymphocytic leukemia/small lymphocytic lymphoma (CLL/SLL)
- Mantle cell lymphoma (MCL)
- Splenic marginal zone lymphoma (SMZL)
- Hairy cell leukemia (HCL)
- Lymphoplasmacytic lymphoma (LPL)
- Extranodal marginal zone lymphoma (EMZL)
- Nodal marginal zone lymphoma (NMZL)
- Follicular lymphoma (FL)
- Pediatric-type follicular lymphoma (PTFL)

**Initial Panel** [p.112]: CD5, CD10, CD21, CD23, cyclin D1, BCL2, BCL6, Ki-67, CD11c, (CD25, CD103)

**Decision Tree - CD5 Positive Cases** [p.112]:
- CD23+ → CLL
- CD23-
  - Cyclin D1+ OR t(11;14)+ → MCL
  - Cyclin D1- AND t(11;14)- → Assess additional markers

**Decision Tree - CD5 Negative Cases** [p.112]:
- CD10+ → FL
- CD10-
  - BCL2+ OR t(14;18)+ AND BCL6- → FL
  - BCL2- AND t(14;18)- → Consider HCL, LPL, other rare B-cell neoplasms
  - CD103+, CD25+, CD11c+ → HCL with confirmation by BRAF sequencing or IHC for mutant protein and annexin A1
  - Cytoplasmic Ig+ → MZL with morphology (MZL pattern), clinical features (extranodal, splenic)
  - Cytoplasmic Ig- with pseudofollicular pattern, clinical features (bone marrow) → CD5- CLL or other splenic B-cell lymphomas
  - Morphology (MZL pattern, plasmacytoid features), genetics [del(7q)], clinical features (splenomegaly, bone marrow involvement, paraprotein) → MYD88 mut+ = LPL; MYD88 mut- = MZL

**Notes on Small Cell Diagnosis** [p.112]:
- Rare cases of HCL may be CD10+ or CD5+ and some cases of FL are CD10- [p.112]
- BCL6 is a useful discriminator if needed (rarely) [p.112]
- Rare cases of MCL are CD5- [p.112]
- Rare cases of cyclin D1- and t(11;14)-negative MCL have been reported; consider SOX11 (positive in cyclin D1-negative MCL) and LEF1 (positive in CLL/SLL) IHC [p.112]
- 85% of FL will be BCL2+ or t(14;18)+ [p.112]
- Flow cytometry on blood or bone marrow done only if HCL is in differential diagnosis by morphology [p.112]
- Ki-67 >30% can confirm PTFL with morphology (large, expansile follicles, grade 3 or blastoid cells, no diffuse component) and clinical features (localized, nodal) [p.112]

### Medium Cell B-Cell Neoplasms [p.113]

**Differential Diagnosis** [p.113]:
- Burkitt lymphoma (BL)
- MCL, blastoid variant
- High-grade B-cell lymphoma (HGBL), NOS
- HGBL with MYC and BCL2 rearrangements (ICC and WHO-5)
- HGBL with MYC and BCL6 rearrangements (ICC)
- LBCL with 11q aberration [ICC]; HGBL with 11q aberrations [WHO]

**Initial Panel** [p.113]: CD5, CD10, cyclin D1, BCL2, BCL6, IRF4/MUM1, Ki-67

**Clinical Presentation** [p.113]: Diffuse pattern medium cells ± starry sky pattern

**Decision Tree** [p.113]:
- CD5+ with Cyclin D1+ → MCL, blastoid variant
- CD5+ with Cyclin D1- → Consider cyclin D1- MCL
- CD5+ with increased prolymphocytes → CLL
- CD5- with BLC6-, IRF4/MUM1- → BL

**Notes on Medium Cell Diagnosis** [p.113]:
- Starry sky pattern is typically present in BL and frequently in HGBL; if blastoid morphology, exclude LL (usually terminal deoxynucleotidyl transferase+ and often CD34+) [p.113]
- Ki-67 is a prognostic factor in some lymphomas (e.g., mantle cell) and is typically >90% in BL; it is not useful in predicting the presence of MYC rearrangement or in classification [p.113]
- Rare MCL may be cyclin D1-; consider SOX11 IHC [p.113]
- Lymphomas resembling BL or HGBL without detectable MYC rearrangement may prompt consideration for LBCL with 11q aberration [ICC]; HGBL with 11q aberrations [WHO]; chromosomal microarray or FISH may be warranted; correlation with morphology and clinical features is essential [p.113]

### Large Cell B-Cell Neoplasms [p.114-115]

**Differential Diagnosis** [p.114]:
- Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL), NOS
  - GCB subtype
  - Non-GCB subtype
  - T-cell/histiocyte-rich large B-cell lymphoma (THRLBCL)
  - Primary DLBCL of the CNS
  - Primary cutaneous DLBCL, leg type
  - EBV-positive DLBCL (EBV + DLBCL), NOS
- DLBCL associated with chronic inflammation
- Lymphomatoid granulomatosis
- Primary mediastinal large B-cell lymphoma (PMBL)
- Intravascular LBCL
- ALK-positive LBCL
- Plasmablastic lymphoma
- HHV8+ LBCL, NOS
- LBCL with IRF4 rearrangement
- Primary effusion lymphoma (PEL)
- Mediastinal gray zone lymphoma (MGZL)
- MCL, pleomorphic variant

**Initial Panel** [p.114]: CD5, CD10, BCL6, IRF4/MUM1, Ki-67

**Decision Tree - CD5 Positive Cases** [p.114]:
- Cyclin D1+ → Pleomorphic MCL
- Cyclin D1- → DLBCL, NOS CD5+; consider SOX11 IHC to exclude cyclin D1-negative pleomorphic MCL

**Decision Tree - CD5 Negative Cases** [p.114]:
- CD10+ → DLBCL, NOS GCB type (BCL6+)
- CD10- with BCL6+ IRF4/MUM1- → DLBCL, NOS GCB type
- CD10- with BCL6+ IRF4/MUM1+ → Non-GCB
- CD10- with BCL6- IRF4/MUM1+ → LBCL with IRF4 rearrangements; confirm by karyotype or FISH
- CD10- with BCL6- IRF4/MUM1- → Non-GCB

**Expanded Panel for Further Characterization** [p.114] (if further characterization is warranted based on clinical or morphologic features; specific panel varies depending on differential diagnosis):
- CD20, PAX5, CD138, ALK, CD30, CD15, EBV-EBER, HHV8, Ig light and heavy chains

**Additional Large Cell Subtype Decisions** [p.115]:
- CD30- EBV+ → Assess location
  - Mediastinal → PMBL (may be BCL6+, IRF4/MUM1-); assess morphology and CD15
  - Older or immunosuppressed → EBV + DLBCL; assess CD20, PAX5, CD79a
  - Extranodal, T-cell rich, angiocentric → Lymphomatoid granulomatosis; assess EBER
  - Chronic inflammation → DLBCL associated with chronic inflammation; assess EBER
- CD30- EBV- with CD20+, PAX5+ → Assess for borderline morphology with CHL, CD15 status, and IRF4/MUM1
  - CD15+, morphologically borderline with CHL → PMBL
  - CD15-, morphologically borderline with CHL, may be BCL6+, IRF4/MUM1- → HGBL, NOS
- CD30- EBV- with CD20-, PAX5- → Assess CD79a and IRF4/MUM1
  - CD79a+, IRF4/MUM1+ → Assess HHV8 and CD138; assess morphology (anaplastic/plasmablastic) and light chains
  - CD79a+, IRF4/MUM1- → THRLBCL
  - CD79a- → Assess morphology (T-cell rich, angiocentric) and location
- CD30+ → Assess EBV, HHV8, and morphology
  - EBV+, HHV8-, ALK+ → ALK + DLBCL; assess immunoglobulin (IgA lambda +)
  - EBV+, HHV8-, ALK-, CD79a+, IRF4/MUM1+ → Assess morphology and light chains; if IgA lambda + and EMA+, consider Anaplastic/plasmablastic myeloma/plasmacytoma; if CD56 +/- and cyclin D1 +/- with IgG, IgA, kappa, or lambda, assess further
  - EBV-, HHV8- → Assess for plasmablastic morphology, MYC FISH, and myc status
    - Plasmablastic morphology, MYC FISH + → Plasmablastic lymphoma
    - Anaplastic morphology or PEL presentation with HHV8+, EBV+/- → PEL (CD30+)
  - EBV+, HHV8+ → HHV8-positive DLBCL, NOS (ICC)/Kaposi sarcoma-associated herpes virus (KSHV)/HHV8-positive DLBCL (WHO); confirm by morphology; assess immunoglobulin (IgM lambda +)

**Notes on Large Cell Diagnosis** [p.114]:
- CD5 is included to identify pleomorphic MCL; if CD5 is positive, cyclin D1 staining is done to confirm or exclude MCL [p.114]
- Ki-67 is a prognostic factor in some lymphomas (e.g., mantle cell) and is typically >90% in BL; it is not useful in predicting the presence of MYC rearrangement or in classification [p.114]

### Cutaneous B-Cell Neoplasms [p.116]

**Differential Diagnosis** [p.116]:
- Primary cutaneous marginal zone lymphoma (PCMZL)
- Primary cutaneous follicle center lymphoma (PCFCL)
- Primary cutaneous DLBCL, leg type (PC-DLBCL, leg type)

**Initial Panel** [p.116]: CD3, CD5, CD10, BCL2, BCL6, IRF4/MUM1, CD21/23 (FDC markers)

**Decision Tree - CD10 Positive Cases** [p.116]:
- BCL6+ IRF4/MUM1- (FDC+/-) with small/medium/large cells and many CD3+ cells → PCFCL
- BCL6- (positive GC) IRF4/MUM1+/- (FDC + follicular, disrupted) with small/medium cells (larger cells in GC) and many CD3+ T cells → PCMZL or PCFCL
- BCL2+ BCL6+/- IRF4/MUM1+ (FDC-) with large round cells and few CD3+ T cells → PC-DLBCL, leg type

**Decision Tree - CD10 Negative Cases** [p.116]:
- BCL2- BCL6- (positive GC) IRF4/MUM1+/- (FDC + follicular, disrupted) with small/medium cells and many CD3+ T cells → PCMZL or PCFCL
- BCL2 weakly+ BCL6+ IRF4/MUM1- (FDC±, follicular) with small/medium/large cells and many CD3+ T cells → PCMZL or PCFCL

**Notes on Assessment** [p.116]:
- CD10 and BCL6 are assessed both in follicles (if present) and in intrafollicular/diffuse areas [p.116]
- CD10+ BCL6+ GCs are present in PCMZL, while both follicular and interfollicular/diffuse areas (tumor cells) are positive for BCL6+/- CD10 in PCFCL [p.116]
