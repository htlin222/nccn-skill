## Staging / Risk Stratification

- Ann Arbor staging system applied to marginal zone lymphomas [p.28-29]
- Risk stratification based on prognostic factors and subtype [p.29]
- FLIPI-1 and FLIPI-2 prognostic indices applicable [p.29-30]

## First-Line Therapy

### Preferred

- Rituximab monotherapy or observation for early-stage disease [p.30]
- Rituximab + bendamustine for intermediate/advanced disease (Category 2A) [p.31]
- Rituximab + cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine, prednisone (R-CHOP) (Category 2A) [p.31]

### Other Recommended

- Obinutuzumab + bendamustine or CHOP [p.32]
- Single-agent rituximab for symptomatic disease [p.32]

### Useful in Certain Circumstances

- Chlorambucil monotherapy (for elderly or unfit patients) [p.32]
- FCR (fludarabine, cyclophosphamide, rituximab) in selected cases [p.33]

## Second-Line and Subsequent Therapy

- Options depend on prior therapy and response [p.34-35]
- Consider clinical trial participation [p.34]
- Alternative chemotherapy combinations for refractory disease [p.35]

## Consolidation / Maintenance

- Rituximab maintenance after induction therapy (Category 2A) [p.35-36]
- Duration and schedule per protocol-specific guidelines [p.36]

## Follow-Up / Surveillance

- Physical examination every 3-6 months [p.44]
- Imaging as clinically indicated [p.44]
- Labs (CBC, LDH) periodically [p.44]

---

**Note:** All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated. [p.27]

For complete clinical guidance on Marginal Zone Lymphoma management, refer to the full NCCN B-Cell Lymphomas Guidelines v3.2025, pages 27-46.
## Second-Line and Subsequent Therapy

Subsequent systemic therapy options include second-line therapy regimens that were not previously given [p.47].

### Preferred

- **CAR T-cell therapy** [p.47]
  - Axicabtagene ciloleucel (CD19-directed) (if not previously given) [p.47]
    - Reference: Three-year follow-up analysis showed durable responses in relapsed/refractory indolent non-Hodgkin lymphoma (ZUMA-5) [p.48]

### Other Recommended

- **Acalabrutinib** [p.48]
  - Reference: Phase 2, multicentre, open-label trial (ACE-LY-003) in patients with relapsed or refractory marginal zone lymphoma [p.48]
  - Note: Studies of acalabrutinib excluded concomitant use of warfarin or equivalent vitamin K antagonists [p.47]
  - Note: Head-to-head clinical trials in other B-cell malignancies have demonstrated a more favorable toxicity profile for acalabrutinib and zanubrutinib compared to ibrutinib without compromising efficacy [p.47]

- **Bendamustine + obinutuzumab** [p.48]
  - Reference: GADOLIN trial--randomised, controlled, open-label, multicentre, phase 3 trial of obinutuzumab plus bendamustine versus bendamustine monotherapy in patients with rituximab-refractory indolent non-Hodgkin lymphoma [p.48]

- **Ibrutinib** [p.48]
  - Reference: Durable ibrutinib responses in relapsed/refractory marginal zone lymphoma: long-term follow-up and biomarker analysis [p.48]

- **Lenalidomide + rituximab** [p.48]
  - Reference: AUGMENT--phase III study of lenalidomide plus rituximab versus placebo plus rituximab in relapsed or refractory indolent lymphoma [p.48]
  - Reference: Magnify phase 3b study of lenalidomide + rituximab (R2) followed by maintenance in relapsed/refractory indolent non-Hodgkin lymphoma [p.48]
  - Reference: Safety and efficacy of lenalidomide in combination with rituximab in recurrent indolent non-follicular lymphoma--final results of phase II study by Fondazione Italiana Linfomi [p.48]

- **Pirtobrutinib** [p.48]
  - Note: Highly selective, non-covalent (reversible) BTK inhibitor [p.48]
  - Reference: Results from phase 1/2 BRUIN study in relapsed/refractory marginal zone lymphoma [p.48]

- **Zanubrutinib** [p.48]
  - Note: Head-to-head clinical trials in other B-cell malignancies have demonstrated a more favorable toxicity profile for zanubrutinib and acalabrutinib compared to ibrutinib without compromising efficacy [p.47]
  - Reference: MAGNOLIA study--safety and efficacy of zanubrutinib in relapsed/refractory marginal zone lymphoma [p.48]

## Second-Line Consolidation or Extended Dosing (optional)

- **Bendamustine + obinutuzumab** [p.48]
  - Reference: GADOLIN trial--randomised, controlled, open-label, multicentre, phase 3 trial [p.48]

## Third-Line and Subsequent Therapy

Preferred regimen:

- **CAR T-cell therapy** [p.47]
  - Axicabtagene ciloleucel (CD19-directed) (if not previously given) [p.47]

### Third-Line Consolidation Therapy (optional)

- **Allogeneic HCT in highly selected cases** [p.47]
  - Selected cases include mobilization failures and persistent bone marrow involvement [p.47]
  - Consider prophylaxis for tumor lysis syndrome [p.47]

## Important Considerations

- **FDA-approved biosimilars**: An FDA-approved biosimilar is an appropriate substitute for any recommended systemic biologic therapy in the NCCN Guidelines [p.47]

- **Rituximab and hyaluronidase human injection**: May be substituted for rituximab after patients have received the first full dose of rituximab by intravenous infusion [p.47]

- **Individualized treatment selection**: The choice of therapy requires consideration of many factors, including age, comorbidities, and future treatment possibilities (eg, HDT/ASCR). Therefore, treatment selection is highly individualized [p.47]

- **Bendamustine and CAR T-cell therapy**: In patients intended to receive CAR T-cell therapy, bendamustine should be used with caution. Delay bendamustine until after CAR-T leukapheresis [p.47]

- **CAR T-cell therapy guidance**: Refer to Guidance for Treatment of Patients with Chimeric Antigen Receptor (CAR) T-Cell Therapy (NHODG-E) [p.47]

- **Monoclonal antibody and viral reactivation**: See NHODG-B guidance [p.47]

- **Default category**: All recommendations are Category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.47, p.48]
