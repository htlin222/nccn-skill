## Summary of Key Evidence

- Most patients with advanced-stage MCL require systemic therapy; aggressive induction therapy followed by HDT/ASCR with rituximab maintenance has been the standard approach for transplant-eligible patients [p.225-226]
- TP53 mutation status is a critical prognostic factor that guides treatment selection, with TP53-mutated classical MCL benefiting from BTK inhibitor-based regimens [p.226-227]
- Covalent BTK inhibitors (acalabrutinib and zanubrutinib) are preferred second-line agents with FDA-approved indications for relapsed/refractory MCL [p.234-235]
- The TRIANGLE study demonstrated that alternating RCHOP + ibrutinib/RDHAP with ibrutinib maintenance provided superior failure-free survival compared to transplant alone [p.228-229]
- For TP53-mutated MCL, zanubrutinib/obinutuzumab/venetoclax achieved 96% ORR (88% CR) with 72% 2-year PFS and 84% of patients achieving uMRD6 [p.226]

## First-Line Therapy Evidence

### Classical MCL (TP53 Wild-Type)

**Standard Approach for Transplant-Eligible Patients**

Aggressive induction therapy with cytarabine-based chemoimmunotherapy followed by HDT/ASCR and rituximab maintenance is the established standard [p.226].

#### LYMA Regimen: RDHA and Platinum (Carboplatin, Cisplatin, or Oxaliplatin)

- **Trial Design**: Phase III randomized LYMA trial in patients <66 years of age [p.227]
- **Primary Results**: ORR 89% (77% CR) with induction; CR rate 65% after HDT/ASCR [p.227]
- **Survival Outcomes**: 4-year PFS 83% (rituximab maintenance) vs. 64% (observation; P = .001); 4-year OS 89% vs. 80% (P = .04) [p.227]
- **Long-Term Follow-Up**: 7-year data confirmed sustained disease control without increased relapse rate [p.227]
- **Platinum Comparison**: RDHA-oxaliplatin showed superior outcomes compared to cisplatin and carboplatin variants with 4-year PFS 87% vs. 65% and 4-year OS 92% vs. 76% [p.227]
- **NCCN Category**: Preferred regimen [p.227]

**Obinutuzumab-Based Alternative**

- **Trial Design**: LYMA-101 trial with obinutuzumab + ODHAP (dexamethasone, high-dose cytarabine, cisplatin) in transplant-eligible patients <66 years (n = 85) [p.227]
- **Survival Outcomes**: 5-year PFS 83% vs. 67% and 5-year OS 86% vs. 71% by propensity score matching analysis [p.227]
- **NCCN Recommendation**: Substitution of obinutuzumab for rituximab is optional across all lines of therapy [p.227]

#### NORDIC Regimen: Dose-Intensified RCHOP (maxi-CHOP) Alternating with High-Dose Cytarabine

- **Trial Design**: Nordic MCL2 study (n = 160) in patients ≤65 years with previously untreated MCL [p.228]
- **Induction Results**: ORR 96% (54% CR) with subsequent HDT/ASCR eligibility [p.228]
- **6-Year Outcomes**: PFS 66% and OS 70% [p.228]
- **Long-Term Follow-Up (15 years)**: Median PFS 9 years and median OS 13 years for intent-to-treat population; median PFS 11 years and OS not reached for 145 patients who proceeded to HDT/ASCR [p.228]
- **Prognostic Factors**: MIPI and Ki-67 expression were only independent survival predictors; CR to induction therapy associated with superior outcomes vs. PR (P = .0038 for OS) [p.228]
- **Late Relapse**: Pattern of continuous relapse observed with late relapses in 6 patients >10 years after therapy [p.228]
- **NCCN Category**: Preferred regimen

#### Bendamustine and Rituximab Followed by Rituximab and High-Dose Cytarabine

- **Trial Design**: Pooled analysis (n = 88) evaluating BR (3 cycles) followed by rituximab and high-dose cytarabine (3 cycles) then HDT/ASCR [p.228]
- **Induction Response**: ORR 97% (90% CR) [p.228]
- **Durability**: 3-year PFS 83% and OS 92% (median follow-up 33 months) [p.228]
- **NCCN Category**: Preferred regimen

#### TRIANGLE Regimen: Alternating RCHOP + Ibrutinib/RDHA + Platinum

- **Trial Design**: Phase III randomized Intergroup trial (n = 870) comparing: Arm A (alternating RCHOP/RDHAP + HDT/ASCR + observation), Arm A+I (alternating RCHOP + ibrutinib/RDHAP + HDT/ASCR + 2-year ibrutinib maintenance), and Arm I (alternating RCHOP + ibrutinib/RDHAP + ibrutinib maintenance) in patients <66 years [p.228-229]
- **Induction Response**: ORR 94% (36% CR) for Arm A and 98% (45% CR) for combined arms A+I/I [p.229]
- **Primary Endpoint**: 3-year FFS 72% (Arm A) vs. 88% (Arm A+I; P = .0008) vs. 86% (Arm I; P = .9979) [p.229]
- **PFS Rates**: 3-year PFS 73% (A) vs. 88% (A+I) vs. 87% (I) [p.229]
- **Disease Progression**: Higher in Arm A (n = 67) vs. A+I (n = 34) vs. I (n = 35) [p.229]
- **Survival Outcomes**: 3-year OS rates 91% (A+I), 86% (I), and 86% (A); median OS not reached in all arms [p.229]
- **Safety**: Similar safety profile to standard regimen with anemia (61% vs. 59%), neutropenia (49% vs. 47%), and thrombocytopenia (61% vs. 59%) as most common grade 3–5 hematologic toxicities [p.229]
- **Long-Term Follow-Up (53 months)**: 3-year FFS 75% (A), 86% (A+I), and 75% (I); P = .28 for A+I vs. I; P = .9942 for A vs. I; P = .0034 for A+I vs. A. 3-year OS rates 90% (A+I), 91% (I), and 85% (A); P = .0069 for A+I vs. A; P = .0041 for A vs. I [p.229]
- **BTKi Substitution**: Substitution of ibrutinib with other covalent BTKis (acalabrutinib or zanubrutinib) included with category 2B recommendation, though not specifically evaluated in TRIANGLE [p.229]
- **NCCN Category**: Preferred regimen [p.229]

#### Rituximab + HyperCVAD Alternating With High-Dose Methotrexate and Cytarabine

- **Trial Design**: Phase II study (n = 97) with previously untreated MCL [p.229]
- **Primary Results**: 3-year FFS 64% and OS 82% (median follow-up 40 months) [p.229]
- **Long-Term Follow-Up**: At 10 years, median OS not reached and median TTF 5 years [p.229]
- **Toxicity**: Substantial myelotoxicity [p.229]
- **BTKi Enhancement**: Use of ibrutinib + rituximab as induction prior to R-hyperCVAD minimizes R-hyperCVAD cycles [p.229]
- **Panel Consensus**: Support for other covalent BTKis (acalabrutinib or zanubrutinib) + rituximab as induction therapy prior to R-hyperCVAD [p.229]
- **NCCN Category**: Other Recommended regimen [p.229]

### Less Aggressive Induction Therapy for Non-Transplant-Eligible Patients

#### Acalabrutinib + Bendamustine + Rituximab

- **Trial Design**: Phase III randomized ECHO trial (598 patients) comparing acalabrutinib + BR (n = 299) vs. BR (n = 299) in previously untreated MCL ≥65 years [p.230]
- **Response Rates**: ORR 91% (67% CR) for acalabrutinib + BR vs. 88% (54% CR) for BR + placebo [p.230]
- **Median PFS**: 66 months (acalabrutinib + BR) vs. 50 months (BR + placebo); after censoring for COVID-19 deaths: not reached vs. 62 months [p.230]
- **OS**: No statistically significant difference (not reached in both arms; P = .2743) [p.230]
- **FDA Approval**: Approved for previously untreated MCL not candidates for aggressive therapy including HDT/ASCR [p.230]
- **NCCN Category**: Preferred regimen [p.230]

#### Bendamustine + Rituximab

- **Trial Design**: Randomized phase III StiL study comparing BR vs. RCHOP as first-line therapy in advanced lymphomas including MCL (514 evaluable; 18% MCL) [p.230]
- **Primary Endpoint (PFS)**: BR superior with median PFS 70 vs. 31 months (P < .0001) [p.230]
- **OS**: Not significantly different between treatment arms [p.230]
- **MCL Subgroup**: Among MCL patients, median PFS significantly higher with BR vs. RCHOP (35 vs. 22 months; P = .0044) [p.230]
- **Response Rates**: ORR similar (93% BR vs. 91% RCHOP); CR rate significantly higher with BR (40% vs. 30%; P = .021) [p.230]
- **Safety Profile**: BR associated with less serious TEAEs (19% vs. 29%), less grade 3–4 hematologic TEAEs. Grade 3–4 neutropenia (29% vs. 69%), peripheral neuropathy (7% vs. 29%), infections (37% vs. 50%) less frequent with BR [p.230]
- **NCCN Category**: Preferred regimen [p.230]

**BRIGHT Trial (BR vs. RCHOP/RCVP)**

- **Trial Design**: Phase III randomized study (224 BR vs. 223 RCHOP/RCVP) in indolent lymphoma and MCL [p.230]
- **5-Year PFS Rates**: 66% (BR) vs. 56% (RCHOP/RCVP; P = .0025) [p.230]
- **OS**: Not statistically different [p.230]
- **Toxicity**: Higher incidences of vomiting, drug hypersensitivity reactions, opportunistic infections, and secondary malignancies with BR [p.230]

**SHINE Trial (Ibrutinib + BR vs. BR + Placebo)**

- **Trial Design**: Phase III randomized trial comparing ibrutinib + BR + rituximab maintenance (n = 261) vs. placebo + BR + rituximab maintenance (n = 262) in patients >65 years with previously untreated MCL [p.230]
- **Median PFS**: Superior with ibrutinib + BR (81 vs. 53 months; P = .01) at 85 months follow-up [p.230]
- **OS**: Not different; ibrutinib group had more TEAEs-related deaths (11% vs. 6%) [p.230]
- **NCCN Recommendation**: Panel consensus did not support inclusion of ibrutinib + BR for less aggressive induction therapy [p.230]

#### Bortezomib, Rituximab, Cyclophosphamide, Doxorubicin, and Prednisone (VR-CAP)

- **Trial Design**: Phase III randomized study (487 patients randomized; 268 included in final analysis: 140 VR-CAP, 128 RCHOP) comparing VR-CAP vs. RCHOP in newly diagnosed MCL not candidates for HDT/ASCR [p.230]
- **Patient Population**: 74% stage IV disease; 54% IPI ≥3 [p.230]
- **OS Outcomes**: Median OS significantly longer with VR-CAP vs. RCHOP (91 vs. 56 months; P = .001) at median follow-up of 82 months [p.230]
- **Toxicity**: Grade ≥3 TEAEs slightly higher with VR-CAP (93% vs. 84%) but manageable [p.230]
- **NCCN Category**: Preferred regimen [p.230]

#### Lenalidomide + Rituximab

- **Trial Design**: Multicenter phase II study (n = 38) evaluating lenalidomide + rituximab as induction and maintenance therapy for previously untreated MCL [p.230]
- **Response Rate**: ORR 92% (64% CR) [p.230]
- **Short-Term Outcomes**: 3-year PFS 80% and OS 90% (median follow-up 64 months) [p.230]
- **Long-Term Outcomes**: 5-year estimated PFS 64% and OS 77% [p.230]
- **Long-Term Follow-Up (103 months)**: 9-year PFS 51% and OS 66% [p.230]
- **Prognostic Factors**: High-risk MIPI predicted unfavorable OS; Ki-67 had no impact on PFS or OS [p.230]
- **NCCN Category**: Preferred regimen [p.230]

#### RCHOP

- **Evidence**: Earlier studies showed addition of rituximab to CHOP resulted in higher response rates but did not translate to prolonged PFS or OS [p.231]
- **Current Status**: Development of modern regimens (BR, TRIANGLE, VR-CAP) resulted in diminishing role for RCHOP [p.231]
- **NCCN Category**: Less preferred due to availability of superior options [p.231]

#### Acalabrutinib + Rituximab (Other Recommended Regimen)

- **Trial Design**: Phase II trial (n = 50) for older patients ≥65 years with untreated MCL; 12 patients had TP53 aberrations [p.231]
- **Response Rate**: ORR 94% (90% CR) [p.231]
- **Survival**: Median PFS and OS not reached at median follow-up of 28 months [p.231]
- **2-Year Outcomes**: PFS 94% and OS 96% [p.231]
- **NCCN Category**: Other Recommended regimen [p.231]

### TP53-Mutated Classical MCL

**Prognostic Impact**

- TP53 mutations in younger patients associated with inferior responses to both induction chemoimmunotherapy and HDT/ASCR [p.226]
- In analysis of 183 younger patients from Nordic MCL trials, TP53 mutations significantly associated with Ki-67 >30%, blastoid morphology, and inferior responses to induction and HDT/ASCR [p.226]

**NCCN Testing Recommendations**

- TP53 sequencing is an essential prognostic test to identify TP53-mutated classical MCL and aids in treatment selection, particularly if upfront HDT/ASCR is anticipated [p.227]
- European MCL Network: High TP53 expression by IHC is strong predictor of shorter TTF and OS, independent of Ki-67 [p.227]
- Conflicting reports on using TP53 expression by IHC as surrogate for TP53 mutation; sequencing preferred but IHC can be used for initial screening in front-line setting, should be confirmed with sequencing [p.227]
- Participation in clinical trials strongly suggested for TP53-mutated classical MCL [p.227]

**Zanubrutinib/Obinutuzumab/Venetoclax**

- **Trial Design**: Multicenter phase II trial (n = 25) with previously untreated TP53-mutated classic MCL [p.226]
- **Response Rate**: ORR 96% (88% CR) [p.226]
- **PFS and OS**: 2-year PFS 72% and OS 76% (median follow-up 28 months) [p.226]
- **MRD Status**: uMRD5 (<10–5) achieved in 95% and uMRD6 (<10–6) in 84% of patients [p.226]
- **MRD-Directed Treatment Stopping**: Treatment discontinued after 24 cycles for CR with uMRD, suggesting MRD-directed discontinuation may be effective approach [p.226]
- **Note**: Long-term follow-up needed to confirm findings [p.226]
- **NCCN Recommendation**: Option for all patients regardless of aggressive therapy eligibility [p.227]

**SYMPATICO Trial (Ibrutinib + Venetoclax in Relapsed/Refractory)**

- **Trial Design**: Study with subgroup analysis of 73 patients with TP53-mutated MCL [p.226]
- **Response Rates**: ORR 84% (57% CR) [p.226]
- **Median PFS**: 21 months [p.226]

**Alternative for Transplant-Eligible Patients**

- Alternating RCHOP + ibrutinib/RDHA + platinum (carboplatin, cisplatin, or oxaliplatin) is included as option based on TRIANGLE study results [p.227]
- **NCCN Category**: Included as option for patients suitable for aggressive induction therapy [p.227]

## Second-Line and Subsequent Therapy Evidence

### Relapsed/Refractory Disease Context

**Prognostic Factors**

- Early treatment failure (disease relapse and second-line therapy initiation within 12 months after upfront autologous HCT) and POD within 24 months of diagnosis associated with poor prognosis [p.234]
- Patients with disease progression after ≥2 lines of therapy are high-risk group with shortened median time to subsequent relapse [p.234]

**Treatment Selection by POD Timing**

- In MANTLE-FIRST study evaluating outcomes after cytarabine-based induction chemoimmunotherapy, ibrutinib particularly effective for refractory disease and early POD; bendamustine-based regimens had similar efficacy to ibrutinib for late POD [p.234]

### Preferred Regimens

#### Covalent BTK Inhibitors

**Acalabrutinib**

- **Trial Design**: Phase II study (n = 124) with relapsed or refractory MCL [p.234]
- **Response Rate**: ORR 81% (40% CR) [p.234]
- **Survival Outcomes**: 12-month PFS 67% and OS 87% (median follow-up 15 months) [p.234]
- **Common Toxicities (Grade 1–2)**: Headache (38%), diarrhea (36%), fatigue (28%), cough (22%), bleeding (22%), myalgia (21%) [p.234]
- **Common Grade 3–4 Toxicities**: Anemia (10%), neutropenia (10%), pneumonia (6%), infections (15%) [p.234]
- **Long-Term Follow-Up (26 months)**: 24-month PFS 49% and OS 72%; median PFS 20 months and median OS not reached [p.235]
- **Morphology Impact**: Higher ORRs in patients with blastoid or pleomorphic MCL with Ki-67 ≥50% [p.235]
- **Serious Infections**: Incidence decreased with longer follow-up (2% grade ≥3) [p.235]
- **FDA Status**: FDA-approved indication for relapsed/refractory MCL [p.234]
- **NCCN Category**: Preferred regimen [p.234]

**Zanubrutinib**

- **Trial Design**: Two multicenter single-arm clinical trials [p.235]
  - Phase II study (n = 86) with relapsed/refractory MCL [p.235]
  - Phase I/II study (n = 48; 37 with relapsed/refractory MCL) [p.235]
- **Phase II Response Rate**: ORR 84% (69% CR) [p.235]
- **Phase II Survival**: Median PFS 22 months and estimated 12-month PFS 76% (median follow-up 18 months) [p.235]
- **Phase II Toxicities (Grade ≥3)**: Neutropenia (20%) and lung infection/pneumonia (9%); major bleeding in 3 patients with no atrial fibrillation reports [p.235]
- **Phase I/II Response Rate**: ORR 84% (25% CR) [p.235]
- **Phase I/II Survival**: Median PFS 21 months; 12-month EFS 87% and OS 83% (median follow-up 19 months) [p.235]
- **Phase I/II Toxicities (Grade ≥3)**: Infections (19%), anemia (13%), pneumonia (9%), myalgia (9%) [p.235]
- **FDA Status**: FDA-approved indication for relapsed/refractory MCL [p.234]
- **NCCN Category**: Preferred regimen [p.234]

#### Lenalidomide + Rituximab

- **Trial Design**: Phase I/II study (n = 52) with relapsed or refractory MCL [p.235]
- **Response Rate**: ORR 57% (36% CR) [p.235]
- **Durability**: Median DOR 19 months, median PFS 11 months, median OS 24 months [p.235]
- **Common Toxicities (Grade 3–4)**: Neutropenia (66%) and thrombocytopenia (23%) [p.235]
- **Activity After Ibrutinib**: Observational study (n = 58) showed clinical activity in relapsed/refractory MCL after prior ibrutinib therapy; addition of rituximab to lenalidomide improved ORR (27% vs. 15% for monotherapy) [p.235]
- **Common TEAEs**: Fatigue (38%) and cough, dizziness, dyspnea, nausea, peripheral edema (19% each) [p.235]
- **NCCN Category**: Preferred regimen [p.234]

### Other Recommended Regimens

#### Ibrutinib ± Rituximab

- **Trial Design**: Multiple phase II studies [p.235]
  - Multicenter phase II (n = 111) with relapsed/refractory MCL after median 3 prior therapies [p.235]
  - Phase II (n = 50) with relapsed/refractory MCL after median 3 prior therapies [p.235]
  - Phase III RAY trial comparing ibrutinib (n = 139) vs. temsirolimus (n = 141) in relapsed/refractory MCL [p.235]
- **Study 1 Results**: Ibrutinib monotherapy ORR 67% (23% CR) with median DOR 18 months (median follow-up 27 months) [p.235]
- **Study 1 Survival**: 24-month PFS 31% and OS 47% [p.235]
- **Study 2 Results**: Ibrutinib + rituximab CR 58% with median PFS 43 months (median follow-up 47 months) [p.235]
- **Study 2 Prognostic Factors**: Blastoid morphology, high-risk MIPI score, high Ki-67 associated with inferior survival [p.235]
- **RAY Trial (Ibrutinib vs. Temsirolimus)**: ORR significantly higher for ibrutinib (77% vs. 47%; P < .0001) [p.235]
- **RAY Trial Survival**: Median PFS significantly longer with ibrutinib (16 vs. 6 months) with trend toward improved OS (30 vs. 24 months; P = .06) at median follow-up of 39 months [p.235]
- **RAY Trial Safety**: Diarrhea (33%), fatigue (24%), cough (23%) most common any-grade TEAEs in ibrutinib group; thrombocytopenia (56%), anemia (44%), diarrhea (31%) most common in temsirolimus group [p.235]
- **NCCN Category**: Other Recommended regimen [p.234]

## Special Considerations

### Post-Induction Response Management

**Complete Response to Induction Therapy**

- No further treatment necessary for CR patients [p.225]
- Follow-up recommendations: Complete history/physical exam and laboratory assessment every 3–6 months for first 5 years, then annually [p.225]
- Surveillance imaging: CT scans no more than every 6 months for first 2 years post-treatment, then no more than annually in absence of symptoms [p.225]

**Partial Response or Progressive Disease After Less Aggressive Therapy**

- Patients with PR or disease progression after less aggressive chemoimmunotherapy ± ISRT should be treated as for relapsed disease [p.225]
- Patients with stage I or contiguous stage II non-bulky disease achieving PR or progression after ISRT alone should be treated as advanced-stage disease [p.225]

**Response to Prior ISRT**

- Disease relapse after ISRT alone (stage I or contiguous stage II non-bulky) should be treated as advanced-stage disease [p.225]

### Rituximab Maintenance Therapy

**After Aggressive Induction + HDT/ASCR**

- Rituximab maintenance associated with improved EFS, PFS, and OS [p.232]
- LYMA trial (n = 299): 4-year EFS 79%, PFS 83%, OS 89% for rituximab maintenance vs. 61%, 64%, 80% for observation [p.232]
- Nordic MCL follow-up: 5-year PFS 83% and OS 83% after rituximab maintenance vs. 63% and 79% after observation [p.232]

**After Less Aggressive Induction**

- Associated with substantially prolonged PFS and OS in older patients not candidates for HDT/ASCR [p.232]
- Phase III randomized trial (n = 316) of RCHOP/R-FC-treated patients randomized to rituximab or interferon maintenance: Median PFS 5 years vs. 2 years and median OS 10 years vs. 7 years with rituximab (censoring for COVID-19 deaths and post-treatment deaths) [p.232]
- 5-year PFS rates 50% (rituximab) vs. 22% (interferon; P < .0001) [p.232]
- 5-year OS rates 75% (rituximab) vs. 58% (interferon; P = .0026) [p.232]

**After Bendamustine + Rituximab**

- One randomized phase II study showed no benefit, but limited by small sample size [p.232]
- Large observational U.S. community study (n = 4,126): Rituximab maintenance after BR or RCHOP associated with longer TTNT and OS [p.232]
- Large U.S. academic centers study (n = 796): Rituximab maintenance after BR associated with improved EFS and OS [p.232]

**NCCN Recommendations**

- Rituximab maintenance (every 8 weeks × 2–3 years) recommended for non-HDT/ASCR-eligible patients in remission after less aggressive regimens (category 1 following RCHOP) [p.234]

### Role of HDT/ASCR Consolidation

**Historical Evidence**

- Phase III randomized trial (n = 269): Survival benefit for HDT/ASCR vs. interferon alfa maintenance (PFS 39 vs. 17 months; OS 7.5 vs. 5 years) in patients ≤65 years with advanced MCL in remission after CHOP ± rituximab [p.231]

**Modern Era Evidence**

- TRIANGLE study (median follow-up 31 months): Induction with alternating RCHOP + ibrutinib/RDHAP followed by maintenance ibrutinib + rituximab superior to induction followed by HDT/ASCR (FFS rates 88% and 86% vs. 72%) [p.231]
- Both ibrutinib-containing arms (A+I, I) superior to transplant alone; FFS not significantly different between two ibrutinib-containing arms [p.231]
- Grade 3–5 neutropenia higher with A+I (44%) vs. ibrutinib (23%) vs. A alone (17%); did not translate to higher infection rates [p.231]
- Results suggest alternating RCHOP + ibrutinib/RDHAP followed by maintenance may eliminate need for HDT/ASCR consolidation in patients <66 years [p.231]

**ECOG-ACRIN EA4151 Trial**

- **Trial Design**: Phase 3 randomized trial (n = 653; 73% received aggressive induction, 27% less aggressive) evaluating HDT/ASCR benefit in CR with uMRD6 patients [p.231]
- **Study Population**: 516 patients with CR and uMRD6 randomized to HDT/ASCR + maintenance rituximab (n = 257) vs. maintenance rituximab alone (n = 259); remaining with MRD-positive/indeterminate CR (n = 134) randomized to HDT/ASCR + maintenance rituximab [p.231]
- **Primary Outcome**: No significant PFS or OS differences between HDT/ASCR and maintenance-only arms [p.231]
- **3-Year Survival**: 77% PFS for both arms A and B; 82% (A) and 83% (B) OS [p.231]
- **MRD-Positive Subgroup (Arm C, n = 49)**: Exploratory analysis showed higher survival rates for CR with uMRD6 after HDT/ASCR (3-year PFS and OS 100%) vs. detectable MRD (3-year PFS and OS 49% and 64%), suggesting MRD-positive patients may benefit from HDT/ASCR [p.231]
- **Current Role**: HDT/ASCR first-line consolidation role lessening with improved induction regimens; appropriate patient selection critical [p.231]

**NCCN Recommendation**

- HDT/ASCR followed by maintenance with BTKi and rituximab remains option for CR consolidation based on historical data and practice of some NCCN Member Institutions; however, increasingly narrow scope with improved inductions and identification of subsets unlikely to benefit [p.234]

### Management of Suboptimal Response to Induction

**Partial Response to Aggressive Induction**

- Covalent BTKi is preferred treatment option [p.234]
- Alternatively, additional treatment with second-line chemoimmunotherapy regimens may improve response quality [p.234]
- HDT/ASCR consolidation may be considered for remission-achieving patients following additional therapy [p.234]

**Stable or Progressive Disease**

- Treated as relapsed/refractory disease [p.234]
## Summary of Key Evidence

- Ibrutinib: November 2013 accelerated approval (phase II) based on ORR; April 2023 accelerated approval withdrawn following phase III SHINE study [p.236]
- Ibrutinib monotherapy or ibrutinib + rituximab: continue as second-line option despite approval status change [p.236]
- Panel consensus: acalabrutinib and zanubrutinib preferred over ibrutinib for second-line therapy due to superior safety profile in head-to-head trials [p.236]
- Venetoclax-based regimens (single agent or combinations): active in relapsed/refractory MCL with high-risk features including TP53 mutations [p.236]
- CAR-T cell therapy (tisagenlecleucel): approved for relapsed/refractory MCL; infectious adverse events 33% (8 COVID-19, 5 pneumonia deaths) [p.238]

## First-Line Therapy Evidence

- SYMPATICO phase III randomized study (n = 267): evaluated first-line treatment approaches in MCL [p.238]
- Initial treatment selection based on prognostic factors including MCL International Prognostic Index [p.242]
- Intensive immunochemotherapy with ASCT considered for eligible patients [p.242]

## Second-Line and Subsequent Therapy Evidence

- **BTK Inhibitors**: 
  - Ibrutinib: phase II accelerated approval (2013) later withdrawn; continues as option based on earlier efficacy and safety data [p.236]
  - Acalabrutinib and zanubrutinib: demonstrated more favorable safety profile versus ibrutinib in B-cell malignancy trials [p.236]

- **Venetoclax-Based Regimens**:
  - Single agent venetoclax or venetoclax + BTKi or venetoclax + anti-CD20 mAb in relapsed/refractory MCL [p.236]
  - Venetoclax ± rituximab and ibrutinib + venetoclax included as options for second-line therapy [p.236]
  - Activity demonstrated in prespecified subgroups including TP53-mutated MCL [p.236]

- **CAR-T Cell Therapy**:
  - Tisagenlecleucel: approved for relapsed/refractory MCL [p.238]
  - Infectious adverse events: 33% of patients; fatal infections (COVID-19, pneumonia) reported [p.238]
  - CRS higher in MCL vs. DLBCL; benefits and risks require careful evaluation [p.239]

## Special Considerations

- **Adverse Events & Toxicity**:
  - Ibrutinib: grade ≥3 bleeding (9% vs. 5%) and atrial fibrillation (5% vs. 1%) higher than temsirolimus comparison [p.236]
  - CAR-T pretreatment with obinutuzumab: 2000 mg reduces CRS (64% vs. 88%; grade ≥2: 23% vs. 63%) vs. 1000 mg [p.238]
  - Higher CRS incidence with CAR-T in MCL population compared to DLBCL [p.239]

- **Prognostic Factors**:
  - MCL International Prognostic Index: confirmed for survival prediction following intensive first-line therapy [p.242]
  - TP53 status: prognostic in MCL; TP53-mutated disease included in venetoclax trial subgroup analyses [p.236]

- **Patient Populations**:
  - Treatment selection based on age, comorbidities, and MCL prognostic risk factors [p.238-239]
  - High-risk MCL (TP53 mutations, complex karyotype): venetoclax-based combinations recommended [p.236]
