## Diagnosis and Definition

PMBL can be defined as a clinical entity presenting with primary site of disease in the anterior mediastinum with or without other sites and has histology of DLBCL [p.82]. Clinical pathologic correlation is required to establish diagnosis [p.82]. PMBL overlaps with MGZL that have intermediate features between Hodgkin lymphoma and PMBL and have unique diagnostic characteristics [p.82].

Note: See Mediastinal Gray Zone Lymphoma (BCEL-B 2 of 2) and Special Considerations for Adolescent and Young Adult (AYA) Patients with B-Cell Lymphomas (NHODG-B 4 of 5) [p.82].

## Initial Assessment and Restaging

**Response Assessment Criteria:** Lugano Response Criteria for Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma [p.82].

**Imaging Interpretation:** PET/CT scan should be interpreted via the PET 5-PS [p.82].

## First-Line Therapy

Note: All recommendations are category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.82]. Optimal first-line therapy for PMBL is more controversial than other subtypes [p.82]. Most commonly used treatment options are listed below [p.82].

### Preferred Regimens

**DA-EPOCH-R (Dose-adjusted EPOCH + rituximab)** (Category 2A) [p.82]
- x 6 cycles [p.82]

**R-CHOP-14** (Category 2A) [p.82]
- x 4–6 cycles [p.82]

**R-CHOP-21** (Category 2A) [p.82]
- x 6 cycles [p.82]

## Response Assessment and Subsequent Management

### Complete Response

PET negative [5-PS 1–3] [p.82]
- Proceed to Follow-Up / Surveillance [p.82]

### Partial Response

PET positive [5-PS 4] [p.82]

**Option 1: ICE Consolidation** (Category 2B) [p.82]
- After DA-EPOCH-R x 6 cycles or R-CHOP-14 x 6 cycles or R-CHOP-21 x 6 cycles or R-CHOP-14 x 4 cycles [p.82]
- Followed by consolidation with ICE x 3 cycles ± rituximab [p.82]

**Option 2: Involved-Site Radiation Therapy (ISRT) + Restaging** [p.82]
- ISRT followed by restaging with PET/CT [p.82]
- If negative: Active surveillance or ISRT [p.82]
- If positive: Repeat biopsy; if biopsy positive, consider relapsed/refractory therapy [p.82]

**Option 3: Active Surveillance** [p.82]
- Persistent PET/CT positive masses at end of treatment after DA-EPOCH-R (5-PS 4 and on visual inspection demonstrate minimal uptake above liver) can be observed (with follow-up scans) without biopsy [p.82]

### No Response or Progressive Disease

PET positive [5-PS 5] [p.82]
- Repeat biopsy [p.82]
- If biopsy positive: Proceed to Relapsed/Refractory Therapy [p.82]

## Relapsed or Refractory Therapy

**Option 1: Pembrolizumab** (Category 2A) [p.82]

**Option 2: Nivolumab ± brentuximab vedotin** (Category 2B) [p.82]

**Option 3: Manage as Relapsed/Refractory DLBCL** [p.82]
- If relapse <12 months: See BCEL-7 [p.82]
- If relapse >12 months: See BCEL-8 [p.82]

## Key Clinical Notes

**Prophylaxis:** Consider prophylaxis for tumor lysis syndrome and viral reactivation [p.82].

**Biosimilars:** An FDA-approved biosimilar is an appropriate substitute for any recommended systemic biologic therapy in the NCCN Guidelines [p.82]. Rituximab and hyaluronidase human injection for subcutaneous use may be substituted for rituximab after patients have received the first full dose of rituximab by intravenous infusion [p.82].

**Residual Mediastinal Masses:** Residual mediastinal masses are common. PET/CT scan is essential post-treatment. Biopsy of PET/CT scan positive mass is recommended if additional systemic treatment is contemplated [p.82].

**CD30 Expression:** Responses with brentuximab vedotin have been seen in patients with a low level of CD30 positivity and any level of CD30 positivity is acceptable for the use of brentuximab vedotin–based regimens [p.82].

**Surveillance Imaging:** Surveillance imaging is used for monitoring asymptomatic patients. When a site of disease can only be visualized on PET/CT scan (e.g., bone), it is appropriate to proceed with PET/CT scans for surveillance [p.82].

## Follow-Up / Surveillance

### Clinical Follow-Up

- H&P and labs, every 3–6 months for 5 years and then annually or as clinically indicated [p.82]

### Imaging Follow-Up

- C/A/P CT scan with contrast no more often than every 6 months for 2 years after completion of treatment, then only as clinically indicated [p.82]
