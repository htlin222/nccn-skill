## Summary of Key Evidence

- Histologic transformation of follicular lymphoma (FL) to DLBCL or high-grade B-cell lymphomas (HGBL) with MYC translocations occurs at an estimated annual rate of 2% to 3% and is generally associated with poor clinical outcome [p.297][3-6].
- HGBL with double-hit cytogenetics (MYC with either BCL2 or BCL6 rearrangement) is associated with inferior survival compared to transformed FL (TFL) without double-hit cytogenetics (2-year survival rates of 50% and 73%, respectively) [p.297][7].
- In a pooled analysis of French and U.S. cohorts, histologically TFL after diagnosis was the leading cause of death (77 of 140 deaths) [p.297][8].
- The incidence of histologic transformation of marginal zone lymphoma (MZL) to DLBCL is lower than that of FL, with higher incidences reported in nodal MZL and splenic MZL [p.297][14,15].
- Early initiation of treatment at diagnosis relative to active surveillance has been suggested to decrease the risk of transformation in some studies; however, a randomized phase III trial found no difference in time to transformation or incidence of histologic transformation between immediate rituximab treatment and active surveillance groups [p.297][13].

## Diagnosis

### Diagnostic Confirmation
- Diagnosis of histologic transformation to DLBCL should be confirmed by excisional lymph node biopsy (if lymph node is accessible) from a site with a maximum standardized uptake value (SUVmax) >5 [p.298][1].
- Fine-needle aspiration (FNA) biopsy alone is not generally suitable for initial diagnosis. When lymph node is not easily accessible for excisional or incisional biopsy, a combination of core needle biopsy (multiple biopsies preferred) and FNA biopsies with appropriate ancillary techniques may be acceptable [p.298][1].

### Role of PET Scan
- Sites of disease with discordantly high SUVmax on PET scan (baseline SUVmax >10 or >20) should raise suspicion of histologic transformation to DLBCL and can direct the optimal site of biopsy [p.298][22-24].
- However, a retrospective analysis of the GALLIUM study showed that higher baseline SUVmax was not associated with subsequent histologic transformation [p.298][25].
- PET scan findings alone are not sufficient for diagnosis; histologic confirmation and evaluation of clinical features are necessary [p.298].

## Risk Factors for Transformation

### Follicular Lymphoma
Risk factors for FL transformation to DLBCL include [p.297][3-5,9-11]:
- Advanced-stage FL
- High-risk Follicular Lymphoma International Prognostic Index (FLIPI) and International Prognostic Index (IPI) scores at diagnosis
- Elevated lactate dehydrogenase (LDH)
- B symptoms at diagnosis

### Marginal Zone Lymphoma
Risk factors for MZL transformation to DLBCL include [p.297][14-20]:
- Nodal MZL subtype
- High-risk IPI scores
- Elevated LDH
- B symptoms
- Disease involvement in multiple mucosal sites and greater than 4 nodal sites at diagnosis
- Inability to achieve complete response (CR) after initial treatment

## Histologic Transformation After Minimal or No Prior Therapy

### Recommended First-Line Treatment
- Anthracycline-based chemoimmunotherapy (with regimens recommended for first-line therapy for DLBCL, unless contraindicated) with or without involved-site radiation therapy (ISRT) is recommended for histologic transformation of FL to either DLBCL or HGBL with MYC and BCL6 rearrangements and transformed MZL [p.300][10,11].
- Histologic transformation of FL to HGBL with MYC and BCL2 rearrangements should be managed with more aggressive chemoimmunotherapy regimens as recommended for HGBL with translocations of MYC and BCL6 with or without BCL2 [p.300].

### Treatment Outcomes with RCHOP
- In a series of 60 patients with biopsy-proven TFL, RCHOP was the most common treatment (n = 35; 59%) associated with a median overall survival (OS) of 50 months [p.298][5].
- The 5-year OS rate was 66% for patients with TFL treated with RCHOP, which was similar to the outcome of patients with de novo DLBCL treated with RCHOP in the validation cohort (5-year OS rate of 73%) [p.298][5].

### Outcomes with Rituximab-Based Therapy
- In the National LymphoCare Study (147 patients with pathologically confirmed TFL and 232 with clinically suspected TFL), the majority of patients were treated with rituximab-based therapy (26% received rituximab monotherapy and 35% received chemotherapy with rituximab) [p.298][11].
- At a median follow-up of 7 years, the median progression-free survival (PFS) and OS were 12 months and 60 months, respectively, for patients with biopsy-proven histologic transformation to DLBCL [p.298][11].

### Maintenance Therapy
- If transformation is coexisting with extensive FL, rituximab maintenance should be considered for patients achieving CR [p.300][49,50].

### Second-Line Management After Initial Therapy
- Active surveillance or enrollment in a clinical trial are included as treatment options for patients achieving CR to initial treatment [p.300][29,31,33].
- CAR T-cell therapy (if eligible) is recommended for patients achieving partial response (PR) or those with progressive or non-responsive disease [p.300].
- For patients not candidates for CAR T-cell therapy, second-line therapy options are same as described for Histologic Transformation After Multiple Lines of Prior Therapies [p.300].
- Repeat biopsy should be strongly considered if PET-positive prior to additional therapy, because PET positivity may represent post-treatment inflammation [p.300].

## Histologic Transformation After Multiple Lines of Prior Therapies

### Treatment Strategy
- Enrollment in an appropriate clinical trial is the preferred option [p.300].
- In the absence of a suitable clinical trial, systemic therapy with or without ISRT, ISRT alone, or best supportive care are included as treatment options [p.300][10].

### First-Line Options for Multiple Prior Therapies
- RCHOP (if not previously used; rituximab, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine, and prednisone), cytarabine-based, or gemcitabine-based chemotherapy (with or without rituximab) [p.300].
- T-cell engager therapy: CD-19 directed CAR T-cell therapy (axicabtagene ciloleucel, tisagenlecleucel, and lisocabtagene maraleucel) and bispecific antibodies (epcoritamab and glofitamab) [p.300].

### Alternative Options for No Intention to Proceed to Transplant
- Tafasitamab in combination with lenalidomide [p.301].
- Loncastuximab tesirine [p.301].
- Selinexor [p.301].
- Epcoritamab or glofitamab [p.301].
- Polatuzumab vedotin (with or without bendamustine or rituximab) [p.301].

### Selinexor-Specific Recommendations
- Selinexor is included as a treatment option for TFL only after at least two lines of systemic therapy, including patients with disease progression after transplant or CAR T-cell therapy [p.301].

### Consolidation Therapy Options
- Consolidation therapy with HDT/ASCR (high-dose therapy followed by autologous stem cell rescue) with or without ISRT (if not previously given) or active surveillance are included as treatment options for patients achieving CR [p.301][29,31,33].
- Allogeneic hematopoietic cell transplantation (HCT) should be considered only in selected patients [p.301].

### Second-Line Therapy Options After Partial Response
- Active surveillance [p.301].
- Allogeneic HCT with or without ISRT [p.301].
- CAR T-cell therapy (if not previously given) [p.301].
- ISRT for localized residual disease and/or residual fluorodeoxyglucose (FDG)-avid disease not previously irradiated [p.301].
- If proceeding to transplant, additional systemic therapy ± ISRT should be considered to induce CR prior to transplant [p.301].
- Data on the efficacy of transplant in patients who have received CAR T-cell therapy are not available [p.301].
- HDT/ASCR is not recommended after CAR T-cell therapy; allogeneic HCT could be considered but remains investigational [p.301].

### Management of Non-Responsive or Progressive Disease
- Patients with non-responsive or progressive disease can be treated with any of the treatment options not received previously if they are candidates for additional therapy [p.301].
- Repeat biopsy should be strongly considered prior to additional therapy, because PET positivity may represent post-treatment inflammation [p.301].

## CAR T-Cell Therapies

### Regulatory Approval and Eligibility
- Chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) T-cell therapy (axicabtagene ciloleucel, lisocabtagene maraleucel, and tisagenlecleucel) is FDA-approved for transformed indolent lymphomas after ≥2 systemic therapy regimens [p.299].
- Axicabtagene ciloleucel and tisagenlecleucel are approved for TFL, whereas lisocabtagene maraleucel is approved for TFL and MZL [p.299].

### ZUMA-1 (Axicabtagene Ciloleucel)
- ZUMA-1 trial included 16% of patients with TFL (n = 16), with the CAR T-cell therapy resulting in favorable clinical outcomes across all subgroups [p.299][37].

### JULIET (Tisagenlecleucel)
- JULIET trial included 18% of patients with TFL (n = 21), with the CAR T-cell therapy resulting in favorable clinical outcomes across all subgroups [p.299][38].

### TRANSCEND NHL 001 (Lisocabtagene Maraleucel)
- TRANSCEND NHL 001 trial included 60 patients with TFL and 18 patients with transformed indolent lymphomas other than FL (transformed indolent non-Hodgkin lymphoma [iNHL]; 10 patients had transformed MZL) [p.299][39].
- Overall response rate (ORR) was 84% and 61% for patients with TFL and transformed iNHL, respectively [p.299][39].
- The median PFS and OS were not reached for patients with TFL; median PFS and OS were 3 months and 7 months, respectively, for patients with transformed iNHL [p.299][39].

### ZUMA-7 (Axicabtagene Ciloleucel vs. Chemoimmunotherapy)
- ZUMA-7 trial included 13% of patients with TFL (n = 46; 19 patients randomized to axicabtagene ciloleucel) [p.299][40].
- ORR was 89% for patients with TFL randomized to axicabtagene ciloleucel compared to 56% for those randomized to chemoimmunotherapy followed by HDT/ASCR [p.299][40].
- CAR T-cell therapy resulted in significantly improved ORR and event-free survival (EFS) across all subgroups [p.299][40].

### TRANSFORM (Lisocabtagene Maraleucel vs. Chemoimmunotherapy)
- TRANSFORM trial included 8% of patients with transformed indolent lymphomas (n = 15; 7 patients randomized to lisocabtagene maraleucel) [p.299][41].
- CAR T-cell therapy resulted in significantly improved ORR and EFS across all subgroups [p.299][41].

### Real-World CAR T-Cell Outcomes
- A real-world retrospective analysis evaluated axicabtagene ciloleucel in relapsed/refractory large B-cell lymphomas (n = 298; 76 patients had TFL) [p.299-300][42].
- A 62% CR at 12 months was reported as the best response rate for patients with TFL [p.300][42].
- The 12-month PFS and OS rates were 51% and 70%, respectively, for patients with TFL [p.300][42].

### Comparative Real-World Outcomes
- A real-world multicenter retrospective analysis included 212 patients with transformed indolent lymphoma (TFL, n = 192; transformed MZL, n = 14) and 576 patients with relapsed/refractory de novo DLBCL [p.300][44].
- ORR was 78% (63% CR) for patients in the transformed indolent lymphoma cohort, which was comparable to the ORR of 77% (55% CR) for patients in the de novo DLBCL cohort [p.300][44].
- At a median overall follow-up of 13 months, median PFS and OS were 11 months and 42 months, respectively, for patients in the transformed indolent lymphoma cohort (corresponding median PFS and OS were 8 months and 30 months, respectively, for patients in the de novo DLBCL cohort) [p.300][44].
- The 24-month PFS and OS rates were 39% and 66%, respectively, for patients in the transformed indolent lymphoma cohort (the corresponding PFS and OS rates were 40% and 53%, respectively, for patients in the de novo DLBCL cohort) [p.300][44].

## Bispecific Antibodies

### Epcoritamab
- Epcoritamab is FDA-approved for TFL and MZL (after ≥2 systemic therapy regimens) [p.300].
- In the EPCORE NHL-1 trial that included 40 patients with transformed indolent lymphomas (without identification of the source of the transformation), epcoritamab resulted in an ORR of 68% (45% CR) in patients with transformed indolent lymphomas [p.300][47].

### Glofitamab
- Glofitamab is FDA-approved for TFL (after ≥2 systemic therapy regimens) [p.300].
- In a phase II study of glofitamab for patients with relapsed or refractory DLBCL (including 27 patients with TFL), the CR rate was 50% for patients with TFL [p.300][48].

## Other Targeted Therapies

### Loncastuximab Tesirine
- Loncastuximab tesirine (anti-CD19 antibody drug conjugate) resulted in an ORR of 45% (24% CR) among patients with transformed indolent lymphomas included in the LOTIS-2 trial (29 patients) [p.299][35].

### Selinexor
- Selinexor (selective small molecule inhibitor of XPO1-mediated nuclear export) resulted in an ORR of 39% among patients with transformed indolent lymphomas included in the SADAL trial (31 patients) [p.299][36].

### Tafasitamab and Lenalidomide
- Tafasitamab in combination with lenalidomide is FDA-approved for the treatment of relapsed/refractory DLBCL including DLBCL arising from transformation of indolent lymphomas [p.299].
- L-MIND study (anti-CD19 antibody tafasitamab in combination with lenalidomide for relapsed/refractory DLBCL) included only eight patients with transformed indolent lymphoma, with three patients achieving a CR and four patients achieving a PR [p.299].

### Polatuzumab Vedotin
- Polatuzumab vedotin (with or without bendamustine or rituximab) is included as an option for patients with no intention to proceed to transplant [p.301].
- Patients with transformed lymphoma were excluded from the clinical trial that evaluated polatuzumab vedotin for relapsed/refractory DLBCL, and its inclusion is based on the extrapolation of data for polatuzumab vedotin for relapsed/refractory DLBCL [p.301][51].

## High-Dose Therapy and Transplantation

### Autologous Stem Cell Rescue (HDT/ASCR)
- HDT/ASCR as consolidation therapy has been evaluated only in retrospective studies [p.298-299][26-33].
- Some series report survival benefit for patients who proceeded to transplant [p.299][29,31,33].
- The efficacy of HDT/ASCR in patients with TFL has not been confirmed in prospective controlled studies [p.299].

### Allogeneic HCT
- Allogeneic HCT has been shown to benefit selected patients with disease relapse following HDT/ASCR [p.299][29,30,32,34].
- Allogeneic HCT is also associated with significant transplant-related mortality (TRM) [p.299][29,30,32,34].
- The efficacy of allogeneic HCT in patients with TFL has not been confirmed in prospective controlled studies [p.299].
