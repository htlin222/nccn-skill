## Overview

The LL category comprises two diseases: T-cell LL (T-LL; 90%) and B-cell LL (B-LL; 10%), which corresponds to T-cell acute lymphoblastic leukemia (T-ALL) and B-cell acute lymphoblastic leukemia (B-ALL), respectively, with presentations in extramedullary sites [p.104]. See --> Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia Guidelines - Cytogenetic and Molecular Prognostic Risk Stratification for B-ALL [UNRESOLVED: acute-lymphoblastic-leukemia.md]

This disease is complex and curable; treatment should occur at centers with expertise in the management of the disease [p.104].

## Diagnosis and Additional Testing

### Essential Diagnostic Testing [p.104]

**Adequate immunophenotyping to establish diagnosis** [p.104]

- **IHC panel:** CD45, CD19, CD20, CD79a, CD3, CD2, CD5, CD7, TdT, CD1a, CD10, cyclin D1, myeloperoxidase, lysozyme, CD34, CD4, CD8 [p.104]
  - **with or without:** Flow cytometry with peripheral blood and/or biopsy specimen: kappa/lambda, CD45, CD3, CD5, CD4, CD7, CD8, CD19, CD20, CD10, TdT, CD13, CD33, CD34, CD1a, cytoplasmic CD3, CD22, myeloperoxidase [p.104]

**Genetic/Molecular Studies** [p.104]

- Karyotype ± FISH: MYC; t(9;22); t(8;14); and variants or PCR for BCR::ABL1 [p.104]

**Typical Immunophenotype** [p.104]

- B-LL: sIg-, CD10+/-, CD19+, CD20-/+, TdT+, CD34+, CD79a+ [p.104]
- T-LL: sIg-, CD10-, CD19/20-, CD3-/+, CD4/8+/+, CD1a+/-, TdT+, CD2+, CD7+ cytoplasmic CD3+, sCD3-/+ [p.104]

### Useful Under Certain Circumstances [p.104]

**Additional immunohistochemical studies to establish lymphoma subtype** [p.104]

- IHC panel: CD22, CD4, CD8, cyclin D1 [p.104]

**Molecular analysis** [p.104]

- Ig gene rearrangements [p.104]

## Workup

### Essential Workup [p.104]

- Physical exam: attention to node-bearing areas, including Waldeyer's ring, and to size of liver and spleen [p.104]
- Performance status [p.104]
- B symptoms [p.104]
- CBC with differential [p.104]
- LDH [p.104]
- Comprehensive metabolic panel [p.104]
- Uric acid, phosphate [p.104]
- C/A/P CT with contrast of diagnostic quality [p.104]
- Lumbar puncture [p.104]
- Flow cytometry of cerebrospinal fluid [p.104]
- Bilateral or unilateral bone marrow biopsy ± aspirate with flow and cytogenetics [p.104]
- Hepatitis B testing: Tests include HBsAg and core antibody for a patient with no risk factors. For patients with risk factors or previous history of hepatitis B, add e-antigen. If positive, check viral load and consider consult with gastroenterologist. Hepatitis B testing is indicated because of the risk of reactivation with immunotherapy + chemotherapy [p.104]
- Echocardiogram or MUGA scan if anthracycline or anthracenedione-based regimen is indicated [p.104]
- Pregnancy testing in patients of childbearing age (if chemotherapy or RT planned) [p.104]

### Useful in Selected Cases [p.104]

- Beta-2-microglobulin [p.104]
- Brain MRI with and without contrast [p.104]
- PET/CT scan: Initiation of therapy should not be delayed in order to obtain a PET/CT scan [p.104]
- Discuss fertility preservation options: sperm banking, semen cryopreservation, IVF, or ovarian tissue or oocyte cryopreservation [p.104]
