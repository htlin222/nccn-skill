## Diagnosis and Additional Testing

**Note:** All recommendations are category 2A unless otherwise indicated [p.14].

### Differential Diagnosis Considerations

- Germinal center (GC) or follicular center cell phenotype is not equivalent to FL and occurs in BL and some DLBCL [p.14]
- Typical immunophenotype: CD10+, BCL2+, CD23+/-, CD43-, CD5-, CD20+, BCL6+. Rare cases of FL may be CD10- or BCL2- [p.14]
- In young patients with localized disease that lacks BCL2 expression or t(14;18), differential diagnosis should include PTFL in adult; BCL2-R negative, CD23-positive follicle center lymphoma (ICC)/dFL (WHO5) and LBCL with IRF4/MUM1 rearrangement [p.14]

### Essential Diagnostic Testing

- **Adequate immunophenotyping to establish diagnosis** [p.14]
  - IHC panel: CD20, CD3, CD5, CD10, BCL2, BCL6, CD21, or CD23, with or without Flow cytometry with peripheral blood and/or biopsy specimen: kappa/lambda, CD19, CD20, CD5, CD23, CD10 [p.14]

### Useful Under Certain Circumstances

- Molecular analysis to detect: Ig gene rearrangements; BCL2 rearrangements [p.14]
- Karyotype or FISH: t(14;18); BCL6, 1p36, IRF4/MUM1 rearrangements [p.14]
  - BCL2-R negative, CD23-positive follicle center lymphoma (ICC)/dFL (WHO5) has a predominantly diffuse pattern, pelvis/inguinal location, and common STAT6 mutations along with 1p36 deletion or TNFRSF14 mutation [p.14]
  - LBCL with IRF4/MUM1 rearrangement are usually DLBCL but occasionally are purely FL3B (ICC/FLBCL [WHO5]) and often DLBCL with FL3B. Patients typically present with Waldeyer's ring involvement and are often children/young adults. These lymphomas are locally aggressive but respond well to chemotherapy ± radiation therapy (RT). They do not have a BCL2 rearrangement and should not be treated as low-grade FL [p.14]
- IHC panel: Ki-67; IRF4/MUM1 for FL grade 3, cyclin D1 [p.14]
  - FL1–2 with a Ki-67 proliferation fraction of >30% may be associated with a more aggressive clinical behavior, but there is no evidence that this should guide treatment decisions [p.14]
- Next-generation sequencing (NGS) panel including CREBBP, EZH2, MAP2K1, TNFRSF14, and STAT6 mutation [p.14]

## Disease Classification

### Classic FL (cFL; WHO5)

- Manage as FOLL-2 [p.14]
- In the 2022 WHO classification (WHO5), follicular lymphoma (FL) grades 1, 2, and 3A are termed as classic FL (cFL). International Consensus classification (ICC) has retained the grading for FL (1–2, 3A, and 3B). FL1–2 (ICC) are managed according to the treatment recommendations for cFL (WHO5) [p.14]
- Any area of DLBCL in FL of any grade should be diagnosed and treated as a DLBCL (BCEL-1) [p.14]

### FL3B (ICC)/Follicular Large B-cell lymphoma (FLBCL; WHO5)

- Commonly treated as DLBCL (BCEL-1) [p.14]

### LBCL with IRF4/MUM1 Rearrangement

- Manage as BCEL-2 [p.14]

### Pediatric-type FL (PTFL) (in adults)

- Manage as cFL (FOLL-2) [p.14]

### BCL2-R negative, CD23-positive follicle center lymphoma (ICC)/FL with predominantly diffuse growth pattern (dFL) (WHO5)

- Manage as cFL (FOLL-2) [p.14]

### FL3A (ICC)

- The management of FL3A (ICC) is controversial and treatment should be individualized [p.14]

## Workup (FOLL-2)

The following workup applies to classic follicular lymphoma staging and risk stratification [p.15]:

### Staging/Imaging

- Positron emission tomography-computed tomography (PET-CT) scan [p.15]
- Computed tomography (CT) scan of chest, abdomen, and pelvis [p.15]
- Bone marrow biopsy (bilateral or unilateral provided core biopsy is >2 cm. If active surveillance is initial therapy, bone marrow biopsy may be deferred) [p.15]

### Laboratory Testing

- Complete blood count (CBC) [p.15]
- Comprehensive metabolic panel (CMP) [p.15]
- Lactate dehydrogenase (LDH) [p.15]
- HIV testing [p.15]
- Hepatitis B testing is indicated because of the risk of reactivation with immunotherapy + chemotherapy. Tests include hepatitis B surface antigen (HBsAg) and core antibody for a patient with no risk factors. For patients with risk factors or previous history of hepatitis B, add e-antigen (NHODG-B). If positive, check viral load and consider consult with gastroenterologist [p.15]
- Hepatitis C testing [p.15]

## First-Line Therapy

### Preferred Regimens (Category 1)

- **Bendamustine + rituximab** [p.25]
- **Bendamustine + obinutuzumab** [p.25]
- **Rituximab** (monotherapy) [p.25]

### Other Recommended Regimens (Category 2A)

- **R-CHOP** (rituximab, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine, prednisone) [p.25]
- **CHOP + obinutuzumab** [p.25]
- **R-CVP** (rituximab, cyclophosphamide, vincristine, prednisone) [p.25]
- **CVP + obinutuzumab** [p.25]
- **Lenalidomide + rituximab** [p.25]
- **Lenalidomide + obinutuzumab** [p.25]

### First-Line Consolidation or Extended Dosing

- **Chemoimmunotherapy followed by rituximab maintenance** [p.25]
- **Extended dosing with rituximab** [p.25]
- **Obinutuzimab-based chemoimmunotherapy followed by obinutuzumab maintenance** [p.25]

## Second-Line and Subsequent Therapy

### Second-Line Therapy

- **Bendamustine + obinutuzumab** [p.26]
- **Lenalidomide ± rituximab** [p.26]
- **Lenalidomide + obinutuzumab** [p.26]
- **Rituximab** (monotherapy) [p.26]
- **Tafasitamab-cxix + lenalidomide + rituximab** [p.26]
- **Tazemetostat** [p.26]

### Second-Line Consolidation or Extended Dosing

- **Rituximab maintenance** [p.26]
- **Obinutuzumab maintenance for rituximab refractory disease** [p.26]

### Third-Line and Subsequent Therapy

- **Loncastuximab tesirine-lpyl + rituximab** [p.26]
- **Zanubrutinib + obinutuzumab** [p.26]
