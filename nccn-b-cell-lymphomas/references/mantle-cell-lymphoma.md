## Overview

This section contains treatment algorithms and clinical guidance for managing diffuse large B-cell lymphoma and related aggressive B-cell lymphomas. The guidelines include staging algorithms, response assessment criteria, prognostic indices, and treatment regimens.

## Diagnosis and Additional Testing

### Immunophenotyping and Genetic Testing

Genetic alterations play important roles in diagnosis and treatment planning: [p.70]

- **Diagnostic significance:** ACTB, BCL2, BCL6, CARD11, CD79B, CREBBP, EZH2, KMT2D, MYC, MYD88, MEF2B, SOCS1 [p.70]
- **Non-GCB DLBCL:** BTK, CARD11, CD79B, MYD88 [p.70]
- **Treatment significance:** EZH2 mutations (associated with EZH2 inhibitor responsiveness), SMARCB1 [p.70]
- **TP53 mutations:** Frequent in DLBCL associated with chronic inflammation; TP53 mutations with MYC rearrangement are associated with worse prognosis [p.70]

**Recommended NGS panel:** A minimal list of genes should be included in lymphoma NGS panels for DLBCL diagnosis. [p.70]

## Staging / Risk Stratification

### International Prognostic Index (IPI)

The International Prognostic Index uses standard risk factors: [p.71]

- Age >60 years
- Serum LDH > normal
- Performance status 2–4
- Stage III or IV
- Extranodal involvement >1 site

**Risk stratification for all patients:** [p.71]
- Low: 0 or 1 factor
- Low-intermediate: 2 factors
- High-intermediate: 3 factors
- High: 4 or 5 factors

### Age-Adjusted IPI (Patients ≤60 Years)

For younger patients: [p.71]
- Low: 0 factors
- Low-intermediate: 1 factor
- High-intermediate: 2 factors
- High: 3 factors

Risk factors include: Stage III or IV, serum LDH > normal, performance status 2–4. [p.71]

### Stage-Modified IPI (smIPI) for Stage I–II Disease

For early-stage patients: [p.71]
- Low: 0 or 1 factor
- High: 2–4 factors

Risk factors: Age >60 years, serum LDH > normal, performance status 2–4, Stage II or IIE. [p.71]

### NCCN-IPI Score

The NCCN-IPI incorporates multiple factors: [p.71]

**Age-based scoring:** [p.71]
- >40 to ≤60 years: 1 point
- >60 to <75 years: 2 points
- ≥75 years: 3 points

**LDH normalized:** [p.71]
- >1 to ≤3: 1 point
- >3: 2 points

**Ann Arbor stage III–IV:** 1 point [p.71]
**Extranodal disease** (bone marrow, CNS, liver/GI tract, lung): 1 point [p.71]
**Performance status ≥2:** 1 point [p.71]

**Risk groups:** [p.71]
- Low: 0–1 points
- Low-intermediate: 2–3 points
- High-intermediate: 4–5 points
- High: ≥6 points

### CNS Disease Risk Assessment

A prognostic model identifies CNS disease risk: [p.72-73]

**Risk factors:** [p.72-73]
- Age >60 years
- Serum LDH > normal
- Performance status >1
- Stage III or IV
- Extranodal involvement >1 site
- Kidney or adrenal gland involvement

**Risk stratification:** [p.72-73]
- Low risk: 0–1 factors
- Intermediate-risk: 2–3 factors
- High-risk: 4–6 factors or kidney/adrenal gland involvement

### CNS Prophylaxis

**Role of CNS prophylaxis:** Remains controversial but can be considered in patients with high-risk factors. [p.72]

**Prophylaxis options:** [p.72]
- Systemic high-dose methotrexate (3–3.5 g/m2 for 2–4 cycles) during or after treatment
- Intrathecal (IT) methotrexate and/or cytarabine (4–8 doses) during or after treatment

**Additional indications for CNS prophylaxis (independent of CNS risk score):** [p.72]
- Testicular lymphoma
- Primary cutaneous DLBCL, leg type
- Stage IE DLBCL of the breast
- Kidney or adrenal gland involvement

## First-Line Therapy

### Stage I–II Disease (Excluding Extensive Mesenteric Disease)

#### Preferred Therapy

**RCHOP x 1 cycle (total of 4 cycles)** [p.63]
- Rituximab [p.63]
- Cyclophosphamide [p.63]
- Doxorubicin [p.63]
- Vincristine [p.63]
- Prednisone [p.63]

**Restaging with PET/CT after 3 cycles** [p.63]

**For smIPI >1:** Pola-R-CHP (Polatuzumab vedotin-piiq + rituximab + cyclophosphamide + doxorubicin + prednisone) (Category 1) [p.75]

#### Response Assessment and Further Therapy

**Complete response** (PET negative [5-PS 1–3]): [p.63]
- End-of-treatment restaging with PET/CT [p.63]

**Partial response** (PET positive [5-PS 4]): [p.63]
- RCHOP x 1–3 cycles (total of 4–6 cycles) ± ISRT [p.63]

**No response or progressive disease** (PET positive [5-PS 5]): [p.63]
- Repeat biopsy [p.63]
- RCHOP x 1–3 cycles (total of 4–6 cycles) ± ISRT [p.63]

### Stage I–II Bulky Disease (≥7.5 cm)

#### Initial Therapy

**RCHOP x 2–3 cycles (total of 6 cycles) ± ISRT** (initial dose) [p.64]
- Restage with PET/CT after 3–4 cycles [p.64]

**Preferred:** Pola-R-CHP for patients with smIPI >1 (Category 1) [p.75]

#### Response Pathways

**Complete response** (PET negative [5-PS 1–3]): [p.64]
- End-of-treatment restaging with PET/CT [p.64]
- Patients in first remission may be candidates for consolidation trials including HDT/ASCR [p.64]

**Partial response** (PET positive [5-PS 4]): [p.64]
- Repeat biopsy [p.64]
- RCHOP x 2–3 cycles (total of 6 cycles) ± ISRT (higher dose) [p.64]

**No response or progressive disease** (PET positive [5-PS 5]): [p.64]
- Repeat biopsy [p.64]

### Stage III–IV or Stage II with Extensive Mesenteric Disease

#### Initial Therapy (Category 1)

**RCHOP (6 cycles)** [p.65]
- Rituximab (Category 1) [p.75]
- Cyclophosphamide [p.75]
- Doxorubicin [p.75]
- Vincristine [p.75]
- Prednisone [p.75]

**Pola-R-CHP** (IPI ≥2) (Category 1) [p.75]
- Polatuzumab vedotin-piiq [p.75]
- Rituximab [p.75]
- Cyclophosphamide [p.75]
- Doxorubicin [p.75]
- Prednisone [p.75]

#### Response Restaging

**Restage with PET/CT or CT after 2–4 cycles** [p.65]

**Response categories:** [p.65]
- Complete response (PET negative [5-PS 1–3]) [p.65]
- Partial response (PET positive [5-PS 4–5]) [p.65]
- No response or progressive disease [p.65]

**End-of-treatment assessment:** Repeat all positive studies [p.65]

### Special Populations

#### Poor Left Ventricular Function, Very Frail Patients, Patients >80 Years with Comorbidities

**Available regimens:** [p.75]
- RCDOP (rituximab + cyclophosphamide + liposomal doxorubicin + vincristine + prednisone)
- RCEOP (rituximab + cyclophosphamide + etoposide + vincristine + prednisone)
- RGCVP (rituximab + gemcitabine + cyclophosphamide + vincristine + prednisone)
- RCEPP (rituximab + cyclophosphamide + etoposide + prednisone + procarbazine) (Category 2B)
- R-mini-CHOP

**Note:** Limited published data; used at NCCN Member Institutions. [p.75]

#### Concurrent CNS Disease Presentation

**Parenchymal involvement:** [p.75]
- Systemic high-dose methotrexate (≥3 g/m2) given with RCHOP cycle supported by growth factors
- Panel prefers day 15 of 21-day cycle for integration

**Leptomeningeal involvement:** [p.75]
- IT methotrexate/cytarabine; consider Ommaya reservoir placement
- Systemic high-dose methotrexate (3–3.5 g/m2) combined with RCHOP or as consolidation

## Follow-Up / Surveillance

### General Follow-Up Schedule

**Clinical assessment:** [p.63-64]
- H&P and labs every 3–6 months for 5 years
- Then annually or as clinically indicated

**Imaging:** [p.63-64]
- C/A/P CT scan with contrast no more often than every 6 months for 2 years after completion of treatment
- Then only as clinically indicated

**Surveillance imaging at 12 months:** Has treatment implications; used for monitoring asymptomatic patients. When disease is only visible on PET/CT (e.g., bone), proceed with PET/CT scans for surveillance. [p.63-64]

### Post-Relapse Follow-Up

**After treatment for relapsed/refractory disease:** [p.68-69]
- H&P and labs every 3–6 months for 5 years
- Then annually or as clinically indicated
- C/A/P CT scan with contrast no more often than every 6 months for 2 years
- Then only as clinically indicated

## ALK-Positive Large B-Cell Lymphomas (ALK+ LBCL)

### Definition and Characteristics

ALK-positive LBCL is a mature aggressive B-cell lymphoma characterized by: [p.73]
- Large cells with plasmablastic differentiation
- Cytoplasmic expression of ALK
- Lacking CD20 expression

**Differentiation from other entities:** Cytoplasmic ALK expression distinguishes this from other aggressive CD20-negative LBCL with plasmacytic differentiation such as plasmablastic lymphoma, primary effusion lymphoma (PEL), and HHV8-positive DLBCL. [p.73]

**Viral and immune status:** EBV and HHV8 are negative in ALK-positive LBCL; no association with immune deficiency. [p.73]

**Cytogenetics:** Most commonly demonstrates t(2;17)(p23;q23), denoting CLTC (clathrin heavy chain)/ALK fusion. [p.73]

### Clinical Presentation

**Demographics:** Median age at diagnosis approximately 40 years with strong male predominance. [p.73]

**Disease characteristics:** Most patients present with rapidly progressive disease at advanced stage; both nodal and extranodal involvement are common. [p.73]

### Initial Therapy

**Recommended approach:** Clinical trial is recommended if available. [p.73]

**Consolidative ISRT:** Preferred for localized disease. [p.73]

**Rituximab:** Not necessary as most are CD20 negative. [p.73]

**Systemic induction regimens:** [p.73]
- DA-EPOCH (doxorubicin + etoposide + prednisone + vincristine + cyclophosphamide)
- CHOEP (cyclophosphamide + doxorubicin + etoposide + vincristine + prednisone)
- CHOP (may be associated with suboptimal outcome; can be considered for early-stage disease receiving combined modality therapy)
- Mini-CHOP (for frail or older patients)

**Potentially toxic regimens:** Performance status and comorbidities should be considered: [p.73]
- HyperCVAD (cyclophosphamide + vincristine + doxorubicin + dexamethasone alternating with high-dose methotrexate + cytarabine)
- CODOX-M/IVAC (cyclophosphamide + vincristine + doxorubicin + methotrexate alternating with ifosfamide + etoposide + cytarabine)

### Relapsed/Refractory Disease

**Clinical trial:** Recommended if available. [p.73]

**Transplant-eligible patients:** Second-line platinum-based chemotherapy (without rituximab) followed by HDT/ASCR should be considered for chemosensitive disease. [p.73]

**Next-generation ALK inhibitors:** Responses have been observed with alectinib and lorlatinib, which should be favored over first-generation inhibitor crizotinib. [p.73]

**Post-ALK inhibitor consolidation:** Patients achieving complete response to a next-generation ALK inhibitor should be considered for allogeneic HCT. [p.73]

## Mediastinal Gray Zone Lymphoma (MGZL)

### Clinical and Pathologic Features

**Clinical presentation:** Present with large anterior mediastinal mass with or without supraclavicular lymph nodes; more common in males, presenting between 20–40 years. [p.74]

**Morphology:** [p.74]
- Expert hematopathology review is essential
- Diagnosis should not be made on core needle biopsy
- Large pleomorphic cells in diffusely fibrous stroma
- Typically larger and more pleomorphic than in PMBL, sometimes resembling lacunar or Hodgkin-like cells
- Necrosis without neutrophilic infiltrate is frequent

**Immunophenotype:** [p.74]
- Atypical immunophenotype with transitional features between PMBL and CHL
- Typical: CD45+, PAX5+, BOB.1+, OCT-2+, CD15+, CD20+, CD30+, CD79a+; CD10- and ALK-; BCL6 variably expressed and EBV usually negative
- If morphology resembles PMBL: CD20 dim/-; CD30+ and CD15+ suggest gray zone lymphoma
- If morphology resembles CHL: Strong uniform CD20+ (with other B-cell markers) and CD15- suggest MGZL

### Prognosis and Treatment

**Prognosis:** Worse than either CHL or PMBL has been suggested. [p.74]

**Treatment approach:** While there is no consensus, aggressive LBCL regimens are preferred. [p.74]

**Rituximab consideration:** If tumor cells are CD20+, addition of rituximab to chemotherapy should be considered. [p.74]

**Recommended regimens:** Data suggest anthracycline-based chemoimmunotherapy as recommended for DLBCL is helpful. [p.74]

**Localized disease:** Consolidative ISRT is preferred. [p.74]

## Important Procedural Notes

### Response Criteria

**Lugano Response Criteria for Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma:** Used to assess treatment response. [p.63-69]

**PET/CT interpretation:** Should be interpreted via PET 5-Point Scale (5-PS). [p.63-69]

### Biopsy and Restaging

**Repeat biopsy:** Should be strongly considered if PET-positive prior to additional therapy. If biopsy negative, follow PET-negative pathway. In cases where biopsy cannot be done or is unsafe, clinical judgment should be used. [p.63-65]

**End-of-treatment PET/CT timing:** Optimal timing unknown; waiting minimum of 8 weeks after RT to repeat PET/CT is suggested. False positives may occur due to post-treatment changes. [p.63-65]

**ctDNA-MRD assessment (Category 2B):** If end-of-treatment PET positive and biopsy not feasible, consider ctDNA-MRD assessment using test with detection limit of <1 part per million prior to additional therapy. If biopsy and/or ctDNA-MRD–negative, follow PET-negative pathway. [p.63-65]

### Radiation Therapy

**ISRT principles:** Detailed in NHODG-D section. [p.63-69]

**Consolidative ISRT:** May be given before or after transplant to sites of previous positive disease. [p.64-67]

## General Supportive Measures

**Tumor lysis syndrome:** Consider prophylaxis (see NHODG-B). [p.75, p.78]

**Monoclonal antibody and viral reactivation:** See NHODG-B. [p.75, p.78]

**Biosimilar substitution:** An FDA-approved biosimilar is an appropriate substitute for any recommended systemic biologic therapy. Rituximab and hyaluronidase human injection for subcutaneous use may be substituted for rituximab after patients have received first full dose of rituximab by IV infusion. [p.75, p.79]

**Palliative care:** See NCCN Guidelines for Palliative Care for management of patients not candidates for or declining active therapy. [p.63-69, p.78]

**Best supportive care:** Available for appropriate patients. [p.63-69, p.78]

## Treatment Decision Notes

**Rituximab in second-line therapy:** Should be included if there is relapse after reasonable remission (>6 months); however, rituximab can be omitted in patients with primary refractory disease. [p.79]

**CAR T-cell therapy guidance:** Refer to NCCN Guidance for Treatment of Patients with Chimeric Antigen Receptor (CAR) T-Cell Therapy (NHODG-E). [p.79]

**Bendamustine precautions:** In patients intended to receive CAR T-cell therapy or CD3 x CD20 bispecific antibody therapy, bendamustine should be used with caution; delay until after CAR-T leukapheresis. [p.79]

**CD20 status verification:** In setting of CD20-negative lymphomas, activity of CD3 x CD20 bispecific antibody therapy is unclear. Rebiopsy to confirm CD20 positivity is recommended prior to initiating CD3 x CD20 bispecific antibody therapy. [p.79]

**Sequential anti-CD19 therapy:** It is unclear if tafasitamab-cxix, loncastuximab tesirine-lpyl, or if any other CD19-directed therapy would have negative impact on efficacy of subsequent anti-CD19 CAR T-cell therapy. [p.79]

**CD30 positivity:** Responses with brentuximab vedotin have been seen in patients with low level of CD30 positivity; any level of CD30 positivity is acceptable for BV-based regimens. [p.79]

**Cardiac monitoring:** Inclusion of any anthracycline or anthracenedione in patients with impaired cardiac functioning should have more frequent cardiac monitoring. If additional anthracycline is administered after full course of therapy, careful cardiac monitoring is essential. Dexrazoxane may be added as cardioprotectant. [p.79]

**Dose modifications:** If upward dose adjustment is necessary, doxorubicin should be maintained at base dose and not increased. [p.79]

**High-dose methotrexate with DA-EPOCH:** Concurrent high-dose methotrexate with dose-adjusted EPOCH can result in unacceptable toxicities. [p.79]
