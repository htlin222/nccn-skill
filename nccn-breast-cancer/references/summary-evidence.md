## Summary of Key Evidence

- Treatment decisions for patients with breast cancer require joint exploration between patient and physician to select the most appropriate option from available alternatives [p.213]
- Evaluation, treatment, and follow-up recommendations are generally based on results from past and present clinical trials [p.213]
- No single clinical situation in breast cancer treatment has been fully optimized with respect to maximizing cure while simultaneously minimizing toxicity and disfigurement [p.213]
- Participation in prospective clinical trials allows patients to receive state-of-the-art treatment while contributing to improving treatment outcomes [p.213]

## Treatment for Possible Primary Breast Cancer

### MRI-Positive Disease
- Patients with MRI-positive breast disease should undergo evaluation with ultrasound or MRI-guided biopsy [p.213]
- Treatment follows according to clinical stage of the breast cancer [p.213]

### MRI-Negative Disease
- Treatment recommendations are based on nodal status [p.213]

### T0, N1, M0 Disease
Treatment options include [p.213]:
- Mastectomy plus axillary nodal dissection, or
- Axillary nodal dissection plus whole breast irradiation with or without nodal irradiation

Systemic therapy is given according to recommendations for stage II or III disease, including systemic chemotherapy, endocrine therapy, or trastuzumab [p.213]

### T0, N2–N3, M0 Disease
- Neoadjuvant chemotherapy, trastuzumab, and endocrine therapy should be considered [p.213]
- Followed by axillary nodal dissection and mastectomy as for patients with locally advanced disease [p.213]
