# Inflammatory Breast Cancer (IBC-1) -- Algorithm Reference

<!-- Source: NCCN Breast Cancer v2.2026, pages 113-114 -->
<!-- Chunk: inflammatory-breast-cancer-algorithm -->

## Diagnosis

**Clinical Presentation** [p.113]

Inflammatory Breast Cancer (IBC) is characterized by: [p.113]

1. Rapid onset (≤6 months) of erythema, edema, peau d'orange occupying ≥1/3 of the breast, with or without a palpable mass [p.113]
2. Pathologic confirmation of invasive breast cancer [p.113]
3. Dermal lymphatic involvement may be seen but is not required for the diagnosis [p.113]

## Workup

**Clinical and Pathologic Diagnosis of IBC** [p.113]

- History and physical exam by multidisciplinary team and obtain medical photography [p.113]
- CBC [p.113]
- Comprehensive metabolic panel, including LFTs and alkaline phosphatase [p.113]
- Pathology review [p.113]
  - The Panel endorses the College of American Pathologists Protocol for pathology reporting for all invasive and noninvasive carcinomas of the breast [p.113]
- Determination of tumor ER/PR status and HER2 status (see Principles of Biomarker Testing) [p.113]
- Fertility counseling if premenopausal [p.113]
- Genetic counseling if patient is at risk for hereditary breast cancer [p.113]
- Assess for distress [p.113]

**Imaging** [p.113]

- Bilateral diagnostic mammogram, ultrasound as necessary [p.113]
- Chest diagnostic CT ± contrast [p.113]
- Abdomen ± pelvis diagnostic CT with contrast or MRI with contrast [p.113]
- Bone scan or FDG-PET/CT [p.113]
  - If FDG-PET/CT is performed and clearly indicates bone metastasis on both the PET and CT component, bone scan or sodium fluoride PET/CT may not be needed [p.113]
  - FDG-PET/CT can be performed at the same time as diagnostic CT [p.113]
  - FDG-PET/CT is most helpful in situations where standard staging studies are equivocal or suspicious [p.113]
  - FDG-PET/CT may also be helpful in identifying unsuspected regional nodal disease and/or distant metastases when used in addition to standard staging studies [p.113]
- Breast MRI (optional) [p.113]

## Preoperative/Adjuvant Therapy Regimens

- **HER2-positive IBC**: A pertuzumab-containing regimen may be administered preoperatively [p.113]

## Response to Preoperative Therapy

**Responsive Disease** [p.114]

- Complete planned cytotoxic regimen course if not completed preoperatively plus endocrine treatment if ER-positive and/or PR-positive (sequential chemotherapy followed by endocrine therapy) [p.114]
- Complete up to one year of HER2-targeted therapy if HER2-positive **(Category 1)** [p.114]
  - May be administered concurrently with RT and with endocrine therapy if indicated [p.114]

**No Response to Preoperative Therapy** [p.114]

- Total mastectomy (skin-sparing and nipple-sparing mastectomy are contraindicated) + level I/II axillary dissection + RT to chest wall and comprehensive RNI with inclusion of any portion of the undissected axilla at risk ± delayed breast reconstruction [p.114]
- Consider additional cytotoxic chemotherapy and/or preoperative radiation [p.114]

## Follow-Up and Subsequent Treatment

**For Responsive Disease Post-Surgery** [p.114]

- Complete planned cytotoxic regimen course if not completed preoperatively plus endocrine treatment if ER-positive and/or PR-positive (sequential chemotherapy followed by endocrine therapy) [p.114]
- Complete up to one year of HER2-targeted therapy if HER2-positive (Category 1) [p.114]

**For No Response to Preoperative Therapy** [p.114]

- See responsive disease pathway above

**Recurrent/Advanced Disease** [p.114]

Patients with recurrent IBC should be treated according to the guideline for recurrence/stage IV (M1) disease. [p.114]

Patients may be candidates for multiple lines of cytotoxic therapy to palliate advanced breast cancer. At each reassessment clinicians should assess: [p.114]
- Value of ongoing treatment
- Risks and benefits of an additional line of cytotoxic therapy
- Patient performance status
- Patient preferences through a shared decision-making process

---

**Note**: All recommendations are category 2A unless otherwise indicated. [p.113, p.114]
