## Overview

Gene expression assays provide prognostic and therapy-predictive information that complements T, N, M and biomarker information [p.81]. Use of these assays is not required for staging [p.81]. The 21-gene assay (Oncotype Dx) is **Preferred** by the NCCN Breast Cancer Panel for prognosis and prediction of chemotherapy benefit [p.82]. Other prognostic gene expression assays can provide prognostic information but the ability to predict chemotherapy benefit is unknown [p.82].

Note: All recommendations are (Category 2A) unless otherwise indicated [p.81].

## 21-Gene (Oncotype Dx) Treatment Recommendations

### For Postmenopausal Patients with pN0 and pN1 (1-3 Positive Nodes)

**Recurrence Score <26** [p.82]

- Patients with T1b/c-2, pN0, HR-positive, HER2-negative tumors, with recurrence scores between 0-10 have a risk of distant recurrence of <4% and those with recurrence scores 11-25 derived no benefit from the addition of chemotherapy to endocrine therapy in the prospective TAILORx study [p.82].
- Postmenopausal patients with pT1-3, pN1, HR-positive, HER2-negative, with recurrence scores <26 derived no benefit from the addition of chemotherapy to endocrine therapy in the prospective RxPONDER study [p.82].

**Recurrence Score ≥26** [p.82]

- In postmenopausal patients with pT1-3, HR-positive, HER2-negative, and pN0 and pN1 (1-3 positive nodes) tumors and a recurrence score ≥26, the addition of chemotherapy to endocrine therapy is recommended [p.82].

### For Premenopausal Patients with pN0

**Recurrence Score ≤15** [p.82]

- Premenopausal patients with T1b/c-2, pN0, HR-positive, HER2-negative tumors with recurrence scores <16 derived no benefit from the addition of chemotherapy to endocrine therapy in the prospective TAILORx study [p.82].

**Recurrence Score 16-25** [p.82]

- In premenopausal patients with recurrence scores between 16-25, a small benefit from the addition of chemotherapy could not be ruled out, but it is unclear if the benefit was due to the ovarian suppression effect promoted by chemotherapy in premenopausal patients [p.82].
- For this group, consider chemotherapy followed by endocrine therapy or alternatively, OFS combined with either tamoxifen or an aromatase inhibitor [p.82].

**Recurrence Score ≥26** [p.82]

- In premenopausal patients with HR-positive, HER2-negative, and pN0 tumors and a recurrence score ≥26, the addition of chemotherapy to endocrine therapy is recommended [p.82].

### For Premenopausal Patients with 1-3 Positive Nodes

**Recurrence Score <26** [p.82]

- In premenopausal patients with pT1-3 and pN1 (1-3 positive nodes) tumors and a recurrence score <26, the addition of chemotherapy to endocrine therapy was associated with a lower rate of distant recurrence compared with endocrine monotherapy but it is unclear if the benefit was due to the ovarian suppression effects promoted by chemotherapy [p.82].
- For this group of patients, consider chemotherapy followed by endocrine therapy or alternatively, OFS combined with either tamoxifen or an aromatase inhibitor [p.82].

**Recurrence Score ≥26** [p.82]

- In premenopausal patients with HR-positive, HER2-negative, pT1-3 and pN1 (1-3 positive nodes) tumors and a recurrence score ≥26, the addition of chemotherapy to endocrine therapy is recommended [p.82].

## 70-Gene (MammaPrint) Treatment Recommendations

### For pN0 and 1-3 Positive Nodes

**High Genomic Risk** [p.83]

- Patients with high clinical risk and low genomic risk were randomly assigned to receive chemotherapy (n = 749) or not (n = 748); this was the intention-to-treat population [p.83].
- The 8-year estimates for distant metastasis-free survival in the intention-to-treat population were 92.0% (95% CI, 89.6-93.8) for chemotherapy versus 89.4% (86.8-91.5) for no chemotherapy (HR, 0.66; 95% CI, 0.48-0.92) [p.83].
- An exploratory analysis confined to the subset of patients with HR-positive, HER2-negative disease (1358 [90.7%] of 1497 randomly assigned patients, of whom 676 received chemotherapy and 682 did not) shows different effects of chemotherapy administration on 8-year distant metastasis-free survival according to age: 93.6% (95% CI, 89.3-96.3) with chemotherapy versus 88.6% (83.5-92.3) without chemotherapy in 464 patients aged ≤50 years (absolute difference 5.0 percentage points [SE, 2.8; 95% CI, -0.5 to 10.4]) and 90.2% (86.8-92.7) versus 90.0% (86.6-92.6) in 894 females >50 years (absolute difference 0.2 percentage points [2.1, -4.0 to 4.4]) [p.83].
- The 8-year distant metastasis-free survival in the exploratory analysis by nodal status in these patients was 91.7% (95% CI, 88.1-94.3) with chemotherapy and 89.2% (85.2-92.2) without chemotherapy in 699 patients who are node-negative (absolute difference 2.5 percentage points [SE, 2.3; 95% CI, -2.1 to 7.2]) and 91.2% (87.2-94.0) versus 89.9% (85.8-92.8) for 658 patients with 1-3 positive nodes (absolute difference 1.3 percentage points [2.4, -3.5 to 6.1]) [p.83].
- Non-randomized data suggest that patients with MammaPrint-high 2 (MP-H2) tumors have significantly worse outcomes when treated with taxane-based regimens without anthracyclines compared to MP-H1 tumors, suggesting benefit of addition of anthracyclines to taxane-based chemotherapy in MP-H2 tumors [p.83].

## 50-Gene (Prosigna) Treatment Recommendations

### For pN0 Tumors

**Low Risk Score (0-40)** [p.83]

- For patients with T1 and T2 HR-positive, HER2-negative, pN0 tumors, a risk of recurrence score in the low range, regardless of T size, places the tumor into the same prognostic category as T1a-T1b, N0, M0 [p.83].

**Intermediate Risk Score (41-60)** [p.83]

(Treatment implications not specified in source)

**High Risk Score (61-100)** [p.83]

(Treatment implications not specified in source)

### For pN1 Tumors (1-3 Positive Nodes)

**Low Risk Score (0-40)** [p.83]

- In patients with HR-positive, HER2-negative, pN+ tumors (1-3 positive lymph nodes) with low risk of recurrence score, treated with endocrine therapy alone, the distant recurrence risk was <3.5% at 10 years and no distant recurrence was seen at 10 years in the TransATAC study in a similar group [p.83].

## 12-Gene (EndoPredict) Treatment Recommendations

### For pN0 and 1-3 Positive Nodes

**Low Risk Score (≤3.3)** [p.83]

- For patients with T1 and T2 HR-positive, HER2-negative, and pN0 tumors, a 12-gene low-risk score, regardless of T size, places the tumor into the same prognostic category as T1a-T1b, N0, M0 [p.83].
- In ABCSG 6/8, patients in the low-risk group had risk of distant recurrence of 4% at 10 years and in the TransATAC study, patients with 1-3 positive nodes in the low-risk group had a 5.6% risk of distant recurrence at 10 years [p.83].
- The assay is prognostic in patients treated with endocrine and chemo-endocrine [p.83].

**High Risk Score (>3.3)** [p.83]

(Treatment implications not specified in source)

## Gene Expression Assays for Consideration of Extended Adjuvant Systemic Therapy

### Breast Cancer Index (BCI)

The benefit of testing BCI (H/I) for extended adjuvant endocrine therapy is unknown in patients who had OFS, CDK4/6 inhibitors, or olaparib in addition to adjuvant endocrine therapy [p.84].

#### BCI (H/I) Low - pN0

**For pT1-T2 HR-Positive, HER2-Negative, pN0 Tumors** [p.84]

- A BCI (H/I) in the low-risk range (0-5), regardless of T size, places the tumor into the same prognostic category as pT1a-T1b, pN0, M0 [p.84].
- Patients with BCI (H/I) low demonstrated a lower risk of distant recurrence (compared to BCI [H/I] high) and no significant improvement in disease-free survival (DFS) or OS compared to the control arm in terms of extending endocrine therapy duration [p.84].

#### BCI (H/I) Low - pN1 (1-3 Positive Nodes)

**For HR-Positive, HER2-Negative pN1 Tumors** [p.84]

- For patients with HR-positive, HER2-negative, and pN1 tumors (1-3 positive lymph nodes), an optimized BCI model integrating tumor size and grade is prognostic for distant recurrence at 10 years [p.84].

#### BCI (H/I) High

**For pT1 HR-Positive, HER2-Negative pN0 or pN1 Tumors (1-3 Positive Lymph Nodes)** [p.84]

- A BCI (H/I) high (5.1-10) demonstrated significant rates of late distant recurrence [p.84].
- In secondary analyses of the MA.17, Trans-aTTom, and IDEAL trials, patients with HR-positive, pT1-T3, pN0 or pN1 tumors (1-3 positive lymph nodes) who had a BCI (H/I) high demonstrated significant improvements in DFS when adjuvant endocrine therapy was extended, compared to the control arm [p.84].

## Special Considerations

Note on RxPONDER Trial Population: In the overall study population of the RxPONDER trial, 10.3% had high-grade disease and 9.2% had 3 involved nodes [p.81].

Note on Special Considerations: Special Considerations for Breast Cancer in Males (Sex Assigned at Birth) (BINV-J) [p.81].

Note on MammaPrint Outcomes: Postmenopausal patients with ultralow risk in the Stockholm Tamoxifen trial had a 20-year breast cancer specific survival of 97% with 2-5 years of tamoxifen [p.83]. Patients with an ultralow risk in the MINDACT trial have shown 8-year breast cancer-specific survival >95.6% [p.83].
